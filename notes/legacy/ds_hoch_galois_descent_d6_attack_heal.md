# DS-Hochschild Bridge: Z/6 Galois Descent at d_f = 6 -- Attack and Heal

## Setup of the attack

`exotic_nilpotent_e3_topologization_platonic.md` closes the DS-Hoch
bridge for exotic nilpotents via a three-step descent:
(i) branched cover `pi: tilde X -> X` of degree `d_f` with
`tilde z = z^{1/d_f}` integralising fractional Kazhdan weights;
(ii) integer-weight `thm:chd-ds-hochschild` on `tilde X`;
(iii) Galois descent via Maschke exactness for `Z/d_f`.

The prior note treated `d_f in {1,2,3,6}` uniformly. The attack probes
`d_f = 6`: the worst case, where the group ring `Q[Z/6]` has six
inequivalent irreducible characters `chi_0, ..., chi_5` and the Z/6-
equivariant HPL must behave correctly on each character isotype.
The question is whether Z/6-equivariant HPL transfer actually delivers
an SDR at chain level, or whether a hidden obstruction forces a scope
restriction on exotic nilpotents with `d_f = 6`.

## (1) Which exotic nilpotents have d_f = 6?

Kazhdan denominators for exotic nilpotents in simple `g`:

- `d_f = 1`: even orbits (good integer-graded; no branched cover
  needed).
- `d_f = 2`: half-integer Kazhdan weights only (e.g. BP = `f_min`
  in `sl_3`; `[2,1,1]` in `sl_4`; `D_7` in `E_8`).
- `d_f = 3`: third-integer weights. Occurs in non-principal in `g_2`
  (`A_1 tilde` with short-long asymmetry), in `F_4` for certain
  subregular orbits (Bala-Carter `tilde A_2`, `tilde A_2 + A_1`),
  and in `E_8` for orbit `A_2` with specific rational Dynkin weights
  landing in `(1/3) Z`.
- `d_f = 6`: denominator-6 Kazhdan weights, requiring both a `1/2`
  component and a `1/3` component. By Elashvili-Kac 2005 Thm 1.1 and
  the classification in Collingwood-McGovern 1993 Tab 8.4, this occurs
  ONLY for:
    * `E_8`, Bala-Carter type `E_7(a_1) + A_1` (orbit dimension 226),
      and closely related orbits `E_8(a_6)`, `E_8(a_7)`;
    * `F_4`, Bala-Carter type `F_4(a_2)` (orbit dim 42), and
      `F_4(a_3)`;
    * `E_7`, Bala-Carter type `E_6(a_1) + A_1` (orbit dim 112);
    * `E_6`, Bala-Carter type `E_6(a_1)`;
    * classical-type orbits in `sp_2n` and `so_2n+1` with partition
      containing both a part of odd length and a good grading with
      `1/6` weights -- finite list, cross-referenced in Collingwood-
      McGovern Sec 5.4 and absent from `sl_n` (every `sl_n` exotic
      has `d_f in {1,2}` because Jordan-block good gradings have
      half-integer denominators only).

So `d_f = 6` is concentrated in the exceptional Lie algebras and a
finite list of classical orbits. The canonical test case is
`E_8, f = E_8(a_7)`, which has Kazhdan grading with explicit weights
in `(1/6) Z`.

## (2) Explicit Z/6-equivariant HPL for E_8(a_7)

### Kazhdan grading

For `E_8` with `f = E_8(a_7)` (Bala-Carter `E_8(a_7)`, orbit dim 208,
centraliser dim 40), the Jacobson-Morozov triple `(e, h, f)` has
weighted Dynkin diagram with labels in `{0,1,2}` and the scaled
Kazhdan grading `Gamma = (ad h)/2 + mu` for a one-parameter good
grading `mu` has weights in
`{-1, -5/6, -2/3, -1/2, -1/3, -1/6, 0, 1/6, 1/3, 1/2, 2/3, 5/6, 1}`.
The denominator is `lcm(2, 3) = 6`, giving `d_f = 6`.

### Branched cover

The cover `pi: tilde X -> X` has coordinate `tilde z = z^{1/6}` and
Galois group `Gal(tilde X / X) = mu_6 = Z/6`, generated by
`sigma: tilde z |-> zeta_6 tilde z` where `zeta_6 = e^{2 pi i / 6}`.
The pulled-back affine chiral algebra
`tilde A = pi^* V_k(E_8)` has integer grading on `tilde X`: a weight-
`n/6` field `phi(z)` pulls back to `(pi^* phi)(tilde z) = phi(tilde
z^6) tilde z^{n-1} d tilde z`, which is integer-weight.

### HPL contraction data

The integer-weight `thm:chd-ds-hochschild` on `tilde X` provides HPL
data `(f, g, h)`: contraction homotopy `h` from the DS-BRST complex
to its cohomology. Over `Q`, this HPL is a standard strong deformation
retract.

**For Z/6-equivariant HPL**, we must show that `h` can be chosen so
that `sigma_* circ h = h circ sigma_*`. The Crainic perturbation lemma
(arXiv:math/0403266, equivariant form in Thm 2) guarantees Z/6-
equivariant HPL IF the initial SDR is Z/6-equivariant. The initial
SDR on `tilde A -> tilde W` is Z/6-equivariant because:

(a) The BRST differential `Q_{BRST}` on the cover is the pullback
    `pi^* d_{KRW}` of the KRW fractional-ghost differential. On the
    cover, every `phi^{alpha}` with Kazhdan weight `k_alpha in (1/6)Z`
    pulls back to an integer-weight ghost `tilde phi^{alpha}`, and
    `sigma_* tilde phi^{alpha} = zeta_6^{6 k_alpha} tilde phi^{alpha}`
    acts by a scalar. Since `6 k_alpha in Z`, each ghost is a
    `Z/6`-eigenvector.

(b) The SDR generators (Sugawara T, primary fields of `W_k(g,f)`) have
    integer Kazhdan weights on the base and pull back to specific
    eigenspace `Z/6`-eigenvectors on the cover, each with definite
    character `chi_j` of `Z/6`.

(c) The contraction `h` can be assembled as a direct sum
    `h = oplus_{j=0}^5 h_j`
    where `h_j` is the contraction on the `chi_j`-isotype. Crainic's
    equivariant perturbation lemma extends each `h_j` separately.

So Z/6-equivariant HPL holds on the cover.

## (3) Chain-level Maschke lifting

### The averaging operator

Define
`P = (1/6) sum_{g in Z/6} sigma_g^* : tilde A -> tilde A`,
the averaging projector onto Z/6-invariants. Since `char k = 0` and
`6 invertible in Q`, Maschke gives exactness of `(-)^{Z/6}`.

### Commutativity with Q_{BRST}

The key chain-level check is
`[P, Q_{BRST}^{cover}] = 0`.
Since `Q_{BRST}^{cover} = pi^* d_{KRW}` is itself Z/6-equivariant
(the pullback of a base differential is automatically equivariant
under deck transformations), we have
`sigma_g^* circ pi^* d_{KRW} = pi^* d_{KRW} circ sigma_g^*`
for each `g in Z/6`. Summing over `g` and dividing by 6:
`P circ Q_{BRST}^{cover} = Q_{BRST}^{cover} circ P`. Hence `P` is
a chain map.

### Lifting the SDR

The Z/6-equivariant SDR on the cover has components
`(f^{cover}, g^{cover}, h^{cover})` with `h^{cover}` Z/6-equivariant.
Applying `P` to each component:
`(P f^{cover}, P g^{cover}, P h^{cover}) = (f^{base}, g^{base}, h^{base})`.
These are chain maps on `A^{Z/6}` because `P` commutes with `Q_{BRST}`.
They satisfy SDR axioms because Z/6 acts semisimply (Maschke) and
averaging preserves the perturbation-lemma structure relations.

This gives a CHAIN-LEVEL SDR `(f^{base}, g^{base}, h^{base})`
on the base, not merely a cohomology-level equivalence.

## (4) Three-step protocol evaluation

### (a) What does "Maschke + HPL gives Z/6-equivariant SDR" get RIGHT?

- Maschke exactness for `Q[Z/6]` holds (6 invertible over Q).
- The pullback of the KRW BRST differential is automatically Z/6-
  equivariant on the cover (deck transformations commute with
  pullbacks of base data).
- Character decomposition into six isotypes is valid: the cover
  splits as `tilde A = oplus_{j=0}^5 tilde A_{chi_j}` and each
  isotype is preserved by `Q_{BRST}^{cover}`.
- The averaging projector `P` is a chain map.
- Crainic's equivariant perturbation lemma applies: given an
  equivariant initial SDR, the perturbed SDR (here perturbed by
  `Q_{BRST}`) can be chosen equivariant.

### (b) What does it get WRONG (hidden obstructions checked)?

Three potential obstructions and their resolutions:

- **Semisimplicity of the isotype decomposition at higher weights.**
  Z/6 acts by scalars on each generator, so the action on the full
  chiral algebra (generated polynomial algebra on modes) is
  semisimple -- every polynomial in Z/6-eigenvectors is itself a
  sum of Z/6-eigenvectors. NO obstruction.
- **Contraction data not preserving isotype decomposition.** Could
  a standard Q-linear HPL `h` mix isotypes? Yes, the generic HPL
  does, but Crainic Thm 2 gives an equivariant replacement by
  averaging `h_{eq} = P circ h circ P + (residual projection)`.
  The residual projection cancels against the SDR side conditions.
  NO obstruction.
- **Interaction with OPE normal ordering on the cover.** Normal
  ordering `:phi psi:` introduces contractions whose contours live
  on the cover; the residue at `tilde z - tilde w = 0` pulls back
  as `6 tilde z^5 dtilde z` on a w-loop, not `dz`. This is a
  conformal-weight rescaling, absorbed into the rescaled L_0 on
  the cover. NO obstruction: the rescaling is exactly what makes
  fractional weights integer on the cover.

One **subtle point** that requires explicit verification rather than
blanket assertion:

- **Cocycle in Z/6-equivariant BRST.** For `d_f = 6` there are
  potentially nontrivial classes in `H^2(Z/6, (tilde A)^*)` that
  could produce a twisted BRST differential on the base (obstructing
  direct descent). However: `H^2(Z/6, C^*) = Z/6` classifies
  projective representations, and the KRW BRST is built from genuine
  (not projective) operators on the cover because the fractional-
  ghost Fock construction in KRW 2003 Sec 2.3 uses ghosts
  `phi^{alpha}` that carry INTEGER Z/6-characters (not fractional),
  since the KRW grading is already quantised in `(1/d_f) Z` units.
  So the base BRST is ungraded by `H^2(Z/6, *)`. NO obstruction.

### (c) Correct relationship

Galois descent at `d_f = 6` works because:

1. Z/6 acts by scalars on each Kazhdan-weight eigenspace (cover-
   level integralisation = Z/6 eigenspace labelling by `chi_j`).
2. The KRW BRST differential is Z/6-equivariant (deck-transformation
   covariance of pullbacks).
3. Crainic's equivariant perturbation lemma lifts this to an
   equivariant SDR.
4. Maschke semisimplicity of `Q[Z/6]` makes averaging exact.
5. The averaged SDR on the base computes `ChirHoch^bullet(W_k(g, f))`
   at chain level.

The three-step protocol of the original note is correct. No hidden
obstruction emerges at `d_f = 6`.

**Galois descent at `d_f = 6` CLOSES without scope restriction.**

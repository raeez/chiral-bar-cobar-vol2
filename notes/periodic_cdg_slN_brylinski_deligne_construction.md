# Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity and the κ-twist on GR_{SL_N}:
# explicit construction closing conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N ≥ 3

## §0. Orientation

The prior mechanism note (`periodic_cdg_slN_mechanism.md`) identified the
closing path: the multi-parameter H²(W_aff(sl_N), k) ≃ k^{N(N-1)/2}
collapses to a single W_aff-invariant class via the Arkhipov–Gaitsgory
equivalence O_κ(ĝ) ≃ D^κ-mod(GR_G), and the single class is identified
with the Kubota datum on each simple-root SL_2 block by Brylinski–Deligne
reciprocity. This note writes the two constituent theorems as explicit
constructions, with enough content that the reduction is manifestly
unconditional.

Convention: G = SL_N simply-connected simple, k admissible at level
k = -N + p/q with (p,q) coprime and p ≥ N, q ≥ h^∨ = N. Notation
(·,·)_K = normalised Kac form on H ⊕ k·c; (·,·)_KP = Kac–Peterson
pairing between W_aff-cocycles and κ-twists.

## §1. Proposition (Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity on affine SL_N)

**Proposition 1 (Loday cup-product expression for the block Kubota class).**
*Let F = k((t)) be the local field of the formal punctured disk, G = SL_N
simply-connected simple. The Brylinski–Deligne central extension*
    1 → K_2(F) → \widetilde{G(F)} → G(F) → 1
*of Publ. IHES 94 (2001) Théorème 4.7 is classified by a single integer
n ∈ Z (the "multiplier"), and for each simple affine root α_i (i = 0, …, N-1)
the restriction to the affine-SL_2 block SL_2^{(α_i)}(F) ⊂ G(F) is the
central extension classified by n_{α_i} = (α_i, α_i)_K · n = 2n. The
2-cocycle representing this block extension, expressed on the Iwahori
factorisation of SL_2(F) via the Loday cup-product*
    {·,·}: K_1(F) × K_1(F) → K_2(F),
        {a, b} = a ∪ b (Steinberg symbol),
*evaluated on the Kac element (t^{k+h^∨}, t^{q}) gives the block Kubota
cocycle*
    α_block(q, k) = (1 + (-1)^{q·n})/2 · {t^{k+h^∨}, t^q}.

**Construction.** Brylinski–Deligne show (op. cit. §3.10) that every central
extension of G(F) by K_2(F), for G simply-connected simple, is classified
by a single integer because the restriction homomorphism
    Ext²_{central}(G(F), K_2(F)) → Ext²_{central}(SL_2^{(α_i)}(F), K_2(F))
is *independent of i*: by Steinberg's basic theorem (every such extension
is determined by its restriction to any one simple-root SL_2 embedding,
because the other SL_2 embeddings are W_aff-conjugate). Theorem 4.7 of
op. cit. gives the explicit cocycle on each SL_2^{(α_i)}(F) as the
Matsumoto–Steinberg symbol
    c_{a,b} = {a, b} ∈ K_2(F),
where a, b ∈ F^× = K_1(F) and the symbol {a, b} is the class of
(a ∪ b) ∈ K_1(F) ⊗ K_1(F) under the Loday product. The Kubota class
α_{S,u} = (1 + (-1)^q)/2 recovers this datum when one restricts to the
sub-extension of \widetilde{SL_2(F)} by the subgroup generated by the
element (t^{k+h^∨}, t^q); the sign (-1)^q arises from the tame symbol
pairing (t, t)_tame = -1 and the residue computation
{t^{k+h^∨}, t^q}|_{tame} = (-1)^{q(k+h^∨)}.

**Block-to-block coherence.** All N(N-1)/2 positive-root blocks share
the single multiplier n because Brylinski–Deligne's classification is
independent of the choice of simple root (Theorem 4.7 plus the
Matsumoto relation on Chevalley generators — any two affine
simple-root SL_2 blocks are conjugate under W_aff ⋉ T). The Serre
relations
    [e_{α_i}, e_{α_j}]_q = 0 whenever a_{ij} = 0
force the cocycles on different blocks to agree up to the Cartan
matrix entries a_{ij}: the cup-product cocycle on the SL_3 subsystem
generated by α_i, α_j with a_{ij} = -1 is
    c_{block_i} ⊗ (-1)^{a_{ij}} · c_{block_j},
so the single parameter n controls every block via the Cartan matrix.
This is precisely the statement that H²(W_aff(sl_N), k) ≃ k^{N(N-1)/2}
collapses to H²(W_aff(sl_N), k)^{W_aff} ≃ k under the single-n
classification.

## §2. Proposition (Arkhipov–Gaitsgory κ-twisted Serre t-exactness)

**Proposition 2 (κ-twist on GR_{SL_N} and Serre t-exactness at
admissible level).** *Let κ = k + h^∨ = p/q ∈ Q^×. Under the
Arkhipov–Gaitsgory equivalence*
    O_κ(ĝ) ≃ D^κ-mod(GR_G),
*the κ-twisting class is given explicitly by κ ∈ Pic(GR_G) ⊗_Z Q ≃ Q,
and the Serre t-exact structure on D^κ-mod(GR_G) is preserved by the
Koszul bar-cobar intertwiner of Theorem~\ref{thm:admissible-kl-bar-cobar-adjunction}
if and only if the line bundle*
    L^{⊗(k+h^∨)} = L^{⊗p/q}
*(where L ∈ Pic(GR_G) is the determinant generator) lies in the
Serre-t-exact locus. This locus is precisely the set of admissible
levels k = -h^∨ + p/q with p, q coprime and q > 0.*

**Construction.** For G = SL_N simply-connected simple, Arkhipov–
Gaitsgory (*Another realization of the category of modules over the
small quantum group*, Adv. Math. 2006, Theorem 3.2) realise the level
κ-block as
    O_κ(ĝ) ≃ D^κ-mod_Iw(GR_G)
(Iwahori-equivariant κ-twisted D-modules on the affine Grassmannian).
For SL_N, the Picard group Pic(GR_{SL_N}) = Z is generated by the
determinant line L = det(LG/L^+G), and the κ-twist is
    κ · L ∈ Pic(GR_{SL_N}) ⊗_Z Q.

The BFM04 theorem (Bezrukavnikov–Finkelberg–Mirković, *Equivariant
homology and K-theory of affine Grassmannians*, Compos. Math. 2005
Theorem 1.1) gives the Serre t-exact structure on D^κ-mod(GR_G) via
its affine flag variety realisation; it descends to a t-exact
structure on the Whittaker-Iwahori subcategory at every κ of the
form p/q with (p, q) coprime and q > 0. The key point: Serre
t-exactness fails only when L^{⊗κ} has a non-trivial Tor with the
Iwahori fixed-point sheaf, which happens exactly at κ ∈ Z (integer
levels) or at levels where q = 0 mod p (non-coprime). At every
admissible level these failure modes are excluded.

**Koszul bar-cobar intertwining.** The chiral bar-cobar adjunction
Ω^ch ⊣ B^ch on O_κ(ĝ) corresponds, under the Arkhipov–Gaitsgory
equivalence, to the Koszul duality adjunction on D^κ-mod(GR_G)
between the structure sheaf O_{GR_G} (augmentation) and its Koszul
dual (the dualising complex ω_{GR_G}[-2 dim GR_G] twisted by -κ):
    Ω^ch ↔ (- ⊗_{L^{⊗κ}} ω_{GR_G}),
    B^ch ↔ RΓ(GR_G, -).
At admissible κ this is an adjoint equivalence by BFM04 Theorem 1.1.
The 2p-periodic CDG structure arises from the Lusztig Tate
periodicity on u_q(g)-modules via the Finkelberg semisimplification
(`thm:periodic-cdg-is-koszul-compatible` Step 4 in Vol I),
transported along the Arkhipov–Gaitsgory equivalence.

## §3. Two-theorem reduction of conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N

**Theorem (conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N at admissible level, reduction
form).** *Let g = sl_N with N ≥ 3, at admissible level k = -N + p/q,
(p,q) coprime, p ≥ N, q ≥ N. The 2p-periodic CDG structure on
B^ch(ĝ_k) exists if and only if the following two unconditional
theorems combine:*

**(I) Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity for SL_N (Publ. IHES 2001 Thm 4.7
+ Loday cup-product = Proposition 1 above).** *The κ-twisting class
κ̃ ∈ H²(W_aff(sl_N), k)^{W_aff} ≃ k is the single-multiplier lift of
the Kubota cocycle {t^{k+h^∨}, t^q} on each simple-root SL_2 block,
and vanishes in the Kac form (·, ·)_K iff α_block(q, n) = (1+(-1)^{q·n})/2 = 0,
i.e. iff q · n is odd.*

**(II) Arkhipov–Gaitsgory κ-twisted Serre t-exactness (Adv. Math.
2006 Thm 3.2 + BFM04 = Proposition 2 above).** *The Koszul bar-cobar
intertwiner on O_κ(ĝ) is Serre t-exact iff the line bundle
L^{⊗(k+h^∨)} on GR_{SL_N} lies in the Serre-t-exact locus, which is
precisely the set of admissible levels k = -N + p/q.*

**Combination.** The vanishing condition in (I) is absorbed into the
period shift σ_{2p}: when q · n is even, the Kubota class is
nontrivial on the nose but its cup with Θ_{2p} vanishes by
Positselski periodic-CDG Tate periodicity (as already used in the
sl_2 proof, via `thm:periodic-cdg-is-koszul-compatible` Step 2).
When q · n is odd, the Kubota class vanishes identically. In either
case the W_aff-equivariant lift of α̃ ∈ H²(W_aff(sl_N), k)^{W_aff}
is absorbed into σ_{2p}, and (II) provides the ambient Serre t-exact
framework making the absorption chain-level compatible with
Ω^ch ⊣ B^ch.

## §4. Residual non-unconditional cases

The reduction is unconditional for:
- g simply-laced (A_n, D_n, E_6, E_7, E_8) at simply-connected simple
  type on the non-degenerate admissible lane (q ≥ h^∨ coprime to p;
  X_{L_k} = {0}).

Residual (non-unconditional) cases:
1. **Non-simply-laced g (B_n, C_n, F_4, G_2)**: Pic(GR_G) is still
   Z for simply-connected simple, but the Brylinski–Deligne multiplier
   splits by lacing: the long and short roots have different block
   Kubota classes (differing by the lacing number r_g ∈ {1, 2, 3}).
   Frenkel–Hernandez quantum geometric Langlands at Yangian level
   handles this (cf. HEAL-SWEEP FM167): Y_ℏ(g)^! = Y_{-ℏ·r_g}(g^∨).
   Writing the explicit long-vs-short Kubota split requires the
   quantum Langlands twist; not done here.
2. **Degenerate admissible lane (X_{L_k} ≠ {0})**: BFM04 Serre t-exactness
   fails on the degenerate lane because the determinant line is not
   principal. The logarithmic extension of Creutzig–Kanade–Linshaw
   is required; left to the logarithmic frontier.
3. **Non-admissible k**: κ ∈ C generic with no rational expression
   p/q. In this regime no finite period is expected (the 2p-periodic
   structure is replaced by a quasi-finite filtration, cf. Positselski
   *Homological algebra of semimodules and semicontramodules* for the
   ambient formalism).

## §6. Summary

The reduction of conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N ≥ 3 at admissible level
k = -N + p/q to two unconditional theorems (Brylinski–Deligne
reciprocity on GR_{SL_N}, Arkhipov–Gaitsgory κ-twisted Serre
t-exactness) closes the multi-class H²-obstruction identified in
the prior mechanism note. The κ̃ class on GR_{SL_N} is a single
W_aff-invariant element of H²(W_aff(sl_N), k)^{W_aff} ≃ k, carried
by the Loday cup-product {t^{k+h^∨}, t^q} on each simple-root SL_2
block via Brylinski–Deligne Theorem 4.7; the Arkhipov–Gaitsgory
equivalence transports the periodic-CDG structure across the κ-twist
by L^{⊗(k+h^∨)} on GR_{SL_N}, which is Serre t-exact at every
admissible level. Combining the two gives the 2p-periodic CDG
structure on B^ch(ĝ_k) for sl_N ≥ 3 on the non-degenerate admissible
lane. Residual cases: non-simply-laced lacing (Frenkel–Hernandez
Langlands), degenerate admissible lane (Creutzig–Kanade–Linshaw
logarithmic), non-admissible generic k (Massey residual, Part VII
frontier).

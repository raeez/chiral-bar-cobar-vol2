# Higher Massey products on the Yangian classical r-matrix: the odd-weight tower

## Setup

Let `t_n` be the Drinfeld–Kohno Lie algebra on `n` strands, generated over `Q` by
`Ω_{ij}` for `1 ≤ i < j ≤ n` subject to the infinitesimal braid relations

```
[Ω_{ij}, Ω_{kl}] = 0         for {i,j} ∩ {k,l} = ∅,
[Ω_{ij}, Ω_{ik} + Ω_{jk}] = 0  for {i,j,k} distinct,
```

and let `t_n^{[w]}` denote the weight-`w` piece in the bracket-length grading
(depth `w` = `w` nested brackets of generators). The centre of `t_n` is generated
in depth 1 by `Ω := Σ_{i<j} Ω_{ij}` for `n ≥ 3`. The quotient
`\bar t_3 := t_3 / center` is a free Lie algebra on two generators, which we
denote `a = Ω_{12}` and `b = Ω_{23}` (so `Ω_{13} = -(a+b)` in the quotient). The
Witt dimensions of `\bar t_3^{[w]}` are computed by the formula

```
dim_Q \bar t_3^{[w]} = (1/w) Σ_{d | w} μ(d) · 2^{w/d},
```

giving the sequence `2, 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18, 30, 56, ...` for `w = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5,
6, 7, 8, 9, ...`.

## The KZ associator and its depth filtration

The Drinfeld–Kontsevich KZ associator `Φ_KZ ∈ exp(t_3^)` has formal expansion

```
Φ_KZ = 1 + Σ_{w ≥ 2} ℏ^w · Φ_w(a, b),
```

where `Φ_w(a, b) ∈ \bar t_3^{[w]} ⊗_Q Z_w` and `Z_w ⊂ R` is the Q-subspace of
multiple zeta values of weight `w`. The canonical MZV generators at each weight
are

```
w = 2: ζ(2) = π²/6                    (dim_Q Z_2 = 1)
w = 3: ζ(3)                            (dim_Q Z_3 = 1)
w = 4: ζ(4) = π⁴/90, i.e. (2/5) ζ(2)²  (dim_Q Z_4 = 1 over Q[ζ(2)])
w = 5: ζ(5), ζ(2) ζ(3)                (dim_Q Z_5 = 2)
w = 6: ζ(6), ζ(3)²                    (dim_Q Z_6 = 2)
w = 7: ζ(7), ζ(2) ζ(5), ζ(4) ζ(3)     (dim_Q Z_7 = 3)
```

Brown's motivic depth filtration on `grt_1(Q) ⊂ \bar t_3^` (the graded
Grothendieck–Teichmüller Lie algebra) has a single generator `σ_{2k+1}` at each
odd weight `2k+1 ≥ 3`, paired with `ζ(2k+1)` under the KZ comparison. No
generator appears at even weight in depth 1; the first depth-2 generator appears
at weight 12 (the double shuffle `σ_{3,5}` relation).

## Depth-3 Massey — recap

The ℏ³ coefficient `Φ_3(a, b) ∈ \bar t_3^{[3]}` lives in a 2-dimensional space
with basis

```
u = [a, [a, b]],         v = [a, [-(a+b), b]] = -[a, [a, b]] - [a, [b, b]] = -[a, [a, b]],
```

modulo the `[b, b] = 0` vanishing (every generator commutes with itself). So
`v = -u` in `\bar t_3^{[3]}`, meaning `\bar t_3^{[3]}` is in fact only
1-dimensional spanned by `u` — a contradiction with the Witt count
`dim \bar t_3^{[3]} = 2`.

Resolving this: the Witt basis of `\bar t_3^{[3]}` in the free Lie algebra on
`a, b` is `{[a, [a, b]], [b, [a, b]]}`. The first computation used the image of
`Ω_{13}` in the quotient but the correct basis is the free-Lie one, which does
NOT reduce the two generators to one.

So the basis is `u = [a, [a, b]]`, `w = [b, [a, b]]`, and both are independent
in the free Lie algebra. Decomposable brackets from depth-1 × depth-2:

```
[a, [a, b]] = u,        [b, [a, b]] = w,        [a+b, [a, b]] = u + w = -[−(a+b), [a, b]]
                                                           = [Ω_{13}, [a, b]] (up to sign).
```

So the decomposable-image from depth 1 × depth 2 is 2-dimensional, filling all
of `\bar t_3^{[3]}`. The depth-3 quotient `\bar t_3^{[3]} / decomposable` is
therefore 0 over the free Lie algebra in two generators.

But the Massey obstruction at depth 3 is NOT zero in grt_1 — it is the
`σ_3`-generator at weight 3. This suggests the "decomposable" filtration in the
bracket sense and the "decomposable" filtration in the grt_1 sense are
different, and the Massey non-vanishing is detected only in the grt_1-quotient.

This is consistent with Brown's motivic depth theorem: in `\bar t_3`, every
weight-3 element is a Lie-bracket of depth-1 and depth-2, so the weight-3 piece
is "decomposable" bracket-wise. In `grt_1`, however, the weight-3 piece is
1-dimensional, generated by `σ_3`, and is NOT the image of any depth-2 grt_1
class under a grt_1 bracket (grt_1 has no depth-2 element at weight less than
12, so bracket [depth 1, depth 1] at weight 3 does exist but the grt_1 generators
there are exactly `σ_3`).

The Massey obstruction is thus: the ℏ³-coefficient of `Φ_KZ`, projected to
`grt_1 ⊂ \bar t_3`, is `ζ(3) · σ_3` — this is the non-decomposable residue, in
the grt_1-intrinsic sense, not in the bracket-length sense.

## The odd-weight tower

At each odd weight `2k+1`, the grt_1 generator `σ_{2k+1}` at depth 1 is paired
with `ζ(2k+1)` by the KZ comparison. This gives rise to an obstruction at the
`ℏ^{2k+1}`-order of the Drinfeld associator, which we interpret as a Higher
Massey product of order `2k+1` for the Yangian classical r-matrix.

Concretely:

```
w = 3: ⟨r, r, r⟩       detected by ζ(3) · σ_3   (depth-3 Massey — the standard case)
w = 5: ⟨r^{×5}⟩        detected by ζ(5) · σ_5
w = 7: ⟨r^{×7}⟩        detected by ζ(7) · σ_7
w = 9: ⟨r^{×9}⟩        detected by ζ(9) · σ_9
...
```

Each odd-weight Massey carries an independent non-decomposable residue, so the
tower of Higher Masseys is infinite-dimensional and genuinely captures the full
weight-graded grt_1 structure.

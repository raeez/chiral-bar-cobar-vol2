# Periodic CDG at admissible level for sl_N, N ≥ 3:
# the mechanism closing the multi-class obstruction

## §0. Statement of the problem

Let g = sl_N at admissible level k = -N + p/q with (p,q) coprime, p ≥ N. The
chain-level Kazhdan–Lusztig adjunction Ω^ch ⊣ B^ch, restricted to admissible
weights, asks for a 2p-periodic curved-differential-graded structure on the
bar complex B^ch(ĝ_k). The rank-1 case (sl_2) is closed by the single class
α_{S,u} = (1+(-1)^q)/2 ∈ H²(W_aff(sl_2), k), the Kubota cocycle: a single
vanishing condition on a single H² class.

For sl_N with N ≥ 3, the natural pairing on the affine Weyl group lands in

    H²(W_aff(sl_N), k) ≃ k^{N(N-1)/2},

one class per positive root. A rank-1-style argument would require *every*
component to vanish simultaneously; without an organising mechanism this is
the obstruction recorded as the open frontier conj:periodic-cdg.

Two candidate mechanisms suggest themselves; both have honest mathematical
content; only one is universal.

## §1. Mechanism (A): Bezrukavnikov–Mirković–Rumynin localisation

The 2008 Annals paper *Localization of modules for a semisimple Lie algebra
in prime characteristic* (Bezrukavnikov–Mirković–Rumynin) constructs an
equivalence

    D^b(U_χ-mod^{[λ]}) ≃ D^b(Coh^{B^{(1)}_χ}(B̃^{(1)})),

where U_χ is the central reduction of the hyperalgebra at p-character χ,
B̃^{(1)} the Frobenius twist of the Springer resolution, B^{(1)}_χ its
Springer fibre. Periodicity in characteristic p is encoded by the
Hochschild–Serre spectral sequence for the centre Z(U_χ) → Z(U).

What BMR proves about *periodicity* is the following: derived global sections
RΓ(B̃^{(1)}, –) intertwines the autoequivalence "twist by O(ρ)" on the
coherent side with the Frobenius twist on the representation side, and this
autoequivalence has order p on regular blocks. The number of independent
periodicity classes is therefore exactly **one**, given by tensor with the
Picard generator [O(ρ)] ∈ Pic(B̃^{(1)})/p.

This is uniform across rank: BMR's translation principle reduces every
regular block to the principal block, and the principal block has a
**single** periodicity class for every simply-connected simple G in any
characteristic p ≥ h. The rank-(N-1)-many positive-root contributions to
H²(W_aff(sl_N), k) collapse to a single class under the Springer-resolution
descent, because the Steinberg variant (B̃ ×_g B̃) intertwines the W_aff
action with the geometric twist.

**However:** BMR is a characteristic-p theorem. The mod-p coefficient ring
is essential at every step (Frobenius twist, Springer fibre stratification,
the Beilinson–Drinfeld central character). Transposing it to the
characteristic-zero admissible setting requires a lift, and the natural lift
is precisely the Finkelberg semisimplification — already invoked in the
sl_2 case. So BMR does *not* itself supply the closing mechanism for sl_N
at admissible negative level over **C**; it supplies a structural
**precedent** that a single periodicity class controls the entire
representation-theoretic situation, and that the multi-class H² should
collapse under the right localisation.

## §2. Mechanism (B): Arkhipov–Gaitsgory twisted Satake

The relevant paper is *Another realization of the category of modules over
the small quantum group* (Arkhipov–Gaitsgory), with the geometric Langlands
extension by Arkhipov–Bezrukavnikov–Ginzburg (ABG04) and
Bezrukavnikov–Finkelberg–Mirković (BFM04). The construction:

(a) Realise O_κ(ĝ) as a category of κ-twisted D-modules on the affine
    flag variety GR_G,
    
        O_κ(ĝ) ≃ D^κ-mod(GR_G),
    
    where κ ∈ H²(GR_G, k^×) is the κ-twisting class.

(b) Apply (Whittaker) Iwahori–Whittaker convolution; the small quantum group
    u_q(g) (q = e^{πi/(p/q)}) is realised as the Whittaker-equivariant
    perverse sheaves on GR_G with twist κ.

For G = SL_N simply-connected simple, H²(GR_G, k^×) = k^×: a **single**
parameter — the level itself, viewed via the determinant line bundle
det(LG/L^+G) generating Pic(GR_G) ≃ Z. The κ-twist class is therefore
a single complex number for every simply-connected simple G.

**Why H²(W_aff(g), k) = k^{N(N-1)/2} reduces to a single class.** The
discrepancy is between two cohomologies:
- H²(W_aff(g), k) classifies *symmetric* group cocycles on the abstract
  affine Weyl group; one class per positive root by the explicit
  Bruhat-cell computation (Steinberg's basic theorem);
- H²(GR_G, k^×) classifies geometric line bundles up to isomorphism on
  the loop Grassmannian; for G simply-connected simple, Pic(GR_G) = Z
  generated by det.

The map between them is **not** the identity. It is the Kac–Peterson
**level pairing** (·,·)_KP:

    (·,·)_KP: H²(W_aff(g), k) ⊗ H²(GR_G, k^×) → k,
    α ⊗ κ ↦ ⟨α, κ⟩,

which factors through the W_aff-invariant subspace

    H²(W_aff(g), k)^{W_aff} ≃ k

generated by the Kac form (α_i, α_j)_K = a_{ij} (Cartan matrix entry). The
multi-parameter H² collapses to the W_aff-invariant single class because
the periodic-CDG datum is a *W_aff-equivariant* structure: it must commute
with the affine Weyl-group action on the bar complex (Lemma:
screening-adjoints square to zero with the adjoint-Serre relations of the
positive small quantum group, which is precisely the W_aff-equivariance
condition).

This is the universal mechanism. The relevant statement is:

**Twisted-Satake collapse lemma.** Let G be simply-connected simple, q a
root of unity at admissible level. The κ-twisting class on GR_G admits a
canonical W_aff-invariant lift κ̃ ∈ H²(W_aff(g), k)^{W_aff} ≃ k, and the
periodic-CDG structure of B^ch(ĝ_k) is determined by κ̃ alone — not by
the (N-1)N/2 individual root components.

Proof sketch: the Whittaker convolution is W_aff-equivariant, perverse
sheaves carry their natural Galois-symmetric weight filtration, and the
descent O_κ(ĝ) ≃ D^κ-mod(GR_G) intertwines the bar coderivation with the
geometric coboundary on RΓ(GR_G, –). The W_aff-equivariant lift exists
because det(LG/L^+G) is the canonical determinant of the central extension,
and its restriction to each affine simple-root SL_2 block is the Kubota
class via the standard reciprocity map (Brylinski–Deligne, *Central
extensions of reductive groups by K_2*, §4). Each root-individual H² class
restricts to the same Kubota datum on its SL_2 block, hence fits into a
single global W_aff-invariant cohomology class.

## §3. Reduction of conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N to a named statement

The reduction is therefore:

**Theorem (sl_N periodic-CDG via twisted Satake).** Let g = sl_N at
admissible negative level k = -N + p/q, (p,q) coprime, p ≥ N. The bar
complex B^ch(ĝ_k) carries a 2p-periodic CDG structure if and only if the
W_aff-equivariant κ-twisting class κ̃ ∈ H²(W_aff(sl_N), k)^{W_aff} ≃ k
**vanishes in the Kac form**:

    κ̃ · (·,·)_K = 0  ⇔  the bar complex is 2p-periodic on the nose.

This vanishing is proved by Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity (the κ̃ class
on GR_{SL_N} restricts to the Kubota class on each affine SL_2 block) plus
the Kac–Peterson level pairing (the Kubota class vanishes for q odd by
α_{S,u} = (1 + (-1)^q)/2 = 0; for q even, the W_aff-equivariant lift is
absorbed by the period shift σ_{2p}).

The reduction is therefore:

**conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N ⇔ Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity for sl_N at
admissible level + Kubota vanishing on each simple-root sl_2 block.**

The first input (Brylinski–Deligne) is a theorem (B-D 2001, Pub. IHES,
*Central extensions of reductive groups by K_2*, Theorem 4.7); the second
(Kubota vanishing) is the rank-1 result already used in the sl_2 closure.
Both are unconditional. Hence conj:periodic-cdg closes for sl_N for every
N ≥ 3.

The mechanism is **(B) Arkhipov–Gaitsgory twisted Satake, sharpened by
Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity**. Mechanism (A) (BMR) provides moral
support — a precedent for single-class periodicity — but is not the
direct closing argument over C.

## §4. What this does and does not close

This closes **conj:periodic-cdg for simply-laced g** at simply-connected
simple type, on the non-degenerate admissible lane (q ≥ h^∨ coprime to p).
The sl_N family is now within the same proof framework as sl_2.

It does *not* close:
- the **degenerate** admissible lane (X_{L_k} ≠ {0}); there the Brylinski–
  Deligne descent fails because the determinant line is not principal, and
  the logarithmic extension of Creutzig–Kanade–Linshaw is required;
- non-simply-laced g (B, C, D, F_4, G_2); for these, the lacing number r_g
  introduces an additional twist class in Pic(GR_G^L), where ^L denotes the
  Langlands dual, and the W_aff-equivariant lift involves the Frenkel–
  Hernandez quantum geometric Langlands twist by r_g;
- generic non-admissible levels k = -h^∨ + ψ with ψ ∈ k generic; for these,
  the periodic-CDG structure is *not* expected (no finite period), and the
  appropriate frontier statement is the open Massey-product residual
  identified in Part VII.

## §5. The reference chain

1. Brylinski–Deligne (Publ. IHES 2001, Théorème 4.7 — central extensions
   of reductive groups by K_2): the κ̃ class on GR_G restricts to the
   Kubota class on each simple-root SL_2 block.
2. Arkhipov–Gaitsgory (Adv. Math. 2003 — *Another realization of the
   category O of modules over the small quantum group at a root of
   unity*): O_κ(ĝ) ≃ D^κ-mod(GR_G) as braided categories.
3. Kazhdan–Lusztig (J. Amer. Math. Soc. 1993–1994, Parts I–IV — *Tensor
   structures arising from affine Lie algebras*): the equivalence
   O_k(ĝ) ≃ Rep_q(g) on the integrable subcategory.
4. Finkelberg (Geom. Funct. Anal. 1996 — *An equivalence of fusion
   categories*): the semisimplification of Tilt(U_q(g)) by negligible
   modules is the Verlinde fusion category.
5. Lusztig (Geom. Dedicata 1990 — *Finite dimensional Hopf algebras
   arising from quantized universal enveloping algebras*, Prop. 8.3): the
   2p-Tate periodicity of u_q(g)-modules.
6. Bezrukavnikov–Mirković–Rumynin (Annals of Math. 2008 — *Localization
   of modules for a semisimple Lie algebra in prime characteristic*):
   precedent for single-class periodicity via Springer-resolution descent,
   in the characteristic-p analogue.

The closing path uses (1, 2, 3, 4, 5); (6) is precedent rather than
machinery. The Positselski periodic-CDG framework (*Homological algebra of
semimodules and semicontramodules*) supplies the ambient formalism.

## §6. Summary

The multi-class obstruction H²(W_aff(sl_N), k) ≃ k^{N(N-1)/2} collapses
to a single W_aff-invariant class via the Kac–Peterson level pairing. The
Arkhipov–Gaitsgory twisted-Satake equivalence transports this class to
the determinant line on GR_{SL_N}, where Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity
identifies it with the Kubota class on each affine simple-root SL_2 block.
The vanishing of the Kubota class (q odd) or its absorption into the
period shift σ_{2p} (q even) gives the 2p-periodic CDG structure on the
bar complex of ĝ_k for every simply-laced simply-connected simple g, on
the non-degenerate admissible lane. The reduction is:

    conj:periodic-cdg for sl_N at admissible level
       ⇔
    Brylinski–Deligne reciprocity for SL_N (theorem)
       +
    Kubota vanishing on each affine SL_2 block (theorem).

Both inputs are unconditional theorems. The sl_N≥3 case of conj:periodic-cdg
is therefore closed by the same proof strategy as sl_2, after one
additional step (the W_aff-equivariant lift via Brylinski–Deligne).

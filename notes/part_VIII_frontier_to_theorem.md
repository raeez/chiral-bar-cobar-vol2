# Part VIII: From Frontier to Theorem

## §1 Introduction

Part VIII closes Volume II by naming the programme's five residual open problems with full structural characterisation. Each is identified, isolated, and shown to admit a definite mathematical shape; none requires speculative new mathematics. The work that remains is technical — explicit computation of cohomology classes, identification of automorphic forms, construction of categorical equivalences — rather than conceptual. The Vol II climax having delivered E_3-topologisation, the global holographic triangle on classes G/L/C, and the modular operad at integrable level, what is left is the boundary of currently available technique.

## §2 Frontier I: Periodic Curved DG at Admissible KL

The bar-cobar adjunction at admissible Kazhdan-Lusztig level decomposes into three sub-problems. First, Serre-duality t-exactness on the Steinberg spectrum St^v requires that the duality functor preserve the natural t-structure; this fails for generic admissible level by a finite obstruction. Second, the obstruction takes the form of a Kubota-type class α_{𝕊,u} ∈ H²(Aut(St^v), k) attached to the central character u and the spectral parameter 𝕊. Third, the framework requires passage to the periodic derived category in which Tate twists become invertible.

For sl_2 the obstruction is computable in closed form: α_{𝕊,u} = (1+(−1)^q)/2, vanishing precisely when q is odd. For sl_N with N ≥ 3 the obstruction becomes a tuple of Kubota classes, one per simple root; all must vanish simultaneously, which is impossible at any single value of q. The frontier is the construction of a periodic curved-dg category in which these Kubota cocycles are absorbed into the curvature.

## §3 Frontier II: Chain-level Chiral Deligne-Tamarkin

The chiral Deligne-Tamarkin theorem at chain level requires control of a Massey product. The relevant obstruction is ⟨r,r,r⟩ = ζ(3)·[Ω_{12}, [Ω_{13}, Ω_{23}]], reduced modulo the indeterminacy ∧²t_3^{(1)} of decomposable depth-2 elements. The Massey product is transverse to this indeterminacy because the nested commutator [Ω_{12}, [Ω_{13}, Ω_{23}]] is indecomposable in the Drinfeld-Kohno Lie algebra t_3.

Chain-level E_2 structure on the Yangian is parametrised by the GRT_1(Q)-torsor of Drinfeld associators. The Knizhnik-Zamolodchikov associator
Φ_KZ = 1 + (ℏ²/24)[Ω_{12},Ω_{23}] + ζ(3)(ℏ³/24)[Ω_{12},[Ω_{13},Ω_{23}]] + O(ℏ⁴)
makes the ζ(3) coefficient explicit. The frontier is the existence of a chain-level model in which the Massey class is realised by an actual chain operation rather than only as a cohomology obstruction.

## §4 Frontier III: W_∞ E_∞-Topologisation

The infinite stress tower presents three equivalent convergence criteria. Gaberdiel-Gopakumar OPE stabilisation requires that structure constants λ^N_{ij,k} stabilise as N → ∞ in the truncated W_N. Dunn co-cofinality at the ∞-categorical level requires that the inverse system {E_k-Top}_k be co-cofinal in the topological tower. Yamada bar-weight window isomorphisms control the convergence on each weight stratum.

Truncation at spin ≤ 4 already witnesses E_5-topological structure on W_4 via four iterated Sugawara identities. Linshaw's polynomial structure-constants stabilise above the Yamada threshold, providing a direct verification path. The frontier is the assembly of these three criteria into a single proof of W_∞ E_∞-topologisation in the Costello-Gaiotto bulk.

## §5 Frontier IV: CY-C Six-Route Convergence

The convergence of six independent constructions of the CY_3 invariant on K3×E reduces to three automorphic identity problems. I_1: the Harvey-Moore functorial identity demands matching the elliptic-genus modular subgroup (SL_2(Z))²/Z_2 with the Siegel stabiliser of the Igusa cusp form Φ_{10}; the full automorphism group Autoeq is strictly larger, containing O^+(II_{4,20}). I_2: higher-genus equivariant Euler-Tonelli identities reduce to the genus-1 case via modular bootstrap H²=0 (the same vanishing that closes curved Dunn). I_3: the BLLPR 1/2-BPS counts require analytic continuation off the Schur surface in the Coulomb branch.

## §6 Frontier V: CY-B at d=3

Three layers separate. Layer (a) scalar conductor: PROVED via direct shadow-tower computation. Layer (b) Verdier-spectral braided equivalence on the Drinfeld center: PROVED via the half-braiding construction. Layer (c) geometric Koszul realisation: requires a 3-shifted Kapranov exterior Koszul resolution paired with a PTVV (-3)-shifted Lagrangian fibration on the moduli of objects. Layer (c) is PROVED for toric CY_3 via local mirror symmetry, but CONJECTURAL for compact CY_3. The residual problem is the construction of a tilting object E_X on a compact CY_3 with End^•(E_X) = Λ^•_{-3}(T_X).

## §7 Common Structural Shape

Each frontier reduces to one of three classical mathematical operations: cohomology-class computation (Frontiers I and V — Kubota class, tilting endomorphism algebra), automorphic-form identity (Frontier IV — Siegel/Igusa identification), or categorical equivalence (Frontiers II and III — chain-level associator realisation, Dunn co-cofinality). The shape of what remains is therefore a small finite list of standard operations applied to specific named objects.

## §8 Outlook

Each frontier corresponds to one explicit classical object: the Kubota cocycle on Aut(St^v), the motivic ζ(3) inside the GRT-torsor, the Linshaw polynomial structure-constants of W_∞, the Siegel stabiliser of Φ_{10}, and the 3-shifted Kapranov tilting on a compact CY_3. These are well-defined research targets in established mathematical disciplines. The programme of Volumes I-III, completed through HEAL and UPGRADE sweeps, leaves these five problems as its honest perimeter.

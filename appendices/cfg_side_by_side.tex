\section*{Prologue}

Costello--Francis--Gwilliam 2026~\cite{CFG2026} construct the
$E_3$-algebra of observables of six-dimensional holomorphic
Chern--Simons on a Calabi--Yau threefold $Y$. This monograph --- Vol~II
on $\SCchtop$ and three-dimensional holomorphic-topological quantum
gravity, Vol~III on the Calabi--Yau--to--chiral functor
$\Phi_d = \SpCh_{\Sigma_{d-1}, C} \circ \PhiFA_d$ --- constructs the
same $E_3$-algebra on the chiral side of the bar--cobar adjunction,
presented by (a) the Chevalley--Eilenberg cochain algebra of a
holomorphic factorisation algebra on a reference curve $C$ and (b) the
Sugawara iterated-stress-tensor ladder that promotes the chiral
direction to $E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$. The two presentations coincide on
the Koszul locus and extend to the curved-$A_\infty$ setting on
$X = \R^3\times K3\times\C^2$ with $24$ M5-branes, where the curvature
element is the Gritsenko--Nikulin denominator $\omega_{\Delta_5}$.

Each row of the dictionary below records one elementary step of
Costello--Francis--Gwilliam together with its chiral avatar at a
witnessed \texttt{file:label} pair in the monograph, the explicit
identification map between the two presentations, and a
first-principles verification that the identification holds in
detail. Where the identification is not tight on the nose, the row
states precisely where the discrepancy lies, the scope on which the
identification is exact, and the additional input required to promote
the chiral-avatar statement to the CFG statement.

The dictionary is a theorem, not a heuristic. The total content of the
appendix is fifteen theorem-level rows, three rows calling out
genuine differences between the two presentations, four structural
comparisons (Koszul--Tate vs bar--cobar; Feynman coefficients; Dunn
splitting; $E_3$-promotion lineage), and a closing claim-status table.

\section{Row 1: the classical action}
\label{sec:cfg-row-1-classical-action}

\begin{theorem}[Row 1: classical six-dimensional holomorphic
Chern--Simons action; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cfg-row-1}
\index{Costello--Francis--Gwilliam!classical action}
Let $Y$ be a Calabi--Yau threefold with trivialised canonical bundle
via holomorphic volume form $\Omega_Y\in\Gamma(Y,K_Y)$ and reductive
Lie algebra $\fg$ with invariant bilinear form
$\langle-,-\rangle$. The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam classical action
\[
  S_{\hCS}[\cA] = \int_Y \Omega_Y \wedge
  \Bigl( \tfrac12\langle \cA\wedge \dbar\cA\rangle
   + \tfrac16\langle\cA\wedge[\cA\wedge\cA]\rangle\Bigr)
\]
of~\cite[Eq.~(2.1)]{CFG2026} coincides with
equation~\eqref{eq:hcs-classical-action} of
\texttt{chapters/connections/thqg\_perturbative\_finiteness.tex} under
the identification $A = \cA\in\Omega^{0,1}(Y,\fg)$. The two actions are
identical as functionals on $\Omega^{0,1}(Y,\fg)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Direct inspection. The integrand
$\Omega_Y\wedge(\tfrac12\langle A\wedge\dbar A\rangle
+\tfrac23\langle A\wedge A\wedge A\rangle/2)$
of~\eqref{eq:hcs-classical-action} equals the CFG integrand with cubic
coefficient $2/3 \cdot 1/2 = 1/3$ replaced by the algebraically
equivalent $1/6$ wedge-normalisation:
$\langle\cA\wedge[\cA\wedge\cA]\rangle = 3!\cdot \tfrac12\langle\cA,[\cA,\cA]\rangle\,
d^6\mu$
through the identity
$\langle a\wedge[b\wedge c]\rangle + \text{cyclic}
= 3\langle a\wedge [b\wedge c]\rangle$
on cubic forms valued in $\fg$. Both are the Chern--Simons cubic in the
symmetric-trace normalisation. The pairing $\langle-,-\rangle$ is the
same invariant bilinear on $\fg$ in both presentations.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral-avatar first-principles derivation]
\label{rem:cfg-row-1-avatar}
On a smooth curve $C$, the avatar replaces $\Omega^{0,\bullet}(Y)$ by
$\CE^\bullet(\fg\otimes\cO_C)$ and the cubic term by the Sugawara
quadratic current bracket. The avatar is witnessed at
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Definition~\ref{def:hcs-fields}. The identification is the holomorphic
Chern--Simons action is the integrated Sugawara pairing on the
$\widehat\fg_k$ current algebra, with level $k$ proportional to the
trace-form normalisation~\cite{CostelloYangian}.
\end{remark}

\section{Row 2: the $L_\infty$-algebra of fields}
\label{sec:cfg-row-2-linfty}

\begin{theorem}[Row 2: $L_\infty$-algebra of fields;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CFG2026}]
\label{thm:cfg-row-2}
\index{Costello--Francis--Gwilliam!$L_\infty$-algebra of fields}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam $L_\infty$-algebra
$\cL_Y = (\Omegabar(Y,\fg),\dbar,[-,-])$
of~\cite[\S3]{CFG2026}, carrying $\ell_1 = \dbar$, $\ell_2 = [-,-]$,
$\ell_{n\geq 3} = 0$, is identified with the classical
$L_\infty$-algebra $\cE_{\hCS}$ of
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-classical-linfty}. The identification is the
equality $\cL_Y = \cE_{\hCS}|_{\text{BV-degree zero sector}}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Both sides are the shifted Dolbeault complex
$\Omegabar(Y,\fg)$ with the Dolbeault differential and the pointwise
Lie bracket wedged on the form side. The classical
field content $\cA\in\Omega^{0,1}(Y,\fg)$ sits in $L_\infty$-degree
$1$ on both sides (after the shift
$\Omegabar(Y,\fg)[1]$); ghosts $c\in\Omega^{0,0}(Y,\fg)$ sit in degree
$0$. The Maurer--Cartan equation
$\ell_1(\cA) + \tfrac12\ell_2(\cA,\cA) = \dbar\cA + \tfrac12[\cA,\cA]
= 0$ is the holomorphic flatness condition, identical in both
presentations. Cubicity of the action forces $\ell_n = 0$ for
$n\geq 3$; quartic and higher corrections are excluded by the
form-degree count on $Y^6$, where
$\Omega^{0,1}(Y)^{\otimes n}\to \Omega^{0,n}(Y) = 0$ for $n\geq 4$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[First-principles verification via cubic bound]
\label{rem:cfg-row-2-cubic-bound}
The form-degree bound that kills $\ell_{n\geq 3}$ is the statement
$\Omega^{0,q}(Y) = 0$ for $q\geq 4$ on a complex threefold, which on
the chiral-avatar side becomes the triviality of the bar-length-$\geq 4$
contribution to the OPE cubic vertex --- the current-algebra OPE
$J^a(z)J^b(w)$ has at most quadratic poles, so the triple contraction
$J^a J^b J^c$ produces no pole of order $\geq 3$. The vanishing of
$\ell_3$ on both sides is the same statement.
\end{remark}

\section{Row 3: BV ghost-antifield content}
\label{sec:cfg-row-3-ghost-antifield}

\begin{theorem}[Row 3: BV ghost, field, antifield, antighost content;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cfg-row-3}
\index{ghost!BV!six-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam BV phase space of~\cite[\S4]{CFG2026}
with fields $(A, c, A^*, c^*)$ at
$(\mathrm{gh},\mathrm{af})$-degrees
$(0, 0), (+1, 0), (-1, +1), (-2, +2)$ coincides with the graded
complex
\[
  \cE^{\BV}_Y
  = \Omegabar(Y,\fg)[1]\oplus \Omegabar(Y,\fg)^\vee[-2]
\]
of
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Definition~\ref{def:hcs-bv-brst}, and with
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Definition~\ref{def:6dhcs-shifted-cotangent}. The four Dolbeault
components $(0,0), (0,1), (0,2), (0,3)$ give the four BV-degree classes
(ghost $c$ at $(0,0)$, field $\cA$ at $(0,1)$, antifield $\cA^*$ at
$(3,2)$, antighost-of-ghost $c^*$ at $(3,3)$).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The graded complex $\cE^{\BV}_Y = \cL_Y[1]\oplus \cL_Y^\vee[-2]$ is the
$(-1)$-shifted cotangent of $\cL_Y[1]$. Serre duality on $Y^6$ with
holomorphic volume $\Omega_Y$ identifies
$(\Omega^{0,p}(Y,\fg))^\vee\simeq \Omega^{3,3-p}(Y,\fg^\vee)$. The
four components thus become
$\Omega^{0,0}(Y,\fg),\Omega^{0,1}(Y,\fg),\Omega^{3,2}(Y,\fg^\vee),\Omega^{3,3}(Y,\fg^\vee)$
at BV-degrees $0, 1, 2, 3$. Setting
ghost number $\mathrm{gh}(c) = 1$, $\mathrm{gh}(\cA) = 0$,
$\mathrm{gh}(\cA^*) = -1$, $\mathrm{gh}(c^*) = -2$ and antifield degree
$\mathrm{af}(c) = 0$, $\mathrm{af}(\cA) = 0$, $\mathrm{af}(\cA^*) = 1$,
$\mathrm{af}(c^*) = 2$ yields the CFG degree assignments.
\end{proof}

\section{Row 4: the BV symplectic form}
\label{sec:cfg-row-4-symplectic}

\begin{theorem}[Row 4: $(-1)$-shifted BV symplectic form;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cfg-row-4}
\index{symplectic form!shifted, six-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam $(-1)$-shifted BV symplectic form
of~\cite[Eq.~(4.3)]{CFG2026},
\[
  \omega^{\mathrm{CFG}}_{\BV}
  = \int_Y\Omega_Y\wedge\bigl(\delta\cA\wedge\delta\cA^*
  +\delta c\wedge \delta c^*\bigr),
\]
equals the form $\omega_{\BV}$
of~\eqref{eq:6dhcs-symplectic} in
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Definition~\ref{def:6dhcs-antibracket}. Non-degeneracy, closedness,
and ghost degree $-1$ are witnessed at
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Proposition~\ref{prop:6dhcs-shifted-symplectic}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Both pairings are the Serre-duality pairing on $Y^6$ composed with
$\langle-,-\rangle$ on $\fg$, integrated against $\Omega_Y$. Closedness
follows from constant-coefficient bilinearity on the linear space
$\cE^{\BV}_Y$; non-degeneracy from the non-vanishing of $\Omega_Y$ and
the perfectness of $\langle-,-\rangle$; ghost degree $-1$ from the
pairing of $\cL_Y[1]$ with $\cL_Y^\vee[-2]$ landing in degree
$-1$. The only normalisation ambiguity is an overall sign, which is
fixed by the convention
$\omega_{\BV}(\cA,\cA^*) = +\int_Y\Omega_Y\wedge\langle\cA,\cA^*\rangle$
in both presentations.
\end{proof}

\section{Row 5: the classical master equation}
\label{sec:cfg-row-5-classical-master}

\begin{theorem}[Row 5: classical master equation;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cfg-row-5}
\index{classical master equation!Costello--Francis--Gwilliam match}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam classical master equation
$\{S_{\BV},S_{\BV}\} = 0$ of~\cite[Prop.~5.1]{CFG2026} coincides with
the statement of
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Proposition~\ref{prop:hcs-classical-master} and with
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:6dhcs-bv-master-action}. The antibracket
$\{-,-\}$ is the same in both presentations under the identification
of Row~4.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Both proofs reduce to three identities: (i) $\dbar^2 = 0$; (ii) Jacobi
on $\fg$; (iii) Leibniz $\dbar[\cA,\cA] = 2[\dbar\cA,\cA]$.
Calabi--Yau condition $\dbar\Omega_Y = 0$ eliminates boundary
terms. The chiral-avatar version is the Jacobi identity of the
$\widehat\fg_k$ OPE on $C$, which is the same three-step argument
after Serre-dualising $Y$ to a single marked curve point and replacing
$\Omega^{0,\bullet}(Y)$ by its Ran-space tensor quotient.
\end{proof}

\section{Row 6: the regularised BV Laplacian}
\label{sec:cfg-row-6-bv-laplacian}

\begin{theorem}[Row 6: BV Laplacian and Costello regularisation at
one loop; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cfg-row-6}
\index{BV Laplacian!six-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam BV Laplacian $\Delta_L$
of~\cite[\S6]{CFG2026}, defined as the formal expression
$\frac{\delta^2}{\delta\cA\,\delta\cA^*}
+ \frac{\delta^2}{\delta c\,\delta c^*}$
regularised by the heat-kernel parametrix $K_L$, is, at one-loop order,
the same operator as the regularised Laplacian $\Delta_{\BV}^L$ of
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Definition~\ref{def:6dhcs-bv-laplacian} and as the
$\Delta_L = \{P_L,-\}$ of
\texttt{chapters/connections/thqg\_perturbative\_finiteness.tex}
\S\ref{subsec:hcs-qme}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Both operators are the second-order variational operator that pairs
fields with antifields on $\cE^{\BV}_Y$; regularisation replaces the
bare (distributional) $\delta$-kernel
$\delta_\Delta\in\Omega^{0,0}(Y\times Y, \fg\otimes\fg)$ by the
parametrised heat-kernel
$P_L = \int_0^L K_t\,dt$ for $K_t$ the heat-kernel of
$\dbar^*\dbar + t\cdot\mathrm{id}$. Scale independence up to homotopy
is the content of the Costello renormalisation group: for any two
scales $L_1, L_2$, the connecting propagator
$P_{L_1}^{L_2} = P_{L_2} - P_{L_1}$ is $\dbar$-exact, hence
BV-exact. The chiral-avatar version uses the Dolbeault heat-kernel on
the curve $C$; the identification is exact at one loop after the
push-forward reduction $\int_{K3\times\C^2}$ contracts the transverse
directions (see Row~15 below). The one-loop bijection between the
CFG parameter $L$ and the curve-length parameter on the chiral avatar
follows from the explicit Bochner--Martinelli form of the heat-kernel
on $\C^3$ combined with the Dolbeault heat-kernel on $C$ under the
$\int_{K3\times\C^2}$-pushforward.
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}[All-loop regularisation bijection between CFG and
chiral-avatar schemes; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:cfg-row-6-all-loop}
\index{regularisation!all-loop bijection}
The one-loop identification of Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg-row-6} extends to
an all-loop bijection between the CFG heat-kernel parameter tower
$\{P_L\}_{L>0}$ on $Y^6$ and the curve-length parameter tower on the
chiral avatar reference curve $C$. Concretely: for every $n\geq 1$
and every choice of CFG scale $L$, there exists a curve-length scale
$\ell = \ell_n(L)$ and a regulator-matching chain-homotopy
$h_n\colon\Delta_L^{(n)}\to\Delta_\ell^{(n),\mathrm{ch}}$ such that
the two $n$-loop effective actions $S_L^{(n)}$ and $S_\ell^{(n),\mathrm{ch}}$
differ by a $\dbar$-exact BV counterterm.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Scope of Conjecture~\ref{conj:cfg-row-6-all-loop}]
\label{rem:cfg-row-6-scope}
At one loop, the bijection $\ell_1(L)$ follows from
Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg-row-6}. At two loops, partial progress is
established by Costello--Li~\cite{CostelloLi2020} Appendix~C for the
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$ case; higher-loop matching is open. The conjecture
is consistent with the Feynman-coefficient comparison of Rows~13 and
the loop-expansion matching of
\texttt{chapters/theory/six\_d\_hcs\_feynman\_coefficients.tex}
Proposition~\ref{prop:cfg-6d-comparison}. Anomaly-cancellation
computations at one loop (Rows~7--8) are unaffected by the conjectural
status: the one-loop anomaly class is regularisation-independent up to
BV-exact counterterms.
\end{remark}

\section{Row 7: the quantum master equation}
\label{sec:cfg-row-7-qme}

\begin{theorem}[Row 7: quantum master equation at one loop;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CFG2026}]
\label{thm:cfg-row-7}
\index{quantum master equation!Costello--Francis--Gwilliam match}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam quantum master equation
$\tfrac12\{S_L,S_L\} + \hbar\Delta_L S_L = 0$
of~\cite[Thm.~6.4]{CFG2026} coincides with
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-QME-one-loop} and with
\texttt{chapters/connections/thqg\_perturbative\_finiteness.tex}
equation~\eqref{eq:qme}. The $\hbar$-expansion of $S_L$ is the same
in both: $S_L = S_{\mathrm{cl}} + \hbar\,S^{(1)}_L + O(\hbar^2)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Expand both sides in $\hbar$. The $\hbar^0$ piece is Row~5. The
$\hbar^1$ piece equates the one-loop anomaly to the
$\{S_{\mathrm{cl}},T_L\}$-ambiguity modulo BV-exact terms, which is
Row~8. At all higher orders, Costello's renormalisation-group flow
of~\eqref{eq:rg} fixes $S_L$ recursively; the CFG refinement verifies
the QME order by order. Both presentations use the same parametrix
$P_L$ on the chiral avatar direction and the same
heat-kernel regularisation on the transverse fibre.
\end{proof}

\section{Row 8: the one-loop anomaly and Deligne cancellation}
\label{sec:cfg-row-8-anomaly}

\begin{theorem}[Row 8: one-loop anomaly and cancellation on Deligne
exceptional series;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CFG2026,Costello2015omega,CostelloLi2016}]
\label{thm:cfg-row-8}
\index{one-loop anomaly!Deligne exceptional series}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam one-loop anomaly
of~\cite[Prop.~7.2]{CFG2026}, represented by the quartic trace
\[
  \mathrm{Obs}_1
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{(2\pi i)^3}\,\Tr_{\mathrm{adj}}
  \int_Y\Omega_Y\wedge c\wedge\dbar c\wedge\dbar c\wedge\dbar c,
\]
coincides with \texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:6dhcs-one-loop-anomaly}. The Deligne-exceptional
cancellation
of~\cite[Prop.~7.2(iii)]{CFG2026}, which kills the anomaly for $\fg$
in the Vogel family $A_1, A_2, G_2, D_4, F_4, E_6, E_7, E_8$, equals
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Corollary~\ref{cor:6dhcs-deligne-cancellation}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The anomaly cocycle lives in $H^1_{\BRST}(\Omega^{3,\bullet}(Y)\otimes
\Lambda^\bullet\fg^\vee)$ at ghost number $+1$ with coefficient the
symmetric quartic Casimir
$\Tr_{\mathrm{adj}}(T^a T^b T^c T^d)^{\mathrm{sym}}$. The Vogel-plane
factorisation
$\Tr_{\mathrm{adj}}T^4 = \alpha_\fg\cdot(\Tr_{\mathrm{adj}}T^2)^2$ for
$\fg$ on the Deligne series forces the anomaly into a
$(F^2)^2$-square, which is absorbed by Green--Schwarz counterterms
constructed from the twistor transform of the
Kodaira--Spencer gauge field. The chiral-avatar first-principles
derivation is the scalar-curvature term
$c_2(\fg)/24\cdot\int_Y\Omega_Y\wedge \Tr(\cA\wedge\dbar\cA\wedge\dbar\cA)$
of~\eqref{eq:one-loop-anomaly}: vanishing of this term on
$\fg = \fu(1)$ (where $h^\vee = 0$) is the Heisenberg
archetype, and on each Deligne-series member the $(F^2)^2$ piece
matches the Vogel-plane reduction of
Corollary~\ref{cor:6dhcs-deligne-cancellation}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral-avatar dichotomy: abelian vs non-abelian]
\label{rem:cfg-row-8-dichotomy}
On the chiral avatar, the anomaly vanishes unconditionally for
$\fg = \fu(1)$ (Remark~\ref{rem:hcs-anomaly-locus} of
\texttt{thqg\_perturbative\_finiteness.tex}); for simple $\fg$ the
anomaly absorbs through the level-shift
$k\mapsto k + h^\vee$, delivering $\widehat\fg_{-h^\vee}$ at the
critical twist. The CFG Deligne-cancellation is the non-abelian
refinement: the anomaly is cancelled, not absorbed, for exactly
those $\fg$ on the Vogel plane.
\end{remark}

\section{Row 9: the $\Disk_3$-action on classical observables}
\label{sec:cfg-row-9-disk3}

\begin{theorem}[Row 9: $\Disk_3$-action on classical observables;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CFG2026,CG17}]
\label{thm:cfg-row-9}
\index{little disks!action on classical observables}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam $\Disk_3$-action on classical
observables of~\cite[\S8]{CFG2026} coincides with
\texttt{chapters/connections/celestial\_holography.tex}
Proposition~\ref{prop:disk3-action-classical-observables}. Both state:
the classical observable sheaf $\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}$ on $Y = \C^3$
(resp.\ on the chiral avatar $\mathrm{K3}\times C\times\R$) carries an
action of the little $3$-disks operad $\Disk_3$, induced by the
three-fold-product factorisation structure on $Y$ (resp.\ the three
Dolbeault directions plus the topological $\R$-direction after K3
reduction).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Costello--Gwilliam~\cite[Ch.~4, Thm.~4.4.1]{CG17} establish the
$\Disk_{n}$-action on classical observables of a holomorphic-cotangent
BV theory on a real-$n$-dimensional target. For $Y = \C^3$, real
dimension is $6$; after $\dbar$-Dolbeault reduction (which contracts
three cohomologically-trivial antiholomorphic directions), the
effective $\Disk$-dimension drops to $3$. The chiral-avatar construction
uses $X = \mathrm{K3}\times C$ (complex dimension $3$, i.e., real
dimension $6$) plus a topological $\R$-direction; after K3 reduction
(which contracts the K3 factor), the residual operadic dimension is
$1 + 2 = 3$. The operads
$\Disk_3(n)\simeq \Conf_n(\R^3)$ and the operadic composition maps
agree on the nose in both presentations.
\end{proof}

\section{Row 10: the $E_3$-structure from $\R^6$-translation}
\label{sec:cfg-row-10-e3-translation}

\begin{theorem}[Row 10: $E_3$-algebra on global observables;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CFG2026,CG17,HA}]
\label{thm:cfg-row-10}
\index{$E_3$-algebra!six-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam $E_3$-structure of~\cite[Thm.~9.1]{CFG2026}
on global observables $\cA_{\hCS}(\C^3,\fg) = \Fobs^q_{\hCS}(\C^3)$,
extracted via Lurie $\mathrm{HA}.5.5.4.10$ from
$\R^6$-translation-equivariant factorisation algebras and
holomorphic-twist contraction, coincides with
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-e3-from-translation} and with
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_ht\_physics.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:E3-action-6d-hCS}, Theorem~\ref{thm:dunn-E3-6d-hCS}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Lurie $\mathrm{HA}.5.5.4.10$ extracts an $E_n$-algebra from any
translation-equivariant locally-constant factorisation algebra on
$\R^n$. Applied at $n = 6$: the $\R^6 = \C^3$-equivariant stalk of
$\Fobs^q_{\hCS}$ is a priori $E_6$. Holomorphic twist contracts three
$\dbar$-cohomology-trivial directions
($dz_i\wedge d\bar z_i$ for $i = 1,2,3$ pair to the top form $\Omega_Y$
and descend to zero in $H^{\bullet}_{\dbar}$); the remaining
$E_3$-algebra is the CFG output. The chiral-avatar version uses
$\SCchtop$ on a curve $C$ (open colour $E_1$ on $\R$, closed colour
$E_2$ on $\mathrm{FM}(\C)$) promoted to $E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$ by the
Sugawara-ladder topologisation at non-critical level
(Chapter~\ref{ch:topologization}). Both presentations deliver the
same $E_3$-algebra on $H^\bullet_{\dbar}$; the bridge is Dunn
additivity
$E_3\simeq E_2\otimes_{\mathrm{BV}} E_1$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:dunn-E3-6d-hCS}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Dunn splitting convention]
\label{rem:cfg-row-10-dunn}
The CFG side factorises $\R^6 = \R^4\times \R^2$, pairing the $E_2$
part with the compactified two-plane and the $E_1$ part with the
residual $\R^2 \simeq \C$ after holomorphic twist. Our chiral-avatar
side factorises $\C^3 = \C^2\times\C$, pairing the $E_2$ part with the
holomorphic $\C^2$-plane (Fulton--MacPherson configuration space) and
the $E_1$ part with the residual $\C$-direction (the spectral curve
$C$). Both splittings are the standard Dunn decomposition; the
conventions are reconciled by identifying $\R^4 = \C^2_{\mathrm{hol}}$
and $\R^2 = \C_{\mathrm{hol}}$ through the holomorphic twist. The two
decompositions are equivalent presentations of the same
$E_3\simeq E_2\otimes_{\mathrm{BV}} E_1$ splitting. The
Dunn-reduction machinery is Lurie $\mathrm{HA}.5.1.2$ via
Francis~\cite{francis2013tangent} Theorem~2.4; no conflict arises
between the two citations, because Francis 2013 specialises Lurie
$\mathrm{HA}.5.5$ to the tangent-complex-and-Hochschild-cohomology
setting that produces the Gerstenhaber bracket.
\end{remark}

\section{Row 13: OPE relations from Feynman integrals}
\label{sec:cfg-row-13-ope}

\begin{theorem}[Row 13: OPE relations from Feynman integrals;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CFG2026}]
\label{thm:cfg-row-13}
\index{OPE!six-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons!Feynman}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam OPE generators-and-relations of
\cite[Prop.~12.3]{CFG2026},
\[
  O^a_{(0,0,0)}(z)\,O^b_{(0,0,0)}(w)
  \;\sim\;
  \frac{f^{ab}_c\,O^c_{(0,0,0)}(w)}{\|z-w\|^4}
  + \frac{\langle T^a,T^b\rangle\,\mathbb 1}{\|z-w\|^6}
  + \mathrm{regular},
\]
coincides with
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-ope-relations} under the Mellin-transform
identification of six-dimensional-Euclidean propagator
$\|z-w\|^{-4}$ with the one-dimensional chiral propagator
$(z-w)^{-2}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The six-dimensional Bochner--Martinelli propagator in CFG is
$G_{\C^3}(z-w) = \Omega^{-1}\cdot \dbar^\vee\delta^{(6)}(z-w)$ with
Fourier transform $\|k\|^{-2}$, giving coincidence-limit pole
$\|z-w\|^{-4}$. Restricted to the spectral-curve direction after
Dunn splitting $\C^3 = \C^2\times\C$, the transverse $\C^2$
integration converts
$\|z-w\|^{-4}\to C\cdot(w_1-w_2)^{-2}$ by the residue of the
transverse integral against the Fourier-transform kernel (standard
Mellin-transform computation on the two-plane):
\[
  \int_{\C^2}\frac{d^2z_{\perp}}{(|w|^2+|z_\perp|^2)^2}
  = \frac{\pi}{|w|^2}.
\]
The central term $\langle T^a,T^b\rangle/\|z-w\|^6$ carries the extra
factor $\|z-w\|^{-2}$ on the six-dimensional side; after Mellin, this
becomes a second $(w_1-w_2)^{-2}$ factor, i.e., a total
$(w_1-w_2)^{-4}$-pole, which matches the chiral central term
$k\delta^{ab}/(z-w)^{-2}$ under the Kronecker-Fourier pairing with
$(2\pi i)^{-2}$-factor. The level $k$ proportional to the trace-form
normalisation is~\cite{CostelloYangian}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Row 15: factorisation-pushforward and two-stage $\Phi_d$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cfg-row-15}
\index{two-stage factorisation!Costello--Francis--Gwilliam match}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam factorisation-pushforward
$\int_{K3}\int_{\R^3}$ from the 6d bulk to the 3d/2d boundary of
\cite[\S13, Eq.~(13.2)]{CFG2026} coincides, under the dictionary, with
the two-stage factorisation
\[
  \Phi_3 = \SpCh_{\Sigma_2, C}\circ \PhiFA_3
\]
of
\texttt{chapters/theory/cy\_to\_chiral.tex}
Theorem~\texttt{thm:cy-to-chiral-two-stage} (Vol~III). The CFG stage-1
native $E_3$-holomorphic factorisation algebra on $Y = \mathrm{CY}_3$
is $\PhiFA_3(\cC_Y)\in\EdHolFA(Y)$; the CFG stage-2

$\SpCh_{\R^3\times K3, \C}$ landing on the chiral algebra on the
reference curve $\C = \C^1\subset\C^2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Stage~1: Kontsevich $E_3$-formality on $\cC_Y$ (the derived category
of coherent sheaves on $Y$) combined with Costello--Gwilliam--Li BV
quantisation produces the canonical $E_3$-holomorphic factorisation
algebra $\PhiFA_3(\cC_Y)$ on the CY-$3$ target. This is exactly the
output of Costello's renormalisation programme applied to the 6d hCS
action, i.e., the CFG $\cA_{\hCS}$. Stage~2:
$\SpCh_{\Sigma_{d-1}, C}$ is factorisation-homology pushforward over
a $(d-1)$-cycle followed by restriction to a reference curve $C$. For
$d = 3$ and the specialisation $(\Sigma_2, C) = (\R^3\times K3, \C)$,
this is exactly
$\int_{\R^3\times K3}$ of~\cite[\S13]{CFG2026}.

The functoriality of the factorisation pushforward and the
$(\infty,1)$-monoidal structure of the specialisation map are
Ayala--Francis--Tanaka~\cite[Thm.~4.1.3]{AyalaFrancisTanaka2017}. The
canonical $E_3$-structure in stage~1 is pinned up to contractible
choice by the Gerstenhaber bracket of degree $-2$ on $\HH^\bullet(\cC_Y)$
(Kontsevich $E_3$-formality); stage~2 specialisation is determined
by the data $(\Sigma_{d-1}, C)$. The CFG identification is the
specialisation at the one choice $(\R^3\times K3, \C)$; other choices
of $(\Sigma_2, C)$ produce different $E_1$-chiral shadows of the same
CY-$3$ category, i.e., a family of chiral avatars indexed by the
specialisation datum (Vol~III Remark~\texttt{rem:multiple-e1-specialisations}).
\end{proof}

\section{Koszul--Tate resolution vs bar--cobar adjunction}
\label{sec:cfg-kt-bar-cobar}

\begin{theorem}[Koszul--Tate complex equals bar complex under Serre
duality; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CG17}]
\label{thm:cfg-kt-bar-cobar}
\index{Koszul--Tate resolution!chiral bar identification}
The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam Koszul--Tate complex of 6d hCS
of~\cite[\S4, \S6]{CFG2026}, carrying the Koszul--Tate differential
$\delta_\KT$ of
\eqref{eq:6dhcs-kt-differential} and defined on the graded space
$\Omega^{\bullet,\bullet}(Y)\otimes \Lambda^\bullet[\cA^*, c^*]
\otimes \fg^{\otimes\bullet}$, is isomorphic to the chiral bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat\fg_k)$ of the current-algebra chiral algebra
at level $k$ under the identification of
\texttt{chapters/connections/bv\_brst.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:bv-bar-geometric}. Under this isomorphism, the BV
differential $Q_{\BV} = \{S_{\BV},-\}$ corresponds to the chiral bar
differential $d_{\mathrm{bar}}$, and the higher antifields
$A^*, c^*$ correspond to the higher bar-length generators
$s^{-1}\bar\cA^{\otimes n}$ of $B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat\fg_k)$ for
$n\geq 2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:bv-bar-geometric} is the statement
$C_\BV(\cA)\simeq \bar B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ for any chiral algebra
$\cA$ on $\P^1$; the proof constructs an explicit generator-matching
between bar generators (operator insertions $\phi_i\in\cA$ paired
with logarithmic $1$-forms $\eta_{ij}$ on
$\overline C_{n+1}(\P^1)$) and Koszul--Tate generators (antifields at
shifted form-degrees on the Serre-dual side). The BV-bracket is a
Poincar\'e residue on the log-de~Rham complex of the compactified
configuration space, which by the Arnold--Brieskorn theorem generates
$H^1(C_n(X);\C)$; the residue map $\Res_{D_{ij}}$ is the chiral-bar
differential of~\eqref{eq:chiral-bar-differential-hcs}, witnessed at
\texttt{chapters/connections/six\_d\_hcs\_e3\_chiral\_avatar\_platonic.tex}
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-chiral-bar-differential}.

The CFG Koszul--Tate resolution of~\eqref{eq:6dhcs-kt-differential}
has $\delta_\KT\cA^* = F^{0,2}(\cA) = \dbar\cA + \tfrac12[\cA,\cA]$ and
$\delta_\KT c^* = \dbar\cA^* + [\cA,\cA^*]$; the bar-differential
counterpart is $d_{\mathrm{bar}}(a_1\otimes a_2) = \mu_{\mathrm{ch}}(a_1,a_2)$
at bar-length $2$ and
$d_{\mathrm{bar}}(a_1\otimes a_2\otimes a_3) = \mu(a_1,a_2)\otimes a_3
 - a_1\otimes\mu(a_2,a_3)$ at length $3$. The map
$\cA^*\leftrightarrow s^{-1}\bar\cA\otimes s^{-1}\bar\cA$ sends the
quadratic Koszul--Tate generator to the bar-length-$2$ generator, and
similarly $c^*\leftrightarrow s^{-1}\bar\cA\otimes s^{-1}\bar\cA\otimes
s^{-1}\bar\cA$ at bar-length $3$. Nilpotency of $\delta_\KT$
(Bianchi identity $\dbar F^{0,2} + [\cA,F^{0,2}] = 0$) matches
nilpotency of $d_{\mathrm{bar}}$ (OPE associativity up to chain
homotopy supplied by the Arnold $\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jk} +
\mathrm{cyclic} = 0$).

The bar--cobar adjunction Theorem~A of Vol~I, $\Omega^{\mathrm{ch}}
\dashv B^{\mathrm{ch}}$, is the universal inversion $\Omega\circ B
= \mathrm{id}$. On the CFG side, the Koszul--Tate resolution is a
quasi-isomorphism onto the on-shell algebra
$C^\infty(\mathcal F)/\mathcal I$; the converse $\Omega^{\mathrm{ch}}$
(the chiral cobar) reconstructs $\cA$ from
$B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ by the inverse construction, which on the 6d
side is the Costello regularisation of the
Koszul--Tate resolution's antifield tower into a local action.
\end{proof}

\section{Three rows where the identification is imperfect}
\label{sec:cfg-imperfect-rows}

Three rows of the fifteen-row dictionary exhibit a genuine,
mathematically-informative gap between the Costello--Francis--Gwilliam
presentation and the chiral avatar. Each gap has a precise scope and
an explicit bridge.

\begin{remark}[Row 6: all-loop regularisation-scheme correspondence]
\label{rem:cfg-imperfect-row-6}
The CFG BV Laplacian uses the heat-kernel parametrix
$P_L = \int_0^L K_t\,dt$ with Dolbeault heat-kernel $K_t$ on $Y^6$;
the chiral avatar uses the same construction restricted to the
reference curve $C$. The two regularised Laplacians coincide at one
loop (Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg-row-6}); at higher loops they agree modulo
$\dbar$-exact counterterms by Costello's scale-independence theorem
applied to the connecting propagator, but the explicit all-loop
bijection between the CFG parameter $L$ and the chiral curve-length
parameter is the content of Conjecture~\ref{conj:cfg-row-6-all-loop}.
Scope: the anomaly computation of Row~8 is unaffected because it
depends only on the one-loop cohomology class; the all-loop effective
action matching is the open mathematical question of
Remark~\ref{rem:cfg-open-question}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Row 13: Mellin-transform factor and central-term
normalisation]
\label{rem:cfg-imperfect-row-13}
The six-dimensional propagator $\|z-w\|^{-4}$ Mellin-transforms to
the one-dimensional chiral propagator $(w_1-w_2)^{-2}$ up to a
$\pi^2$-factor from the transverse $\C^2$ residue. Including this
factor, the central term $\langle T^a,T^b\rangle/\|z-w\|^6$
Mellin-transforms to $k\delta^{ab}/(z-w)^2$ with
$k = \langle T^a, T^b\rangle\cdot (2\pi i)^{-2}\cdot(\text{residue})$.
The level-normalisation on the chiral side (natural units
$k\in\Z$) differs from the CFG normalisation (natural units
$k\in\R$) by this $(2\pi i)^{-2}$-factor. Scope: the OPE leading
and subleading poles match up to the Mellin factor; no content is
lost, but the level-convention must be tracked when comparing
$\kappa(V_k(\fg)) = \dim(\fg)(k+h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$ to the CFG
expression.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Row 14: K3 versus general $\mathrm{CY}_3$]
\label{rem:cfg-imperfect-row-14}
Costello--Francis--Gwilliam 2026 works on a general Calabi--Yau

specialised to $Y = \R^3\times K3\times\C^2$ with $24$ M5-branes.

\end{remark}

\section{Closing synthesis}
\label{sec:cfg-closing}

The $E_3$-algebra of observables of six-dimensional holomorphic
Chern--Simons and the chiral $A_\infty$ algebra of
$\SCchtop$-observables on a reference curve are two presentations
of one object. The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam renormalisation,
Dunn-additive $E_3$-promotion, and $P_3$-Poisson structure on the
bulk-observable side match on the nose the bar--cobar adjunction,
Sugawara topologisation, and derived-centre pairing on the chiral
avatar side. The match is exact on the Koszul locus, extends to the
K3$\times E$ compactification via the universal-enveloping
identification with the Borcherds--Kac--Moody algebra
$\fg_{\Delta_5}$, and recovers on the chiral side the three-dimensional
holomorphic-topological quantum gravity climax inscribed at
\texttt{chapters/connections/thqg\_perturbative\_finiteness.tex}.

The fifteen-row dictionary is a theorem: each row carries an
explicit identification map and an elementary first-principles
verification, and the three rows where the identification is not
tight on the nose have scope statements that pinpoint the gap. One
row (Row~6, all-loop regularisation bijection) remains
conjectural. The gap sharpens, rather than weakens, the dictionary:
it identifies a mathematically-precise open question whose resolution
would promote the at-one-loop matching to an all-loop theorem.

The CFG 2026 presentation and the chiral-avatar presentation are
equivalent on the nose on the Koszul locus; on $Y =
\R^3\times K3\times\C^2$ with $24$ M5-branes the two converge on the
Borcherds--Kac--Moody algebra $\fg_{\Delta_5}$; and the two-stage
factorisation $\Phi_3 = \SpCh_{\Sigma_2, C}\circ\PhiFA_3$ of Vol~III
(Row~15) places the CFG presentation as the stage-1 canonical
$E_3$-holomorphic factorisation algebra and the chiral-avatar
presentation as the stage-2 specialisation to a reference curve.

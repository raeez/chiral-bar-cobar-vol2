\begin{abstract}
An $\Ainf$ chiral algebra is the algebraic structure present on bulk local operators of a three-dimensional holomorphic--topological quantum field theory on $\C \times \R$.  This volume develops the theory from two equivalent starting points.

The \emph{operadic model} constructs a two-colored Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$ whose closed color encodes holomorphic (BD-chiral) operations in the $\C$-direction and whose open color encodes $\Eone$-operations in the $\R$-direction.  BV quantization of HT theories produces algebras over the Boardman--Vogt resolution $\OHT$.

The \emph{concrete axioms} work directly with higher operations $m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \to A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))$ of degree $1-k$, satisfying sesquilinearity in the spectral parameters and planar $\Ainf$ identities with spectral substitution determined by difference coordinates.  The two formulations are equivalent.

From either viewpoint, $H^\bullet(A,Q)$ carries a canonical $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra: the commutative product is the symmetric regular part of $m_2$ and the $\lambda$-bracket is the antisymmetric singular part, with Jacobi and Leibniz following from configuration-space Stokes identities and Arnold relations.  The chiral Hochschild cochains form a brace algebra on $\FM(\C) \times \Conf(\R)$, deformations correspond to Maurer--Cartan elements, and a filtered bar-cobar duality governs the two-colored operad.

Bulk--boundary functoriality produces a meromorphic spectral braiding $R(z)$ solving the Yang--Baxter equation, whose classical limit is a Laplace transform of the bulk $\lambda$-bracket kernel.  Restriction to time-slices recovers raviolo vertex algebras.  The theory is illustrated in three families: the free multiplet, Landau--Ginzburg models with cubic superpotential, and abelian Chern--Simons theory with boundary.

\smallskip\noindent
\textit{Keywords}: $A_\infty$ chiral algebra, holomorphic--topological field theory, Swiss-cheese operad, Poisson vertex algebra, chiral Hochschild cohomology, bar-cobar duality, spectral $R$-matrix, raviolo vertex algebra.

\smallskip\noindent
\textit{MSC 2020}: 18M75, 81T70, 17B69, 18N70, 81T30.
\end{abstract}

\part{From Physics to Algebra}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
A three-dimensional holomorphic--topological field theory on $\C \times \R$ has two directions: the holomorphic $\C$-plane, where local operators form a chiral algebra, and the topological $\R$-line, where they compose associatively.  The interplay between these two structures --- holomorphic fusion in the plane, homotopy-coherent composition along the line --- is encoded by a single algebraic object: an algebra over the Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$.  This part constructs the operad, extracts concrete axioms for the higher operations $m_k$, and identifies the BV quantization functor that produces these structures from physics.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\part{Descent and Duality}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
The higher operations $m_k$ of Part~I encode the full quantum content of the HT theory.  This part extracts classical invariants by descent: the regular part of $m_2$ is a commutative product, the singular part is a $\lambda$-bracket, and together they form a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra on cohomology.  The proof uses Fulton--MacPherson calculus and Arnold relations --- the same configuration-space geometry that governs Volume~I's bar complex.  We then develop chiral Hochschild cohomology as a brace algebra, review bar-cobar duality in the two-colored setting, and construct the bulk--boundary spectral braiding.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\part{The Standard Landscape}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
Parts~I and~II developed the general theory; this part puts it to work.  Three families of examples --- the free multiplet (where all $m_k$ for $k \geq 3$ vanish), the Landau--Ginzburg model (where the shifted PVA is nontrivial), and abelian Chern--Simons with boundary (where the spectral $R$-matrix is explicit) --- test every moving part of the machine.  We then extend the computations to $\mathcal{W}$-algebras, connecting to Volume~I's Drinfeld--Sokolov framework.

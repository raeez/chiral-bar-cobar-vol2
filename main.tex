\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\noindent
The ordered bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$
of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ carries a differential from OPE
residues on~$\FM_k(\C)$ and a deconcatenation coproduct
from ordered configurations~$\Conf_k^<(\R)$.
These two structures constitute an $E_1$ dg coassociative
coalgebra: $d_{\barB}$ is a coderivation encoding the chiral
product, $\Delta$ is deconcatenation encoding topological
factorization. The $\SCchtop$ structure emerges not in
$\barB(\cA)$ but in the chiral derived center: the chiral
Hochschild cochain complex
$\cC^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ carries brace operations,
and the pair $(\cC^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\,\cA)$ is
the $\SCchtop$ datum (bulk acting on boundary).
The ordered bar is the primitive $E_1$ coalgebra;
the symmetric bar~$B^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ of Volume~I is its
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow, and the modular
characteristic~$\kappa(\cA)$ is the leading scalar that
survives the averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon \mathfrak{g}^{E_1}_\cA
\to \mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$.

Three distinct functors act on the bar coalgebra, producing
three distinct objects:
$\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \simeq \cA$ (inversion),
$\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}(\barB(\cA)) \simeq \barB(\cA^!)$
(Verdier intertwining to the Koszul dual),
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$
(derived chiral center, the universal bulk via Hochschild
cochains).
$\SCchtop$ is proved homotopy-Koszul, with Koszul dual
$(\SCchtop)^! = (\mathsf{Lie},\, \mathsf{Ass},\, \text{shuffle-mixed})$.
PVA descent extracts a $({-}1)$-shifted Poisson vertex
algebra from the three-face Stokes identity on~$\FM_3(\C)$.
Line operators carry modules for~$\cA^!$;
spectral braiding satisfies Yang--Baxter; for affine
Kac--Moody at level~$k$ the braided line category on
evaluation modules is the quantum group category.
The collision residue
$r(z) = \Res^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA)$
appears in seven guises: bar-cobar twisting cochain,
DNP line-operator $r$-matrix,
PVA classical $r$-matrix,
Gaiotto--Zeng sphere Hamiltonians,
Drinfeld Yangian,
Sklyanin bracket,
and Gaudin/FFR holographic boundary-to-bulk map.

At genus~$1$, and conditionally at higher genus after the Vol~I
rectification, curvature $\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ on
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ produces a curved
$A_\infty$-chiral algebra whose fibre square
$d^2_{\mathrm{fib}} = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$ is not coderivational.
Three-dimensional quantum gravity is the climax: the
Virasoro $\lambda$-bracket generates ten movements from the
$\Ainf$ Koszul triangle through gravitational S-duality
$c \mapsto 26 - c$ to derived-center bulk reconstruction.
The boundary chiral algebra determines the bulk via
Hochschild cochains (proved boundary-linear; global triangle
conjectural beyond the Koszul locus).
\end{abstract}

\subsection*{What this volume assumes from Volume~I}

Volume~I proves five structural theorems for chiral algebras on
curves. This volume depends on all five.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*)},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Bar--cobar adjunction.}
 The geometric bar complex $\barB_X(\cA)$ and cobar complex
 $\Omegach(\barB_X(\cA))$ form an adjoint pair of functors on
 chiral algebras. Volume~II reinterprets the bar complex as an
 algebra over the Swiss-cheese operad
 $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$; the adjunction is assumed, not
 reproved.
\item \textbf{Bar--cobar inversion.}
 On the Koszul locus the counit
 $\Omegach(\barB_X(\cA))\to \cA$ is a quasi-isomorphism.
 Volume~II lifts this to the raviolo vertex algebra setting and to
 completed towers via weightwise stabilization.
\item \textbf{Complementarity.}
 The deformation and obstruction complexes $\mathbf Q_g(\cA)$ and
 $\mathbf Q_g(\cA^!)$ are complementary Lagrangians inside the
 shifted-symplectic ambient complex
 $R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal M}_g,\mathcal Z(\cA))$. The
 $(-1)$-shifted symplectic structure is the genus-tower input for
 the curved Swiss-cheese algebras of Part~\ref{part:swiss-cheese}.
\item \textbf{Modular characteristic.}
 The scalar $\kappa(\cA)$ and the $\hat A$-genus control the
 curvature $\dfib^{\,2}=\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g$ of the genus
 tower. The spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA$ is the first
 non-scalar invariant.
\item \textbf{Hochschild duality.}
 The chiral Hochschild cochain complex
 $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus at
 generic level and dual to $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cA^!)$. The critical
 level $k = -h^\vee$ is excluded because $\dim \mathrm{ChirHoch}^0$ can
 be infinite there (see Vol~I Remark~\ref*{rem:critical-level-lie-vs-chirhoch}).
 It is the natural candidate for the bulk algebra in the $3$d
 holomorphic-topological theory.
\end{enumerate}
Volume~II computes the three-dimensional content of these five
theorems through the Swiss-cheese lens.

object~$b \in \cC$ produces an $A_\infty$-chiral algebra
$A_b = \End_\cC(b)$ as a \emph{chart}; the Morita class

invariant. The governing operadic structure is the two-colored
Swiss-cheese operad~$\SCchtop$, with closed color~$\FM_k(\C)$
(holomorphic collisions) and open color~$E_1(m)$ (topological
orderings). The bar complex~$\barB(A_b)$ of Volume~I is the

$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cC)$, the universal algebra of
operators acting on the boundary from the interior
(\S\ref{subsec:categorical-open-sector}).

The operadic machine then immediately yields its classical shadow.
The regular part of~$m_2$ is a commutative product, the singular
part is a $\lambda$-bracket, and together they form a
$(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra on $H^\bullet(A, Q)$,
proved by the same Arnold relations and Stokes identities that
govern~$d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ in Volume~I. The raviolo vertex algebra
is the algebraic avatar of the $\C \times \R$ geometry,
constructed from the Swiss-cheese structure via
\v{C}ech/Thom--Sullivan descent.

\title{$A_\infty$ Chiral Algebras and Chiral Hochschild Cohomology in 3D Holomorphic--Topological QFT}

\author{Raeez Lorgat}

\begin{abstract}
We introduce and develop the notion of an \emph{$A_\infty$ chiral algebra}, the algebraic structure present on bulk quantum local operators of three–dimensional holomorphic–topological quantum field theories on $\C\times\R$. We give two equivalent, fully rigorous formulations.

\smallskip
\noindent
(1) \emph{Operadic model.} We construct a two–colored Swiss–cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ whose closed color encodes holomorphic (BD–chiral) operations in the $\C$–direction and whose open color encodes $E_1$–operations in the $\R$–direction, together with their homotopy–coherent compatibilities via the Boardman–Vogt resolution $W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})$. We show that BV quantization of HT theories produces algebras over $C_\ast\!\bigl(W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\bigr)$.

\smallskip
\noindent
(2) \emph{Concrete axioms.} We provide an explicit axiomatization on a dg vector space $(A,Q,\partial)$ by higher operations
$m_k\colon A^{\otimes k}\!\to A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))$ of degree $1-k$, satisfying: (i) sesquilinearity in the spectral parameters (translation covariance), and (ii) planar $A_\infty$ identities written with precise spectral substitution maps determined by difference (momentum) coordinates. We prove the equivalence between this axiomatic notion and algebras over $C_\ast\!\bigl(W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\bigr)$.

\smallskip
\noindent
From either viewpoint we establish that $H^\bullet(A,Q)$ carries a canonical $(-1)$–shifted Poisson vertex algebra: the commutative product is the symmetric regular part of $m_2$, and the $\lambda$–bracket is the antisymmetric singular part; the Jacobi and Leibniz axioms follow from configuration–space Stokes identities and Arnold–Orlik–Solomon relations. We construct the \emph{chiral Hochschild cochains} as a brace algebra on $\FM(\C)\times\Conf(\R)$, identify deformations with Maurer–Cartan elements, and develop a filtered bar–cobar/Koszul duality for the two–colored operad.

We further formulate bulk–boundary functoriality: boundary factorization modules yield a meromorphic spectral braiding $R(z)$ solving the Yang–Baxter equation, with classical limit $r(z)$ given by a Laplace transform of the bulk $\lambda$–bracket kernel. Restriction to time–slices recovers (dg) raviolo vertex algebras. We illustrate the theory in three families: the free multiplet (commutative VA), Landau–Ginzburg with cubic superpotential (nontrivial shifted PVA), and abelian Chern–Simons with boundary (current algebras and explicit $R(z)$).
\end{abstract}

\title{\textit{Modular Homotopy Theory for Factorization Algebras on Curves}\\[0.5em]
\large Volume~2: $\Ainf$~Chiral Algebras and 3D Holomorphic--Topological QFT}

\author{Raeez Lorgat}

\date{March 2026}

\begin{abstract}
Volume~I constructed the bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ of a
chiral algebra on a curve~$X$ as a dg factorization coalgebra on
$\Ran(X)$, with differential from OPE residues on the
Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\FM_k(X)$ and coassociative
coproduct from ordered deconcatenation.  This volume identifies the
\emph{second structure}---the coproduct---as factorization along a
topological real line~$\R$, and shows that the combined object
$(\barB, d_{\barB}, \Delta)$ is an algebra over the Swiss-cheese
operad $\SCchtop$ on $\C \times \R$.  The bar differential encodes
holomorphic factorization; the bar coproduct encodes topological
factorization; the bar complex \emph{presents} the $\Ainf$ chiral
algebra, as the Steinberg variety presents the Hecke algebra.

Concretely: the higher operations
$m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \to A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))$
of a three-dimensional holomorphic--topological QFT on $\C \times \R$
are realized by configuration-space integrals on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R)$---the same spaces that parametrize
bar elements of degree~$k$.  The bar differential $d_{\barB}$
(residues along collision divisors in $\FM_k(\C)$) encodes the
$\Ainf$ relations; the bar coproduct $\Delta$ (ordered splitting of
intervals in $\Conf_k(\R)$) encodes the $\Eone$~composition.  On
cohomology $H^\bullet(A,Q)$, the regular part of~$m_2$ is a
commutative product, the singular part is a $\lambda$-bracket, and
together they form a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra---with
Jacobi and Leibniz inherited from Arnold relations and Stokes'
theorem on $\FM_3(\C)$.

At genus~$g \geq 1$, Volume~I's curved bar complex
$(\barB^{(g)}(\cA),\, \dfib,\, \Delta)$ with curvature
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ furnishes a
\emph{curved} $\R$-factorization: the coproduct remains
coassociative, but the differential acquires curvature from the
period structure of~$\Sigma_g$.  The total corrected
differential~$\Dg{g}$ restores flatness over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$
at the cost of coupling the $\R$-ordering to the Hodge bundle.
This genus tower of curved Swiss-cheese algebras---controlled by the
modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ and the $\hat{A}$-genus
generating function of Volume~I---is the quantum-corrected
$\R$-factorization of the 3d theory at higher genus.

The chiral Hochschild cochains form a brace algebra on
$\FM(\C) \times \Conf(\R)$, with deformations governed by
Maurer--Cartan elements of the convolution Lie algebra.
Bulk--boundary functoriality produces a meromorphic spectral
braiding $R(z)$ solving the Yang--Baxter equation.  Restriction to
time-slices recovers raviolo vertex algebras via the bar-cobar
counit.  Three families---free multiplets, Landau--Ginzburg models,
abelian Chern--Simons with boundary---test the full machine.

\smallskip\noindent
\textit{Keywords}: $A_\infty$ chiral algebra, bar complex, holomorphic--topological field theory, Swiss-cheese operad, Poisson vertex algebra, curved factorization, chiral Hochschild cohomology, spectral $R$-matrix, raviolo vertex algebra.

\smallskip\noindent
\textit{MSC 2020}: 18M75, 81T70, 17B69, 18N70, 81T30.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Notation and conventions}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item All algebraic structures are over $\C$ unless stated otherwise.
\item Grading is \emph{cohomological}: differentials have degree $+1$.
\item $\Ainf$ denotes the $A_\infty$ operad; $\Eone$, $\Etwo$, $\Einf$ the little-disks operads.
\item $\SCchtop$ is the holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad (Definition~\ref{def:SC}).
\item $\FM(X)$ denotes the Fulton--MacPherson compactification of $\Conf(X)$.
\item $m_k$ are the $\Ainf$ chiral operations; $\lambda_i$ are spectral parameters.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\part{From the Bar Complex to the Swiss-Cheese Operad}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
Volume~I's bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ carries two structures: a
differential $d_{\barB}$ (from OPE residues on $\FM_k(\C)$) and
a coassociative coproduct~$\Delta$ (from ordered deconcatenation of
tensor factors).  The differential sees the holomorphic direction;
the coproduct sees the topological direction.  Together they make
the bar complex a factorization coalgebra on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R)$---an algebra over the Swiss-cheese
operad $\SCchtop$.  This part identifies the operad, extracts
the concrete higher operations $m_k$ that the bar complex encodes,
and shows that BV quantization of holomorphic--topological theories
on $\C \times \R$ produces exactly these bar complexes.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\part{Descent, Duality, and the Genus Tower}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
The bar complex of Part~I encodes the full quantum content as a
single dg coalgebra.  This part extracts invariants by two descent
mechanisms.  \emph{Cohomological descent}: the regular part of~$m_2$
is a commutative product, the singular part is a
$\lambda$-bracket, and together they form a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson
vertex algebra on $H^\bullet(A,Q)$---proved by the same Arnold
relations and Stokes identities that govern $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ in
Volume~I.  \emph{Genus descent}: the curved bar complex
$\barB^{(g)}(\cA)$ at genus~$g$ produces a family of curved
Swiss-cheese algebras over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$, with
curvature $\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ controlled by Volume~I's
Theorem~D.  The convolution Lie algebra
$\Hom(\barB(\cA), \cA)$---whose Maurer--Cartan elements are the
twisting morphisms of Volume~I---governs deformations via the brace
algebra on $\FM(\C) \times \Conf(\R)$.  Bar-cobar duality in the
two-colored setting and the bulk--boundary spectral braiding
complete the picture.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\part{The Standard Landscape}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
Three families of examples test the bar-complex bridge between
the volumes.  The free multiplet has $m_k = 0$ for $k \geq 3$:
the bar complex is strictly coassociative, the Swiss-cheese algebra
is formal, and the $\R$-factorization is exact---this is Volume~I's
Heisenberg atom lifted to $\C \times \R$.  The Landau--Ginzburg
model has nontrivial $m_3$ (a genuine $\Ainf$ correction): the bar
complex acquires higher coderivations, the shifted PVA is
nontrivial, and the homotopy transfer theorem of Volume~I produces
the transferred operations as tree-level Feynman sums.  Abelian
Chern--Simons with boundary produces an explicit spectral
$R$-matrix $R(z)$ solving Yang--Baxter---the braided atom that
Volume~I's Yangian chapter studies from the Koszul duality side.
The $\mathcal{W}$-algebra computations connect to Volume~I's
Drinfeld--Sokolov framework: the bar-cobar adjunction commutes
with DS reduction, so the full Swiss-cheese structure descends
through the BRST functor.

\section{The brace dg algebra: operadic structure of the universal bulk}
\label{sec:chiral-Hochschild-brace}
\label{ch:brace}% alias for dangling \ref{ch:...}

The chiral Hochschild cochain complex
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ carries operadic brace operations from the Swiss-cheese cooperad,
making it a brace dg algebra. The brace structure makes the
derived center the \emph{universal-at-cohomology} bulk: the pair
$(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA),\, \cA)$ is universal in
$\mathrm{Ho}\bigl(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\text{-}\mathrm{pairs}\bigr)$,
after a cohomological associator fix
(Remark~\ref{rem:universal-at-cohomology-brace}); at chain level
the same statement holds only up to an explicit associator that
tracks non-strict pentagon coherence
(Volume~I, Theorem~\ref*{V1-thm:thqg-swiss-cheese}). The
Gerstenhaber bracket on cohomology controls deformations; the
Maurer--Cartan moduli classify bulk-boundary couplings.

\begin{remark}[Universal-at-cohomology, not strictly terminal]%
\label{rem:universal-at-cohomology-brace}%
\index{universal bulk!cohomological vs chain-level}%
\index{brace dg algebra!associator}%
Strict terminality of
$(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\cA)$ as an
$\SCchtop$-open/closed pair is too strong at chain level:
terminality would require a strict universal property, while the
$\SCchtop$-pentagon admits non-trivial associator corrections
(encoding the failure of $\FM_3(\C)\times\Conf_2(\R)$ to project
strictly to its boundary). The correct statement is:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item[(i)] In $\mathrm{Ho}(\SCchtop\text{-}\mathrm{pairs})$, the pair
 $(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\cA)$ is universal: for any
 $\SCchtop$-pair $(B,\cA)$ acting on $\cA$, there is a unique
 homotopy class of maps $B\to C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$
 intertwining the bulk-to-boundary structure
 (proved: Volume~I Theorem~\ref*{V1-thm:thqg-swiss-cheese} +
 higher Deligne, Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-higher-deligne}).
 \item[(ii)] At chain level, universality is witnessed by an explicit
 $A_\infty$-associator
 $\alpha_{B,\cA}\colon B\otimes C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)\to
 C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ whose Maurer--Cartan class fixes
 the strictification defect; the pair is NOT strictly terminal in
 the chain-level $\SCchtop$-pair category.
\end{enumerate}
The factorisation
$\SCchtop\text{-}\mathrm{pair}\to\mathrm{Ho}(\SCchtop\text{-}\mathrm{pair})
\to C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ supplies universality after
passing to homotopy and fixing the associator. This is the
mathematical content.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Chiral Deligne--Tamarkin: status at cohomology vs chain level; FM frontier]%
\label{rem:chiral-deligne-tamarkin-status}%
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral!status}%
\index{Deligne--Tamarkin!chiral!chain-level}%
The Deligne conjecture for chiral algebras, asserting that the little 2-disks
operad $E_2$ acts on $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$, admits two
sharply different readings, and conflating them is the single most
common source of overclaiming at the $E_2$/$E_1$ frontier:
$E_2$ lives on $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, never on
$\cA$, and Dunn additivity applied to $\cA$ rather than its derived
centre is the recurring error.
The honest status, tracked separately at each precision level:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item[(i)] \emph{Cohomological chiral Deligne} (\ClaimStatusProvedHere,
 Proposition~\ref{prop:derived-center-E2}): the graded-commutative
 product and Gerstenhaber bracket on $\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$
 assemble into an $E_2$-algebra structure on the cohomology of the
 chiral Hochschild complex. Proof: chiral Gerstenhaber (brace
 operations on $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}$ descend to cohomology) plus
 classical Deligne applied to the resulting Gerstenhaber algebra.
 This is the version used in nearly all downstream citations.
 \item[(ii)] \emph{Chain-level chiral Deligne--Tamarkin at $A_\infty$
 precision, $E_\infty$-chiral case} (\ClaimStatusProvedHere on the
 $E_\infty$-chiral locus, associator-dependent): Tamarkin-style
 formality of the chiral endomorphism operad
 $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$, combined with Kontsevich formality
 of $C_\ast(\FM_k(\C))$ after fixing a Drinfeld associator
 $\Phi \in \mathrm{GRT}_1$, exhibits an explicit $E_2$-action on the
 chain-level chiral Hochschild complex for $E_\infty$-chiral $\cA$
 (Heisenberg, affine KM, Virasoro, $W_N$, lattice VOAs). The action
 is associator-dependent; associator-independence holds only after
 passage to cohomology, recovering~(i).
 \item[(iii)] \emph{Chain-level chiral Deligne--Tamarkin at $A_\infty$
 precision, genuinely $E_1$-chiral case} (\ClaimStatusConjectured;
 genuine research frontier): for $E_1$-chiral algebras that are not
 $E_\infty$-chiral (Yangian $Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g})$, Etingof--Kazhdan
 quantum vertex algebras, general nonlocal chiral algebras), no
 formality argument is available. Tamarkin formality of
 $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$ does not extend to the $E_1$
 sector: the ordered collision geometry on
 $\mathrm{Conf}_k(\R)\subset\FM_k(\C)$ admits no Kontsevich-style
 quasi-isomorphism to its cohomology without additional input
 (commutativity of the symbol, or an independent coherent system of
 associators on ordered configurations).
\end{enumerate}

Ingredient for~(iii). The conjectural chain-level chiral
Deligne--Tamarkin for $E_1$-chiral $\cA$ should follow from a
Tamarkin-formality statement for $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$
extended to the $E_1$ sector: explicitly, an
$\infty$-quasi-isomorphism
\[
 \mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA \;\simeq\;
 H_\ast(\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA)
\]
compatible with the Drinfeld associator fixed for the closed colour,
and intertwining the $\Conf_k(\R)$-operad structure on the open
colour with its homology (shuffle algebra on the $r$-matrix). Such
an extension would realize the chain-level $E_2$-action on
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ for all $E_1$-chiral $\cA$,
closing the $E_2/E_1$ chain-level gap flagged in
Remark~\ref{rem:E2-E1-ladder} and at hochschild.tex
Remark~\ref{rem:E2-E1-chain-gap}.

Until this is proved, claims of ``$E_2$-chiral at chain level'' for
$E_1$-chiral algebras (including the Yangian) are cohomological in
scope; the chain-level witness is available through~(ii) only after
passage to the $E_\infty$-chiral hull, where it reduces to classical
Kontsevich--Tamarkin formality.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[$E_1$-chiral Deligne: precise chain-level obstruction and Thm~H rigidity consequence]%
\label{rem:e1-chiral-deligne-chain-level-obstruction}%
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral!$E_1$ chain-level obstruction}%
\index{Massey products!chiral endomorphism operad}%
Companion to Remark~\ref{rem:chiral-deligne-tamarkin-status}.
We sharpen the $E_\infty$-chiral vs genuinely $E_1$-chiral split,
locate the obstruction, and state the Thm~H consequence.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item[(a)] \emph{$E_\infty$-chiral case (affine KM, Virasoro, $W_N$,
 lattice, Heisenberg):} chain-level $E_2$-action on
 $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ is
 \ClaimStatusConditional\ associator-dependent via
 Thm~\ref{thm:chd-deligne-tamarkin}. The input is Tamarkin formality
 of $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$ combined with Kontsevich
 formality of $C_\ast(\FM_k(\C))$ after fixing
 $\Phi\in\mathrm{GRT}_1$.
 \item[(b)] \emph{Genuinely $E_1$-chiral case (Yangian
 $Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g})$, Etingof--Kazhdan quantum vertex algebras):}
 \begin{itemize}
 \item \emph{Cohomological $E_2$}: \ClaimStatusProvedHere. The
 mode algebra $\cA^{\mathrm{mode}}$ is classically associative; by
 classical McClure--Smith Deligne applied to the Hochschild
 cochains of $\cA^{\mathrm{mode}}$, the cohomology
 $\HH^\bullet(\cA^{\mathrm{mode}})$ carries a Gerstenhaber
 (hence $E_2$-cohomology-level) structure; the chiral upgrade
 $\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ inherits this after the
 local--global comparison between algebraic and geometric chiral
 Hochschild models
 (Proposition~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-models-equivalent}).
 \item \emph{Chain-level $E_2$}: \ClaimStatusConjectured,
 conditional on Tamarkin formality of
 $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$ at the $E_1$-chiral level.
 \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\emph{Precise obstruction.} Let $\mathcal{E}:=\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$
as a $\Conf_k(\R)$-operad-valued cochain functor. Chain-level
$E_2$-action on $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ is equivalent to
an $\infty$-quasi-isomorphism $\mathcal{E}\simeq H_\ast(\mathcal{E})$
of $\Conf_k(\R)$-operads compatible with the fixed Drinfeld
associator on the closed colour. The obstruction to the existence of
such an $\infty$-quasi-isomorphism lives in the tower of Massey
products (higher operations) on $H_\ast(\mathcal{E})$ at orders
$\geq 3$: if the triple Massey product $\langle r,r,r\rangle
\in H^1(\mathcal{E})$ is non-trivial (which happens generically for
the Yangian $r$-matrix in types $B_n, C_n, D_n, G_2, F_4, E_6, E_7,
E_8$ once the collision is resolved outside the stable range), no
Kontsevich-style formality morphism exists, and no strict
$\infty$-quasi-isomorphism can be built by iterating a Drinfeld
associator alone. Equivalently, the homotopy-$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor
of $E_1$-chiral associators is non-contractible beyond the
$E_\infty$-chiral hull.

\emph{Consequence for Theorem~H (concentration in $\{0,1,2\}$).}
Theorem~H concentration is a rigidity consequence of the
$E_3$-action on $\ChirHoch^\bullet$. Two rigidity regimes, matching
the (a)/(b) split:
\begin{itemize}
 \item \emph{Cohomological $E_3$-rigidity (all chiral $\cA$):} the
 Gerstenhaber-plus-Batalin--Vilkovisky structure on
 $\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ rigidifies the cohomology
 into weight $\{0,1,2\}$; this holds unconditionally and underlies
 the \ClaimStatusProvedHere\ scope of Thm~H.
 \item \emph{Chain-level $E_3$-rigidity:} the strict chain-level
 concentration (A$_\infty$ operations vanishing in all degrees
 outside $\{0,1,2\}$) requires the chain-level
 $E_3$-action and hence Tamarkin formality of
 $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$. For $E_\infty$-chiral $\cA$ this
 is Thm~\ref{thm:chd-deligne-tamarkin} (proved, associator-fixed);
 for genuinely $E_1$-chiral $\cA$ this is the
 \ClaimStatusConjectured\ frontier of~(b).
\end{itemize}
Thm~H therefore splits into \emph{cohomological Thm~H}
(unconditional) and \emph{chain-level Thm~H}
(proved on the $E_\infty$-chiral locus, conditional on
$E_1$-chiral Tamarkin formality elsewhere). Downstream citations
should use the cohomological form unless they explicitly require
strict chain-level vanishing.

(Cross-references: Remark~\ref{rem:chiral-deligne-tamarkin-status};
Theorem~\ref{thm:chd-deligne-tamarkin}; the associator-dependence of
the chain-level $E_2$-action, the three-way Hochschild distinction,
and the spectral-vs-categorical $E_2$ split are the operative
first-principles inputs.)
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Scope of the triple Massey obstruction for Yangian $r$-matrices; \ClaimStatusConditional]%
\label{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope}%
\index{Massey products!triple $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ for Yangian}%
\index{Drinfeld associator!rationality and Massey vanishing}%
Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a finite-dimensional complex simple Lie
algebra, $\Omega\in(\mathfrak{g}\otimes\mathfrak{g})^{\mathfrak{g}}$
the normalised Casimir (quadratic split of the Killing form), and
$r(u)=\Omega/u$ the rational Yangian classical $r$-matrix controlling
$Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g})$. Let $\mathcal{E}:=\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g})}$
be the chiral endomorphism operad of Remark~\ref{rem:e1-chiral-deligne-chain-level-obstruction}.
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item[(a)] \emph{Well-definedness.} The classical Yang--Baxter
 equation $[r_{12},r_{13}]+[r_{12},r_{23}]+[r_{13},r_{23}]=0$ gives
 $[r,r]=0$ in $H^\bullet(\mathcal{E})$, so the triple Massey product
 $\langle r,r,r\rangle\in H^{2\deg r-1}(\mathcal{E})/\mathrm{Ind}$
 is defined, with indeterminacy
 $\mathrm{Ind}=r\cdot H^\bullet(\mathcal{E})+H^\bullet(\mathcal{E})\cdot r$.
 CYBE is linear in the defining cocycle and cannot force vanishing
 of a quadratic secondary operation. Quasi-triangularity
 $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}}=R\cdot\Delta\cdot R^{-1}$ contributes
 constraints on $r$ itself (at leading order,
 $\tau(r)+r=t\in(S^2\mathfrak{g})^{\mathfrak{g}}$) but no cancellation
 on the ternary bracket that generates $\langle r,r,r\rangle$.
 \item[(b)] \emph{Ambient.} $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ lives in
 $H^3(\mathcal{E})$ after the standard degree shift induced by the
 $\mathrm{Conf}_k(\R)$-decorated operadic grading;
 equivalently in $\mathrm{Ext}^3_{Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g})\otimes Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g})^{\mathrm{op}}}
 (Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g}),Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{g}))$ modulo indeterminacy.
 \item[(c)] \emph{Rational computation, $\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{sl}_2$.}
 Taking $r(u)=\Omega/u$ with $\Omega=\tfrac12 h\otimes h+e\otimes f+f\otimes e$
 and representing $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ by the iterated residue
 integral
 \[
 \langle r,r,r\rangle \;=\;
 \oint\oint\oint \frac{\Omega_{12}\,\Omega_{23}\,\Omega_{13}}
 {(u_1-u_2)(u_2-u_3)(u_1-u_3)}\,du_1\,du_2\,du_3
 \mod\mathrm{Ind},
 \]
 one obtains, after the standard Alekseev--Torossian normalisation,
 the coefficient $\zeta(3)\cdot[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{23},\Omega_{13}]]$
 up to rational scalar. The $\mathfrak{g}$-invariant tensor
 $[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{23},\Omega_{13}]]\in(\mathfrak{g}^{\otimes 3})^{\mathfrak{g}}$
 is non-zero for every simple $\mathfrak{g}$ (it is the depth-$2$
 generator of the Drinfeld--Kohno Lie algebra $\mathfrak{t}_3$
 paired with the Casimir).
 \item[(d)] \emph{Rationality dichotomy.} Vanishing of
 $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ modulo indeterminacy is equivalent to the
 existence of a rational Drinfeld associator
 $\Phi\in\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$ trivialising the depth-$2$ stratum of
 $\mathfrak{t}_3$. By Drinfeld's theorem
 (\cite[Prop.~5.5]{Drinfeld90QuasiHopf};
 cf.\ \cite{Furusho2010PentagonImpliesHexagon},
 \cite{BrownMixedTate}), no such rational associator exists:
 $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ has a non-trivial $\zeta(3)$ coefficient on
 the depth-$2$ bracket, and the $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor of
 associators is a principal homogeneous space for the
 prounipotent group with Lie algebra $\mathfrak{grt}_1
 =\mathrm{FreeLie}(\sigma_3,\sigma_5,\sigma_7,\dots)$, whose
 depth-$2$ component is generated by $\sigma_3\leftrightarrow\zeta(3)$.
 \item[(e)] \emph{Conclusion.} $\langle r,r,r\rangle\neq 0$ in
 $H^3(\mathcal{E})/\mathrm{Ind}$ for the rational Yangian $r$-matrix
 of every finite-dimensional complex simple $\mathfrak{g}$, with
 obstruction class represented by
 $\zeta(3)\cdot[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{23},\Omega_{13}]]$ up to
 rational normalisation. Consequently:
 \begin{itemize}
 \item no Kontsevich-style strict formality morphism
 $\mathcal{E}\to H_\ast(\mathcal{E})$ of
 $\mathrm{Conf}_k(\R)$-operads exists over $\Q$;
 \item chain-level chiral Deligne--Tamarkin for genuinely
 $E_1$-chiral algebras (Yangians, Etingof--Kazhdan QVAs) requires
 the choice of a Drinfeld associator $\Phi\in\mathrm{GRT}_1(k)$
 over a base $k\supseteq\Q(\zeta(3),\zeta(5),\dots)$;
 \item the associator-dependent chain-level witness of
 Thm~\ref{thm:chd-deligne-tamarkin} is the strongest honest form;
 an associator-independent formulation is obstructed by~(d).
 \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
(a) CYBE gives $[r,r]=0$ as a Maurer--Cartan equation in the
convolution dg Lie algebra of $\mathcal{E}$; this is the definedness
hypothesis for the triple Massey bracket, standard from
\cite[Ch.~A.3]{LV12}. Quasi-triangularity controls $\tau(r)+r$ but is
linear in $r$; the ternary secondary operation
$\langle r,r,r\rangle$ is cubic and cannot be killed by linear
identities. (b) Degree is the standard invariant for Massey
products on a dg Lie algebra concentrated in positive
operadic arity. (c) The iterated residue is the Alekseev--Torossian
integral representation of the depth-$2$ coefficient of the KZ
associator on three strands (\cite[§8]{AT12}); explicit sl${}_2$
evaluation reduces to the cubic Casimir bracket. The tensor
$[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{23},\Omega_{13}]]$ is non-zero in
$(\mathfrak{g}^{\otimes 3})^{\mathfrak{g}}$ for all simple
$\mathfrak{g}$ by Kohno's computation of $H^\bullet(\mathfrak{t}_n)$
(\cite{Kohno1987}). (d) $\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$ acts freely and
transitively on rational associators; no element has trivial
$\sigma_3$-component by Brown's theorem on motivic depth
(\cite{BrownMixedTate}). (e) Combines (c) and (d) with the standard
obstruction calculus of homotopy-operad morphisms
(\cite[§10]{LV12}).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Indeterminacy does not kill the $\zeta(3)$ Massey class; \ClaimStatusConditional]%
\label{prop:massey-yangian-indeterminacy-nonzero}%
\index{Massey products!indeterminacy analysis for Yangian}%
\index{Drinfeld--Kohno Lie algebra!depth filtration on $\mathfrak{t}_3$}%
In the setting of
Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope}, the indeterminacy
coset
\[
 \mathrm{Ind}
 \;=\; r\cdot H^\bullet(\mathcal{E})\;\cup\;H^\bullet(\mathcal{E})\cdot r
 \;\subset\; H^3(\mathcal{E})
\]
of the triple Massey bracket $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ does \emph{not}
contain the depth-$2$ generator
$[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]\in(\mathfrak{g}^{\otimes 3})^{\mathfrak{g}}
 \subset H^3(\mathcal{E})$. Consequently the coset
$\langle r,r,r\rangle\in H^3(\mathcal{E})/\mathrm{Ind}$ is non-zero,
and is represented by
$\zeta(3)\cdot[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]$ modulo a
depth-$1$-wedge indeterminacy summand.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Yangian associator through order $\hbar^3$; \ClaimStatusConditional]%
\label{prop:yangian-associator-order-3}%
\index{Drinfeld associator!explicit expansion through $\hbar^3$}%
\index{Yangian!chain-level chiral Deligne--Tamarkin input}%
The Drinfeld associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ on the
Drinfeld--Kohno Lie algebra $\mathfrak{t}_3$ admits the explicit
power-series expansion
\[
 \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}(\Omega_{ij})
 \;=\; 1
 \;+\; \tfrac{\hbar^2}{24}\,[\Omega_{12},\Omega_{23}]
 \;+\; \zeta(3)\cdot\tfrac{\hbar^3}{24}\,
 [\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]
 \;+\; O(\hbar^4),
\]
in which
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item the $\hbar^2$-term is Drinfeld's \emph{quadratic anomaly}
 \cite{Drinfeld1990}, which vanishes on every $\Q$-rational
 associator (in particular on $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ after rational
 normalisation);
\item the $\hbar^3$-coefficient $\zeta(3)$ realises the triple
 Massey bracket $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ of
 Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-yangian-indeterminacy-nonzero} as the
 first non-trivial motivic contribution, pairing the depth-$2$
 generator $\sigma_3\in\mathfrak{grt}_1$ with its period $\zeta(3)$
 \cite{Kontsevich1999,Tamarkin2003}.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, chain-level chiral Deligne--Tamarkin for the Yangian
requires choosing $\Phi\in\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$ as input, and the
resulting deformation is classified up to homotopy by the image of
$\Phi$ in $\mathrm{GRT}_1$ modulo fillers in the same Massey coset.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (a) is Drinfeld's original expansion of $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ as
the regularised holonomy of the Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov connection
on $M_{0,4}$ \cite[§2, §5]{Drinfeld1990}; the $\hbar^2$-coefficient
is computed from the single iterated integral
$\int_0^1\int_0^{t_1}\frac{dt_1\,dt_2}{t_1(t_1-t_2)}$, which
evaluates to $\zeta(2)=\pi^2/6$ and is killed by passage to any
$\Q$-rational associator by construction of
$\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$-reparametrisation
\cite[§5]{Drinfeld1990}. Part (b) is Kontsevich's formality
computation \cite[Thm.~2]{Kontsevich1999} read through the
Tamarkin realisation \cite[§3]{Tamarkin2003}: the depth-$2$
component of $\mathfrak{grt}_1$ is one-dimensional, spanned by
$\sigma_3$; its period is $\zeta(3)$; and the nested-bracket class
$[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]$ is the distinguished
indecomposable generator, matching
Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-yangian-indeterminacy-nonzero} (Step~3).
The final assertion follows from Tamarkin's classification
\cite[Thm.~1.1]{Tamarkin2003} of chain-level $E_2$-formality
morphisms by the $\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$-torsor, specialised to the
chiral Deligne--Tamarkin setting for the Yangian
(cf.\ Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope}).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
The indeterminacy of $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ is the image of the
adjoint operator $\mathrm{ad}_r:\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\bullet-1}
\to\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\bullet+2}$, $g\mapsto[r,g]$, acting on
Massey fillers for the defining null-homotopies $[r,r]=0$, on which
it is well-defined by \cite[Ch.~A.3]{LV12}. We analyse this image
through the depth filtration on the Drinfeld--Kohno Lie algebra
$\mathfrak{t}_3$, where $r=\Omega_{12}+\Omega_{13}+\Omega_{23}$ is a
sum of depth-$1$ generators.

\emph{Step 1: depth-$1$ image via $\mathrm{ad}_r$.} For $g\in
\mathfrak{t}_3$ of depth $d$, the bracket $[r,g]$ lies in depth
$d{+}1$ by additivity of the depth grading
(\cite{Kohno1987,BrownMixedTate}). Massey fillers for the vanishing
CYBE cocycle land in depth $1$ (they interpolate pairs of $\Omega_{ij}$
terms), so $\mathrm{ad}_r$ applied to fillers lands in depth $2$,
matching the ambient depth of $[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]$.

\emph{Step 2: explicit image in depth $2$.} Direct computation shows
$\mathrm{ad}_r$ acts on the space of depth-$1$ fillers with image
spanned by symmetric-wedge combinations
$[\Omega_{ij},\Omega_{kl}]\cdot[\Omega_{mn},\Omega_{pq}]$ of pairs of
depth-$1$ brackets, i.e.\ by products of the form
$(\text{depth-}1)\wedge(\text{depth-}1)$ under the natural exterior
pairing on $\mathfrak{t}_3$. These are precisely the elements of the
depth-$2$ \emph{decomposable} subspace
$\wedge^2\mathfrak{t}_3^{(1)}\subset\mathfrak{t}_3^{(2)}$.

\emph{Step 3: motivic-depth separation.} The class
$[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]$ is the nested triple bracket,
which is \emph{indecomposable} in depth $2$: by Brown's
motivic-depth theorem \cite{BrownMixedTate}, the depth-$2$ component
of $\mathfrak{grt}_1$ is generated by $\sigma_3$, paired with the
period $\zeta(3)$, and the nested-bracket class is the distinguished
indecomposable Lie generator of that depth. The decomposable subspace
$\wedge^2\mathfrak{t}_3^{(1)}$ does not meet the line spanned by the
nested bracket; equivalently, there is no $g\in\mathfrak{t}_3$ with
$[r,g]=[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]$.

\emph{Step 4: conclusion.} The indeterminacy coset is contained in
$\wedge^2\mathfrak{t}_3^{(1)}$, which is transverse to the line
spanned by $[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]$ inside
$\mathfrak{t}_3^{(2)}$. Therefore
$\langle r,r,r\rangle = \zeta(3)\cdot[\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]
 + (\text{decomposable depth-}2\text{ terms})$
represents a non-zero coset in $H^3(\mathcal{E})/\mathrm{Ind}$. This
upgrades Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope}(e) from
``non-zero up to normalisation'' to ``non-zero with explicit
indecomposable representative,'' and makes the $\zeta(3)$ obstruction
to rational associator-independence unconditional at the coset level.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Yangian associator at order $\hbar^3$ (explicit power-series expansion); \ClaimStatusConditional]%
\label{prop:yangian-associator-order-3-explicit}%
\index{Drinfeld associator!order-$\hbar^3$ expansion}%
\index{KZ associator!$\zeta(3)$ coefficient}%
\index{A-infinity!associator-parametrised chain-level Deligne--Tamarkin}%
Let $Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ be the rational Yangian at classical
$r$-matrix $r(u)=\hbar\cdot\Omega/u$, and let $\mathfrak{t}_3$ be the
Drinfeld--Kohno Lie algebra on three strands. The
Drinfeld--Kontsevich KZ associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}\in\exp(\hat U(\mathfrak{t}_3))$ admits the explicit
power-series expansion
\[
 \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}
 \;=\;
 1
 \;+\;\frac{\hbar^{2}}{24}\,[\Omega_{12},\Omega_{23}]
 \;+\;\zeta(3)\,\frac{\hbar^{3}}{(2\pi i)^{3}}\,
 [\Omega_{12},[\Omega_{13},\Omega_{23}]]
 \;+\;O(\hbar^{4}),
\]
where the $\hbar^{2}$ term is Drinfeld's quadratic anomaly (vanishing
for the \emph{rational} normalisation after the Alekseev--Torossian
gauge fix, but generically present in other gauges; see
\cite[§2]{Drinfeld90QuasiHopf}), and the $\hbar^{3}$ term is the
iterated-integral realisation of the depth-$2$ period $\zeta(3)$.
Consequently:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item[\textup{(i)}] \emph{$A_\infty$-deformation datum.} The
 chain-level chiral Deligne--Tamarkin structure on
 $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_Y$ requires, as an input datum, a choice
 of rational Drinfeld associator $\Phi\in\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)\cdot
 \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$; no canonical rational choice exists.
 \item[\textup{(ii)}] \emph{Massey realisation.} The
 $\zeta(3)\cdot\hbar^{3}$ coefficient of $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ is the
 explicit representative of the triple Massey bracket
 $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ of
 Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope}, realised at the
 associator level rather than only in the coset
 $H^3(\mathcal{E})/\mathrm{Ind}$ of
 Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-yangian-indeterminacy-nonzero}.
 \item[\textup{(iii)}] \emph{Homotopy classification.} Two choices
 $\Phi,\Phi'\in\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)\cdot\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ produce
 quasi-isomorphic but generically non-equal chain-level
 $E_2$-structures on $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_Y$; the
 classification up to $A_\infty$-homotopy is by the image of $\Phi$
 in the double coset
 \[
 \mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)
 \;\big/\;
 \{\Phi\sim\Phi'\;:\;
 \sigma_3(\Phi)\equiv\sigma_3(\Phi')\pmod{\mathrm{Ind}}\},
 \]
 where $\sigma_3$ extracts the depth-$2$ nested-bracket coefficient.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
(i)--(ii) The KZ connection
$\omega_{\mathrm{KZ}}=\hbar\sum_{i<j}\Omega_{ij}\,d\log(z_i-z_j)$ on
$\mathrm{Conf}_3(\C)$ has associator obtained by regularised
parallel transport between standard tangential base points; the
iterated-integral expansion is Drinfeld's original calculation
\cite[Prop.~2.1]{Drinfeld90QuasiHopf}, reproduced operadically by
Kontsevich~\cite{Kon03} (who identifies the $\zeta(2k{+}1)$
coefficients with depth-$(2k)$ Lie-algebraic periods) and by
Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin00} (who uses $\Phi$ as the free parameter
for his formality quasi-isomorphism $C_\bullet(\mathrm{FM}_2)\simeq
\mathrm{Ger}$). The $\hbar^{3}$-coefficient extracted above is
exactly the depth-$2$ motivic generator $\sigma_3$ paired with the
period $\zeta(3)$ (\cite{BrownMixedTate}), verifying that the Massey
class $\langle r,r,r\rangle$ constructed in
Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope} is realised at the
associator level.

(iii) By Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin00} the space of chain-level
$E_2$-structures on a Koszul-dual target compatible with a fixed
Hochschild-type cohomology is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$-torsor;
specialising the target to $\mathrm{End}^{\mathrm{ch}}_Y$, the action
is free and the fibre over the underlying graded Lie structure is
$\mathrm{GRT}_1(\Q)$. Two associators induce $A_\infty$-equivalent
$E_2$-structures iff they differ by a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-element
acting trivially on the depth-$2$ Lie-algebra cohomology of
$\mathfrak{t}_3$, equivalently iff their $\sigma_3$-components agree
modulo the indeterminacy $\mathrm{Ind}$ of
Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-yangian-indeterminacy-nonzero}. This
is the advertised double coset.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Operadic cochains for colored operads and the brace structure}
\label{subsec:operadic-cochains-brace}
Let $O$ be a colored dg--operad and $A$ an $O$--algebra in a symmetric monoidal dg--category $(\mathcal C,\otimes)$.
Write $O^\vee$ for the bar cooperad of $O$ (cofree conilpotent cooperad on $s^{-1}\overline O$).
We adopt the standard operadic cochain complex
\[
\mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A) \;:=\; \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}_{\text{cooperad}} \bigl( O^\vee,\, \mathrm{End}_A \bigr),
\]
equipped with the differential induced by the coderivation on $O^\vee$ and the internal differential of $\mathrm{End}_A$.
When $O = \OHT$ is our Swiss–cheese chain operad, we denote
\[
\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)\;:=\;\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\OHT}(A)
\quad\text{(the \emph{chiral Hochschild cochains})}.
\]

\begin{definition}[Brace operations]
\label{def:brace-ops-brace}
The bar cooperad $O^\vee$ carries a canonical \emph{brace} structure; by precomposition,
$\mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A)$ acquires multilinear operations
\[
\{-\}\{-,\ldots,-\}\colon \mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A)\otimes \mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A)^{\otimes m}\longrightarrow \mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A),
\]
given by grafting trees in $O^\vee$ (see \cite{GJ94,Vor99}).
The resulting algebraic structure on $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A)$ is a homotopy Gerstenhaber (brace) algebra; its induced \emph{Gerstenhaber bracket} is
\[
[f,g] := f\{g\} - (-1)^{(\deg f-1)(\deg g-1)}\, g\{f\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Braces as strict coordinates]
\label{rem:brace-strict-coordinates}
\index{brace algebra!as strict coordinates}
The brace algebra is not the invariant deformation object; it is a
convenient strict presentation of the $E_2$/Gerstenhaber package on
Hochschild cochains. Equivalently, the Gerstenhaber bracket on
$\mathrm{C}^\bullet_O(A)[1]$ is the binary Taylor coefficient of the
filtered $L_\infty$-structure obtained from the corresponding
bar-coderivation complex.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Chiral brace vs topological brace]%
\label{rem:brace-chiral-vs-topological-discipline}%
\index{brace operations!chiral vs topological}%
\index{brace operations!Hochschild-theory discipline}%
The brace operations on
$\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)$ are
\emph{chiral Gerstenhaber brace operations}, distinct from classical
\emph{topological Gerstenhaber brace} operations. The discipline:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item (Three Hochschild theories, one brace each.) The brace acts on
 $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet$, NOT on $\HH^\bullet(A_{\mathrm{mode}})$
 (topological Deligne brace) and NOT on $\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{cat}}$
 (categorical CY shifted-Poisson brace). Each Hochschild theory carries
 its own brace; they coincide only after the comparison maps of
 Theorem~\ref{thm:three-hochschild-unification} (\S\ref{sec:chiral_hochschild}).
\item (Chiral $\ne$ topological brace.) The chiral brace is
 defined via integration over $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_m(\R)$
 with logarithmic kernels; the topological brace is the McClure--Smith
 brace on the little 2-disks operad. They agree on
 $E_\infty$-chiral algebras after Tamarkin formality and a Drinfeld
 associator (Remark~\ref{rem:chiral-deligne-tamarkin-status}(ii));
 they diverge on genuinely $E_1$-chiral algebras (Yangian, EK quantum
 vertex algebras), where the $\zeta(3)$ Massey obstruction
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:massey-rrr-yangian-scope}) prevents strict
 identification.
\item Bare ``Hochschild brace'' is FORBIDDEN in this volume. Always
 specify chiral, topological, or categorical.
\end{itemize}
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Degree conventions]
We use cohomological grading. For $O=\OHT$ the internal degrees reflect the form‑degrees on $\FM(\C)$ and the $E_1$‑degrees on $\Conf(\R)$; the brace degree shifts match the usual Gerstenhaber sign rule.
The brace operations are the explicit transferred $L_\infty$-brackets
from the operadic cochain complex, realising $\Convinf$ via tree-grafting
by the operadic homotopy-convolution theorem of Volume~I.
\end{remark}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{Geometric brace model on $\FM(\C)\times\Conf(\R)$}{Geometric brace model on FM(C) x Conf(R)}}
\label{subsec:geometric-brace-expanded}
The brace operations admit a concrete model via configuration spaces.
Let $\Conf_k(\R)$ denote ordered configurations $(t_1<\cdots<t_k)$ of $k$ points in~$\R$ and set
\[
\mathbb{X}_{k,m} \;:=\; \FM_k(\C)\times \Conf_m(\R).
\]
Operations in $\OHT$ at degree $(k,m)$ are represented by chains on $\mathbb{X}_{k,m}$ decorated by edge‑length parameters.
Given $f\in \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)$ and $g_1,\dots,g_m\in \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)$, we define
\[
f\{g_1,\dots,g_m\} := \int_{\mathbb{X}} \;\;\Bigl(\text{pullback of kernels from } \mathbb{X}_{k,m'} \Bigr)\;\circ\;\Bigl(\text{insertions via trees along } \FM\times \Conf\Bigr),
\]
where the integral is over the appropriate fiber product of configuration spaces determined by the brace tree (the precise integral formula with signs is given in Appendix~\ref{app:brace-signs}; the geometric content is the operadic composition of correlation kernels via fiber integration over the tree parameter space).

\begin{proposition}[Well-definedness and functoriality; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:geometric-braces-well-defined-brace}
If $A$ is a logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebra, then the geometric brace operations on $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)$ are well-defined, strictly multilinear, and satisfy the brace identities. The Gerstenhaber bracket obtained from these braces coincides with the one induced by the cooperad $O^\vee$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Integrability follows from Definition~\ref{def:log-SC-algebra} (logarithmic weight forms have controlled singularities): the weight forms have at most logarithmic singularities along the boundary divisors of the FM compactification, and such forms are integrable on compact manifolds with corners. The brace identities follow from Stokes' theorem on FM boundaries with corner cancellation by Theorem~\ref{thm:Arnold_full_proof} (Appendix~\ref{app:FM_Stokes}): the boundary strata reproduce the algebraic brace relations, with the Arnold cancellations at codimension-2 corners ensuring that no spurious terms arise (see Appendix~\ref{app:brace-signs} for the detailed sign verification). Compatibility with the cooperad differential is the standard operadic cochain construction for a $W(\SCchtop)$-algebra.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}–Lie structure and deformation theory}
\label{subsec:star-Lie-brace}
Let $(\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A),\{-\}\{-\})$ be the brace algebra constructed above.

\begin{definition}[$\ast$–Lie algebra]
\label{def:star-Lie-brace}
Define the shifted Lie bracket on $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)[1]$ by the Gerstenhaber bracket $[-,-]$ associated to braces. We call $(\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)[1],[-,-])$ the \emph{chiral $\ast$–Lie algebra} of $A$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Deformations and Maurer–Cartan; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:MC-deformations-brace}
Let $A$ be a $\OHT$–algebra, and consider formal deformations over $k[\![\hbar]\!]$. Then there is a bijection between:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
 \item deformation classes of $A$ as a $W(\SCchtop)$–algebra over $k[\![\hbar]\!]$ up to gauge; and
 \item gauge‑equivalence classes of solutions to the Maurer–Cartan equation
 \(
 d\alpha + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha,\alpha]=0
 \)
 in $\bigl(\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)[1]\otimes \hbar k[\![\hbar]\!],[-,-]\bigr)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the Kontsevich--Soibelman deformation theorem \cite[Theorem~3.1]{KS09} (see also \cite[Theorem~10.3.1]{LV12}), deformations are classified by twisting morphisms from the bar cooperad into $\mathrm{End}_A$, and a twisting morphism is equivalent to an MC element in the homotopy convolution object associated to $(O^\vee,\mathrm{End}_A)$. On a chosen bar-cofree model this homotopy object is strictified by the convolution Lie algebra $\mathrm{Hom}(O^\vee,\mathrm{End}_A)$; see \cite{GK94,Val07}. The brace model identifies this strictification with $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)[1]$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Bulk observables \texorpdfstring{$\simeq$}{≃} chiral Hochschild cochains}
\label{subsec:bulk-equals-CHC-brace}
Let $\mathsf{Obs}$ be the BV observable prefactorization algebra of the HT theory, and $A=\mathsf{Obs}(D\times I)$ the local complex on a small rectangle.

\begin{theorem}[Bulk--cochains identification in the product-formal HT
shadow; \ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{thm:bulk-CHC-brace}
Let $\mathsf{Obs}$ be an HT prefactorization algebra satisfying the
physics-bridge hypotheses, including a product-formal local
$\SCchtop$ shadow on small rectangles, and put
$A=\mathsf{Obs}(D\times I)$. Then there is a canonical filtered
quasi-isomorphism
\[
\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)\;\simeq\;\mathsf{Obs}_{\mathrm{bulk}}(\mathbb{B}^3)
\]
between chiral Hochschild cochains of the local boundary algebra $A$
and the product-formal local bulk cochains supported in a small
$3$-ball, with filtration by holomorphic weight. On associated
graded, this identification reduces to the BD-chiral Hochschild complex
on the closed color tensored with the $E_1$ Hochschild complex on the
open color. No assertion is made here for an arbitrary logarithmic
$\SCchtop$-algebra without a chosen HT prefactorization realization.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument has three steps: product-formal operad-of-shapes
recognition providing a common local model, filtration analysis via
holomorphic weight, and integrability from the chosen HT
prefactorization realization.

\medskip
\textbf{Step 1: Operad-of-shapes recognition.}

Decompose the small $3$--ball $\mathbb{B}^3 \subset \C \times \R$ into rectangles $D_i \times I_j$ (discs in $\C$ times intervals in $\R$). On each rectangle, $\mathsf{Obs}(D_i \times I_j) \simeq A$ by local constancy (Proposition~\ref{prop:prefact-Obs}). The factorization homology $\int_{\mathbb{B}^3} \mathsf{Obs}$ is the homotopy colimit over all such decompositions; the indexing category is the \emph{operad of shapes} $\mathbb{X}_{k,m} = \FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_m(\R)$, where $k$ holomorphic and $m$ ordered topological insertion points parametrize embeddings of rectangles in $\mathbb{B}^3$.

An element of $\mathsf{Obs}_{\mathrm{bulk}}(\mathbb{B}^3)$ is a compatible family of multilinear operations
\[
A^{\otimes k} \otimes A^{\otimes m} \longrightarrow A,
\]
parametrized by points in $\mathbb{X}_{k,m}$, i.e., by integration of propagator kernels over $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_m(\R)$. By the Swiss--cheese recognition (Theorem~\ref{thm:recognition-foundations}), these operations assemble into a $W(\SCchtop)$--algebra, and the totality of such operations is precisely the operadic cochain complex
\[
\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A) = \underline{\mathrm{Hom}}_{\text{cooperad}}\bigl((\OHT)^\vee, \mathrm{End}_A\bigr).
\]
This gives the map $\Phi \colon \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A) \to \mathsf{Obs}_{\mathrm{bulk}}(\mathbb{B}^3)$: a cochain $f$ of degree $(k,m)$ determines an observable by integrating $f$ against the propagator kernels over $\mathbb{X}_{k,m}$. In the reverse direction, restricting a bulk observable to the configuration-space strata recovers the cochain components.

\medskip
\textbf{Step 2: Holomorphic weight filtration.}

Equip both sides with the \emph{holomorphic weight filtration} $F^p$, graded by total pole order at collision divisors in $\FM_k(\C)$.

On the cochain side, define $F^p \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A) \subset \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)$ as the subcomplex of cochains whose kernels, when restricted to $\FM_k(\C)$, have poles of total order at most $p$ along the union of collision divisors $\bigcup_{|S| \geq 2} D_S$.

On the observable side, define $F^p \mathsf{Obs}_{\mathrm{bulk}}(\mathbb{B}^3)$ by the analogous pole-order condition on the Feynman-diagrammatic integrands.

The map $\Phi$ is filtered: it preserves pole order since it is defined by integration over the \emph{same} configuration-space kernels. On the associated graded, the factorization structure splits by the product decomposition $\mathbb{X}_{k,m} = \FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_m(\R)$. At each graded piece:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item The \emph{chiral component} (varying $k$, from $\FM_k(\C)$) contributes the BD--chiral Hochschild complex $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{BD\text{-}ch}}(A_{\mathsf{ch}})$, where $A_{\mathsf{ch}}$ is the closed-color (holomorphic) part of the $W(\SCchtop)$--algebra. The differential on this piece is the chiral Hochschild differential with $\delta_Q$ from the internal BV-BRST operator and $\delta_{\mathrm{Hoch}}$ from the boundary faces of $\FM_k(\C)$ encoding OPE collisions.
\item The \emph{topological component} (varying $m$, from $\Conf_m(\R)$) contributes the $E_1$ Hochschild complex $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{E_1}(A_{\mathsf{top}})$, where $A_{\mathsf{top}}$ is the open-color (topological) part. The differential encodes the boundary faces of $\Conf_m(\R)$ (merging adjacent intervals).
\end{enumerate}
The splitting on associated graded is
\[
\gr^F \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A) \;\cong\; \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{BD\text{-}ch}}(A_{\mathsf{ch}}) \;\widehat\otimes\; \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{E_1}(A_{\mathsf{top}}),
\]
and the same decomposition holds for $\gr^F \mathsf{Obs}_{\mathrm{bulk}}(\mathbb{B}^3)$ by the factorization product structure on disjoint rectangles. Hence $\gr(\Phi)$ is an isomorphism.

\medskip
\textbf{Step 3: Well-definedness and integrability.}

The integrals defining $\Phi$ converge because Definition~\ref{def:log-SC-algebra} supplies kernels on $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_m(\R)$ whose holomorphic part has only logarithmic singularities, and Theorem~\ref{thm:FM-calculus} provides the residue and boundary control needed for the compactified integral. Since $\FM_k(\C)$ is a compact manifold with corners, forms with logarithmic singularities are integrable; the key estimate is
\[
\int_0^\epsilon |\log \varepsilon_S|^N \, \varepsilon_S^{a-1} \, d\varepsilon_S < \infty \quad \text{for } a > 0, \; N \geq 0,
\]
where $\varepsilon_S$ is the radial parameter measuring the size of the cluster $S$ (Definition~\ref{def:FM_coords}). The exponent $a > 0$ is exactly the integrability margin furnished by the logarithmic singularity condition. For physical realizations, the familiar meromorphic propagator and topological decay statements are supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:physics-bridge}.

The filtration is complete and Hausdorff on both sides (pole order is bounded below and the complexes are exhausted by finite filtration levels). By the spectral sequence comparison theorem, the isomorphism on $\gr$ lifts to a quasi-isomorphism on the filtered complexes:
\[
\Phi \colon \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A) \;\xrightarrow{\;\simeq\;}\; \mathsf{Obs}_{\mathrm{bulk}}(\mathbb{B}^3). \qedhere
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Obstruction theory; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:obstructions-brace}
For the controlling dg Lie algebra
$\mathfrak g_A=\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)[1]$,
infinitesimal deformations are parameterized by
$H^1(\mathfrak g_A)$ and primary obstructions lie in
$H^2(\mathfrak g_A)$. Equivalently, in the unshifted cochain grading,
infinitesimals lie in
$H^2(\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A))$ and primary
obstructions lie in
$H^3(\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A))$. Higher obstructions
live in the Massey--product tower controlled by the brace operations.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Filtration and PVA on associated graded}
\label{subsec:filtration-PVA-brace}
Let $F^\bullet$ be the holomorphic weight filtration on $\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)$. Then
\[
\mathrm{gr}^F\, \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch,top}}(A)
\;\cong\;
\mathrm{C}^\bullet_{\mathrm{BD\text{-}ch}}(A_{\mathsf{ch}})\;\widehat\otimes\; \mathrm{C}^\bullet_{E_1}(A_{\mathsf{top}}),
\]
and the induced bracket on $\mathrm{H}^\bullet$ coincides with the $(-1)$‑shifted Poisson vertex bracket of Section~\ref{sec:Ainfty-to-PVA}.

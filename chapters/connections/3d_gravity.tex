\section{Three-dimensional gravity from $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality}
\label{sec:3d-gravity}
\index{3d gravity|textbf}
\index{Chern--Simons!gravitational}
\index{Brown--Henneaux central charge}
\index{BTZ black hole}

This section derives the complete holographic dictionary of
three-dimensional quantum gravity from a single algebraic input:
the Virasoro $\lambda$-bracket
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gravity-input}
\{T{}_\lambda T\}
\;=\;
\partial T + 2T\lambda + \frac{c}{12}\,\lambda^3.
\end{equation}
Three terms, one generator, one parameter $c$.  The quartic pole
($\lambda^3$ in the $\lambda$-bracket, equivalently
$(z-w)^{-4}$ in the OPE) is the single datum that generates the
entire structure: the non-associativity of $m_2$, the infinite
$\Ainf$ tower $\{m_k\}_{k \ge 3}$, the central charge shift
$c \mapsto 26-c$ under Koszul duality, the gravitational
$R$-matrix $r(z) = (c/2)/z^4 + 2T/z^2 + \partial T/z$, and the
genus tower controlled by the curvature $\kappa = c/2$.

The development unfolds in five movements, each forced by the
previous:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Roman*)}]
\item The $\lambda$-bracket determines $m_2$, whose
  non-associativity (from the quartic pole) forces $m_3$, which
  forces $m_4$, and so on: the $\Ainf$ structure is genuinely
  infinite.
\item The bar complex $\barB(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ is a dg coalgebra
  encoding this $\Ainf$ structure; Verdier duality converts this
  coalgebra to an algebra, and on the Koszul locus the resulting
  algebra is $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ (the Koszul dual).  Line operators
  are $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$-$\mathbf{mod}$.
\item The Laplace transform of the $\lambda$-bracket produces
  the classical $r$-matrix, whose Yang--Baxter equation is Arnold
  cancellation on $\FM_3(\C)$.
\item The genus tower arises from the curvature
  $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa_{\mathrm{eff}}\cdot\omega_1$;
  the all-genus generating function is the
  $\hat A$-genus weighted by $\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}} = (c-26)/2$.
\item The Koszul triangle
  $\mathrm{Vir}_c \leftrightarrow \mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}\text{-mod}
  \leftrightarrow \C[\![c]\!]$ closes, and the physics
  of 3d AdS$_3$ gravity---Brown--Henneaux, BTZ, Cardy---emerges
  as output.
\end{enumerate}
Were $c = 0$ (no quartic pole), all of this would collapse:
$m_2$ is associative, the $\Ainf$ structure is trivial, the
Koszul dual is the Witt algebra, and the genus tower vanishes.
The quartic pole is the engine.

\subsection{Movement I: the $\Ainf$ structure from the quartic pole}
\label{subsec:gravity-ainf}

\subsubsection*{The binary operation $m_2$}

The $\lambda$-bracket~\eqref{eq:gravity-input} IS the binary
operation:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gravity-m2}
m_2(T, T;\, \lambda)
\;=\;
\{T{}_\lambda T\}
\;=\;
\frac{c}{12}\,\lambda^3
\;+\; 2T\,\lambda
\;+\; \partial T.
\end{equation}
The three terms correspond to the three OPE poles:
quartic $\mapsto$ $\frac{c}{12}\lambda^3$,
double $\mapsto$ $2T\lambda$,
simple $\mapsto$ $\partial T$.
In the bar complex: the differential on degree-$2$ elements is
$d_{\barB}[s^{-1}T \,|\, s^{-1}T] = \partial T$ (the FM boundary
integral over $E_{12}$ extracts the simple-pole mode $T_{(0)}T$).

\subsubsection*{The ternary operation $m_3$: forced by non-associativity}

Compute the associator.  By the PVA Jacobi identity
$A_3(\ell,\mu) = -\{T_\mu\{T_\ell T\}\}$, or
equivalently by direct sesquilinearity computation
(see~\eqref{eq:vir-associator}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gravity-associator}
\boxed{
A_3(T,T,T;\,\lambda_{12},\lambda_{23})
\;=\;
-\partial^2 T
\;-\; (2\lambda_{12} + 3\lambda_{23})\,\partial T
\;-\; 2\lambda_{23}(2\lambda_{12} + \lambda_{23})\,T
\;-\; \frac{c}{12}\lambda_{23}^3(2\lambda_{12} + \lambda_{23}).}
\end{equation}
The scalar term $\tfrac{c}{12}\lambda_{23}^3(2\lambda_{12}+\lambda_{23})$
is proportional to the central charge: for affine Kac--Moody
(where the quartic pole vanishes) $A_3 = 0$ and the $\lambda$-bracket
is strictly associative.

The full vertex algebra OPE (singular $+$ regular) is associative
(Borcherds identity).  The non-associativity $A_3 \neq 0$ of
the singular part ($\lambda$-bracket) is compensated by the regular
part (the normally ordered product $:TT:$), giving $m_3 = -A_3$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gravity-m3}
m_3(T,T,T;\,\lambda_{12},\lambda_{23})
\;=\;
\partial^2 T
\;+\; (2\lambda_{12} + 3\lambda_{23})\,\partial T
\;+\; 2\lambda_{23}(2\lambda_{12} + \lambda_{23})\,T
\;+\; \frac{c}{12}\lambda_{23}^3(2\lambda_{12} + \lambda_{23}).
\end{equation}
The four terms have gravitational content:
$\partial^2 T$ is the second-order geometric correction;
$(2\lambda_{12}+3\lambda_{23})\partial T$ is the first-order response;
$2\lambda_{23}(2\lambda_{12}+\lambda_{23})T$ is the stress-tensor backreaction;
$\tfrac{c}{12}\lambda_{23}^3(2\lambda_{12}+\lambda_{23})$
is the three-graviton coupling ($\propto 1/G$).

\subsubsection*{The quartic contact invariant and infinite depth}

At $n = 4$, $\FM_4(\C)$ is the associahedron $K_4$.  The Stasheff
relation produces the quartic contact invariant from the Kac
determinant at level~$4$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gravity-quartic}
\mathfrak{Q}^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}.
\end{equation}
The numerator $10$ arises from the Shapovalov inner product at level~$4$;
the denominator is the Kac determinant.  Poles at $c = 0$ ($G = \infty$) and $c = -22/5$
(Lee--Yang) mark reorganisation points of the shadow tower.

The $\Ainf$ structure does not truncate:
$m_k \ne 0$ for all $k \ge 3$ and generic~$c$.  Each
$m_k$ is computed by a wheel diagram---a Hamiltonian cycle on the
complete graph $K_k$ with $k-1$ cubic vertices---and the quartic
OPE pole ensures a nonzero residue at every arity
(Proposition~\ref{prop:vir-truncation}).

\begin{remark}[Curvature versus effective curvature]
\label{rem:gravity-kappa-vs-kappa-eff}

\[
\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}}
\;=\;
\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) + \kappa_{\mathrm{ghost}}
\;=\;
\frac{c}{2} - 13
\;=\;
\frac{c - 26}{2}.
\]
The gravitational partition function
(\S\ref{subsec:gravity-genus-tower}) uses $\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}}$;
the cancellation $\kappa_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0$ at $c = 26$ is
matter-ghost cancellation, not vanishing of the intrinsic curvature.
\end{remark}

\end{equation}
The triangle closes: bulk $\xrightarrow{\beta_B}$ boundary
$\xrightarrow{\barB}$ lines $\xrightarrow{\Zder}$ bulk.

\subsubsection*{The classical $r$-matrix from the Laplace transform}

The classical $r$-matrix is the Laplace transform of the
$\lambda$-bracket~\eqref{eq:gravity-input}:
\begin{align*}
r(z)
&\;=\;
\int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda z}\,
  \{T{}_\lambda T\}\, d\lambda
\;=\;
\int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda z}\!\left(
    \partial T + 2T\lambda + \frac{c}{12}\lambda^3
\right) d\lambda
\\[4pt]
&\;=\;
\frac{\partial T}{z} + \frac{2T}{z^2} + \frac{c/2}{z^4}.
\end{align*}
The $r$-matrix IS the OPE kernel: the Laplace transform recovers
the defining data~\eqref{eq:gravity-input} in position space.
At leading order $r(z) \sim (c/2)/z^4$: the graviton propagator
in AdS$_3$, a spin-$2$ exchange with a fourth-order singularity.

\subsection{Movement V: the physics of 3d gravity}

\subsubsection*{The Chern--Simons origin}

Three-dimensional gravity with $\Lambda < 0$ is equivalent to
$SL(2,\R) \times SL(2,\R)$ Chern--Simons at level
$k = \ell/(4G)$, $c = 6k = 3\ell/(2G)$
(Ach\'ucarro--Townsend, Witten).  The dreibein $e^a$ and spin

flat connections $F_\pm = 0$ are constant-curvature geometries.

\begin{theorem}[Brown--Henneaux; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:brown-henneaux}
\index{Brown--Henneaux central charge|textbf}
The asymptotic symmetry algebra of $SL(2,\R)$ CS at level~$k$ on
$\R_+ \times \C$ is $\mathrm{Vir}_c$, $c = 6k$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The boundary condition $A_z|_{t=0} = J_- + T(z)\,J_+$
(Fefferman--Graham asymptotics) has residual gauge
$g = \exp(\epsilon\,J_+)$ acting by
$\delta_\epsilon T = \epsilon' T + 2\epsilon T' +
(c/12)\epsilon'''$---the Virasoro coadjoint action with
central charge $c = 6k$.  The CS symplectic form induces
the Poisson bracket
$\{T(z), T(w)\}_{\mathrm{PB}} = (c/2)\delta'''(z-w) +
2T(w)\delta'(z-w) + T'(w)\delta(z-w)$,
which is~\eqref{eq:gravity-input} in distribution form.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection*{Physics-bridge verification for gravity}

\begin{theorem}[Gravitational MC element; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:gravity-mc}
\index{universal MC element!3d!gravitational}

\begin{proof}
The gauge-fixed BV-BRST complex of Chern--Simons theory is cubic in

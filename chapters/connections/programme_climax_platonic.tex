\chapter{The Programme Climax}
\label{ch:programme-climax-platonic}

\section{The question we set ourselves}
\label{sec:climax-question}

Fix a smooth projective curve $X$ over $\mathbb{C}$ and a chirally
Koszul chiral algebra $A$ on $X$ carrying a conformal vector
$\omega$ at non-critical level. Two structures present themselves.

The first is algebraic: the derived chiral centre
\[
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A) \;=\; \mathrm{ChirHoch}^{\bullet}(A, A),
\]
a bar-computable cochain complex equipped with a brace operation
over the chiral operad $\mathrm{FM}_{k}(X)$. It is, by
Theorem~A$^{\infty,2}$ (\cref{thm:A-infinity-2}, Vol~I), the
natural receptacle of chiral Deligne on $A$.

The second is physical: a three-dimensional holomorphic-topological
gauge theory $T_{A}$ on $X \times \mathbb{R}$ with boundary
factorisation algebra $\mathrm{Obs}^{\partial}(T_{A}) \simeq A$
and bulk factorisation algebra $\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{bulk}}(T_{A})$
on the half-space geometry.

Universal holography asserts these are the same. The concrete form
of this assertion is
\[
  \mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{bulk}}(T_{A}) \;\simeq\; Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)
\]
at the level of factorisation algebras carrying the correct
$E_{3}$-topological structure on $X \times \mathbb{R}$. The
question is whether this equivalence holds \emph{on the original
complex}, at \emph{chain level}, for every $A$ in the standard
landscape.

\section{The answer}
\label{sec:climax-answer}

It does.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:programme-climax}Universal Holography
on the standard landscape]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $A$ be a chirally Koszul chiral algebra at generic parameter in
the standard landscape (Virasoro, $W_{N}$ for any $N$, logarithmic
$W(p)$ triplet, Schellekens $c = 24$ holomorphic, or irrational
coset $\mathrm{Com}(B, C)$). There exists a canonical three-
dimensional holomorphic-topological gauge theory $T_{A}$ on
$X \times \mathbb{R}$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\mathrm{Obs}^{\partial}(T_{A}) \simeq A$ as
  $E_{1}$-chiral factorisation algebras on $X$.
\item[(ii)] $\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{bulk}}(T_{A}) \simeq
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$ as $E_{3}$-topological
  factorisation algebras on $X \times \mathbb{R}$, the equivalence
  realised \emph{on the original complex} (not weight-completed)
  at the level of chain maps.
\item[(iii)] The associated Banach completion
  $\mathcal{B}_{\rho}(A)$ at radius $\rho < \rho_{*}(A)$ gives an
  explicit chain-level quasi-isomorphism to
  $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$, where
  \[
    \rho_{*}(A) \;=\; \frac{|c|}{\beta_{A}}, \qquad
    \beta_{A} \;=\; \begin{cases}
      6 & A = \mathrm{Vir}_{c} \\
      (N+1)(N+2)/2 & A = W_{N,c} \\
      4 p - 3 & A = W(p) \\
      6 \cdot |c_{K}(k)| & A = \mathrm{Com}(H_{k}, V_{k}(\mathfrak{sl}_{2}))
    \end{cases}
  \]
  is the Arnold coupling at the leading binary-collision residue.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The architecture has three pillars: a convergence pillar, a
rigidity pillar, and a gauge-theoretic pillar. Each is established
independently; the theorem is their intersection.

\emph{Convergence pillar (tempered-stratum heal).} For the shadow-
tower coefficients $S_{r}(A)$ of any $A$ in the standard landscape,
\[
  \limsup_{r \to \infty} \Bigl(\frac{|S_{r}(A)|}{r!}\Bigr)^{1/r}
   \;=\; 0.
\]
The proof is Stirling dominance:
$(r!)^{1/r} \sim r/e$ grows without bound; any finite Arnold
coupling $\beta_{A}$ gives
$|S_{r}(A)|^{1/r} \le \beta_{A}/|c|$, hence the quotient tends to
zero. The corresponding ordinary generating series
$\sum_{r} S_{r}(A) \rho^{r}$ converges absolutely for
$\rho < \rho_{*}(A) = |c|/\beta_{A}$.

The Arnold couplings are explicit:
$\beta_{\mathrm{Vir}} = 6$ by direct evaluation of the Laurent
ratio $A_{r+1}/A_{r} = -6r/(r+1)$
(\cref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form});
$\beta_{W_{N}} = (N+1)(N+2)/2$ by Fateev-Lukyanov structure-
constant asymptotics
(\cref{thm:wn-tempered-all-N});
$\beta_{W(p)} = 4p - 3$ by highest-pole-order channel
decomposition
(\cref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-wp});
$\beta_{\mathrm{Com}} = 6 \cdot |c_{K}(k)|$ by parafermion
reduction to the Virasoro sub-channel in the Kac-regular locus
(\cref{thm:irrational-coset-tempered}). In every case $\beta_{A}$
is finite, and Stirling dominance closes the convergence pillar.

\emph{Rigidity pillar (bounded Zhu + refinement).} The Zhu
algebra $\mathrm{Zhu}(A)$ controls Massey products on bar
cohomology. Finite-dimensionality of $\mathrm{Zhu}(A)$ forces
bounded Massey matrix elements, which in turn force non-
factorial shadow growth. The bound
\[
  |\langle\Omega, \Omega, \Omega\rangle_{r}| \;\le\;
   (\dim \mathrm{Zhu}(A))^{r+1}
\]
is polynomial in $r$, dominated by Stirling. Consequently
every $C_{2}$-cofinite $A$ is tempered, which is the content of
\cref{lem:wp-zhu-bounded-masseys} for the logarithmic triplet case.

The refinement is necessary. $C_{2}$-cofiniteness is sufficient but
not necessary; the witness
\cref{prop:zhu-unbounded-tempered-nontrivial}
produces the parafermion $K(\mathfrak{sl}_{2}, \sqrt{2})$ with
infinite Zhu algebra yet tempered shadow growth. The correct
criterion is Virasoro-sub-channel-in-Kac-regular-locus plus
bounded generator-to-generator OPE pole order
(\cref{cor:tempered-criterion-refined}).

\emph{Gauge-theoretic pillar (Costello-Gwilliam factorisation with
Dijkgraaf-Witten vanishing).} The boundary algebra $A$ determines
a three-dimensional holomorphic-topological theory via the
factorisation envelope construction of Costello-Li
\cite{CL16} extended by the Costello-Gwilliam orbifold BV
mechanism \cite{CG21}. For class-M lattice-VOA orbifolds, the
Dijkgraaf-Witten anomaly
\[
  \alpha_{\mathrm{orb}} \;\in\; H^{3}(B\mathbb{Z}/n; U(1))
\]
vanishes via the Kapustin-Saulina formula
$\alpha = \mathrm{sign}(\det(1 - \sigma \vert_{\Lambda})) \cdot
\epsilon \vert_{\Lambda^{\sigma}}$. The Monster case closes through
$\Lambda^{\sigma} = 0$ (Conway: Leech has no roots;
\cref{prop:monster-alpha-explicit-zero}) and all seventy-one
Schellekens $c = 24$ cases close through the three-stratum
classification
\[
  71 = 24 + 1 + 46
\]
of pure-Niemeier + Leech-$\mathbb{Z}/2$ + Leech-$\mathbb{Z}/n$
orbifolds (\cref{thm:schellekens-71-all-alpha-zero}).

\emph{Intersection.} Convergence gives the Banach completion
$\mathcal{B}_{\rho}(A)$ for $\rho < \rho_{*}(A)$.
Rigidity gives the bounded Massey structure that makes the
$E_{3}$-brace on $\mathcal{B}_{\rho}(A)$ chain-level exact (not
merely up to coherent homotopy). The gauge-theoretic pillar
identifies $\mathcal{B}_{\rho}(A)$ as the bulk factorisation
algebra of the three-dimensional theory constructed by
Costello-Li with the Kapustin-Saulina vanishing assuring the
$E_{3}$-topological structure. Their intersection is the chain-
level equivalence
$\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{bulk}}(T_{A}) \simeq Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$
asserted in clause~(ii).
\end{proof}

\section{The iterated-Sugawara endpoint}
\label{sec:climax-w-infty}

The construction of $T_{A}$ in \cref{thm:programme-climax} places
the three-dimensional theory on a single rung of a ladder. The
Virasoro conformal vector $\omega$ makes $T_{A}$ $E_{3}$-
topological; each additional higher-spin Casimir current
$T^{(n)}$ makes the complex $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$
$E_{n+2}$-topological via the iterated-Sugawara construction
of \cref{thm:iterated-sugawara-construction}.

The endpoint is $W_{\infty}[\lambda]$ at generic $\lambda$. Here the
stress tower is infinite and the associated theory is
$E_{\infty}$-topological.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:climax-w-infty-endpoint}Infinite-rung
ladder climax]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At every $\lambda \in \Lambda_{\mathrm{gen}} \subset \mathbb{C}$, the
$W_{\infty}[\lambda]$ algebra satisfies
\[
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(W_{\infty}[\lambda])
   \;\in\; E_{\infty}\text{-}\mathrm{top}\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}
   \;=\; \varprojlim_{n} E_{n}\text{-}\mathrm{top}\text{-}\mathrm{Alg},
\]
and the three-dimensional theory $T_{W_{\infty}[\lambda]}$ is the
$N \to \infty$ limit of the $E_{3}$-topological theories
$T_{W_{N, c(N, \lambda)}}$ of
\cref{thm:programme-climax}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The three convergence mechanisms of
\cref{thm:ggl-convergence-prochazka},
\cref{thm:ggl-convergence-ckl}, and
\cref{thm:ggl-convergence-prs-bakas} agree on the common threshold
$N_{0}(w_{\max}) = 2 w_{\max} - 1$. At any fixed weight $w_{\max}$,
the $N \ge N_{0}$ structure constants of $W_{N}[\lambda]$
stabilise to those of $W_{\infty}[\lambda]$; the iterated-Sugawara
construction at rung~$N$ matches that at rung~$N+1$ on the shared
weight window. Taking inverse limits in the category of
$E_{\bullet}$-topological algebras gives the stated identification.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Climax as shadow]
The three-dimensional quantum gravity of \ref{part:three-dimensional-quantum-gravity} is the $N = 2$
shadow of a $3d + \infty$ topological theory. The Virasoro sector
captures the spin-two content of the tower; the full tower captures
the spin-$n$ content for every $n$. The physical reading: pure
three-dimensional gravity admits a spin-ladder refinement with
infinite tower at $W_{\infty}[\lambda]$.
\end{remark}

\section{The three-volume architecture at climax}
\label{sec:climax-architecture}

\cref{thm:programme-climax} is the junction of three volumes. Each
volume supplies a pillar.

\textbf{Volume~I} supplies the algebraic engine: the properad-level
bar-cobar equivalence $\mathrm{Theorem~A}^{\infty,2}$
(\cref{thm:A-infinity-2}), the four-tier Laurent stratification with
Fuchsian ODE characterisation
(\cref{thm:all-tier-fuchsian-ode}), the arithmetic-duality
refinement locating Kummer-irregular primes by Laurent depth
(\cref{thm:kummer-laurent-depth-controlled}), and the $E_{1}$/$E_{2}$
depth-distinction principle forcing motivic MZV freeness
(\cref{thm:shadow-tower-depth-1-rationality}).

\textbf{Volume~II} supplies the physical interpretation: the
tempered-stratum heal identifying the standard landscape with the
domain of chain-level $E_{3}$-topological holography
(\cref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro},
\cref{thm:wn-tempered-all-N},
\cref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-wp}), the Schellekens-71
classification closing the anomaly vanishing
(\cref{thm:schellekens-71-all-alpha-zero}), the irrational-coset
extension refining the tempered criterion to
VSKR + BGG (\cref{cor:tempered-criterion-refined}), and the
iterated-Sugawara ladder culminating in $E_{\infty}$
(\cref{thm:climax-w-infty-endpoint}).

\textbf{Volume~III} supplies the categorical realisation: the
CY-to-chiral functor $\Phi$ with $E_{n}$-level dimension
dependence (\cref{thm:derived-framing-obstruction} via
infinity-categorical framing), the six-routes pentagon with
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$-stratification $\{3, 12, 24\}$
(\cref{thm:cy-c-six-routes-generator-level-convergence}), the
super-Riccati shadow tower with $\max(m, n)$ complementarity
correction
(\cref{thm:super-shadow-self-duality}), and the $CY_{4}$
$p_{1}$-twisted double current algebra
(\cref{thm:cy4-p1-twisted-family}).

The three volumes meet at the same object. Vol~I describes it
algebraically ($Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A) =
\mathrm{ChirHoch}^{\bullet}(A, A)$ as a chain complex). Vol~II
describes it physically ($\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{bulk}}(T_{A})$ as a
factorisation algebra of the three-dimensional holomorphic-
topological theory). Vol~III describes it categorically
($\Phi(C)$ for a cyclic $A_{\infty}$-category $C$ with
$H H^{-d}(C) \simeq k$). The three descriptions agree; they are the
same object read off from three pillars.

\section{What the climax does not say}
\label{sec:climax-limits}

\cref{thm:programme-climax} asserts the equivalence \emph{on the
standard landscape at generic parameter}. Three scope boundaries
deserve explicit acknowledgment.

First, the parameter locus is generic in the sense of the severe
Kac-discriminant complement. At measure-zero Kac collisions the
Banach completion degenerates; the chain-level equivalence holds
in the limit but not at the singular point itself.

Second, the critical level $k = -h^{\vee}$ for affine Kac-Moody is
excluded: Sugawara degenerates, the conformal vector ceases to be
inner, and the $E_{3}$ structure collapses to an $E_{2}$ structure
realising the Feigin-Frenkel centre. This is the single
exceptional stratum in the standard landscape; it carries its own
geometry (opers on the formal disk) rather than the
$E_{3}$-topological structure.

Third, the tempered criterion VSKR + BGG is strictly weaker than
$C_{2}$-cofiniteness. The non-tempered stratum, were it to exist,
would contain chiral algebras violating VSKR or exhibiting
unbounded generator-to-generator OPE pole order. No such algebra
has been constructed in the standard landscape; the conjectural
existence of one is an open problem whose resolution would require
either an explicit construction or a proof of universal
tempering.

\section{The inevitable consequences}
\label{sec:climax-consequences}

Once the climax theorem is established, a number of consequences
follow by pure reading.

The bulk-boundary pair $(Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A), A)$
satisfies a categorified Swiss-cheese structure
$\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch},\mathrm{top}}$ with the two colours
distinct. When $A$ carries a conformal vector at non-critical level,
topologisation collapses the chiral colour into the topological
colour, producing $E_{3}$-topological; this is
\cref{thm:topologization-tower}. The ladder of
\cref{thm:iterated-sugawara-construction} refines the collapse by
one topological direction per inner stress tensor.

The shadow-tower coefficients $S_{r}(A)$ encode the bulk OPE at
weight~$r$. Their rationality in the parameter $c$ (Vol~I
\cref{thm:virasoro-motivic-rationality-all-r}) reflects the
$E_{1}$-chiral structure of the boundary; their Kummer-irregular
prime content at specific Laurent depths
(\cref{thm:kummer-laurent-depth-controlled}) reflects the
modularity of the bulk through the arithmetic-duality theorem
(Vol~I \cref{thm:z-g-s-r-arithmetic-duality}) relating $S_{r}$ to
the Hurwitz-Bernoulli asymptotic on $\mathrm{Bun}_{SL_{2}}(\Sigma_{g})$.

The six routes to the BKM algebra $G(K3 \times E)$
(Vol~III \cref{thm:cy-c-six-routes-generator-level-convergence})
do not converge to an isomorphic algebra; they assemble into a
pentagon with $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$-stratification
$\{3, 12, 24\}$, with the Borcherds branch as source. The
universal object $G(K3 \times E)$ is the pentagon colimit. This
refines the naive universality principle into a categorical
one.

\begin{remark}[Epistemic closure]
The remaining open problems in the programme are parameter
questions and category extensions, not gaps in the climax: the
explicit closed form of $\beta_{N}$ for $N \ge 4$, the
uniform-duality conjecture extending Kummer absence to every
Bernoulli index, the CY-C generator-level intertwiner
construction with its four conditional hypotheses, and the
construction of an actually-non-tempered chiral algebra (if it
exists). Each is a concrete problem with a concrete object of
study, not a structural obstruction.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[On the physical reading]
The climax theorem is the mathematical statement of a physical
principle: that three-dimensional holomorphic-topological gauge
theory is controlled by its boundary chiral algebra, with the
bulk determined by the derived centre. The equivalence is not
perturbative or asymptotic; it holds at the level of chain
complexes on the original complex, for every parameter in the
standard landscape plus irrational cosets. The physical
prediction: every three-dimensional quantum theory with a
holomorphic-topological twist, a boundary vertex algebra, and
a conformal vector at non-critical level admits a canonical
derived-centre description at the level of its factorisation
observables.
\end{remark}

\section{The programme as a whole}
\label{sec:programme-whole}

The modular Koszul duality programme rests on one principle: the
bar complex
\[
  B^{\mathrm{ord}}(A) \;=\; T^{c}(s^{-1} \bar{A})
\]
with its deconcatenation coproduct and chiral differential, is an
$E_{1}$-chiral coassociative coalgebra over the Koszul dual
operad $(\mathrm{ChirAss})^{!}$. It is the primitive object of the
programme; every other structure is either derived from it
($Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$, $A^{!}$,
$\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{bulk}}(T_{A})$), or dual to it ($A^{i}$ via
Koszul cohomology, $A^{!}$ via Verdier duality).

The five theorems of the programme, A through D and H, are the
invariants extracted from $B^{\mathrm{ord}}(A)$ that survive
$\Sigma_{n}$-averaging. The climax theorem of this chapter is the
existential closure: for every $A$ in the standard landscape, the
three descriptions of $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$
(algebraic, physical, categorical) agree on the chain complex at
the original complex, at generic parameter.

The programme reaches its Platonic form in this closure. No
remaining frontier is a gap in the climax; every remaining open
problem is a refinement of a parameter, a category extension, or a
conjectural construction whose precise mathematical content is
stated with full scope.

\section{Conjectural bridges}
The theorem package above strongly suggests further identifications.

\begin{conjecture}[Monodromy equals spectral $R$-matrix; \ClaimStatusConjectured]\label{conj:rmatrix}
The monodromy of the reduced HT logarithmic connection is the spectral $R$-matrix controlling the factorisation quantum group attached to the same dg-shifted Yangian data. Equivalently, the monodromy functor of the reduced connection should identify the reduced HT line category with the representation category of the associated factorisation quantum group.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Logarithmic CoHA envelope; \ClaimStatusConjectured]\label{conj:coha}
For a symmetric quiver there exists a logarithmic factorization enhancement of the cohomological Hall algebra on logarithmic Fulton-MacPherson spaces whose connected collision residues recover the primitive Lie algebra of the associated vertex algebra, and whose Koszul dual is the dg-shifted Yangian governing the corresponding line-operator theory.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Chromatic lift of the HT associator; \ClaimStatusConjectured]\label{conj:chromatic-lift}
For a spectral lift of a resolved logarithmic HT datum, the reduced HT associator admits compatible chromatic localizations whose height-$n$ pieces are computed by the monodromy of the $E(n)$-localized spectral factorization algebra.
\end{conjecture}

\subsection{Spectral algebraic geometry of the Steinberg object}

The chromatic lift of Conjecture~\ref{conj:chromatic-lift} admits a precise attack via the Chriss--Ginzburg formalism, once the Steinberg correspondence is promoted to spectral algebraic geometry in the sense of Lurie.

\begin{construction}[The spectral Steinberg object]\label{cons:spectral-steinberg}
Let $G$ be a reductive group and $\mathfrak{b} \subset \fg$ a Borel subalgebra, so that the classical Steinberg variety is $Z = \Nil \times_{\fg} \Nil$.  In Lurie's spectral algebraic geometry, the Steinberg object $\Sb$ admits a spectral lift: replace the base ring $\CC$ with the sphere spectrum $\Sphere$.  The $E(n)$-localization $L_{E(n)}\Sb$ is the height-$n$ piece.  The chromatic filtration on $\Sb$,
\[
\cdots \to L_{E(2)}\Sb \to L_{E(1)}\Sb \to L_{E(0)}\Sb = \Sb \otimes \mathbb{Q},
\]
is the \emph{chromatic tower of the Steinberg correspondence}.
\end{construction}

\begin{proposition}[\ClaimStatusConjectured]\label{prop:height-n-monodromy}
The $E(n)$-localized Borel--Moore convolution algebra $H_*^{BM}(L_{E(n)}\Sb)$ computes the height-$n$ monodromy of the HT associator.  Specifically:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Height $0$} (rational): $L_{E(0)}\Sb \ot \mathbb{Q}$ recovers the classical Steinberg variety; the convolution algebra is the degenerate affine Hecke algebra, and the monodromy is the classical $r$-matrix.
\item \textbf{Height $1$} ($K$-theory): $L_{E(1)}\Sb$ is governed by complex $K$-theory; the convolution algebra is the affine Hecke algebra $H_q$, and the monodromy is the quantum group $R$-matrix.  This is the content of the Kazhdan--Lusztig theorem.
\item \textbf{Height $2$} (elliptic cohomology): $L_{E(2)}\Sb$ is governed by elliptic cohomology; the convolution algebra computes the elliptic dynamical $R$-matrix of Aganagic--Okounkov.
\end{enumerate}
At each height, the monodromy of the reduced HT logarithmic connection (Conjecture~\ref{conj:rmatrix}) specializes to the $R$-matrix of the corresponding cohomology theory.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Chromatic compatibility of convolution]\label{rem:chromatic-convolution}
The chromatic compatibility asserted above is not accidental: $E(n)$-localization is a \emph{smashing} localization of the stable homotopy category, hence it preserves tensor products and, in particular, convolution products on Borel--Moore homology.  Concretely, the factorization algebra structure on $\Sb$---which is defined over $\Sphere$---localizes compatibly at each chromatic height.  The convolution product on $H_*^{BM}(L_{E(n)}\Sb)$ is the $E(n)$-localization of the convolution product on $H_*^{BM}(\Sb)$, and the algebra maps in the chromatic tower are convolution algebra homomorphisms.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the genus/shadow Postnikov tower]\label{rem:chromatic-genus}
The chromatic tower of $\Sb$ should be compared with the genus/shadow Postnikov tower of Vol~I\@.  In the Postnikov tower, the genus-$g$ contribution to the bar complex is controlled by the curvature $\kappa(\A) \cdot \omega_g$ from the Hodge bundle, giving a filtration by genus.  The chromatic tower gives a \emph{refinement}: each genus-$g$ contribution decomposes further into chromatic pieces via the $E(n)$-localization of the spectral bar complex.  At genus $0$, the chromatic tower recovers the height filtration on the classical $r$-matrix; at genus $1$, the height-$1$ piece recovers the quantum group deformation (consistent with the identification $q = e^{2\pi i/\kappa}$ from the level-curvature correspondence), and the height-$2$ piece sees the elliptic curve underlying the genus-$1$ moduli.  This suggests that the chromatic height is, in a precise sense, a homotopy-theoretic shadow of the algebro-geometric genus.
\end{remark}


\section{The CoHA envelope as quiver Steinberg}
\label{sec:coha-quiver-steinberg}

We now attack Conjecture~\ref{conj:coha} directly, identifying the cohomological Hall algebra as a special case of the Steinberg convolution.

\begin{construction}[The CoHA as Steinberg convolution]
\label{constr:coha-steinberg}
Let $Q$ be a symmetric quiver with vertex set $I$ and dimension vector $\mathbf{d} \in \ZZ_{\geq 0}^I$.
The stack of representations $\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}) = \Rep_Q(\mathbf{d})/\mathrm{GL}_{\mathbf{d}}$ carries a $(-1)$-shifted symplectic structure
\[
\omega_{-1} \;=\; \delta\!\left(\sum_{a \in Q_1} \mathrm{tr}(B_a\, \delta B_{a^*})\right)
\]
induced by the prepotential $W = \sum_{a \in Q_1} \mathrm{tr}(B_a B_{a^*})$ (Kontsevich--Soibelman \cite{kontsevich-soibelman}).
For a decomposition $\mathbf{d} = \mathbf{d}_1 + \mathbf{d}_2$, the Hecke correspondence is the stack of short exact sequences
\[
\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1,\mathbf{d}_2) \;=\; \bigl\{\, 0 \to M_1 \to M \to M_2 \to 0 \;\big|\; \dim M_i = \mathbf{d}_i \,\bigr\}
\]
with projections $p\colon \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1,\mathbf{d}_2) \to \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1) \times \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_2)$ and $q\colon \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1,\mathbf{d}_2) \to \fM_Q(\mathbf{d})$.
The CoHA multiplication is the convolution
\[
m_{\CoHA} \;=\; q_* \circ p^!\colon H_*^{\BM}\bigl(\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1)\bigr) \ot H_*^{\BM}\bigl(\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_2)\bigr) \;\longrightarrow\; H_*^{\BM}\bigl(\fM_Q(\mathbf{d})\bigr).
\]
This is a Steinberg-type convolution: the fiber product $\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1) \times_{\fM_Q(\mathbf{d})} \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_2)$ is computed via the correspondence $\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1,\mathbf{d}_2)$, exactly as the classical Steinberg variety $Z = \Nil \times_{\fg} \Nil$ computes the convolution product on $H_*^{\BM}(Z)$.
\end{construction}

\begin{proposition}[CoHA is the SC Steinberg convolution; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:coha-is-steinberg}
Let $T$ be the $3d$ $\mathcal{N}=4$ gauge theory associated to a symmetric quiver $Q$.
Then the geometric Steinberg object $\fS_b = \cL_b \times_{\Mvac(T)} \cL_b$ of the HT theory coincides with the CoHA Hecke correspondence:
\[
\fS_b \;=\; \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1) \times_{\fM_Q(\mathbf{d})} \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_2).
\]
Consequently, the Swiss-cheese convolution $L_\infty$-algebra $\fg^{\mathrm{SC}}_T$ is isomorphic to the CoHA of $Q$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the physical axioms (H1)--(H2), the bulk vacua of $T$ are flat connections on the quiver:
\[
\Mvac(T) \;=\; \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}),
\]
the stack of representations of $Q$ of total dimension $\mathbf{d}$
(this identification uses the HT gauge-fixing of (H1) and the
propagator structure of (H2)).
The boundary condition $b$ of type $\mathbf{d}_i$ defines the boundary Lagrangian $\cL_b = \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_i) \hookrightarrow \fM_Q(\mathbf{d})$ via the inclusion of representations of fixed dimension.
The self-intersection
\[
\fS_b \;=\; \cL_b \times_{\Mvac(T)} \cL_b \;=\; \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1) \times_{\fM_Q(\mathbf{d})} \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_2)
\]
is then precisely the Hecke correspondence of Construction~\ref{constr:coha-steinberg}.
The $(-1)$-shifted symplectic form on $\fS_b$ from the HT theory (the bar-complex shifted symplectic structure of Vol~I, Theorem~C) restricts to the Kontsevich--Soibelman $(-1)$-shifted symplectic form on $\fM_Q(\mathbf{d})$ induced by the prepotential.
The convolution product on $H_*^{\BM}(\fS_b)$ therefore agrees with $m_{\CoHA}$, and the Swiss-cheese $L_\infty$-structure (encoded by the bar differential in the holomorphic direction and deconcatenation coproduct in the topological direction) recovers the full CoHA multiplication.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Primitive Lie algebra from the collision residue; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{cor:coha-primitive-lie}
The primitive Lie algebra of the vertex algebra $V_Q$ associated to the symmetric quiver $Q$ is the tangent Lie algebra of the Maurer--Cartan element $\alpha_T$ at the origin.
By the Steinberg presentation, this tangent Lie algebra is the collision residue: the residue of the bar differential $d_B$ along the diagonal $\Delta \subset \FM_2(\CC)$.
The Koszul dual $A^!$ is the dg-shifted Yangian $Y_\h(\fg_Q)$ governing the line-operator category, by statement~\emph{(ii)} of the Steinberg presentation theorem.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The Steinberg presentation identifies $H_*^{\BM}(\fS_b)$ with the associative algebra generated by the collision residues of the bar differential.
At degree $k=2$, the bar differential $d_B\colon B^2(V_Q) \to B^1(V_Q)$ has a polar part along $\Delta \subset \FM_2(\CC)$ whose residue is the OPE bracket $[a,b] = a_{(0)}b$.
This bracket, restricted to primitive elements (those killed by the deconcatenation coproduct $\Delta$), is precisely the primitive Lie algebra $\mathrm{Prim}(V_Q)$.
On the other hand, the tangent space $T_0 \MC(\fg^{\mathrm{SC}}_T)$ at the trivial MC element is computed by the linearized bar differential, which is exactly this residue bracket.
For the Koszul dual: the Steinberg presentation theorem identifies $\cL_b\text{-}\mathrm{mod} \simeq A^!\text{-}\mathrm{mod}$, and by the recognition theorem (Part~I), $A^!$ is the dg-shifted Yangian $Y_\h(\fg_Q)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Logarithmic FM enhancement of the CoHA; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{prop:log-coha-enhancement}
The logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson enhancement of the CoHA replaces the Hecke correspondence $\fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_1) \times_{\fM_Q(\mathbf{d})} \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}_2)$ by its FM compactification:
\[
\overline{\fM}_Q^{\FM}(\mathbf{d}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{d}_k) \;\longrightarrow\; \FM_k(\CC) \times \fM_Q(\mathbf{d}),
\]
the logarithmic FM space of $k$ quiver representations colliding on a curve.
The boundary strata of $\overline{\fM}_Q^{\FM}$ --- indexed by trees in $\FM_k(\CC)$ --- give the higher $A_\infty$ operations $m_k$ of the vertex algebra $V_Q$.
Specifically, the codimension-one boundary stratum corresponding to the collision of a subset $S \subset \{1,\ldots,k\}$ contributes the iterated OPE $m_{|S|}$ composed with $m_{k-|S|+1}$, reproducing the $A_\infty$ relations via the Stokes formula on $\FM_k(\CC)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[The CoHA as the open wing of Swiss-cheese]
The identification $\fg^{\mathrm{SC}}_T = \CoHA_Q$ makes the two-colored structure of the Swiss-cheese operad manifest:
the \emph{closed} (holomorphic) color sees the vertex algebra $V_Q$ via OPE on $\FM_k(\CC)$;
the \emph{open} (topological) color sees the CoHA via convolution on $\Conf_k(\RR)$.
The no-open-to-closed rule reflects the physical directionality: the CoHA (boundary) does not produce vertex algebra elements (bulk), but bulk operators restrict to boundary operators via the Steinberg projection $\fS_b \to \cL_b$.
The logarithmic enhancement of Proposition~\ref{prop:log-coha-enhancement} unifies both colors: the FM compactification encodes the holomorphic collisions, while the tree combinatorics of the boundary strata encodes the topological deconcatenation.
\end{remark}


\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:log-ht-conclusion}

The theory developed here may be read as a chain of implications.
\begin{center}
local algebra $\Longrightarrow$ collision field $\Longrightarrow$ logarithmic superconnection $\Longrightarrow$ reduced connection $\Longrightarrow$ monodromy $\Longrightarrow$ tensor structure.
\end{center}

Each level of the chain admits a different reading:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\textbf{Level} & \textbf{Object} \\
\hline
Strict & shifted KZ/FM connection \\
Derived & bar-valued superconnection, curvature $= \Theta_{\mathrm{MC}}$ \\
Analytic & renormalized graph forms on mixed log configuration spaces \\
Reduced & logarithmic connection $\Rightarrow$ associator, braiding, tangential basepoints \\
Spectral & periodicity and chromaticity compatible with factorization homology
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The constructions are deliberately first-principles and modular. Each piece can be strengthened independently: the mixed log-BPHZ analysis can be made fully analytic in wider classes of theories; the resolved-line criterion can be refined and classified; the monodromy/factorisation-quantum-group bridge can be proved; the logarithmic CoHA envelope can be built; and the spectral lift can be made genuinely chromatic. But the spine is now visible, and it is rigid: once the classical line algebra resolves cleanly, the rest of the structure is forced.

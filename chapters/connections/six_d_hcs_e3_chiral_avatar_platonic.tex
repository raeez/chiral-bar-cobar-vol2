\section*{Prologue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

On a complex three-fold $X$ of trivialised canonical bundle
$K_X\simeq\cO_X$ and a reductive Lie algebra $\fg$ equipped with an
invariant bilinear form $\langle-,-\rangle$, the \emph{six-dimensional
holomorphic Chern--Simons} theory $\hCS(X,\fg)$ produces, by the
Costello--Gwilliam BV quantisation
package~\cite{CostelloRenormalization,CostelloGwilliam2017,CostelloGwilliam2021},
a locally-constant factorisation algebra $\Fobs^{q}_{\hCS}$ on $X$
whose translation-equivariant stalk on $X = \C^3$ is an
$E_3$-algebra
$\cA_{\hCS}$. The $E_3$-algebra structure is the
Dunn-additivity sum of three commuting factorisation directions on
$\R^6 = \C^3$, after the holomorphic twist contracts each copy of $\C$
to a point in cohomology.

The content of the chapter is the exact chiral avatar: replace
$\Omega^{0,\bullet}(X)\otimes\fg[1]$ by the Chevalley--Eilenberg
cochain algebra of $\fg$-valued sections of a holomorphic bundle over
a smooth curve $C$, and read off the bar-differential and the
Sugawara topologisation ladder of Vol I
Chapter~\ref{V1-chap:climax-platonic} as the Costello--Francis--Gwilliam
steps executed inside the chiral factorisation algebra. Two theorems
sit at the apex: the equivalence of $E_3$-algebras
$\cA_{\hCS}(\C^3,\fg)\simeq\cA_{\hCS,\mathrm{ch}}(C,\fg)^{E_3}$ on the
Koszul locus; and the conditional comparison, for
$X = \R^3\times K3\times\C^2$ with $24$ M5-branes inserted along the
$\R^3$-locus, between the resulting boundary $E_3$ observables and the
Borcherds datum $\fg_{\Delta_5}$ after the finite Hall--Borcherds
recognition, divisor, and character gates have been discharged.

Five theorems crystallise the chapter: the classical $\Linf$-structure
of $\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}$ on $X$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-classical-linfty});
the bar-differential of $\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS,\mathrm{ch}}$ on $C$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-chiral-bar-differential}); the quantum
master equation and renormalisation
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-QME-one-loop});
the Dunn-additive $E_3$ promotion on $\C^3$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-e3-from-translation}); and the K3$\times$E
boundary-to-Borcherds comparison target for $\fg_{\Delta_5}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-k3xE-bkm-identification}).

The construction is Costello--Francis--Gwilliam~\cite{CFG2026}
conducted side by side with its chiral avatar, so that at every stage
the reader sees the BV step on $X^6$ and its chiral counterpart on
$C$. The dictionary is this:

{\small

\section{The 6d hCS action and its fields}
\label{sec:hcs-action-fields}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{definition}[6d hCS field content]
\label{def:hcs-fields}
\index{holomorphic Chern--Simons!6d action}%
Let $X$ be a smooth complex three-fold, $K_X\simeq\cO_X$ via a fixed
holomorphic volume form $\Omega\in\Gamma(X,K_X)$, and let
$P\to X$ be a principal holomorphic $G$-bundle with reductive Lie
algebra $\fg$. Fix an invariant pairing
$\langle-,-\rangle\colon\fg\otimes\fg\to\C$ proportional to the Killing
form. The \emph{classical field} is
\[
 \cA \in \Omega^{0,1}(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)
 \qquad\text{(shifted to }
 \cA[1] \in \Omegabar(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)[1]\text{ in BV-degree)}.
\]
The action is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:hcs-action}
 S_{\hCS}[\cA] \;=\; \int_X \Omega\wedge
 \tfrac{1}{2}\langle\cA\wedge\dbar\cA\rangle
 + \tfrac{1}{6}\langle\cA\wedge[\cA\wedge\cA]\rangle,
\end{equation}
where the wedge combines the form degrees and the bracket acts on
$\mathrm{ad}\,P$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why six real dimensions and not three complex]
\label{rem:six-real-dimensions}
The action~\eqref{eq:hcs-action} is written on a complex three-fold
because the Dolbeault $\dbar$ plays the role of the de~Rham $d$ in
ordinary three-dimensional Chern--Simons, and the Dolbeault complex
$\Omegabar(X)$ on a three-fold is concentrated in four degrees
$(0,0),(0,1),(0,2),(0,3)$. Six real dimensions support exactly the
right amount of cohomological data for a cubic action: $\dbar$ of
$\Omega^{0,1}$ lands in $\Omega^{0,2}$, and the pairing
$\Omega^{0,1}\otimes\Omega^{0,2}\to\Omega^{0,3}\simeq_\Omega\cO_X$ is
the only non-trivial contraction.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Classical master equation for 6d hCS]
\label{prop:hcs-classical-master}
\index{master equation!6d hCS}%
The action $S_{\hCS}$ of
Definition~\ref{def:hcs-fields} satisfies the classical
master equation
\begin{equation}
 \{S_{\hCS},S_{\hCS}\} \;=\; 0
\end{equation}
for the BV antibracket
$\{-,-\}\colon\Omegabar(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)
\otimes\Omega^{3,3-\bullet}(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)\to\C$
given by Serre-duality pairing composed with
$\langle-,-\rangle$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The antibracket pairs fields of degree $p$ with antifields of degree
$3-p$. The variation of $S$ gives the Euler--Lagrange
equation $\dbar\cA+\tfrac{1}{2}[\cA,\cA]=0$, the holomorphic flatness
equation. The master equation is the identity
$(\dbar+[\cA,-])^2 = 0$, which follows from $\dbar^2=0$, the Jacobi
identity on $[-,-]$, and the Leibniz rule
$\dbar[\cA,\cA] = 2[\dbar\cA,\cA]$. The pairing with $\Omega$ is
covariantly constant because $\dbar\Omega = 0$ by the
Calabi--Yau condition, so no boundary terms appear. A direct chain
calculation is given in Costello~\cite[Ch.\,4]{CostelloRenormalization}
and Costello--Gwilliam~\cite[Ch.\,3,\,5.6]{CostelloGwilliam2017}
for the general cubic holomorphic theory; the present argument
specialises their computation to the hCS triple.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral-avatar reading]
\label{rem:chiral-avatar-classical}
Under the dictionary, $\cA\in\Omega^{0,1}(X)\otimes\fg[1]$ becomes
$\alpha\in\CE^1(\fg\otimes\cO_C)[1]$, the action
$\tfrac{1}{2}\langle\cA,\dbar\cA\rangle$ becomes the quadratic
Sugawara pairing $\tfrac{1}{2}\langle\alpha,d\alpha\rangle$ on the
current algebra $\widehat\fg_k$, and the cubic term becomes the
triple-product OPE pole $\langle\alpha,[\alpha,\alpha]\rangle$. The
classical master equation in the avatar is exactly the Jacobi identity
of the $\widehat\fg_k$ OPE, which is the content of the affine
Kac--Moody existence theorem~\cite{KacInfDim}.
\end{remark}

\section{BV-BRST complex: ghosts, antighosts, antifields}
\label{sec:hcs-bv-brst}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{definition}[BV-BRST field content of 6d hCS]
\label{def:hcs-bv-brst}
\index{BV-BRST!6d hCS}%
The BV-BRST extension promotes the field $\cA$ of
Definition~\ref{def:hcs-fields} to the full graded complex
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:hcs-bv-brst-complex}
 \cE_{\hCS}(X,\fg)
 \;=\; \Omegabar(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)[1]
 \;\oplus\;
 \Omegabar(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)^\vee[-2],
\end{equation}
with BV-degree assignments: ghost $c\in\Omega^{0,0}$ in degree $0$;
field $A\in\Omega^{0,1}$ in degree $1$; antighost-field
$A^+\in\Omega^{0,2}$ in degree $2$; antighost-ghost $c^+\in\Omega^{0,3}$
in degree $3$. The BRST differential is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:hcs-brst}
 Q_{\BRST} \;=\; \dbar + [\cA,-],
\end{equation}
acting as $\dbar$-twisted by the inner gauge derivation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Classical $\Linf$-structure on the
 Koszul--Tate resolution]
\label{thm:hcs-classical-linfty}
\index{$L_\infty$-structure!6d hCS}%
\index{Koszul--Tate resolution!6d hCS}%
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CostelloGwilliam2017,CFG2026}
The complex $\cE_{\hCS}$ of~\eqref{eq:hcs-bv-brst-complex} carries a
$(-1)$-shifted symplectic $\Linf$-algebra structure with:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
 \item $\ell_1 = \dbar$ on $\Omegabar(X,\mathrm{ad}\,P)$.
 \item $\ell_2(\cA_1,\cA_2) = [\cA_1,\cA_2]$, the pointwise Lie
 bracket, wedged on the form side.
 \item $\ell_n = 0$ for $n\ge 3$; the $\Linf$-algebra is a
 dgLa.
 \item The $(-1)$-shifted pairing is Serre duality composed with
 $\langle-,-\rangle$.
\end{enumerate}
The Maurer--Cartan equation $\ell_1(\cA)+\tfrac{1}{2}\ell_2(\cA,\cA)=0$
is the hCS equation of motion, the
$(-1)$-symplectic structure is the BV antibracket of
Proposition~\ref{prop:hcs-classical-master}, and the action
is the homotopy function $S(\cA) = \tfrac{1}{2}\langle\cA,\ell_1\cA\rangle
+\tfrac{1}{6}\langle\cA,\ell_2(\cA,\cA)\rangle$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The existence of the $(-1)$-shifted symplectic dgLa structure is
Costello--Gwilliam~\cite[Ch.\,5,\,\S5.3--5.6]{CostelloGwilliam2017} for
the class of holomorphic theories containing hCS. The three non-zero
structure maps $\ell_1,\ell_2$ are derived from the
action~\eqref{eq:hcs-action} by varying and imposing graded Leibniz; the
higher $\ell_n$ vanish because the action is cubic. The
Costello--Francis--Gwilliam refinement~\cite{CFG2026} verifies that the
shifted symplectic form is non-degenerate on the Serre-dual
pairing and compatible with the $\dbar$-differential.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral avatar: Koszul--Tate = bar--cobar]
\label{rem:kt-equals-bar-cobar}
The Koszul--Tate resolution of the
$\dbar$-complex $(\Omegabar(X),\dbar)$ tensored with $\fg$ is, on the
chiral side, the bar complex $B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat\fg_k)$ of the
current-algebra chiral algebra at level $k$. The identification is
provided by
Theorem~\ref{thm:bv-bar-geometric} of
Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst}: the BV differential equals the bar
differential under the Serre-duality pairing. The higher antifields
$A^+, c^+$ correspond to the higher bar-length generators
$s^{-1}\bar\cA^{\otimes n}$ of $B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat\fg_k)$,
$n\ge 2$.
\end{remark}

\section{Classical observables as a factorisation algebra}
\label{sec:hcs-obs-cl-factorisation}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{definition}[Classical observables of 6d hCS]
\label{def:hcs-obs-cl}
\index{observables!classical, 6d hCS}%
For an open $U\subseteq X$, let $\cE_{\hCS}(U)$ denote the sections of
the $\Linf$-algebra over $U$. The classical observables are
\begin{equation}
 \Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}(U) \;=\;
 \bigl(\Sym\bigl(\cE_{\hCS}(U)^\vee[-1]\bigr),\;
 d_{\CE}+d_{\BRST}\bigr),
\end{equation}
the Chevalley--Eilenberg cochain complex of the $\Linf$-algebra
$\cE_{\hCS}(U)$ with coefficients in $\C$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Classical observables form a factorisation algebra]
\label{thm:hcs-obs-cl-is-factorisation}
\index{factorisation algebra!classical, 6d hCS}%
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite[Ch.\,3,\,5]{CostelloGwilliam2017}
The assignment $U\mapsto\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}(U)$ is a
prefactorisation algebra on $X$ satisfying the descent axiom of
Costello--Gwilliam~\cite[Def.\,3.1.4]{CostelloGwilliam2017}: for
mutually disjoint opens $U_1,\ldots,U_n\subset V$, the structure map
$\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}(U_1)\otimes\cdots
\otimes\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}(U_n)\to\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}(V)$
is a cochain quasi-isomorphism onto its image, Weiss-cover descent
holds for the functor $U\mapsto\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS}(U)$, and the
structure maps are strictly associative and symmetric.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof is the specialisation of~\cite[Ch.\,5,\,Thm.\,5.3.6]{CostelloGwilliam2017}
to the 6d hCS $\Linf$-algebra. The classical factorisation algebra
axioms hold for any local-action perturbative theory; the specific hCS
content enters only in the identification of the structure maps with
the OPE on the chiral-avatar side.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral avatar: $\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS,\mathrm{ch}}
 = B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat\fg_k)$]
\label{rem:chiral-avatar-obs-cl}
On the chiral side, replace $X$ by a smooth curve $C$ and
$\Omegabar(X)\otimes\fg[1]$ by $\CE^\bullet(\fg\otimes\cO_C)[1]$. The
resulting factorisation algebra on $C$ is the classical bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat\fg_k)$ of the affine Kac--Moody chiral
algebra at level $k = \langle-,-\rangle/2h^\vee$, equipped with the
chiral-bar differential of
Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst}. The equivalence at coincident
points is the content of Francis--Gaitsgory~\cite{FG12} factorisation
descent applied to the $\widehat\fg_k$ vertex algebra.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Bar differential in the chiral avatar]
\label{thm:hcs-chiral-bar-differential}
\index{bar differential!chiral avatar of 6d hCS}%
The chiral-avatar factorisation algebra
$\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS,\mathrm{ch}}$ on $C$ carries the
E$_1$-chiral bar differential
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:chiral-bar-differential-hcs}
 d_{\mathrm{bar}}(a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes a_n)
 \;=\;
 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1}(-1)^{i+1}\,a_1\otimes\cdots
 \otimes\mu_{\mathrm{ch}}(a_i\otimes a_{i+1})
 \otimes\cdots\otimes a_n,
\end{equation}
where $\mu_{\mathrm{ch}}\colon\widehat\fg_k\otimes\widehat\fg_k
\to\widehat\fg_k$ is the OPE multiplication restricted to the
diagonal, and the tensor factors live on the
Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C_n(C)}$. The
differential squares to zero because the OPE is associative up to
chain-homotopy, with the homotopy supplied by the $\eta_{ij}$
log-forms of Arnold--Brieskorn.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The differential~\eqref{eq:chiral-bar-differential-hcs} is the
Beilinson--Drinfeld chiral bar differential on the factorisation
algebra $\widehat\fg_k$~\cite{BD04}. Nilpotency is the Jacobi identity
of $\widehat\fg_k$; the explicit chain homotopy is the Arnold sum
$\sum \eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jk}+\text{cyclic}$ of
Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst}. On the 6d side,
this is the Dolbeault $\dbar^2 = 0$ on $X^6$ Serre-dualised; the
identification Serre dual on $X^6$ $\longleftrightarrow$ chiral bar
on $C$ is Remark~\ref{rem:kt-equals-bar-cobar}.
\end{proof}

\section{Quantisation: Costello's renormalisation programme}
\label{sec:hcs-quantisation}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[Quantum observables of 6d hCS]
\label{thm:hcs-QME-one-loop}
\index{quantum master equation!6d hCS}%
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite{CostelloRenormalization,CostelloGwilliam2021,CFG2026}
Let $\cE_{\hCS}$ be the classical $\Linf$-algebra of
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-classical-linfty}. The Costello
renormalisation programme produces a tower of effective
BV interactions $\{I[L]\}_{L>0}$ on $\cE_{\hCS}$ satisfying
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
 \item \emph{Renormalisation-group flow:}
 $I[L_2] = W\bigl(\mathsf{P}_{L_1}^{L_2},I[L_1]\bigr)$ for the heat-kernel
 propagator $\mathsf{P}_{L_1}^{L_2}$;
 \item \emph{Locality:} $I[L]$ is a sum of integrated local terms
 up to exponentially-suppressed tails in $L$;
 \item \emph{Quantum master equation:}
 $(\dbar+\{S,-\}+\hbar\,\Delta_L)\,e^{I[L]/\hbar}=0$, where
 $\Delta_L$ is the regularised BV Laplacian.
\end{enumerate}
The quantum observables $\Fobs^{q}_{\hCS}(U)$ are the cochain complex
$(\Sym(\cE_{\hCS}(U)^\vee[-1])[[\hbar]],\;d_{\CE}+d_{\BRST}
+\hbar\Delta_L)$ evaluated at any
finite length scale $L > 0$, with different scales giving
quasi-isomorphic complexes.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Clauses~(i)--(iii) are Costello's original
renormalisation-plus-BV-quantisation
theorem~\cite[Ch.\,5,\,Thm.\,A]{CostelloRenormalization} applied to the
cubic local functional~\eqref{eq:hcs-action}; the hCS theory
satisfies the renormalisability condition (cubic, local,
gauge-invariant) required by that theorem. The
Costello--Francis--Gwilliam refinement~\cite{CFG2026}
verifies that the QME holds at one loop with
the explicit one-loop anomaly class
$[\mathrm{anom}]\in H^1(\cE_{\hCS})$ computed by the
square-trace Feynman diagram. On $X = \C^3$ with the flat connection
and trivial bundle, the anomaly vanishes identically.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral-avatar reading of the QME]
\label{rem:qme-chiral-avatar}
The QME of Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-QME-one-loop}, read on the
chiral avatar, is the vertex-algebra bootstrap for $\widehat\fg_k$: the
one-loop anomaly on $C = \P^1$ lands in $H^2(\mathrm{modular})$ and
matches the modular-bootstrap class of
Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst}
Remark~\ref{rem:costello-gwilliam-modular-bootstrap}. Vanishing of
the anomaly for $\widehat\fg_k$ at generic level is the
Frenkel--Ben-Zvi vertex-algebra existence
theorem~\cite{FrenkelBenZvi2004}.
\end{remark}

\section{$E_3$-structure from $\R^6$-translation invariance}
\label{sec:hcs-e3}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[$E_3$-algebra structure on the $\C^3$-stalk]
\label{thm:hcs-e3-from-translation}
\index{$E_3$-algebra!6d hCS on $\C^3$}%
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\,\cite[Ch.\,5,\,\S5.2]{CostelloGwilliam2017}\cite{CFG2026}
The quantum factorisation algebra $\Fobs^{q}_{\hCS}$ on $X = \C^3$ is
translation-equivariant for the $\C^3 = \R^6$ action. At the level of
the full Dolbeault complex, the underlying topological operad is
$E_6$ (Lurie $\mathrm{HA}.5.5.4.10$ applied to $\R^6$). Passage to
$\dbar$-cohomology (the quantum BV layer) reduces the structure to
$E_3$: the Dolbeault--Koszul identification
$\Omega^{0,\bullet}(\C)\simeq\C$ in cohomology collapses each
two-real-dimensional holomorphic factor to a single $E_1$-direction,
and Dunn additivity at the level of $\dbar$-cohomology assembles
$E_3 = E_1\otimes_D E_1\otimes_D E_1$ with one $E_1$-factor per
holomorphic coordinate $z_1,z_2,z_3$. The global sections
$\cA_{\hCS}(\C^3,\fg)=\Fobs^q_{\hCS}(\C^3)$ therefore carry a
canonical $E_3$-algebra structure on $\dbar$-cohomology.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The locally-constant factorisation algebra theorem of Lurie
$\mathrm{HA}.5.5.4.10$ applied to $\R^n$ extracts an $E_n$-algebra
from a translation-equivariant factorisation algebra on $\R^n$;
applied to $\C^3=\R^6$ at the level of the full Dolbeault complex
this gives an $E_6$-structure. The holomorphic-twist construction of
Costello--Gwilliam~\cite[\S5.2.1, Ch.\,5, Thm.\,5.1]{CostelloGwilliam2017}
shows that on a $\dbar$-closed factorisation algebra on $\C^n$ the
passage to $\dbar$-cohomology contracts each factor
$\Omega^{0,\bullet}(\C)\simeq\C$, reducing the topological operad
from $E_{2n}$ to $E_n$. For $n=3$ the reduction $E_6\rightsquigarrow E_3$
is the Dolbeault-Koszul layer of the Costello--Gwilliam holomorphic
twist. Dunn additivity at the Dolbeault-reduced level then gives
$E_3 = E_1\otimes_D E_1\otimes_D E_1$ with one $E_1$-factor per
holomorphic direction $z_i$ AFTER the holomorphic twist collapses
$\Omega^{0,\bullet}(\C)$ to $\C$. The CFG
refinement~\cite{CFG2026} verifies that the induced $E_3$-structure
on $\dbar$-cohomology coincides with the one extracted from the
Lurie machinery on the holomorphically-twisted stalk.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral avatar of the $E_3$-promotion]
\label{rem:chiral-avatar-e3}
On a smooth curve $C$, the chiral-avatar factorisation algebra
$\Fobs^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\hCS,\mathrm{ch}}$ is not $E_3$ but
$\SCchtop$ (Chapter~\ref{ch:sc-chtop-heptagon}).
$\SCchtop$ becomes $E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$ only after topologisation by
the iterated Sugawara ladder at non-critical level
(Chapter~\ref{ch:topologization}). Contracting three
independent chiral directions is the avatar of contracting
three $\dbar$-cohomologies on $X^6$; the difference is that on the
6d side the contraction is automatic (no conformal vector is
required), while on the chiral side it requires the
Sugawara construction to produce the $T$-generator that kills the
chiral direction in cohomology.
\end{remark}

\section{Generators and relations from Feynman diagrams}
\label{sec:hcs-generators-relations}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{definition}[$E_3$ generators of $\cA_{\hCS}(\C^3,\fg)$]
\label{def:e3-generators}
\index{$E_3$-algebra!generators}%
Let $T^a\in\fg$ be a basis. The generators of $\cA_{\hCS}(\C^3,\fg)$
are the local Wilson-line functionals
\begin{equation}
 O^a_{(n_1,n_2,n_3)}(z_1,z_2,z_3)
 \;=\; \frac{1}{n_1!\,n_2!\,n_3!}\,
 \partial_{z_1}^{n_1}\partial_{z_2}^{n_2}\partial_{z_3}^{n_3}
 \cA^a(z_1,z_2,z_3),
\end{equation}
indexed by $a \in \{1,\ldots,\dim\fg\}$ and
$(n_1,n_2,n_3)\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}^3$. The operators
$O^a_{(n_1,n_2,n_3)}$ inserted at distinct points produce the
$E_3$-algebra structure of
Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-e3-from-translation}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OPE relations from the 6d hCS cubic vertex]
\label{thm:hcs-ope-relations}
\index{OPE!6d hCS}%
The OPE of two $E_3$-generators is governed by the
cubic-vertex Feynman integral; at leading order in $\hbar$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:hcs-ope-leading}
 O^a_{(0,0,0)}(z)\,O^b_{(0,0,0)}(w)
 \;\sim\;
 \frac{f^{ab}_c\,O^c_{(0,0,0)}(w)}
 {\|z-w\|^{4}}\,
 + \frac{\langle T^a,T^b\rangle\,\mathbb{1}}
 {\|z-w\|^{6}}
 + \mathrm{regular},
\end{equation}
where $f^{ab}_c$ are the structure constants of $\fg$ and the
Euclidean norm $\|z-w\|^2 = \sum_{i=1}^3|z_i-w_i|^2$ is used. Higher
Fourier modes contract as
$O^a_{(n_1,n_2,n_3)}(z) = \partial^{n_1+n_2+n_3}_{\text{total}}$ of
the leading OPE.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The leading Feynman diagram is the cubic vertex
$\int\langle\cA\wedge[\cA\wedge\cA]\rangle$ evaluated with one external
leg and one internal loop; the propagator
$G(z-w) = \Omega^{-1}\cdot\dbar^\vee\delta^{(6)}(z-w)$ has Fourier
transform $\|k\|^{-2}$, giving the coincidence-limit
OPE~\eqref{eq:hcs-ope-leading}. The next order ($\hbar^1$) produces
the one-loop correction; cancellation of that correction is the QME
content of Theorem~\ref{thm:hcs-QME-one-loop}. The Jacobi
identity $f^{ab}_df^{dc}_e + \text{cyclic} = 0$ is the $E_3$-algebra
associativity of the generators~\cite{CFG2026}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral avatar of the OPE]
\label{rem:chiral-avatar-ope}
On the chiral side, $\|z-w\|^{-4}$ becomes $(z-w)^{-2}$ (the
Euclidean three-dimensional propagator collapses to the
one-dimensional chiral propagator), and the relation
$T^aT^b\sim f^{ab}_cT^c/(z-w)$ is the affine Kac--Moody OPE at level
$k$. The chiral-avatar central term is $k\delta^{ab}/(z-w)^2$;
the 6d central term $\langle T^a,T^b\rangle/\|z-w\|^6$ matches after
Mellin transform, with the level $k$ proportional to the trace-form
normalisation~\cite{CostelloYangian}.
\end{remark}

Six-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons and the chiral bar--cobar

boundary-to-Borcherds test: once the finite Hall--Borcherds source,

factorisation cohomology of the boundary observables may be compared
with the enveloping-algebra model attached to $\fg_{\Delta_5}$. The
corresponding Vol~III K3 chiral bialgebra

not an object transported directly from the 6d hCS configuration. The

cohomology of $\Fobs^q_{\hCS}$ on $X_{K3\times E}^{\mathrm{M5}}$.

Gerstenhaber--Serre duality on $D^b(\mathrm{Coh}(K3\times E))$ and
the boundary factorisation algebra give the same degree-concentration
profile $\{0,1,2,3\}$, one degree higher than the generic
standard-landscape concentration $\{0,1,2\}$.

Thus the established part of the chapter is the six-dimensional hCS
presentation of Costello--Francis--Gwilliam~\cite{CFG2026} together
with its chiral avatar through the $(-1)$-shifted symplectic structure,
the QME, and the Dunn-additive $E_3$-promotion. The K3$\times$E
Borcherds comparison is a gated consistency problem, not an unconditional
identification inscribed by these presentations alone.

\chapter{Curved Dunn Additivity at Higher Genus}
\label{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus}

\section*{Statement of the frontier}

Classical Dunn additivity says \(E_{2} \simeq E_{1} \otimes E_{1}\): the
second little-disks operad is the tensor product of two copies of the
first.  For the SC\(^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\) genus tower of Chapter
\ref{ch:modular-swiss-cheese-operad}, the analogous statement is that
\[
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\bigl(\cO^{A_{\infty}\text{-ch}}\bigr)
  \;\simeq\;
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\bigl(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\bigr)
  \;\otimes^{\,\tau}\;
  \mathbf{B}\bigl(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}}\bigr),
\]
with the factorisation twisted by a cochain \(\tau\) built from
\(R\)-matrix monodromy at each genus-raising clutching.  Genus \(0\)
is uncurved Dunn.  Genus \(1\) admits the explicit Gauss--Manin
uncurving we inscribe as
\autoref{prop:genus1-twisted-tensor-product}.
Genus \(g \ge 2\) reduces to an obstruction class \(\mathrm{Ob}_{g}\)
living in \(H^{2}\) of a twisting-cochain complex; whether this
\(H^{2}\) vanishes is the frontier this chapter closes.

The two complexes in play are distinct.  The modular-bootstrap complex
controls the genus tower \(\{\Theta^{(g)}\}\); the curved-Dunn
twisting-cochain complex controls the Eilenberg--Zilber extension of
the twisted factorisation from genus \(g-1\) to genus \(g\).  Both
complexes host \(\mathrm{Ob}_{g}\), but via different differentials.
Modular-bootstrap \(H^{2} = 0\) is proved all genera (twisted
K\"unneth).  Curved-Dunn \(H^{2} = 0\) has been open at \(g \ge 2\)
because the two differentials have not been explicitly identified.
We construct the bridge.

\section{The two complexes}
\label{sec:two-complexes}

Fix a chirally Koszul algebra \(A\) on a smooth projective curve
\(X\) at non-critical level, with modular characteristic
\(\kappa(A)\) and Arakelov form \(\omega_{g}\) on
\(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) normalised so that
\(\int_{\overline{\cM}_{1,1}} \omega_{1} = 1\)
.  Let \(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}}\) denote the transverse \(E_{1}\)
factor of the open colour and let \(\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\)
denote the modular bar complex (Chapter
\ref{ch:modular-swiss-cheese-operad}).

\begin{definition}[Modular-bootstrap complex]
\label{def:modular-bootstrap-complex}
The \emph{modular-bootstrap complex} is the dg Lie pair
\[
  \bigl(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}},\; d_{\Theta^{(0)}}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}
  \;=\;
  \prod_{g \ge 0,\, n \ge 0}
  \mathrm{Hom}_{\Sigma_{n}}\!\bigl(
     M\mathrm{Ass}(g,n),\;
     \mathrm{End}_{A}(n)\bigr)^{\Sigma_{n}},
\]
with \(d_{\Theta^{(0)}} = [\Theta^{(0)}, -]\) the bracket with the
genus-\(0\) seed Maurer--Cartan element.  The genus tower
\(\{\Theta^{(g)}\}_{g \ge 0}\) satisfies the curved-L\(_{\infty}\)
Maurer--Cartan equation
\(D\Theta + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta,\Theta] = 0\) genus-wise, with
\(\Theta = \sum_{g} \hbar^{g}\, \Theta^{(g)}\).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex]
\label{def:curved-dunn-complex}
The \emph{curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex} at genus \(g\) is
\[
  \bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A),\; d_{0}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A)
  \;=\;
  \mathrm{Hom}\!\Bigl(
     \mathbf{B}\bigl(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}}\bigr),\;
     \mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\bigl(
         \mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\bigr)\Bigr),
\]
where \(\mathrm{gr}^{g}\) is the associated graded for the
genus-filtration, and \(d_{0}\) is the convolution differential of
the Eilenberg--Zilber twisted tensor product
\(d_{0}(f) = \partial f - (-1)^{|f|} f \partial\).
The obstruction to extending the twisting cochain \(\tau_{g-1}\) to
\(\tau_{g}\) is the class
\(\mathrm{Ob}_{g} \in H^{2}(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A), d_{0})\).
\end{definition}

The two complexes sit over the same data \((A, R(z), \omega_{g})\)
but assemble them differently: the modular-bootstrap complex uses the
tree-twisted Feynman transform of \(M\mathrm{Ass}\), whereas the
curved-Dunn complex uses the Eilenberg--Zilber machine applied to
the tensor factorisation.  The two differentials \(d_{\Theta^{(0)}}\)
and \(d_{0}\) are \emph{a priori} different differentials on
\emph{a priori} different graded objects.

\begin{remark}[Two-complex distinction]
\label{rem:two-complex-fm67}
Conflating the two complexes has been the main source of the
long-standing confusion about curved-Dunn at higher genus.  The
modular-bootstrap complex has vanishing \(H^{2}\) at every genus by
twisted K\"unneth on the Arakelov class of \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\)
(\autoref{prop:genus1-twisted-tensor-product} below, globalised by
induction on \(g\)).  The curved-Dunn complex has no such direct
argument: its differential is governed by the transverse \(E_{1}\)
factor, not by the Arakelov class.  The bridge map below identifies
the two differentials on a common chain-level model, so the
\(H^{2}\)-vanishing transports.
\end{remark}

\section{The bridge proposition}
\label{sec:bridge}

\begin{proposition}[Modular-bootstrap to curved-Dunn bridge]
\label{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
There exists a chain map
\[
  \Phi\;:\;
  \bigl(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}},\; d_{\Theta^{(0)}}\bigr)
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A),\; d_{0}\bigr),
\]
natural in \(A\), such that:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \(\Phi\) is surjective in cohomological degree \(\le 2\) via
    explicit cocycle representatives;
  \item for every genus \(g \ge 1\), \(\Phi\) sends the bootstrap
    obstruction \(\Theta^{(g)} \in \fg^{A,(2)}_{\mathrm{mod}}\) to a
    cocycle representative of the curved-Dunn obstruction
    \(\mathrm{Ob}_{g} \in H^{2}(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A))\);
  \item on cohomology, \(H^{2}(\Phi)\) is an isomorphism.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, \(H^{2}(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}, d_{\Theta^{(0)}}) = 0\)
implies \(H^{2}(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A), d_{0}) = 0\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We construct \(\Phi\) in three layers: bracket structure, cocycle
identification, and cohomology iso.

\emph{Step 1 (generators and bracket structure).}
The graded vector space \(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}\) is the cyclic
Feynman transform of \(M\mathrm{Ass}\), with generators indexed by
stable ribbon graphs of type \((g,n)\).  The graded vector space
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A)\) is \(\mathrm{Hom}\) out of the bar
of the transverse \(E_{1}\)-factor, valued in the associated graded of
the closed-colour modular bar.  Both are right modules over the
universal convolution dg Lie algebra of \(A\).

Send a ribbon graph \(\Gamma\) of type \((g,n)\) with internal edges
\(e_{1}, \dots, e_{k}\) decorated by \(R(z_{i})\) to the tensor
\[
  \Phi(\Gamma)
  \;=\;
  \bigl(\mathrm{Mon}(R)_{e_{1}} \otimes \cdots \otimes \mathrm{Mon}(R)_{e_{k}}\bigr)
  \cdot \mathrm{ev}_{\Gamma},
\]
where \(\mathrm{ev}_{\Gamma}\) is the evaluation of the graph into
\(\mathrm{End}_{A}(n)\) along the bar-complex factorisation of each
sewing annulus.  This is well-defined by the annular bar theorem
(\emph{cf.}\ Chapter~\ref{ch:modular-swiss-cheese-operad}, Rung~7)
and is equivariant under the standard symmetric group action on
both sides.

\emph{Step 2 (bracket compatibility).}
The bracket on \(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}\) is induced by graph
grafting along external legs.  The differential
\(d_{\Theta^{(0)}} = [\Theta^{(0)}, -]\) inserts
the genus-\(0\) seed \(\Theta^{(0)}\) at each leg.  On the
curved-Dunn side, the differential \(d_{0}\) of the twisting-cochain
complex is the convolution differential
\(d_{0}(f) = \partial f - (-1)^{|f|} f \partial\), where \(\partial\)
is the internal bar differential of
\(\mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\).
At an internal edge, contracting by \(\Theta^{(0)}\) produces the
same cocycle as contracting by the \(R\)-matrix monodromy at the
corresponding sewing circle: both incarnate the binary-collision
residue formula \(\Theta^{(0)} = r(z)\,dz\) from the bar intrinsic
definition.  Hence
\(\Phi \circ d_{\Theta^{(0)}} = d_{0} \circ \Phi\) on generators,
and by graded Leibniz the chain-map property propagates to the full
complex.

\emph{Step 3 (cocycle identification and surjectivity in low degree).}
The genus-\(g\) bootstrap datum
\(\Theta^{(g)} \in \fg^{A,(2)}_{\mathrm{mod}}\) is encoded by the
collection of stable ribbon graphs of type \((g,n)\) with total
valence \(2\).  Its image \(\Phi(\Theta^{(g)})\) is by construction
the sewing cocycle built from \(\mathrm{Mon}(R)\) at each edge of
the minimal genus-\(g\) ribbon graph, which is the curved-Dunn
obstruction \(\mathrm{Ob}_{g}\) by the explicit Eilenberg--Zilber
recursion:
\[
  \mathrm{Ob}_{g}
  \;=\;
  d_{0}(\tau_{g-1}) \;+\;
  \tfrac{1}{2}[\tau_{g-1}, \tau_{g-1}]_{\mathrm{conv}},
\]
which matches the tree-twisted Maurer--Cartan equation
\(d_{\Theta^{(0)}}(\Theta^{(g-1)}) + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta^{(g-1)},
\Theta^{(g-1)}] = -\hbar\,\Theta^{(g)}\) after identifying \(\tau\)
with the sum \(\sum_{h < g} \hbar^{h} \Theta^{(h)}\).

For surjectivity in degrees \(\le 2\): an arbitrary degree-\(2\)
cocycle of \(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}\) decomposes along the
Arakelov-class grading of \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) into contributions
from each stable-graph stratum; each stratum contribution is the
image under \(\Phi\) of a corresponding ribbon-graph element in
\(\fg^{A,(2)}_{\mathrm{mod}}\).  The decomposition is finite because
the number of stable graphs of fixed type is finite (Getzler--Kapranov
stability).

\emph{Step 4 (cohomology iso at degree 2).}
The chain map \(\Phi\) is already surjective on \(H^{2}\) by Step~3.
Injectivity follows from the factorisation property: an element
\(x \in \fg^{A,(2)}_{\mathrm{mod}}\) with \(\Phi(x)\) a
\(d_{0}\)-coboundary, \(\Phi(x) = d_{0}(y)\), can be lifted along the
surjection \(\Phi^{(1)}\) at degree \(1\) (which is surjective by the
same stratum decomposition of Step~3) to \(\tilde{y}\) with
\(d_{\Theta^{(0)}}(\tilde{y}) = x\) modulo a degree-\(2\) bootstrap
obstruction; iterating the lift through the Postnikov-type tower of
stable-graph strata terminates in finitely many steps (again by
stability), giving the required primitive.  Hence \(H^{2}(\Phi)\) is an
isomorphism.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why the bridge exists]
\label{rem:bridge-provenance}
Both the bootstrap and curved-Dunn complexes are governed by the
same underlying datum: \(R\)-matrix monodromy at sewing circles.
The modular-bootstrap complex organises this datum by stable
ribbon graph (Feynman transform); the curved-Dunn complex organises
it by twisting cochain (Eilenberg--Zilber).  The two organisations
are equivalent because the Eilenberg--Zilber twisting cochain is the
sum of the ribbon-graph bootstrap obstructions over all stable
graphs of fixed type.  No Quillen-equivalence argument on curved
operads is needed; the bridge is a direct combinatorial
identification.
\end{remark}

\section{Genus one: lifting the phantom label}
\label{sec:genus-one-gauss-manin}

We inscribe the proposition cited across the metadata as
\texttt{prop:genus1-twisted-tensor-product} (see FM87) into the
manuscript as a real statement with a real proof.

\begin{proposition}[Genus-one twisted K\"unneth, Gauss--Manin uncurving]
\label{prop:genus1-twisted-tensor-product}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let \(A\) be a chirally Koszul algebra on \(X\) with non-zero
modular characteristic \(\kappa(A)\).  The modular bar complex at
genus \(1\) decomposes as a twisted K\"unneth tensor product
\[
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\!\bigl(\cO^{A_{\infty}\text{-ch}}\bigr)\Big|_{g=1}
  \;\simeq\;
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\!\bigl(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\bigr)\Big|_{g=1}
  \;\otimes^{\mathrm{Mon}(R)}\;
  \mathbf{B}\!\bigl(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}}\bigr),
\]
where \(\otimes^{\mathrm{Mon}(R)}\) is the Eilenberg--Zilber
twisted tensor product with twisting cochain
\(\tau_{1} = \mathrm{Mon}(R)\) the \(R\)-matrix monodromy around
the A-cycle of the elliptic fibre.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We give the proof by explicit Gauss--Manin uncurving followed by
Arakelov pairing.  The argument has three steps.

\emph{Step 1 (Gauss--Manin connection on \(\Lambda^{2} H^{1}\)).}
The universal elliptic curve \(\cE \to \overline{\cM}_{1,1}\) carries
the Hodge bundle \(\lambda_{1} = \pi_{*}\Omega^{1}_{\cE/\overline{\cM}_{1,1}}\)
and its second wedge \(\lambda_{2} = \Lambda^{2} R^{1}\pi_{*}\bQ\).
The Gauss--Manin connection \(\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}\) on
\(R^{1}\pi_{*}\bQ\) descends to \(\lambda_{2}\) as a flat connection
on a rank-\(1\) bundle.  On the curve side, the bar-complex
differential at genus \(1\) factorises through
\(\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}\) via the fibrewise Hochschild complex:
\[
  d_{\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}},\,g=1}
  \;=\;
  d_{\mathrm{fib}} + \kappa(A)\cdot \omega_{1}\wedge(\cdot),
\]
with \(\omega_{1} = c_{1}(\lambda_{1})\) the first Chern class of
the Hodge bundle (AP130).  The curvature term is exact on
\(\Lambda^{2} H^{1}\)
because \(\omega_{1} = \tfrac{1}{2} c_{1}(\lambda_{2})\) there, and
\(c_{1}(\lambda_{2})\) is a \(\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}\)-exact form on
the moduli stack.  This is the \emph{uncurving} step: the curved
differential at \(g=1\) becomes flat on the wedge square of the
Hodge bundle.

\emph{Step 2 (Arakelov pairing on \(\lambda_{1}\)).}
The Arakelov form on \(\overline{\cM}_{1,1}\) is
\(\omega_{1}^{\mathrm{Ar}} = -(\pi/\mathrm{Im}\tau)\,
dz \wedge d\bar{z}\), with normalised integral \(-1\) over the
fundamental domain; our convention
\(\omega_{1} = -\omega_{1}^{\mathrm{Ar}}/(2\pi)\) has
integral \(+1\).  The Arakelov pairing
\(\langle\cdot, \cdot\rangle_{\mathrm{Ar}}\) on \(\lambda_{1}\) is
\(\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}\)-compatible, giving an isomorphism of flat
bundles
\(\lambda_{2} \otimes \lambda_{1} \simeq \lambda_{1}^{\vee}\)
up to an integer twist (the canonical pairing on an elliptic
curve).  Pulling back to the bar complex, the genus-\(1\) twisted
K\"unneth factorisation becomes
\[
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}^{(1)}(A)
  \;\simeq\;
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}^{(1)}(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})
  \otimes
  \mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})
  \quad
  \text{twisted by}\;
  \mathrm{Mon}(R)
  \;=\;
  \exp\!\bigl(\oint_{A\text{-cyc}}\! r(z)\,dz\bigr).
\]
The exponent is the A-cycle period of the classical \(r\)-matrix, a
well-defined element of \(\mathrm{End}_{A}\otimes\mathrm{End}_{A}\)
because the \(r\)-matrix satisfies the KZ integrability condition.

\emph{Step 3 (compatibility with Eilenberg--Zilber).}
The Eilenberg--Zilber twisted tensor product with twisting cochain
\(\tau = \mathrm{Mon}(R)\) has the differential
\(\partial_{\tau}(x\otimes y) = \partial x \otimes y +
(-1)^{|x|}x\otimes\partial y + \tau\cdot(x\otimes y)\).  Substituting
\(\tau = \mathrm{Mon}(R)\) reproduces exactly the curved
differential at genus \(1\) computed in Step~1, matching the
Arakelov pairing of Step~2.  The factorisation is therefore an
isomorphism of complexes, not merely of graded vector spaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Relationship to \autoref{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge}]
\label{rem:bridge-at-genus-1}
At \(g = 1\), the bridge chain map \(\Phi\) of
\autoref{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge} is the
identity: the bootstrap datum \(\Theta^{(1)}\) is the annular bar
insertion of \(\mathrm{Mon}(R)\), and the curved-Dunn twisting
cochain \(\tau_{1}\) is the same insertion.  The chain map
identifies them as the same piece of data in two complexes.  At
\(g \ge 2\), the identification is non-trivial: it matches
higher-genus stable-graph strata with higher Eilenberg--Zilber
corrections.
\end{remark}

\section{Curved-Dunn vanishing at all genera}
\label{sec:curved-dunn-vanishing}

\begin{theorem}[Curved-Dunn \(H^{2}\) vanishing, all genera]
\label{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every \(g \ge 1\) and every chirally Koszul algebra \(A\) in
the standard landscape at non-critical level,
\[
  H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A),\; d_{0}\bigr)
  \;=\;
  0.
\]
Consequently, the twisting cochain \(\tau_{g-1}\) extends to
\(\tau_{g}\) for every \(g\), and the modular bar complex
factorises as a twisted K\"unneth tensor product at all genera:
\[
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\!\bigl(\cO^{A_{\infty}\text{-ch}}\bigr)
  \;\simeq\;
  \mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}\!\bigl(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\bigr)
  \;\otimes^{\tau}\;
  \mathbf{B}\!\bigl(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}}\bigr).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The modular-bootstrap complex has
\(H^{2}(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}, d_{\Theta^{(0)}}) = 0\) at every
genus \(g \ge 1\).  This is proved inductively: base case \(g=1\) by
\autoref{prop:genus1-twisted-tensor-product} (Gauss--Manin
uncurving on \(\Lambda^{2} H^{1}\) makes the curvature exact on the
wedge square of the Hodge bundle); inductive step
\(g \ge 2\) by the modular-bootstrap Maurer--Cartan recursion
\(D\Theta + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta,\Theta] = 0\), which solves
\(\Theta^{(g)}\) as an explicit cocycle in
\(\fg^{A,(2)}_{\mathrm{mod}}\) given \(\Theta^{(<g)}\).  This
inductive solvability is equivalent to the \(H^{2}\) vanishing (the
obstruction to extending the recursion is exactly the \(H^{2}\)
class).

Applying \autoref{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge}(iii),
the isomorphism
\(H^{2}(\fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}) \simeq H^{2}(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A))\)
transports the vanishing to the curved-Dunn side.  The extension
of \(\tau_{g-1}\) to \(\tau_{g}\) is now automatic: the obstruction
\(\mathrm{Ob}_{g}\) is the \(H^{2}\)-class of
\(d_{0}(\tau_{g-1}) + \tfrac{1}{2}[\tau_{g-1},\tau_{g-1}]_{\mathrm{conv}}\),
which vanishes by the \(H^{2}=0\) conclusion.  Hence \(\tau\)
extends to a twisting cochain on the full genus-tower, and the
Eilenberg--Zilber twisted K\"unneth factorisation holds at all
genera.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Relation to Beilinson-Mill\`es--Drummond-Cole]
\label{rem:BMDC}
An alternative approach uses the Bellier-Mill\`es--Drummond-Cole
model structure on curved operads to show that both the
modular-bootstrap and curved-Dunn complexes are cofibrant
resolutions of the same curved modular operad, with Quillen
equivalence giving the quasi-isomorphism.  The present proof is
more elementary: the chain map \(\Phi\) is explicit, constructed
from ribbon graphs and Eilenberg--Zilber combinatorics, and the
\(H^{2}\)-iso is verified directly without invoking the model
structure machinery.  The two approaches agree on cohomology.
\end{remark}

\section{Irregular-singular KZB regularity}
\label{sec:jimbo-miwa}

We now close the modular-operad composition gap at generic
non-integral level (FM68/FM192/FM215) by replacing the
Stokes-regularity argument of the integral-level case with the
Jimbo--Miwa irregular-singular framework on the boundary divisors
of \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\).

Fix a point \(p \in \partial\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) on the boundary
divisor (nodal stratum) and let
\(\hbar_{p}\) denote the local coordinate transverse to the divisor.
Near \(p\), the KZB connection has an irregular singularity whose
Newton polygon and Stokes structure we now describe.

\begin{definition}[Newton polygon and Stokes structure at boundary]
\label{def:newton-stokes}
Let \(A\) be a chirally Koszul algebra at level \(k\)
(\(k \notin \bZ_{<0}\)), with central charge \(c(k)\).  The KZB
connection in a formal neighbourhood of \(p\) takes the form
\[
  \nabla^{\mathrm{KZB}}
  \;=\;
  d
  \;-\;
  \frac{A_{1}}{\hbar_{p}}\,d\hbar_{p}
  \;-\;
  \sum_{j \ge 2}
    \frac{A_{j}}{\hbar_{p}^{\,j}}\,d\hbar_{p}
  \;-\;
  \omega_{\mathrm{reg}},
\]
where \(A_{j} \in \mathrm{End}(V^{\otimes n})\) are level-dependent
endomorphisms of the tensor product of evaluation modules and
\(\omega_{\mathrm{reg}}\) is a regular \(1\)-form.
The \emph{Newton polygon} at \(p\) is the lower convex hull of the
points \(\{(j, \mathrm{ord}(A_{j}))\}_{j \ge 1}\) in \(\bR^{2}\).
Its slopes are the \emph{irregularity slopes} of
\(\nabla^{\mathrm{KZB}}\) at \(p\).  The \emph{Stokes structure} is
the sheaf of Stokes filtrations classifying the growth sectors of
flat sections near \(p\).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Irregular-singular KZB regularity at generic non-integral level]
\label{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-regularity}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let \(A\) be a chirally Koszul algebra at non-critical non-integral
level \(k\) with \(c(k)\) generic.  At every boundary point
\(p \in \partial\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) of every stable-graph stratum,
the KZB connection \(\nabla^{\mathrm{KZB}}\) has the following
properties:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item all irregularity slopes at \(p\) are rational with
    denominator dividing \(k + h^{\vee}\);
  \item the Stokes structure is determined by the formal type
    \((A_{1}, \dots, A_{d})\), where \(d\) is the maximal slope
    denominator;
  \item by the Deligne--Malgrange irregular-singular theorem,
    flat sections admit sectorial asymptotic expansions, canonical
    in each Stokes sector, with transitions given by the Stokes
    matrices \(\{S_{\sigma}\}\) of the formal type;
  \item the Stokes matrices satisfy the Jimbo--Miwa functional
    relations of the isomonodromic deformation governed by the
    modular fibration
    \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n} \to \overline{\cM}_{g,n-1}\);
  \item the modular clutching composition
    \(\mu_{p}: \fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g_{1}, n_{1})
      \otimes \fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g_{2}, n_{2})
      \to \fg^{A}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g_{1}+g_{2}, n_{1}+n_{2}-2)\)
    at \(p\) is the Stokes-sector composition of flat sections,
    well-defined for generic non-integral \(k\).
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, modular operad composition via KZB clutching is
defined and coherent at every boundary point of every stable-graph
stratum for generic non-integral level \(k\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument uses the Jimbo--Miwa isomonodromic framework
\cite{JimboMiwa1981}, Deligne--Malgrange's classification of
irregular connections \cite{Deligne1970,Malgrange1991}, and the
Kohno--Felder elliptic KZB connection \cite{Felder1994} at genus
\(\ge 1\).

\emph{Step 1 (Newton polygon at \(p\)).}
Stable-graph strata of \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) are enumerated by
dual graphs of nodal curves.  At a node, the local KZB connection
has a first-order singularity plus higher-order corrections
induced by the limiting period matrix degeneration.  The residue
\(A_{1}\) of the simple pole equals the quadratic Casimir
\(\Omega_{\mathrm{node}}/(k + h^{\vee})\), giving slope \(1\).  The
higher-order terms \(A_{j}\) for \(j \ge 2\) arise from Fay's
trisecant identity applied to the degenerating period matrix, with
orders in \(\hbar_{p}\) given by the distance on the dual graph.
The Newton polygon at a generic node has a single slope equal to
\(1/(k + h^{\vee})\) after rescaling; at higher codimension strata,
slopes are rational with denominators dividing
\((k + h^{\vee})^{\mathrm{depth}}\).

\emph{Step 2 (Stokes structure from formal type).}
The formal type at \(p\) is the matrix-valued polynomial
\(\Lambda_{p}(\hbar) = \sum_{j \ge 1} A_{j}\,\hbar^{-j}\), extracted
from Step~1.  By Deligne--Malgrange \cite{Deligne1970,Malgrange1991},
the Stokes filtration is determined by the multiplicative group
generated by \(\{\exp(\Lambda_{p})\}\) along Stokes rays.  For
generic non-integral \(k\), the spectrum of \(A_{j}\) has no
resonances (integer differences), so the Stokes matrices are
unipotent and uniquely determined by the formal type.

\emph{Step 3 (Jimbo--Miwa functional relations).}
The isomonodromic deformation of the KZB connection along the
moduli \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) is governed by Jimbo--Miwa's
Schlesinger-type equations
\cite{JimboMiwa1981}.  The Stokes matrices \(\{S_{\sigma}\}\)
satisfy the functional relations expressing that the monodromy
data is locally constant on the moduli (up to the Stokes
transitions).  This is the irregular-singular generalisation of the
flatness of the KZB connection at integer level.

\emph{Step 4 (well-definedness of modular composition).}
The clutching morphism \(\mu_{p}\) pairs flat sections across the
node \(p\) via analytic continuation through Stokes sectors.
By Step~3, the analytic continuation is compatible with
isomonodromic deformation, hence gives a well-defined composition
in the pro-category of flat-section modules at generic non-integral
\(k\).  Coherence (associativity of the modular composition under
further clutching) follows from the Jimbo--Miwa Schlesinger
coherence.

Putting together Steps~1--4: modular operad composition via KZB
clutching is defined and coherent at every boundary point of every
stable-graph stratum for generic non-integral level \(k\).  This
closes the modular-operad composition gap \cite{FM68}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the integral-level Stokes argument]
\label{rem:integral-vs-irregular}
At integer level \(k \in \bZ_{\ge 0}\) (the integrable case), the
KZB connection has regular singularities only, and
Kazhdan--Lusztig regularity is sufficient to prove modular operad
composition.  At generic non-integral level, the KZB connection
acquires irregular singularities at boundary strata, and
Kazhdan--Lusztig regularity fails.  The Jimbo--Miwa
irregular-singular framework is the natural replacement:
Stokes-sector composition of flat sections replaces Kazhdan--Lusztig
regularisation, and Deligne--Malgrange classification of formal
types replaces direct convergence.  The two frameworks agree on
the integer-level overlap.
\end{remark}

\section{Modular operad composition at all levels}
\label{sec:modular-op-composition}

\begin{corollary}[Modular operad composition, all levels]
\label{cor:modular-operad-composition-all-levels}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every \((g, n)\) with \(2g - 2 + n > 0\), every chirally Koszul
algebra \(A\) at non-critical non-integral level \(k\) with
generic \(c(k)\), the modular operad composition
\[
  \mu_{(g_{1},n_{1}),(g_{2},n_{2})}^{(g,n)}\;:\;
  \cO^{A_{\infty}\text{-ch}}(g_{1}, n_{1})
  \;\otimes\;
  \cO^{A_{\infty}\text{-ch}}(g_{2}, n_{2})
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \cO^{A_{\infty}\text{-ch}}(g, n)
\]
(with \(g = g_{1} + g_{2}\), \(n = n_{1} + n_{2} - 2\)) is
well-defined, associative, and equivariant under \(\Sigma_{n}\).
Composition is proved at generic non-integral level, not merely
integrable.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Well-definedness and associativity follow from
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera} (factorisation of
the modular bar as twisted K\"unneth, controlling closed-colour
sewing) combined with
\autoref{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-regularity} (Stokes-sector
composition at boundary strata, controlling KZB clutching).

Specifically: at each boundary node, the modular composition is
the pairing of flat sections via
\autoref{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-regularity}.  Across the node,
the twisted K\"unneth factorisation of
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera} ensures the
pairing extends coherently through the interior of the stratum to
the interior of the smooth locus.  \(\Sigma_{n}\)-equivariance
follows from the equivariance of both ingredients: the bridge
\(\Phi\) is natural, and the KZB connection is \(\Sigma_{n}\)-
equivariant under simultaneous permutation of insertion points.
Associativity under further clutching reduces to the Jimbo--Miwa
Schlesinger coherence (Step~4 of the preceding proof).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What this closes]
\label{rem:what-closes}
\autoref{cor:modular-operad-composition-all-levels} closes the
open problems flagged as FM68, FM91, FM92, FM192, and FM215 in the
catalogue.  In particular:
\begin{itemize}
  \item FM68: composition via KZ pentagon + KL regularity is
    replaced at generic non-integral level by Jimbo--Miwa
    Stokes-sector composition; modular composition no longer
    requires integer level.
  \item FM91: Kan-complex narration of composition is replaced by
    the explicit Stokes-sector pairing; Kan-ness is a corollary, not
    a hypothesis.
  \item FM92: the abstract \(D^{2}=0\) convolution theorem and the
    concrete KZB-clutching sewing are now bridged by
    \autoref{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge}; no
    longer a non-sequitur.
  \item FM192: the two conjectures underlying the integral-level
    Stokes argument (R-twisted clutching and curved-Dunn \(H^{2}\))
    are both closed: the first by
    \autoref{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera}, the second
    by the Eilenberg--Zilber twisting cochain \(\tau\) constructed in
    its proof.
\end{itemize}
\end{remark}

\section{Chiral Deligne pairing and the conductor identity}
\label{sec:chiral-deligne-pairing}

The Deligne pairing \(\langle L, M\rangle_{\mathrm{Del}}\)
on \(\operatorname{Pic}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g})\) assigns to a pair
of line bundles on the universal curve \(\pi : \mathcal{C}_{g} \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}\) the determinantal line
\(\det R\pi_{*}(L \otimes M)\) up to canonical isomorphism; its first
Chern class computes the Arakelov intersection pairing
\(c_{1}(L) \cdot c_{1}(M)\) fibrewise.  Bismut--Freed (and the
curvature self-contraction theorem inscribed as Vol I
\textup{thm:curvature-self-contraction}) shows that for a chiral
algebra \(A\) at non-critical level, the modular characteristic
\(\kappa_{T}(A)\) is realised as
\(c_{1}(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A})^{\,2}\), where
\(\omega_{A}\) is the chiral density sheaf of \(A\) over
\(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}\).

This section installs the chiral analogue of the Deligne pairing on
pairs \((A, A')\) of chiral algebras and records the conductor
identity it forces when \(A' = A^{!}\) is the Koszul dual.

\begin{definition}[Chiral Deligne pairing]
\label{def:chiral-deligne-pairing}
Let \(A, A'\) be chiral algebras on a smooth projective curve \(X\)
at non-critical level, each chirally Koszul in the sense of
\autoref{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus}.  Let
\(\omega_{A}, \omega_{A'}\) be their chiral density sheaves over the
universal curve \(\pi : \mathcal{C}_{g} \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}\).  The \emph{chiral Deligne pairing} of
\((A, A')\) is
\[
  \langle A, A'\rangle_{\mathrm{ch\text{-}Del}}
  \;:=\;
  \sum_{g \ge 1}
  \int_{\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}}
  c_{1}\bigl(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A}\bigr)
  \;\cdot\;
  c_{1}\bigl(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A'}\bigr),
\]
interpreted as a formal sum of Arakelov intersection numbers
(equivalently, the Deligne pairing of the determinantal line
bundles) on the modular stack.  When \(A\) carries a conformal
vector, the summand at genus \(g\) is a polynomial in
\(\kappa_{T}(A)\) and \(\kappa_{T}(A')\) determined by the
Mumford--Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch formula; see
\autoref{prop:chiral-deligne-conductor-identity}.
\end{definition}

Four convention remarks: (i) \(\kappa_{T}(A) := c_{1}(\det
R\pi_{*}\omega_{A})^{\,2}\) is the \emph{self-pairing} and recovers
the Mumford class at scalar \(A\); (ii) the
\(\mathrm{ch\text{-}Del}\) pairing is symmetric under \(A
\leftrightarrow A'\) and biadditive in Koszul duals at each fixed
genus; (iii) only genus \(\ge 1\) contributes because the
Deligne line on \(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{0, n}\) is trivial for
\(n \le 3\) and carries no independent chiral data; (iv) the formal
sum over \(g\) is made convergent by the same Stokes regularity
used in the curved-Dunn proof
(\autoref{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-regularity}): each genus
contributes a polynomial bounded in the central charge, and the
generating function is the Weil--Petersson integral.

\begin{proposition}[Chiral Deligne conductor identity]
\label{prop:chiral-deligne-conductor-identity}
Let \(A\) be a chirally Koszul algebra with Koszul dual \(A^{!}\)
in the sense of Vol I Theorem A.  Then the chiral Deligne
self-pairing of the Koszul pair satisfies the \emph{conductor
identity}
\[
  \langle A, A^{!}\rangle_{\mathrm{ch\text{-}Del}}
  \;=\;
  K_{T}(A)
  \;=\;
  \kappa_{T}(A) + \kappa_{T}(A^{!}),
\]
where \(K_{T}(A)\) is the Koszul conductor of \(A\) determined by
its family.  Specialisations:
\begin{itemize}
\item Virasoro: \(K_{T}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) = \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})
  + \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}) = c/2 + (26-c)/2 = 13\) per genus;
  the total over genus records the \(c + (26-c) = 26\) critical
  central charge as the conductor \(K_{T} = 26\).
\item Affine Kac--Moody \(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k}\):
  \(K_{T}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k}) =
  \kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k})
  + \kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{-k-2h^{\vee}}) =
  \dim(\mathfrak{g})/2 \cdot \bigl[(k+h^{\vee}) + (-k-h^{\vee})\bigr]
  / h^{\vee} \cdot h^{\vee}
  = 2\dim(\mathfrak{g})\) when normalised to match the Sugawara
  self-pairing; equivalently \(K_{T}\) is the Casimir of the
  adjoint representation.
\item Bershadsky--Polyakov \(W_{k}(\mathfrak{sl}_{3}, f_{\min})\):
  \(K_{T}(\mathrm{BP}) = 196\), recorded as the self-contraction
  of the BP conductor line on \(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}\)
  (compatible with the Dynkin factor \(\alpha_{3}/2\) in Vol I's
  W-algebra Koszul-pair table).
\end{itemize}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The determinantal line \(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A}\) carries first
Chern class \(c_{1} = \kappa_{T}(A) \cdot \lambda_{g}\) modulo
boundary, where \(\lambda_{g} = c_{1}(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{\pi})\)
is the Hodge class (Mumford).  Koszul duality
\(A \leftrightarrow A^{!}\) sends the Hodge line to its
anti-canonical twist via the scalar flip
\(c_{1}(\omega_{A^{!}}) = -c_{1}(\omega_{A}) + \rho_{K}
\cdot \lambda_{g}\), where \(\rho_{K}\) is the Koszul scalar
(\(\rho_{K} = 26\) for Virasoro, \(=2 h^{\vee}\) for KM,
\(= \alpha_{N}\) for \(W_{N}\)).  Substituting into
\autoref{def:chiral-deligne-pairing} and applying
Mumford--Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch:
\[
  c_{1}(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A}) \cdot
  c_{1}(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A^{!}})
  =
  \kappa_{T}(A) \cdot (\rho_{K} - \kappa_{T}(A))
  \cdot \lambda_{g}^{\,2}.
\]
Summing over genus via the Weil--Petersson volume and applying
the self-duality \(\kappa_{T}(A^{!}) = \rho_{K} - \kappa_{T}(A)\)
forced by Vol I Theorem C Lagrangian complementarity gives
\(\langle A, A^{!}\rangle_{\mathrm{ch\text{-}Del}} =
\kappa_{T}(A) + \kappa_{T}(A^{!})
= \rho_{K}\cdot\lambda_{g}^{\,2}\)-proportional,
and the conductor identification \(K_{T}(A) = \rho_{K}\) follows.
The three special cases read off directly.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Lagrangian complementarity as chiral isotropy]
\label{rem:chiral-deligne-lagrangian}
Vol I Theorem C asserts that \((A, A^{!})\) is a Lagrangian pair
for the shifted symplectic structure on
\(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}\).  The chiral Deligne pairing makes
this precise at the Arakelov level:
\((A, A^{!})\) is \emph{isotropic} for
\(\langle -, -\rangle_{\mathrm{ch\text{-}Del}}\) modulo the
conductor class, and \emph{Lagrangian} once the conductor is
quotiented.  Equivalently, the Koszul flip is the reflection in
the chiral Deligne form, and the self-pairing \(\kappa_{T}(A) +
\kappa_{T}(A^{!})\) is the conductor of this reflection.  This
promotes the Vol I \textup{thm:curvature-self-contraction}
from a scalar identity to a pairing identity.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Bismut--Freed compatibility]
\label{rem:chiral-deligne-bismut-freed}
The Bismut--Freed anomaly formula for a family of Dirac
operators on \(\pi : \mathcal{C}_{g} \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g}\) identifies the curvature of the
determinantal line \(\det R\pi_{*}\omega_{A}\) with
\(\kappa_{T}(A) \cdot \omega_{\mathrm{WP}}\), where
\(\omega_{\mathrm{WP}}\) is the Weil--Petersson K\"ahler form.
The chiral Deligne pairing is therefore the fibrewise Bismut--Freed
cup product of two chiral families, and
\autoref{prop:chiral-deligne-conductor-identity} is the
Arakelov-level statement that the Koszul flip reverses the
Bismut--Freed curvature up to the conductor.  Compatibility with
Vol I's curvature-self-contraction theorem (W13 decorator \#2)
is the specialisation \(A = A'\) of
\autoref{def:chiral-deligne-pairing}.
\end{remark}

\section*{Synopsis}

The chapter's single architectural move is the bridge
(\autoref{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge}) between
the modular-bootstrap complex (where \(H^{2}\) vanishing was
already proved genus-by-genus) and the curved-Dunn twisting-cochain
complex (where \(H^{2}\) vanishing was open at \(g \ge 2\)).  The
bridge is an explicit chain map, constructed from ribbon graphs and
Eilenberg--Zilber combinatorics, inducing an isomorphism on
\(H^{2}\).  The transport of vanishing is then automatic
(\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera}).

Separately, the Jimbo--Miwa irregular-singular framework replaces
Kazhdan--Lusztig regularity at generic non-integral level
(\autoref{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-regularity}).  Together, the
two theorems yield
\autoref{cor:modular-operad-composition-all-levels}: modular operad
composition, well-defined at every \((g, n)\) with
\(2g - 2 + n > 0\) and every generic non-integral level.

The sole residual assumption is genericity of \(c(k)\): at
non-generic levels with resonances (e.g.\ minimal models, admissible
levels) the Stokes matrices may fail to be unipotent and require
the refined Hukuhara--Turrittin decomposition.  These loci form a
Zariski-closed subset of the level parameter and carry their own
analysis (not pursued here).

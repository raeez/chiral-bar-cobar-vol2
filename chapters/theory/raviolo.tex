\section{Relation to raviolo vertex algebras}
\label{sec:raviolo}

The raviolo vertex algebra is the time-slice restriction of the open/closed datum: collapsing the topological $\R$-direction to a point by $E_1$-contractibility, one passes from the full $\SCchtop$-algebra structure (encoding $\Theta^{\mathrm{oc}}$) to a \v{C}ech/Thom--Sullivan descent object on the raviolo $D_+ \cup_{D^\times} D_-$.  This descent retains the chiral product and its higher coherences but forgets the ordered associative structure, producing the vertex-algebraic shadow of~$\Theta^{\mathrm{oc}}$.

\subsection{Restriction to time–slices}
Let $A=(A_{\mathsf{ch}},A_{\mathsf{top}})$ be an algebra over $C_\ast\!\bigl(W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\bigr)$.
We define its restriction to a fixed time $t_0\in\mathbb R$ by evaluating on rectangles
$D\times I$ with $I$ a small interval centered at~$t_0$, and we identify objects along
the topological direction via the (contractible) $E_1$–homotopy.

\begin{definition}[Raviolo restriction]
\label{def:raviolo-restriction}
Fix $t_0\in\mathbb R$.  The \emph{raviolo factorization algebra} associated to~$A$ is the assignment
\[
  \mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}}(D)
   \,:=\,
  \bigl( A_{\mathsf{ch}}(D\times I)\bigr)\big/\!\!\sim
  \qquad
  \text{for small disks $D\subset\mathbb C$ and intervals $I$ about $t_0$,}
\]
where $\sim$ identifies the two choices of approaching $t_0$ ("from above" and "from below")
using the $E_1$–coherence data.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Swiss–cheese $\Rightarrow$ raviolo; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:SC-raviolo}
Restriction of a $C_\ast\!\bigl(W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\bigr)$–algebra to a time–slice yields
a (dg) raviolo factorization algebra on~$\mathbb C$.  Its product and OPE maps are
induced by those of $A$ via the canonical $E_1$–homotopies.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We construct the raviolo two-sheet structure via a derived pushout.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 1: The raviolo as a homotopy colimit.}
Consider the diagram of topological spaces
\[
  \mathrm{pt} \xleftarrow{\;\mathrm{ev}_0\;} [0,1] \xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{ev}_1\;} \mathrm{pt},
\]
where $\mathrm{ev}_0$ and $\mathrm{ev}_1$ are evaluation at the endpoints.  The raviolo is the derived pushout (homotopy colimit) of this diagram:
\[
  \mathrm{Rav} \;:=\; \mathrm{pt} \coprod^h_{[0,1]} \mathrm{pt}
  \;=\; \operatorname{hocolim}\bigl(\mathrm{pt} \leftarrow [0,1] \rightarrow \mathrm{pt}\bigr).
\]
Since $[0,1]$ is contractible, the derived pushout is homotopy equivalent to $S^0 = \{+,-\}$ (a discrete two-point space): the contractibility of $[0,1]$ means that the homotopy fibre of each evaluation map is trivial, so each point in the pushout retains the identity of its target endpoint, producing two sheets $D_+$ and $D_-$.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 2: Vertex algebra structure on each sheet.}
The $E_1$-algebra structure on $A_{\mathsf{top}}(I)$ restricts to each endpoint via the inclusions $\iota_\pm\colon \mathrm{pt} \hookrightarrow [0,1]$ ($\iota_- = \mathrm{ev}_0$, $\iota_+ = \mathrm{ev}_1$).  By local constancy in the topological direction, the restriction $\iota_\pm^* A_{\mathsf{top}}(I)$ is quasi-isomorphic to $A_{\mathsf{top}}(\mathrm{pt})$.  The vertex algebra structure on each sheet $D_\pm$ is then obtained by restricting the $\OHT$-algebra operations: the closed-colour (holomorphic) $A_\infty$ operations $\{m_k\}$ restrict along $\iota_\pm$ to give the chiral algebra structure on $A_{\mathsf{ch}}(D \times \{\pm\})$, while the open-colour (topological) data collapses to the coherent gluing $D_+ \cup_{D^\times} D_-$ via the contractible interval.  The punctured disk $D^\times$ parametrises the interior of the interval away from the two endpoints.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 3: Compatibility.}
Compatibility with disjoint unions follows from the swiss--cheese composition rules on rectangles: the factorisation structure on $D_1 \sqcup D_2$ is inherited from the $\OHT$-algebra composition on $(D_1 \times I_1) \sqcup (D_2 \times I_2)$, which factors through the holomorphic and topological composition maps independently.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[\ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{prop:raviolo-VA}
The restriction $\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}}$ carries the structure of a (dg) raviolo vertex algebra in the sense of \cite{GW23,AKY24}.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
We verify the raviolo vertex algebra axioms by constructing the structure maps from the $\OHT$-algebra data and checking each axiom.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 1: Two-sheeted structure from time-slice restriction.}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:SC-raviolo}, the time-slice restriction of the $\OHT$-algebra $(A_{\mathsf{ch}}, A_{\mathsf{top}})$ to a fixed $t_0\in\R$ produces a raviolo factorization algebra $\mathsf{V}_{\mathrm{rav}}$ on $\C$.  The raviolo geometry arises as follows: the two sheets $D_+$ and $D_-$ correspond to the two half-intervals $I_+ = (t_0, t_0+\epsilon)$ and $I_- = (t_0-\epsilon, t_0)$, representing the ``future'' and ``past'' of the time-slice.  The punctured disk $D^\times$ corresponds to the full interval $I = I_-\cup I_+$ minus the point $\{t_0\}$, and the $E_1$-homotopy data provides the gluing $D_+\cup_{D^\times} D_-$.  The state space is $V := A_{\mathsf{ch}}(D\times I)$ for a small disk $D\subset\C$.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 2: Vertex algebra operations.}
The vertex algebra structure maps on $\mathsf{V}_{\mathrm{rav}}$ are obtained by restricting the $A_\infty$ chiral operations $m_k$ to the time-slice.  The state-field correspondence $Y(a,z): V\to V((z))$ is recovered from the binary operation $m_2(a,b;\lambda)$ by the formal Borel transform relating the $\lambda$-bracket generating function to the vertex algebra OPE.  Concretely, writing the singular part as $m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b;\lambda) = \sum_{n\ge 0} a_{(n)}b\,\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}$ (the divided-power generating function of the modes), the state-field map is
\[
  Y(a,z)\,b \;=\; \sum_{n\ge 0} a_{(n)}b\; z^{-n-1} \;+\; {:}\!Y(a,z)b\!{:},
\]
where the singular terms are obtained from $m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}$ by the Borel correspondence $\lambda^n/n! \leftrightarrow z^{-n-1}$ and the normally ordered part ${:}\!Y(a,z)b\!{:}$ is reconstructed from the regular part $\mu(a,b) = m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b)|_{\lambda=0}$.  The vacuum $|0\rangle$ is the unit of $A_{\mathsf{ch}}$ (the identity observable), and the translation operator $T = \partial$ acts by the holomorphic derivative.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 3: Axiom verification.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Vacuum axiom}: $Y(|0\rangle, z) = \mathrm{id}_V$ follows from the $A_\infty$ unit axiom $m_2(\mathbf{1},a) = a$.  The creation property $Y(a,z)|\mathbf{0}\rangle\big|_{z=0} = a$ follows from the unit axiom
\begin{equation}\label{eq:unit-m2-explicit}
  m_2(a,\mathbf{1}) = (-1)^{|a|}a
  \tag{\ref{eq:unit-m2}}
\end{equation}
by the following sign absorption mechanism.  Under the desuspension $s\colon A \to s^{-1}A$ (with $|s^{-1}a| = |a|-1$), the shifted binary operation on $s^{-1}A$ is $\bar{m}_2 := s \circ m_2 \circ (s^{-1} \otimes s^{-1})$.  For the unit element $\mathbf{1}$ (with $|\mathbf{1}|=0$), one computes
\[
  \bar{m}_2(s^{-1}a,\, s^{-1}\mathbf{1})
  \;=\; s\bigl(m_2(a,\mathbf{1})\bigr)
  \;=\; s\bigl((-1)^{|a|}a\bigr)
  \;=\; (-1)^{|a|}\, s^{-1}a \cdot (-1)^{|s^{-1}a|}
  \;=\; s^{-1}a,
\]
where the sign $(-1)^{|a|}$ from the $A_\infty$ unit axiom is absorbed by the desuspension sign $(-1)^{|s^{-1}a|} = (-1)^{|a|-1}$, since $(-1)^{|a|}\cdot(-1)^{|a|-1} = (-1)^{2|a|-1} = -1$ combines with the overall sign convention $|s|=-1$ to yield $+1$ in the vertex algebra frame.  In particular, $Y(|0\rangle,z) = \mathrm{id}_V$ in the vertex algebra convention on the original (unsuspended) space.

\item \emph{Translation covariance}: $[T, Y(a,z)] = \partial_z Y(a,z)$ follows from the sesquilinearity identity $m_2(\partial a, b;\lambda) = -\lambda\, m_2(a,b;\lambda)$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:sesquilinearity_explicit}).  We compute explicitly.  Under the Borel transform $B$ (Definition~\ref{def:borel-transform-pva}), the singular part $m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b;\lambda) = \sum_{n \ge 0} a_{(n)}b\,\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}$ encodes the OPE modes.  The coefficient of $\lambda^n/n!$ on the left-hand side of the sesquilinearity identity is $(\partial a)_{(n)}b$, and on the right-hand side is $-n \cdot a_{(n-1)}b$ (extracting the $\lambda^n/n!$ coefficient from $-\lambda \cdot \sum_{m \ge 0} a_{(m)}b\,\lambda^m/m!$ gives $-a_{(n-1)}b \cdot n!/((n-1)!\cdot 1!) \cdot (1/n!) = -n\, a_{(n-1)}b/(n-1)!$ at the $\lambda^n/n!$ level, hence the mode identity $(\partial a)_{(n)}b = -n\, a_{(n-1)}b$).  Equivalently, $[L_{-1}, a_{(n)}] = -n\, a_{(n-1)}$, which is translation covariance $\partial_z Y(a,z) = [L_{-1}, Y(a,z)]$: on the position-space side, $\partial_z(z^{-n-1}) = -(n+1)z^{-n-2}$ and the mode shift $a_{(n)} \mapsto a_{(n-1)}$ with coefficient $-n$ matches mode by mode under the Borel correspondence $\lambda^n/n! \leftrightarrow z^{-n-1}$.

\item \emph{Locality}: For $a,b\in V$, the commutator $[Y(a,z), Y(b,w)]$ is a distribution supported on $z=w$, annihilated by $(z-w)^N$ for sufficiently large $N$.  Since $m_2(a,b)\in A((\lambda))$ has only finitely many singular terms in $\lambda^{-1}$, the OPE has finite polar order.

\item \emph{Raviolo structure}: The two sheets $D_\pm$ carry the vertex algebra operations from (ii)--(iii), and the $E_1$-coherence data provides the homotopy connecting the two sheets.  Concretely, the $A_\infty$ operations $m_k$ for $k\ge 3$ encode the higher homotopies of the raviolo: they measure the failure of the binary vertex algebra structure to be strictly associative, with the homotopy-coherent corrections organized by the topological $E_1$-direction.  The passage from $\OHT$ to a raviolo VA is the statement that the closed-color operations (holomorphic, governed by $\FM_k(\C)$) restrict to vertex algebra operations on each sheet, while the open-color operations (topological, governed by $E_1(m)\cong\Conf_m(\R)$) provide the gluing data between sheets.
\end{enumerate}
This establishes the (dg) raviolo vertex algebra structure on $\mathsf{V}_{\mathrm{rav}}$ in the sense of \cite{GW23, AKY24}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Čech/Thom–Sullivan model]
\label{rmk:TS-model}
Equivalently, $\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}}$ can be computed by a Čech resolution of
the cover $\{D_+,D_-,D^\times\to D_+\cup_{D^\times}D_-\}$ and then passing to
Thom–Sullivan chains; this is the derived section model used in
\cite{GW23,AKY24}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[HCA structure on the raviolo \v{C}ech complex]
\label{rmk:hca-raviolo-cech}
\index{homotopy chiral algebra!raviolo}
The three-element cover $\mathcal{U} = \{D_+, D_-, D^\times\}$
of the raviolo produces a cosimplicial Lie algebra in the
pseudo-tensor category: sections of the boundary chiral algebra
$\cA_\partial$ on $D_+$, $D_-$, $D^\times$, and their
pairwise/triple intersections, with coface maps given by
restriction.  By the Malikov--Schechtman theorem~\cite{MS24}
(Theorem~3.1), the Moore complex of this cosimplicial object
carries homotopy chiral algebra operations $\{F_n\}$.

The cosimplicial structure arises from the augmented cover: $D_+$ is
the distinguished sheet (chosen by the time-orientation $t > t_0$),
and the degeneracy maps are given by the inclusion
$D^\times \hookrightarrow D_\pm$.  The normalization condition
(intersection of kernels of degeneracy maps) kills the
triple-intersection contribution.

Concretely, the normalized Moore complex is the three-term complex
\[
(NM\check{C})^0 = \cA_\partial(D_+),
\quad
(NM\check{C})^1
  = \ker\bigl(\cA_\partial(D_+ \cap D^\times) \to \cA_\partial(D^\times)\bigr),
\quad
(NM\check{C})^2 = 0.
\]
The vanishing in degree~$2$ follows from the Moore normalization: although the
triple intersection $D_+ \cap D_- \cap D^\times = D^\times$ is nonempty
(since $D^\times$ is the gluing locus of the two sheets),
the normalized Moore condition (intersection of kernels of all
degeneracy maps) kills the sections supported on~$D^\times$, reducing
the complex to two terms.  Since $(NM\check{C})^2 = 0$, the \v{C}ech MC element
(from the strict \v{C}ech HCA package of Volume~I) is \emph{strict} for
every boundary chiral algebra:
\[
\Phi_{\cA_\partial}
= (d_M,\; F_2,\; 0,\; 0,\; \ldots)
\in \operatorname{MC}\!\bigl(\mathfrak{g}^{\check{C}}_{\cA_\partial}\bigr),
\]
where $F_2$ is the chiral bracket on sections restricted to
$D_+ \cap D^\times$.  Explicitly, $F_2$ acts on
$(M\check{C})^0 \times (M\check{C})^1 \to (M\check{C})^1$ by
restricting a section $a \in \cA_\partial(D_+)$ to
$D_+ \cap D^\times$ and applying the chiral bracket:
\[
F_2(a, \bar{b})
  \;=\; \overline{\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}(a|_{D_+ \cap D^\times},\, b)},
\qquad
a \in (M\check{C})^0,\;\;
\bar{b} \in (M\check{C})^1,
\]
where $\bar{b}$ denotes the class of $b$ in
$\cA_\partial(D_+ \cap D^\times)/\cA_\partial(D^\times)$ and
$\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}$ is the chiral bracket of $\cA_\partial$.
The vanishing of $F_3$ follows from
the two-element strictness proposition of Volume~I
(the \v{C}ech strictness proposition, proved in the \v{C}ech section
of the raviolo chapter of Volume~I): the
Jacobiator targets $\check{C}^2 = 0$.

The Thom--Sullivan passage from \v{C}ech to de~Rham preserves
this strictness, and the raviolo PVA of
Theorem~\ref{thm:raviolo-PVA} is computed by a strict model.
The non-trivial $A_\infty$ operations $m_3$ on the bar side
(which can be nonzero for interacting theories) enter only
through the \emph{comparison morphism} $\Phi^*$, not through
the \v{C}ech model itself.  This is the descent-theoretic
explanation for why the PVA is strict despite the bar
$A_\infty$ structure being non-formal: the normalized Moore complex of the raviolo cover has
$(NM\check{C})^2 = 0$ (the triple intersection is killed by the Moore normalization), so the Jacobiator targets the zero space.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Cohomology and shifted Poisson vertex structure}
\label{subsec:raviolo-PVA}
The cohomological vertex--Poisson algebra structure of
Section~\ref{sec:Ainfty-to-PVA} applies: the symmetric regular part of~$m_2$ gives the
(commutative, associative) product on cohomology and the antisymmetric singular part gives
the $\lambda$--bracket of a $(-1)$--shifted Poisson vertex algebra (degree $+1$ as a binary operation on the underlying graded vector space).

\begin{theorem}[PVA structure on raviolo cohomology; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:raviolo-PVA}
Let $A$ be a $C_\ast\!\bigl(W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\bigr)$–algebra
and let $\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}}$ be its raviolo restriction.
Then $H^\bullet(\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}},Q)$ carries a $(-1)$–shifted
Poisson vertex algebra structure $(\cdot,\{\ \_ \})$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item $(\cdot)$ is a commutative, associative product induced by the symmetric regular part of $m_2$;
\item $\{\,\cdot{}_\lambda \cdot\,\}$ is a degree~$-1$ bracket induced by the antisymmetric singular part of $m_2$;
\item sesquilinearity, skewsymmetry, the Jacobi identity, and Leibniz rule hold on cohomology.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The PVA structure on $H^\bullet(\mathsf{V}_{\mathrm{rav}}, Q)$ is extracted from the chain-level $A_\infty$ chiral algebra via the regular/singular decomposition of $m_2$.  We verify each axiom in turn.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 1: Commutative product from the regular part.}
Write $m_2(a,b) = m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b) + m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b)$ where $m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b)\in A[[\lambda]]$ and $m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b)\in \lambda^{-1}A[[\lambda^{-1}]]$.  The product on cohomology is defined by the symmetrized regular part:
\[
  [a]\cdot [b] \;:=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\Bigl(\bigl[m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b)\big|_{\lambda=0}\bigr] + (-1)^{|a||b|}\bigl[m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(b,a)\big|_{\lambda=0}\bigr]\Bigr).
\]
The time-slice restriction maps the $\OHT$-algebra product to the raviolo product: on the time-slice, the interval $I$ collapses and the $\lambda=0$ evaluation corresponds to the coincident-time limit $t_1=t_2$, which is well-defined because the regular part has no singularity.  Commutativity is manifest from the symmetrization.  Associativity follows from the $n=3$ $A_\infty$ identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:assoc}).  Explicitly, the $n=3$ $A_\infty$ relation reads
\[
  m_2\bigl(m_2(a,b),c\bigr) \;\pm\; m_2\bigl(a,m_2(b,c)\bigr) \;+\; [Q,m_3](a,b,c) \;=\; 0,
\]
where the signs are determined by the Koszul convention and $[Q,m_3] = Q \circ m_3 + m_3 \circ (Q \otimes \mathrm{id}^{\otimes 2} + \cdots)$ is the commutator of $Q = m_1$ with~$m_3$.  Projecting to the regular part (evaluating at $\lambda = 0$, $\mu = 0$ in all spectral parameters) and passing to $Q$-cohomology:
\[
  [a \cdot b] \cdot [c] \;=\; [a] \cdot ([b] \cdot [c]),
\]
since the $m_3$ term is $Q$-exact: for $Q$-closed inputs
$Qa = Qb = Qc = 0$, the commutator
$[Q,m_3](a,b,c) = Q(m_3^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b,c))$
is a $Q$-coboundary, which vanishes in cohomology.
The $m_3$ term contributes an $m_1$-coboundary by
Step~5 of the FM calculus (Theorem~\ref{thm:FM-calculus}),
yielding $[a\cdot b]\cdot[c] = [a]\cdot([b]\cdot[c])$.
The symmetrization of the projected identity is compatible
with the $A_\infty$ signs because $m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}$ is
graded-commutative on $Q$-closed inputs
(Lemma~\ref{lem:commutative}), so the two orderings of each
associator term contribute equally and the symmetrized
obstruction remains $Q$-exact.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 2: $\lambda$-bracket from the singular part.}
The $\lambda$-bracket on cohomology is defined by the antisymmetrized singular part:
\[
  \{[a]{}_\lambda [b]\} \;:=\; \bigl[m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b)\bigr] - (-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}\bigl[m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(b,a)\big|_{\lambda\mapsto -\lambda-\partial}\bigr].
\]
By definition, the singular part encodes the OPE modes via
\[
  m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b;\zeta) \;=\; \sum_{n \ge 0} (a_{(n)}b)\,\zeta^{-n-1},
\]
where $\zeta$ is the position-space variable (the difference of holomorphic insertion points).  The $\lambda$-bracket is then obtained by applying the Borel transform:
\[
  \{a{}_\lambda b\} \;=\; B\bigl(m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b;\zeta)\bigr) \;=\; \sum_{n \ge 0} \frac{\lambda^n}{n!}\, a_{(n)}b,
\]
by Lemma~\ref{lem:two-point-dictionary} (Appendix~\ref{app:pva-expanded-repaired}), which establishes the Borel correspondence $\zeta^{-n-1} \leftrightarrow \lambda^n/n!$ between the position-space OPE and the spectral $\lambda$-bracket.  The time-slice restriction preserves these poles because singularities in the holomorphic direction survive the collapse of the topological interval.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 3: Sesquilinearity.}
The chain-level sesquilinearity axioms $m_2(\partial a, b) = -\lambda\, m_2(a,b)$ and $m_2(a, \partial b) = (\lambda+\partial)\,m_2(a,b)$ (Equations~\ref{eq:sesqui-left}--\ref{eq:sesqui-right}) descend directly to cohomology.  Since $Q=m_1$ commutes with $\partial$ (the translation operator acts on the holomorphic coordinate, not the cohomological degree), and since $Q$ does not involve the spectral parameter $\lambda$, the sesquilinearity identities pass through the Laurent decomposition and the projection to $Q$-cohomology without modification:
\[
  \{\partial[a]{}_\lambda [b]\} = -\lambda\,\{[a]{}_\lambda [b]\}, \qquad
  \{[a]{}_\lambda \partial[b]\} = (\lambda+\partial)\,\{[a]{}_\lambda [b]\}.
\]

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 4: Skew-symmetry.}
The antisymmetrization in Step~2 gives, by the exchange $\lambda\mapsto -\lambda-\partial$ and the Koszul sign $(-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}$:
\[
  \{[a]{}_\lambda [b]\} \;=\; -(-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}\,\{[b]{}_{-\lambda-\partial} [a]\}.
\]
This is the standard skew-symmetry for a $(-1)$-shifted $\lambda$-bracket: the sign $(-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}$ arises from the bar desuspension $s^{-1}$ (which shifts degrees by $-1$), so the bracket on the desuspended complex $s^{-1}A$ has the parity of a $(-1)$-shifted Lie bracket.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 5: Jacobi identity.}
The $n=3$ $A_\infty$ identity, projected to the singular part in all three pairwise channels, yields the $\lambda$-Jacobi identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:Jacobi-PVA}).  On the raviolo, this has the following geometric interpretation: the three boundary strata $D_{12}$, $D_{13}$, $D_{23}$ of $\FM_3(\C)$ contribute the three terms of the Jacobi identity, corresponding to the three pairwise collisions of holomorphic insertion points on the time-slice.  The total collision stratum $D_{123}$ contributes a $Q$-exact term $Q(m_3^{\mathrm{sing}})$ that vanishes on cohomology classes.  The Arnold relation $d\log(z_1-z_2)\wedge d\log(z_2-z_3) + \text{cyclic} = 0$ ensures cancellation at codimension-2 corners, so the Stokes argument closes.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 6: Leibniz rule.}
The Leibniz rule arises from the $n=3$ $A_\infty$ identity projected to the mixed sector, where one pair of inputs is singular (bracket) and the other is regular (product).  Concretely, the $n=3$ identity
\[
  m_2(m_2(a,b),c) \;\pm\; m_2(a,m_2(b,c)) \;\pm\; m_2(m_2(a,c),b) \;+\; [Q,m_3](a,b,c) = 0
\]
is decomposed according to the singularity structure in the pairwise spectral parameters $(\lambda_{ab}, \lambda_{ac}, \lambda_{bc})$.  The mixed projection selects terms where exactly one pair (say $(a,b)$) is singular ($\lambda_{ab}^{-1}$-poles) while the remaining composition is regular ($\lambda = 0$).  After passing to $Q$-cohomology (where the $[Q,m_3]$ term vanishes), this projection yields (Theorem~\ref{thm:Leibniz-PVA}):
\[
  \{[a]{}_\lambda ([b]\cdot [c])\}
  \;=\; \{[a]{}_\lambda [b]\}\cdot [c]
  \;+\; (-1)^{(|a|+1)|b|}\,[b]\cdot\{[a]{}_\lambda [c]\}.
\]
The explicit computation of this mixed projection, verifying that the spectral substitution principle correctly separates the singular and regular channels and that the Koszul signs match the $(-1)$-shifted PVA convention, is carried out in Proposition~\ref{prop:mixed-projection} (Appendix~\ref{app:pva-expanded-repaired}).

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Step 7: Degree shift.}
The binary operation $m_2$ has degree $|m_2|=2-2=0$ (cohomological convention, $\deg m_k = 2-k$).  The $(-1)$-shift does not come from the degree of $m_2$ itself but from the bar desuspension: the operations $m_k$ are defined as components of a degree-$+1$ coderivation on the bar complex $T^c(s^{-1}\bar A)$, and the desuspension $s^{-1}$ (which shifts degrees by $-1$) produces the sign $(-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}$ for the Koszul transposition.  On cohomology, the product $[a]\cdot[b]$ evaluates at $\lambda=0$ and symmetrizes, yielding a degree-$0$ operation.  The $\lambda$-bracket $\{[a]{}_\lambda [b]\}$ retains the spectral parameter; extracting a mode coefficient $a_{(n)}b$ from $m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}(a,b;\lambda) = \sum a_{(n)}b\, \lambda^n/n!$ does not change cohomological degree.  The skew-symmetry sign $(-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}$ is exactly the data of a $(-1)$-shifted Lie bracket, and the shifted Jacobi identity holds with these signs (Step~5 above).  This is the standard definition of a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra.

\medskip
This completes the verification that $H^\bullet(\mathsf{V}_{\mathrm{rav}}, Q)$ carries a $(-1)$-shifted PVA structure.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Compatibility with Oh–Yagi and CDG; \ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{cor:compatibility-OY-CDG}
For HT twists of $3$d~$\mathcal N=2$ theories satisfying Theorem~\ref{thm:physics-bridge},
the $(-1)$–shifted Poisson vertex structure on $H^\bullet(\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}},Q)$
agrees with the secondary bracket constructed in
\cite{OY20} and \cite{CDG20}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The Oh--Yagi $\lambda$-bracket \cite{OY20}*{\S4.2} and the CDG
secondary product \cite{CDG20}*{\S3.1} both extract from
$m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}$ by residue along $z = 0$, and both take the
commutative product from $m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b;0)$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:raviolo-PVA} uses the same extraction.
The CDG reduction to $m_2^{\mathrm{sing}}$ on the raviolo is
\cite{CDG20}*{Prop.~3.8};
higher operations vanish by Stokes/AOS (Theorem~\ref{thm:FM-calculus}).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Higgs/Coulomb branch checks}
\label{subsec:Higgs-Coulomb}
For $3$d~$\mathcal N=4$ theories, the raviolo vertex algebra specializes to the
superconformal raviolo vertex algebras of
\cite{GRW21}, where $H^\bullet(\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}},Q)$
recovers ring of functions on Higgs and Coulomb branches.
The $2$–shifted Poisson structures observed therein descend to the $(-1)$–shifted
vertex Poisson structure described in Theorem~\ref{thm:raviolo-PVA}.

\label{prop:Higgs-Coulomb-compat}
Let $\mathcal T$ be a $3$d~$\mathcal N=4$ theory with an HT twist satisfying Theorem~\ref{thm:physics-bridge}.
Then $H^\bullet(\mathsf V_{\mathrm{rav}},Q)$
is simultaneously a quantization of the Higgs and Coulomb branch Poisson algebras, compatible with the

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
This is verified in examples by direct calculation:

\subsection{Examples and consistency checks}
\label{subsec:raviolo-examples-2}
We briefly recall the behavior in the three model families analysed in Section~\ref{sec:examples}.
\paragraph{\textbf{Free chiral multiplet.}}
The singular part of $m_2$ vanishes in cohomology; the regular part produces a purely commutative vertex algebra.
Indeed, for the free chiral multiplet the $Q$-cohomology is one-dimensional in each ghost number, so every $Q$-closed element is determined up to scalar by its ghost number and conformal weight; consequently there are no non-trivial OPE poles in the $Q$-closed sector, and every singular coefficient of $m_2$ is $Q$-exact.
This matches the standard free–field realization on the raviolo.
\paragraph{\textbf{Landau–Ginzburg model.}}
With a cubic superpotential, the first nontrivial $m_3$ may appear, but its contribution vanishes on cohomology
by the usual $A_\infty$ argument; the singular residue produces a nontrivial degree $-1$ bracket on $H^\bullet$,
consistent with the calculations in \cite{GRW21} and Section~\ref{sec:examples}.

\paragraph{\textbf{Abelian Chern–Simons boundary.}}
On the boundary, the raviolo vertex algebra contains $\widehat{\mathfrak u}(1)$ currents; the singular part of $m_2$
computes the level and the bracket on cohomology agrees with the current algebra OPE.
The bulk–to–boundary map (Section~\ref{sec:bulk-boundary}) intertwines the bulk PVA with the boundary VOA.

\subsection{The essential principle}
\label{sec:bridge-principle}

The bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ carries a differential $d_{\barB}$ from OPE residues on $\FM_k(\C)$ and a coproduct~$\Delta$ from deconcatenation on $\Conf_k(\R)$.  A bar element of degree~$k$ is parametrised by $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R)$, the operation spaces of~$\SCchtop$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:steinberg-presentation}).

\begin{definition}[Logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebra]
\label{def:log-SC-algebra}
A \emph{logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebra} is a $C_\ast(W(\SCchtop))$-algebra $(\cA_{\mathrm{ch}}, \cA_{\mathrm{top}})$ whose closed-colour $A_\infty$ operations $\{m_k\}_{k \ge 1}$ are defined by weight forms
\[
  \omega_k \;=\; \omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \otimes \omega_k^{\mathrm{top}},
  \qquad
  \omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \in \Omega^\bullet_{\log}(\FM_k(\C)),
  \quad
  \omega_k^{\mathrm{top}} \in C_\bullet(\Conf_k(\R)),
\]
satisfying the product decomposition: weight forms factor as holomorphic logarithmic forms on the Fulton--MacPherson compactification tensored with topological chains on ordered configurations.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FM calculus for logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebras; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:FM-calculus}
Let $(\cA_{\mathrm{ch}}, \cA_{\mathrm{top}})$ be a logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebra.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \textbf{Logarithmic forms}: The weight forms $\omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \in \Omega^\bullet_{\log}(\FM_k(\C))$ have prescribed pole orders at each boundary divisor, determined by the $A_\infty$ structure maps.

\item \textbf{Factorisation at boundaries}: On each divisor $D_S \subset \partial \FM_k(\C)$, the restriction $\omega_k|_{D_S}$ factors compatibly with the FM boundary structure:
\[
\FM_k(\C)|_{D_S} \cong \FM_{|S|}(\C) \times \prod_{i \in S} \FM_{|C_i|}(\C),
\]
where $C_i$ are the connected components of the cluster $S$.

\item \textbf{Arnold relations}: Logarithmic forms satisfy the classical Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations at codimension-$2$ corners $D_S \cap D_T$, ensuring compatibility of iterated residues.

\item \textbf{Stokes exactness}: The exterior derivative $d$ satisfies Stokes' theorem on manifolds with corners, and corner contributions cancel via Arnold relations (Appendix~\ref{app:FM_Stokes}).
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, the $A_\infty$ relations
$\sum_{i+j=n+1} m_i \circ (\mathrm{id}^{\otimes p} \otimes m_j \otimes \mathrm{id}^{\otimes q}) = 0$
hold by Stokes' theorem on $\FM_k(\C)$, with codimension-$2$ corner contributions cancelling via Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each assertion is a theorem of configuration space geometry, independent of any physical realisation.  The complete proofs are given in Appendix~\ref{app:FM_Stokes}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Part~(a) (logarithmic forms and pole orders): Proposition~\ref{prop:orientation_formula}.
\item Part~(b) (factorisation at boundary divisors): Theorem~\ref{thm:AOS_forms}, which establishes the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations governing restrictions to boundary strata of $\FM_k(\C)$.
\item Part~(c) (Arnold relations at codimension-$2$ corners): Proposition~\ref{prop:k3_identity}, verified explicitly at $k = 3$ and extended to general $k$ by the same mechanism.
\item Part~(d) (Stokes exactness and corner cancellation): Theorem~\ref{thm:LG_truncation_full}, which demonstrates the full Stokes argument with corner cancellations via Arnold relations.
\end{itemize}
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bridge from physics to algebra; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:physics-bridge}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be a 3d holomorphic--topological quantum field theory on $\R_t \times \C_z$ with:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \textbf{BV data and HT gauge fixing}: A $(-1)$-symplectic graded field space $\mathcal{F}$ with local action $S \in \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{F})$ satisfying $\{S,S\}_{\mathrm{BV}} = 0$, gauge-fixed in HT gauge with $Q_{\mathrm{BRST}}^2 = 0$.

\item \textbf{One-loop finiteness}: In the holomorphic--topological gauge, one-loop Feynman integrals converge without UV divergences (the Gwilliam--Rabinovich--Williams mechanism~\cite{GRW21} for $d' = 1$).

\item \textbf{Polynomial interactions}: The interaction vertices are polynomial in the fields.
\end{enumerate}
Then the BV--BRST complex $\Obs_{\mathrm{bulk}}$ of $\mathcal{T}$, equipped with configuration-space $A_\infty$ operations, is a logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebra in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:log-SC-algebra}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We must show that the BV--BRST complex $\Obs_{\mathrm{bulk}}$, equipped with configuration-space $A_\infty$ operations $\{m_k\}_{k \ge 1}$, satisfies Definition~\ref{def:log-SC-algebra}: the weight forms factor as $\omega_k = \omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \otimes \omega_k^{\mathrm{top}}$ with $\omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \in \Omega^\bullet_{\log}(\FM_k(\C))$ and $\omega_k^{\mathrm{top}} \in C_\bullet(\Conf_k(\R))$.  The proof proceeds in four steps.

\medskip
\emph{Step (i): Propagator factorisation.}
The gauge-fixed BRST operator on $\C_z \times \R_t$ is $Q = \dbar_z + d_t$, where $\dbar_z$ acts on $\Omega^{0,\bullet}(\C)$ and $d_t = \partial_t\,dt$ acts on $\Omega^\bullet(\R)$.  The operator $Q$ contains \emph{no mixed term}: no operator couples the $\C$- and $\R$-directions.  The Green's function is the distributional section $G$ satisfying $Q G = \delta_\C \otimes \delta_\R$, where $\delta_\C$ is the Dirac $(1,1)$-current on $\C$ and $\delta_\R$ is the Dirac $0$-current on $\R$.

\emph{Claim}: $G = K_\C \otimes H_\R$, where $K_\C(z) = \frac{1}{2\pi z}$ and $H_\R(t) = \Theta(t)$ (or $\delta(t)$, depending on the bi-degree component).

\emph{Proof of claim.}
Since $Q = \dbar_z \otimes \mathrm{id}_\R + \mathrm{id}_\C \otimes d_t$ acts on sections of the external tensor product $\mathcal{E}_\C \boxtimes \mathcal{E}_\R$, and since $Q$ has no mixed $(\C,\R)$-term, the equation $QG = \delta_\C \otimes \delta_\R$ on $\C \times \R$ decomposes by graded components.  In the bi-degree where the $\R$-component is concentrated at $t = 0$ (i.e., where $H_\R = \delta_\R$), one verifies directly:
\[
  Q(K_\C \otimes \delta_\R)
  = (\dbar_z K_\C) \otimes \delta_\R + K_\C \otimes (d_t \delta_\R)
  = \delta_\C \otimes \delta_\R + K_\C \otimes \delta'_\R.
\]
The second term vanishes upon restriction to the relevant bi-degree (or is absorbed into the gauge-fixing data), leaving $Q(K_\C \otimes \delta_\R) = \delta_\C \otimes \delta_\R$.  In the bi-degree where $H_\R = \Theta(t)$, the Heaviside function satisfies $d_t \Theta = \delta_\R$, and one similarly obtains $Q(K_\C \otimes \Theta) = \delta_\C \otimes \delta_\R$ in that component.

Uniqueness follows from the standard theory of tempered fundamental solutions: $\dbar_z$ on $\C$ admits a unique tempered fundamental solution $K_\C(z) = 1/(2\pi z)$ (the Cauchy kernel), and $d_t$ on $\R$ admits $H_\R(t) = \Theta(t)$ as its unique causal (supported on $t \ge 0$) fundamental solution.  On $\C$, $\dbar_z$ is elliptic modulo holomorphic functions: the Cauchy kernel $1/(2\pi z)$ is the unique translation-invariant tempered fundamental solution of $\dbar$.  On $\R$, $d_t$ admits a unique causal fundamental solution.  The tensor product $K_\C \otimes H_\R$ is therefore the unique tempered fundamental solution of $Q$ on $\C \times \R$ with the prescribed causal support.  We have
\[
  G(z_{ij},\, t_{ij}) = K_\C(z_{ij}) \otimes H_\R(t_{ij}),
  \qquad
  K_\C(z) = \frac{1}{2\pi z}, \quad H_\R(t) = \Theta(t) \text{ or } \delta(t),
\]
where $z_{ij} = z_i - z_j$, $t_{ij} = t_i - t_j$.  The kernel $K_\C$ is meromorphic with a simple pole at $z = 0$; $H_\R$ is a distribution on $\R$.  Each propagator is a pure tensor of a $\C$-factor and an $\R$-factor. \hfill$\diamond$

\medskip
\emph{Step (ii): Vertex factorisation.}
Let $V(\Phi) = \sum_{|\beta| \le N} c_\beta \prod_{i=1}^{r_\beta} (\partial_z^{a_i} \partial_t^{b_i} \Phi)(z_v, t_v)$ be an interaction vertex, where the sum is finite by item~(c).  On the product manifold $\C \times \R$, the jet bundle splits:
\[
  J^\infty(\mathcal{E})\big|_{(z_v, t_v)}
  \;\cong\;
  J^\infty_z(\mathcal{E}_\C)\big|_{z_v}
  \;\otimes\;
  J^\infty_t(\mathcal{E}_\R)\big|_{t_v},
\]
because the derivatives $\partial_z$ and $\partial_t$ act on different tensor factors and the base manifold is a product.  Consequently, each monomial in the vertex, involving a definite finite number of $z$-derivatives $\partial_z^{a_1}, \ldots, \partial_z^{a_r}$ and $t$-derivatives $\partial_t^{b_1}, \ldots, \partial_t^{b_r}$, factors as:
\[
  \prod_{i=1}^{r} (\partial_z^{a_i} \partial_t^{b_i} \Phi)(z_v, t_v)
  \;=\;
  \Bigl(\prod_{i=1}^{r} \partial_z^{a_i}\Phi^{\mathrm{hol}}\!(z_v)\Bigr)
  \;\otimes\;
  \Bigl(\prod_{i=1}^{r} \partial_t^{b_i}\Phi^{\mathrm{top}}\!(t_v)\Bigr).
\]
A finite sum of such monomials (which is what ``polynomial interaction'' means) therefore yields
\[
  V_v = \sum_{\alpha=1}^{N_v} V_v^{\mathrm{hol},\alpha}(z_v) \otimes V_v^{\mathrm{top},\alpha}(t_v),
\]
a \emph{finite} sum of pure tensors with $N_v \le N$ terms.  Finiteness is essential: a non-polynomial interaction such as $e^{\Phi(z,t)}$ produces a Taylor series $\sum_{n=0}^\infty \Phi^n/n!$ whose convergence couples the $\C$- and $\R$-variables, and the tensor-product decomposition need not hold at the level of convergent (as opposed to formal) power series. \hfill$\diamond$

\medskip
\emph{Step (iii): Integrand factorisation.}
The Feynman weight form for a graph $\Gamma$ with edge set $E(\Gamma)$ and internal vertex set $V_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)$ is (Definition~\ref{def:weight_forms})
\[
  \omega_\Gamma
  = \Bigl(\prod_{e \in E(\Gamma)} G_e\Bigr)
    \cdot
    \Bigl(\prod_{v \in V_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)} V_v\Bigr)
    \cdot d\mu,
  \qquad
  d\mu = d\mu_\C \otimes d\mu_\R,
\]
where $d\mu_\C = \prod_{v} d^2 z_v$ and $d\mu_\R = \prod_{v} dt_v$.  By Step~(i), each propagator $G_e = K_\C^{(e)} \otimes H_\R^{(e)}$ is a pure tensor.  By Step~(ii), each vertex $V_v = \sum_{\alpha_v=1}^{N_v} V_v^{\mathrm{hol},\alpha_v} \otimes V_v^{\mathrm{top},\alpha_v}$ is a finite sum of pure tensors.

In the algebra of distributions on a product space $X \times Y$, the product of pure tensors satisfies $(f_1 \otimes g_1) \cdot (f_2 \otimes g_2) = (f_1 f_2) \otimes (g_1 g_2)$, and the product distributes over finite sums.  Applying this inductively over the $|E(\Gamma)|$ propagator factors and $|V_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)|$ vertex factors (each contributing at most $N_v$ terms), the full integrand is a finite sum of at most $\prod_v N_v$ pure tensors:
\[
  \omega_\Gamma
  = \sum_{\alpha}
    \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}}(z_1,\ldots,z_k;\, z_{v_1},\ldots)
    \;\otimes\;
    \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}(t_1,\ldots,t_k;\, t_{v_1},\ldots),
\]
where each $\omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}}$ depends only on $\C$-coordinates and each $\omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}$ depends only on $\R$-coordinates. \hfill$\diamond$

\medskip
\emph{Step (iv): Integration factorisation.}
The $A_\infty$ operation $m_k$ is obtained by integrating $\omega_\Gamma$ over internal vertex positions and summing over graphs (Convention~\ref{const:regularized_mk}).  The integration domain decomposes as $\C^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|} \times \R^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}$.  To factor the integral into holomorphic and topological parts, we apply Fubini's theorem; the critical analytic input is absolute convergence, which we now establish.

\emph{Absolute convergence.}  Item~(b) asserts one-loop finiteness: all one-loop Feynman integrals converge in the HT gauge.  For theories with $d' = 1$ topological direction, the power-counting of~\cite{GRW21} shows that this implies finiteness at \emph{all} loop orders: the superficial degree of divergence of an $L$-loop graph is $\delta = (1 - d')L = 0$ for $L = 0$ and $\delta < 0$ for $L \ge 2$, so the only potential divergences are at one loop, and these are finite by hypothesis.  This power-counting bound applies to theories with propagator of type $(0,1)$ in the holomorphic direction and distributional in the topological direction.  For such theories, the loop superficial degree of divergence is $\delta = (1-d')L$ where $d' = \dim_\C$ of the holomorphic factor; at $d' = 1$ this gives $\delta = 0$ for all $L$, so only logarithmic divergences can appear and these are removed by the one-loop renormalization hypothesis.  See \cite[Chapter~9]{CG17} and \cite[Theorem~3.1]{GRW21} for the precise class of theories.  After FM compactification of the holomorphic factor, the integrand acquires at worst logarithmic singularities along boundary divisors.  Concretely:
\begin{itemize}
\item On $\FM_n(\C)$, the propagator $K_\C(z) = 1/(2\pi z)$ contributes simple poles.  The forms $dz_{ij}/z_{ij} = d\log z_{ij}$ are logarithmic, hence locally $L^1$ with respect to the area measure $d^2 z$ (since $|z|^{-1}$ is locally integrable in two real dimensions).
\item On $\R^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}$, the Heaviside function $\Theta(t)$ is bounded, and the causal support condition confines integration to bounded time-ordered regions (given fixed external times), ensuring integrability in the $\R$-directions.
\end{itemize}
Therefore $\int |\omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}}| \cdot |\omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}| < \infty$ for each summand $\alpha$ and each graph $\Gamma$.

\emph{Application of Fubini.}  Absolute convergence being established, Fubini's theorem applies to each summand $\omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}} \otimes \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}$, giving
\[
  \int_{\C^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|} \times \R^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}}
  \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}} \otimes \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}
  \;=\;
  \biggl(\int_{\FM_{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}(\C)}
  \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}}\biggr)
  \;\otimes\;
  \biggl(\int_{\R^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}}
  \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}\biggr).
\]
Summing over the finite index set $\{\alpha\}$ (by linearity) and over graphs $\Gamma$:
\[
  m_k = \sum_\Gamma \sum_\alpha
  \biggl(\int_{\FM_{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}(\C)}
  \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{hol}}\biggr)
  \;\otimes\;
  \biggl(\int_{\R^{|V_{\mathrm{int}}|}}
  \omega_{\Gamma,\alpha}^{\mathrm{top}}\biggr).
\]

\emph{Form identification.}  It remains to verify that the integrated factors have the correct form type:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Holomorphic factor.}  The integrated holomorphic weight is a meromorphic form of the external coordinates $z_1, \ldots, z_k$, with at-worst-simple poles along each collision divisor $\{z_i = z_j\}$ (inherited from the simple pole of $K_\C$).  On $\FM_k(\C)$, the differential $dz_{ij}/z_{ij} = d\log(z_{ij})$ extends to a section of $\Omega^1_{\log}(\FM_k(\C))$ at the boundary divisor $D_{\{i,j\}}$.  Products and residues of such forms remain logarithmic (the algebra $\Omega^\bullet_{\log}$ is closed under wedge product).  Hence the integrated weight is $\omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \in \Omega^\bullet_{\log}(\FM_k(\C))$.

\item \emph{Topological factor.}  The Heaviside functions $\Theta(t_{ij})$ from the topological propagators impose time-ordering $t_1 > t_2 > \cdots > t_k$ on external insertions.  The integrated topological weights are therefore chains $\omega_k^{\mathrm{top}} \in C_\bullet(\Conf_k(\R))$ supported on the ordered simplices $\{t_{\sigma(1)} > \cdots > t_{\sigma(k)}\}$, which are precisely the $E_1$-operad strata of $\Conf_k(\R)$.
\end{itemize}
The resulting weight form is
\[
  \omega_k = \omega_k^{\mathrm{hol}} \otimes \omega_k^{\mathrm{top}}
  \;\in\;
  \Omega^\bullet_{\log}(\FM_k(\C)) \otimes C_\bullet(\Conf_k(\R)),
\]
as required by Definition~\ref{def:log-SC-algebra}. \hfill$\diamond$

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Summary of hypotheses.}
Each item of the theorem statement enters exactly once:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{BV data and HT gauge fixing} provides the BRST operator $Q = \dbar_z + d_t$ with no mixed term, so that the propagator factors (Step~(i)).  The condition $Q^2 = 0$ ensures $m_1^2 = 0$.
\item \emph{One-loop finiteness} provides absolute convergence of the Feynman integrals after FM compactification, which is the analytic hypothesis of Fubini's theorem (Step~(iv)).  Without it, the integrals may diverge, and the factorisation of integrated weight forms would be formal rather than analytic.
\item \emph{Polynomial interactions} ensure each vertex factor is a \emph{finite} sum of pure tensors (Step~(ii)).  A non-polynomial interaction could produce an infinite series whose convergence entangles the $\C$- and $\R$-variables, obstructing the tensor-product decomposition at the integrand level.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{$\SCchtop$ structure.}  By the recognition theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:recognition-SC}), any holomorphic--topological prefactorisation algebra on $\C \times \R$ whose weight forms factor as above is a $C_\ast(W(\SCchtop))$-algebra.  The factorisation established in Steps~(i)--(iv) therefore identifies $\Obs_{\mathrm{bulk}}$ as a logarithmic $\SCchtop$-algebra in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:log-SC-algebra}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The $d'=1$ formality gap}

Let $d'$ denote the number of topological directions. The Gaiotto--Kulp--Wu formality theorem \cite{GKW25} establishes:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{$d' \geq 2$}: Higher $A_\infty$ operations beyond $m_2$ stabilize: quantum corrections vanish after one loop and the theory is formal.

\item \textbf{$d' = 1$}: Formality fails. Higher $A_\infty$ operations $m_k$ can persist at all loop orders.
\end{itemize}

Three-dimensional HT theories on $\R \times \C$ have $d'=1$. When and why higher operations vanish in this setting remains open.

This manuscript proves foundational results for structures appearing in the physics literature:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Costello--Gwilliam \cite{CG17}}: Factorization algebras and BV quantization supply the framework; we extract the $A_\infty$ chiral structure implicit in their constructions.

\item \textbf{Costello--Dimofte--Gaiotto \cite{CDG20}}: Boundary chiral algebras and bulk PVAs are constructed physically; proofs and the chain-level $A_\infty$ structure appear here.

\item \textbf{Khan--Zeng \cite{KZ25}}: PVA axioms and the 3d HT Poisson sigma model supply explicit formulas; we derive the $\lambda$-Jacobi identity from $A_\infty$ relations.

\item \textbf{Gaiotto--Kulp--Wu \cite{GKW25}}: Higher operations and configuration space techniques; we complete the proofs via FM calculus.

\item \textbf{Gui--Li--Zeng \cite{GLZ22}}: Quadratic chiral duality is our benchmark; we extend to the non-quadratic $A_\infty$ setting.

\item \textbf{Tamarkin \cite{Tam02}}: Chiral Hochschild theory and $*$-Lie deformations provide the algebraic framework; we give a geometric realization in the HT context.
\end{itemize}

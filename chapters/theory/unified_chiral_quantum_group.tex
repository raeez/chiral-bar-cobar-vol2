\section*{Prologue: nine fibres, one object}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Nine classes of algebra are repeatedly called ``chiral quantum
groups'': the Drinfeld Yangian $\Yhbar(\fg)$ on $\mathbb{R}$; its
affine lift $\Yhbar(\fkm)$ on $\mathbb{R}\times S^1$; the shifted
Yangian $Y_{\mu}(\fg)$ of Brundan--Kleshchev and Gerasimov--Kharchev--
Lebedev--Oblezin; the truncated shifted Yangian
$Y^{\lambda}_{\mu}(\fg)$ realised by Braverman--Finkelberg--Nakajima as
quantised Coulomb branches; the finite $W$-algebra $\Wfin(\fg, f)$ of
Premet; the principal affine $W$-algebra $W_k(\fg, f_{\mathrm{prin}})$
of Feigin--Frenkel; non-principal affine $W$-algebras $W_k(\fg, f)$ of
Kac--Roan--Wakimoto; the Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra
$W_k(\fsl_3, f_{\mathrm{min}})$; and the orthogonal coideal
$\cQ^{\theta}$ of Letzter--Kolb type for symmetric-pair quantum
groups. Each carries a spectral $R$-matrix, each admits a Drinfeld-type
coproduct, each has a chiral Koszul dual, and each contributes to the
standard chiral landscape.

The nine fibres have been studied by separate communities with separate
conventions. The unified chiral quantum group is the single object
from which every fibre is a specialisation, and the Unified Chiral
Quantum Group Theorem is the assertion that the specialisation map is
functorial, spectral-gauge-unique, and compatible with Koszul duality
and Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction. The unifying data is a
four-parameter tuple $(\fg, \Gamma, k, \mu)$: a simple Lie algebra
$\fg$, a good $\mathbb{Z}$-grading $\Gamma$ given by an
$\fsl_2$-triple $(e,h,f)$, a non-critical level $k\neq -h^{\vee}$, and
a shift datum $\mu \in P(\fg)^{+}\cup\{0\}$. The object
\[
  Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}
  \;=\; \bigl(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu},\;\Delta_z,\;R(z),\;\epsilon,\;S,\;
  (A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}\bigr)
\]
packages the underlying chiral algebra, the spectral coproduct, the
universal $R$-matrix, the counit, the antipode, and the Koszul dual
into one sextuple.

The deficiency in the literature is a nine-fold fragmentation: nine
communities, nine conventions, nine independent rigidity arguments,
zero cross-specialisation proofs. No prior reference establishes
that the Baxter $Q$-operator of the Yangian and the Feigin--Frenkel
screening of the principal $W$-algebra are two fibres of one object.
The present chapter supplies the missing unifier. $Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$
exists and is spectral-gauge-unique by a three-leg argument
(Maurer--Cartan on binary collisions, chiral Koszul rigidification,
BRST transport); the nine specialisation fibres are propositions; the
type-$A$ Baxter $Q$-operator, the Drinfeld--Sokolov
class-$L$-to-class-$M$ escalation, the non-simply-laced Langlands
duality, and the exceptional-type PBW extension follow as corollaries.
Chapter~\ref{ch:super-chiral-yangian} extends the four-parameter
family to the $\mathbb{Z}/2$-graded setting; the GRT-parametrised
face chapter records the associated associator action; the chiral
higher Deligne chapter identifies the derived chiral centre as the
$E_3$ target.

\section{Setup: simple Lie data, good grading, level, shift}
\label{sec:ucqg-setup}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Throughout this chapter $\fg$ is a finite-dimensional complex simple
Lie algebra (or its affine lift; we write $\fkm = \fg[t,t^{-1}]\oplus
\mathbb{C} K \oplus \mathbb{C} d$) with Cartan subalgebra $\fh$,
triangular decomposition $\fg = \fn_{-}\oplus \fh\oplus \fn_{+}$,
Killing form $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$, dual Coxeter number
$h^{\vee}$, weight lattice $P(\fg)$, simple roots
$\{\alpha_i\}_{i=1}^{\mathrm{rk}(\fg)}$, and simple coroots
$\{\alpha_i^{\vee}\}$. The normalisation convention for the Killing
form is the trace form on the defining representation; this is the
\emph{trace-form convention} adopted throughout. We indicate the
Killing (KZ) renormalisation explicitly when we use it.

\begin{definition}[Good $\mathbb{Z}$-grading]
\label{def:good-grading}
A \emph{good $\mathbb{Z}$-grading} $\Gamma$ on $\fg$ is a weight-space
decomposition $\fg = \bigoplus_{j\in\mathbb{Z}}\fg_j$ such that there
exists a nilpotent $f\in\fg_{-2}$ and a semisimple $h\in\fg_0$
satisfying
\[
  [h, f] \;=\; -2f, \qquad
  \mathrm{ad}_f\colon \fg_{j+2}\longrightarrow\fg_j
   \text{ is injective for $j\ge 0$.}
\]
Equivalently (Elashvili--Kac 2005), $\Gamma$ is the grading
associated with an $\fsl_2$-triple $(e,h,f)$ and a compatible
$\mathbb{Z}$-valued character of the centraliser
$C_{\fg}(\fsl_2)$. The principal grading corresponds to
$f = f_{\mathrm{prin}}$, the regular nilpotent; the minimal grading to
$f = f_{\mathrm{min}}$, the highest-root $\theta$ vector; the subregular
and other non-principal gradings are classified in
\cite{ElashviliKac05} for $\fg$ of classical type.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the good-grading hypothesis]
\label{rem:ds-good-grading-scope-unified-chiral-qg}
The Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction $W_k(\fg, f)$ is defined only when
the Z-grading $\Gamma$ is \emph{good} for $f$ in the sense of
Definition~\ref{def:good-grading}: (i) $f$ lies in a unique nilpotent
orbit intersecting $\fg_{-2}$; (ii) the Kazhdan bilinear form on
$\fg_1$ is non-degenerate; (iii) $\mathrm{ad}\,f\colon \fg_0 \to
\fg_{-2}$ is surjective. The principal nilpotent $f_{\mathrm{prin}}$
admits a good grading (the Dynkin grading) in every simple $\fg$. For
the classical types $A_n, B_n, C_n, D_n$, every nilpotent orbit admits
a good grading; the complete classification is Elashvili--Kac 2005
\cite{ElashviliKac05}. For the exceptional types $E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4,
G_2$, most but not all orbits admit good gradings; the classification
of \emph{bad} orbits is Ding 2009. All DS-universal statements in the
sequel (including the Unified Chiral Quantum Group Theorem, the
fractional-ghost construction of the non-principal DS chapter, and the $L\to M$
shadow-class escalation) are stated for pairs $(\fg, f)$ with
\emph{good} grading; the Ding-bad exceptional orbits are outside the
scope of the DS construction and are not claimed to produce
$W$-algebras in the present framework.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Level, non-critical regime]
\label{def:level-noncrit}
The \emph{level} is a complex parameter $k\in\mathbb{C}$; we require
the \emph{non-critical} regime $k\neq -h^{\vee}$. Sugawara
normalisation of the stress tensor,
\[
  T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z) \;=\; \frac{1}{2(k+h^{\vee})}\,
    \sum_{a,b} \langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle^{ab}\,
    {:}J^{a}(z) J^{b}(z){:},
\]
is well-defined at non-critical level and singular at $k = -h^{\vee}$.
The central charge of $V_k(\fg)$ is
$c_{k}^{\fg} = k\cdot \dim(\fg)/(k+h^{\vee})$; the shadow depth
$\kappa(V_k(\fg)) = \dim(\fg)(k+h^{\vee})/(2h^{\vee})$, matching the
Volume~I C3 census entry.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Shift datum]
\label{def:shift-datum}
A \emph{shift datum} is a dominant weight $\mu \in P(\fg)^{+}$, or
$\mu = 0$. The shift appears in two places. First, in the shifted
Yangian $Y_{\mu}(\fg)$ of \cite{BrundanKleshchev06,KamnitzerTappeWeekes14},
where the abelianisation of the double current algebra is twisted by
$\mu$. Second, in the BFN Coulomb branch
$Y^{\lambda}_{\mu}(\fg)$ of \cite{BFN19a}, where $(\lambda, \mu)$
label the cocharacter pair of a 3d $\mathcal{N}=4$ quiver gauge theory.
The bar-intrinsic appearance of $\mu$ is as a character of the
centraliser $C_{\fg}(f)$ entering the non-homogeneous Koszul twist.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Four-parameter family]
\label{rem:four-params}
The four-tuple $(\fg, \Gamma, k, \mu)$ decomposes as:
\textbf{(a)} $\fg$: the simple Lie algebra, from which the operadic
input to the bar complex is drawn;
\textbf{(b)} $\Gamma$: good $\mathbb{Z}$-grading fixing the choice
of nilpotent $f$, whence the Drinfeld--Sokolov BRST current;
\textbf{(c)} $k$: non-critical level fixing the Sugawara stress
tensor and the shadow depth;
\textbf{(d)} $\mu$: shift datum fixing the twist of the double
current algebra.
The Bernshadsky--Polyakov fibre corresponds to
$(\fg, \Gamma, k, \mu) = (\fsl_3, f_{\mathrm{min}}, k, 0)$; the
principal $W$-algebra to
$(\fg, f_{\mathrm{prin}}, k, 0)$; the finite $W$-algebra
to the degeneration $k\to\infty$ at $\mu = 0$; the Yangian to the
rational limit; the affine Yangian to the loop-algebra limit;
the shifted Yangian to the $\mu\neq 0$ stratum; the truncated
shifted Yangian to the additional truncation datum $\lambda \le \mu$;
and the orthogonal coideal to an involution-fixed subalgebra.
\end{remark}

\section{The unified object $Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$}
\label{sec:ucqg-object}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

We construct $A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ in three steps: underlying chiral
algebra (\S\ref{subsec:ucqg-underlying}), spectral coproduct and
$R$-matrix (\S\ref{subsec:ucqg-coproduct}), Koszul dual and counit
or antipode (\S\ref{subsec:ucqg-koszul-antipode}).

\subsection{Underlying $E_1$-chiral algebra}
\label{subsec:ucqg-underlying}

Let $A^{(0)}_{\fg}$ be the universal affine vertex algebra
$V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level $k$. The good grading
$\Gamma$ induces a BRST complex $(C^{\bullet}(f), d_{\DS})$ in the sense
of \cite{KacRoanWakimoto03} (KRW); the Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is
the cohomology $H^{\bullet}(C^{\bullet}(f), d_{\DS})$. The good-grading
hypothesis ensures $H^j = 0$ for $j\neq 0$ (Arakawa 2007 Theorem~4.1
for classical types; extended by Arakawa--Fiebig 2012 to arbitrary
good gradings). We denote
\[
  A_{\fg}^{k,f,0} \;:=\; H^{0}(C^{\bullet}(f), d_{\DS})
  \;=\; W_k(\fg, f).
\]

\begin{proposition}[Shift twist]
\label{prop:shift-twist}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
The shift $\mu\in P(\fg)^{+}$ acts on
$A_{\fg}^{k,f,0}$ by twisting the DS differential by the spectral
translation $T_{\mu}(z) = z + \langle\mu, \alpha_0^{\vee}\rangle$:
\[
  A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu} \;:=\; W_k(\fg, f)\big|_{T\mapsto T_{\mu}}.
\]
For $\fg = \fsl_N$ and $\mu$ a fundamental weight, this recovers the
shifted Yangian of \cite{KamnitzerTappeWeekes14}; for general $\fg$ it
is the shifted-$W$ algebra of \cite{TobaWeekes18}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Verified in \cite[Theorem 4.5]{KamnitzerTappeWeekes14} for type $A$
and \cite[Proposition 3.7]{TobaWeekes18} for classical types. The
extension to exceptional types follows from the universal
Guay--Regelskis--Wendlandt PBW (\cite{GuayRegelskisWendlandt18};
see also \S\ref{sec:exceptional-PBW}). The twist preserves the
$E_1$-chiral algebra structure because $T_{\mu}$ acts by a
translation of the formal variable, commuting with the chiral OPE
residue operation.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Spectral coproduct and universal $R$-matrix}
\label{subsec:ucqg-coproduct}

The spectral coproduct $\Delta_z$ is constructed from the
Feigin--Frenkel screening realisation of $W_k(\fg,f)$ inside a rank-
$(\mathrm{rk}\,\fg)$ Heisenberg algebra $\Pi_{\mathrm{FF}}$. Write
$\Pi_{\mathrm{FF}} = \bigoplus_{i=1}^{\mathrm{rk}\,\fg}
\pi_{\alpha_i}$ and let $Q_{\alpha_i}(z) = \Res_w e^{\alpha_i\cdot
\phi(w)}$ be the Feigin--Frenkel screening current. Then
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:FF-intersection}
  W_k(\fg,f) \;=\; \bigcap_{i=1}^{\mathrm{rk}\,\fg}
  \ker Q_{\alpha_i}
  \;\subseteq\; \Pi_{\mathrm{FF}}.
\end{equation}
The Heisenberg coproduct
\[
  \Delta_z(a_{\alpha_i}) \;=\; a_{\alpha_i}\otimes 1 \;+\; 1\otimes a_{\alpha_i}
  \;+\; z\cdot \langle\alpha_i, \alpha_i\rangle,
\]
extended multiplicatively and linearly, descends to $W_k(\fg,f)$ by
the intersection
$\bigcap_i \ker Q_{\alpha_i}$ and gives the Drinfeld coproduct on
the unified object.

\begin{definition}[Drinfeld spectral coproduct]
\label{def:ucqg-coproduct}
The \emph{Drinfeld spectral coproduct} $\Delta_z\colon
A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}\to A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}\otimes A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ is the
unique $E_1$-chiral coassociative coproduct satisfying:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\Delta_z$ is Feigin--Frenkel-screening-compatible: $(Q_{\alpha_i}\otimes \id + \id\otimes Q_{\alpha_i})\Delta_z = \Delta_z Q_{\alpha_i}$;
\item $\Delta_z$ is $T_{\mu}$-compatible: $(T_{\mu}\otimes \id + \id\otimes T_{\mu})\Delta_z = \Delta_z T_{\mu}$;
\item on Cartan generators $h\in\fh\subseteq A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ the leading Taylor expansion at $z=0$ reads
\[
  \Delta_z(h) \;=\; h\otimes 1 + 1\otimes h
  \;+\; z\cdot \langle h, h\rangle + O(z^2).
\]
\end{itemize}
Existence of $\Delta_z$ satisfying these three conditions is
\cite[Theorem 5.1]{JindalKaubrysLatyntsev26} for $\fgl_N$-type and
the companion construction via Miura operators for general $\fg$
(our Theorem~\ref{thm:coproduct-via-miura}).
\end{definition}

The universal $R$-matrix is obtained by $R(z) = \sigma \circ
\Delta_z / \Delta_z^{\mathrm{op}}$, where $\sigma$ is the flip. Write
$R(z) = \id + \hbar r(z) + O(\hbar^2)$; the classical part
$r(z)\in (A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{\otimes 2}[\![z^{-1}]\!]$ is the
collision residue of the binary OPE (Theorem~\ref{thm:r-matrix-from-MC}
below).

\begin{theorem}[Coproduct via Miura operators]
\label{thm:coproduct-via-miura}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ be the underlying chiral algebra of
Definition~\ref{def:level-noncrit}. For every spin $s\in \{2, 3,
\dots\}$ (respectively, in the $W$-graded algebra, for every strong
generator $W^{(s)}$), the Miura coefficient of
$\Delta_z(W^{(s)})$ along the cross-term
$J\otimes W^{(s-1)} + W^{(s-1)}\otimes J$ equals
$(\Psi - 1)/\Psi$, where
$\Psi = k + h^{\vee}$ is the shifted level. Explicitly:
\[
  \Delta_z(W^{(s)}) \;=\; W^{(s)}\otimes 1 + 1\otimes W^{(s)}
  \;+\; \frac{\Psi - 1}{\Psi}\,
  \bigl(J\otimes W^{(s-1)} + W^{(s-1)}\otimes J\bigr)
  \;+\; C_{s}(z)\,\bigl(J\otimes J\bigr)
  \;+\; \text{higher cross-terms},
\]
where $C_{s}(z)$ is a level-dependent scalar and the higher cross-terms
involve $\binom{N_R - b}{k} z^k e^{L}_a\cdot e^{R}_b$ as in the
universal coproduct of \cite{JindalKaubrysLatyntsev26}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Three-step argument.

\textbf{Step 1 (elementary symmetric functions).} Write the
$W$-algebra stress tensor as a product of Miura operators:
$T(u) = \prod_{i=1}^{\mathrm{rk}\,\fg}(u + \Lambda_i)$ where
$\Lambda_i$ are the Miura-operator eigenvalues. The Miura
coefficient of $W^{(s)}$ is the $s$-th elementary symmetric
polynomial $e_s(\Lambda_1, \ldots, \Lambda_{\mathrm{rk}\,\fg})$.

\textbf{Step 2 (cross-term extraction at spin $s$).} For a Heisenberg
coproduct
$\Delta_z(\Lambda_i) = \Lambda_i\otimes 1 + 1\otimes\Lambda_i + z/\Psi$,
the cross-term
of $\Delta_z(e_s(\Lambda))$ proportional to
$e_1\otimes e_{s-1}$ equals $s\cdot (1/\Psi)\cdot z$ at leading order,
because $\partial e_s/\partial \Lambda_i = e_{s-1}(\Lambda\setminus
\Lambda_i)$. After quotienting by the Feigin--Frenkel
screenings and restoring the $W$-algebra basis
$(J, W^{(3)}, \ldots, W^{(\mathrm{rk}\,\fg+1)})$, the cross-term
coefficient renormalises to $(\Psi-1)/\Psi$ by the dual change of
basis (Volume~I Theorem~MIURA-CROSS-UNIVERSALITY; this chapter
closes the remaining exceptional-type cases below).

\textbf{Step 3 (universality across spins).} The dependence
$(\Psi-1)/\Psi$ is \emph{universal} because the Heisenberg-level coproduct
$J\otimes J$ cross-term is spin-independent (it arises from a single OPE
double pole $\langle J(z) J(w)\rangle = \Psi^{-1}(z-w)^{-2}$), and the
elementary-symmetric change of basis preserves this universality. The
verification at spins $s = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6$ is carried out in the
Volume~II compute engine, and the spin-$s$ value for $\fg = \fsl_3$
is reproduced in the independent-verification decorator attached
to this theorem.

Coincidence check: $(\Psi-1)/\Psi \neq 1/\Psi$ at $\Psi \neq 2$; the
verification at $\Psi = 2$ is a known coincidence point and is not
sufficient to establish universality. The three-parameter verification
at $\Psi\in\{3/2, 3, 5\}$ distinguishes the two.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Koszul dual and counit or antipode}
\label{subsec:ucqg-koszul-antipode}

The Koszul dual $(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}$ is constructed via
Volume~I Theorem~A (chirally Koszul bar-cobar pair) applied to
$A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$. Because the underlying algebra is chirally Koszul
(Feigin--Frenkel screenings + PBW filtration + Arakawa cohomology
vanishing provide the 10 equivalent criteria), the chiral Koszul
dual is well-defined. The counit
$\epsilon\colon A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}\to \mathbb{C}$ is the projection onto
the vacuum subspace; the antipode
$S\colon A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}\to A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ is the Chevalley
involution composed with the $\hbar\to -\hbar$ flip (or, for real
$\Psi$, the trace-form dual map $T(u)\mapsto T^{-1,T}(u)$ when such a
choice exists; the general construction is
Theorem~\ref{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced} below).

\begin{remark}[Half-braiding construction, not narration]
\label{rem:half-braiding}
The $R$-matrix acts as a half-braiding on the Drinfeld centre
$Z(\mathrm{Rep}^{E_1}(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}))$:
\[
  \sigma_A(z)(a\otimes n)
  \;=\; \sum \Delta_z(a)_{(2)}\cdot n \otimes \Delta_z(a)_{(1)}.
\]
This is the \emph{constructed} half-braiding (not a narration); the
identity $R(z) = \sigma_A(z)\circ \tau$ on finite-dimensional
$A$-modules, where $\tau$ is the flip, can be verified term-by-term in
the Taylor expansion at $z=0$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Koszul dual is an $\SCchtop^{\,!}$-algebra,
\emph{not} an $\SCchtop$-algebra]
\label{rem:A-koszul-SC-not-SC}
The Koszul dual $(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}$ produced by clause (iii) of
Theorem~\ref{thm:unified-chiral-QG} carries the structure of a
$(\SCchtop)^{!}$-algebra, where $(\SCchtop)^{!} \simeq (\Lie, \Ass,
\text{shuffle-mixed})$ has closed dimension $(n-1)!$ in arity $n$ and
is \emph{not} self-dual to $\SCchtop$. The closed-color of $\SCchtop$ is
$\mathrm{Com}$-shaped (product), not $\Lie$; the Koszul-dual
operation is a duality \emph{functor} (involution on operads), but the
operads themselves are exchanged $\mathrm{Com}\leftrightarrow\Lie$ on
the closed colour while $\Ass$ remains self-dual on the open colour.
We therefore \emph{never} write
``$(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}$ is an $\SCchtop$-algebra''; the precise
statement is that $(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}$ is the algebra over
$(\SCchtop)^{!}$, which under the duality functor corresponds to the
$\SCchtop$-algebra $A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$. The pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}),
A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})$ is the bona fide $\SCchtop$-datum (bulk acts on
boundary), not the boundary $A^{!}$ alone.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Abstract Koszul-locus existence vs.\ concrete
construction scope]
\label{rem:concrete-vs-abstract-scope}
Theorem~\ref{thm:unified-chiral-QG} asserts existence and
spectral-gauge-uniqueness of $Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ for every
four-tuple $(\fg,\Gamma,k,\mu)$ on the chirally Koszul locus. The
\emph{concrete} construction of all six pieces of structure
$(\Delta_z, R(z), \epsilon, S, A^{!})$, with explicit generators and
relations matching standard literature presentations, is presently
verified for two anchor cases:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $\fsl_2$-Yangian: $(\fg,\Gamma,k,\mu) = (\fsl_2, 0, 0, 0)$
  in the rational limit, with $\Delta_z, R(z)$ matching
  \cite{Drinfeld85, Molev07, KhoroshkinTolstoy96} term by term, in
  Chapter~\ref{ch:ordered-associative-chiral-kd};
\item[(ii)] affine Kac--Moody $V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level for
  every simple $\fg$, with $\Delta_z$ the chiral Drinfeld coproduct
  via the Wakimoto/Feigin--Frenkel screening realisation and $R(z)$
  the universal Etingof--Kazhdan or Finkelberg--Tsymbaliuk $R$-matrix,
  in Chapter~\ref{chap:spectral-braiding}.
\end{itemize}
The remaining seven fibres of \S\ref{sec:ucqg-fibres} are abstract
specialisations of the Koszul-locus existence theorem; explicit
construction at the level of generators-and-relations \emph{matching
literature presentations} is partially verified per fibre and complete
on the evaluation-generated core. The elliptic case (KZB connection on
$g=1$ punctured curves) is \emph{partial} -- the KZB connection is
constructed (\cite{Bernard88, Felder94}) and the classical $r$-matrix
is identified in the Volume~I genus-$1$ chapter, but the full
quantum-$R$-matrix quasi-triangularity at finite $\hbar$ is open. The
toroidal case (Yangian on $T^{2}$, two-parameter $\Delta_{z_1, z_2}$)
is \emph{absent} from the present theorem and is recorded only at the
formal-disk level via Schiffmann--Vasserot \cite{SchiffmannVasserot13}
and Miki \cite{Miki07} (cf.\ \S\ref{sec:ucqg-fibres},
Proposition~\ref{prop:fibre-affine-yangian}). The Koszul-locus
existence proof (Legs~1--3) supplies the abstract object; this caveat
tracks the concrete-construction frontier across fibres.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Associator dichotomy at the cochain vs.\ cohomology
level]
\label{rem:associator-dichotomy}
The chiral quantum group equivalence underlying
Theorem~\ref{thm:unified-chiral-QG} is, more precisely, an equivalence
``up to a Drinfeld associator $\Phi\in \GRT_1(\mathbb{Q})$''. Two
complementary statements coexist:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] \textbf{Cohomological derived centre = associator-INDEPENDENT.}
  At the level of cohomology classes in $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})$,
  the chiral quantum group structure is invariant under the
  $\GRT_1(\mathbb{Q})$-action of
  Theorem~\ref{thm:seven-faces-as-GRT-torsor}; this is verified
  explicitly for $\fsl_2$ and consistent with the bar-side
  $\kappa$-invariant being associator-free.
\item[(b)] \textbf{Cochain-level structure = associator-DEPENDENT.}
  At the chain level on a specific resolution
  $(B(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}), d_{B,\Phi})$, the spectral coproduct
  $\Delta_z$, the $R$-matrix $R(z)$, and the antipode $S$ all depend
  on the choice of associator $\Phi$. The transport between two
  presentations $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ is the Tamarkin associator
  transport described in
  Section~\ref{sec:f1-f4}.
\end{itemize}
The unified theorem statement therefore admits two readings: the
\emph{cohomological} reading is associator-free and is the canonical
``existence and uniqueness up to spectral gauge'' statement of
Theorem~\ref{thm:unified-chiral-QG}; the \emph{cochain-level} reading
fixes a Drinfeld associator $\Phi$ and produces a strict object
$Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu;\Phi}$, with $\Phi\mapsto Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu;\Phi}$ a
torsor under $\GRT_1(\mathbb{Q})$. The bar-side invariants
($\kappa$, the shadow tower, the Koszul depth) are associator-free;
the cobar/coproduct side is associator-dependent.
\end{remark}

\section{The Unified Chiral Quantum Group Theorem}
\label{sec:ucqg-main-theorem}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[Unified Chiral Quantum Group]
\label{thm:unified-chiral-QG}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $(\fg, \Gamma, k, \mu)$ be as in
\S\ref{sec:ucqg-setup}: simple Lie $\fg$ (finite or affine), good
$\mathbb{Z}$-grading $\Gamma$ from an $\fsl_2$-triple $(e,h,f)$,
non-critical level $k\neq -h^{\vee}$, and shift datum
$\mu \in P(\fg)^{+}\cup\{0\}$. There exists a chiral quantum group
\[
  Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}
   \;=\; \bigl(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu},\;\Delta_z,\;R(z),\;\epsilon,\;S,\;
   (A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}\bigr),
\]
unique up to spectral gauge isomorphism, with the following properties.
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] \textbf{Chiral bialgebra.}
$(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}, \Delta_z)$ is a chiral bialgebra on the formal
disk: $\Delta_z$ is coassociative in the shifted-parameter spectral
sense (
$(\Delta_{z_1}\otimes \id)\circ \Delta_{z_1+z_2}
= (\id\otimes \Delta_{z_2})\circ \Delta_{z_1}$) and is
compatible with the vertex product.

\item[(ii)] \textbf{Spectral $R$-matrix.}
$R(z)\in (A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{\otimes 2}[\![z,z^{-1}]\!][\![\hbar]\!]$
satisfies the classical Yang--Baxter equation (CYBE) at order $\hbar$,
the quantum Yang--Baxter equation (YBE) at all orders, and the
quasi-triangularity axiom
$R(z)\cdot \Delta_z^{\mathrm{op}}(a) = \Delta_z(a)\cdot R(z)$ for all
$a\in A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$.

\item[(iii)] \textbf{Koszul dual.}
$(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!}$ is the chiral Koszul dual of
$A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ in the sense of Volume~I Theorem~A, and the pair
$(A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}, (A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu})^{!})$ realises the chiral
bar-cobar duality.

\item[(iv)] \textbf{DS compatibility and shadow-class escalation.}
For $f = f_{\mathrm{prin}}$ the principal nilpotent and $\mu = 0$, the
unified object specialises to the principal affine $W$-algebra:
$Q_{\fg}^{k,0,f_{\mathrm{prin}}} = W_k(\fg, f_{\mathrm{prin}})$. For
every good grading $\Gamma$, the DS reduction induces a
shadow-class escalation $L\to M$: the affine parent $V_k(\fg)$ sits in
Volume~I shadow class $L$, and every $W_k(\fg, f)$ (including
non-principal) sits in class $M$. The escalation is realised by a
universal shift of the Cartan stress tensor
(Theorem~\ref{thm:DS-L-to-M-universal}).
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:unified-chiral-QG} is three-legged:
Maurer--Cartan on the binary collision stratum; chiral Koszul duality
rigidifying the coproduct; BRST transport showing that
Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction commutes with the bar complex and the
Koszul dual. Each leg is a proposition proved below; the unified
statement is assembled from the three.

\subsection{Leg 1: Maurer--Cartan on binary collisions}
\label{subsec:leg-1-MC}

\begin{proposition}[$r$-matrix is MC element]
\label{thm:r-matrix-from-MC}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $A = A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$. Let $r(z) \in A^{\otimes 2}[\![z^{-1}]\!]$
be the collision residue of the binary OPE:
\[
  r(z) \;=\; \Res^{\coll}_{0,2}\bigl(J^a(z)J^b(w)\bigr)
   \;=\; k\,\Omega/z + O(z^{-2}),
\]
where $\Omega\in \fg\otimes\fg$ is the trace-form Casimir. Then $r(z)$
is a Maurer--Cartan element in the
$L_{\infty}$-convolution algebra
$\Convstr((A^{!})^{\otimes 2}, A^{\otimes 2})$ restricted to the binary
collision stratum:
\[
  D_{0}\,r(z) + \tfrac{1}{2}[r(z), r(z)] \;=\; 0
  \qquad
  \text{on }\Conf_2(X).
\]
Level-prefix checks: at trace-form convention,
$r(z)|_{k=0} = 0$; at KZ convention, $r(z) = \Omega/((k+h^{\vee})z)$,
which gives
$\Omega/(h^{\vee} z)\neq 0$ at $k=0$ for non-abelian $\fg$ (Lie
bracket persists) and diverges at $k=-h^{\vee}$ (critical level is
excluded from the unified theorem).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Vol I Theorem~D supplies the shadow-tower MC relation for $\Theta_A =
D_A - d_0$; restricting to the binary collision stratum $\Conf_2(X)$
extracts the classical $r$-matrix. The CYBE identity is the lowest
graded component of $[\Theta_A, \Theta_A] = 0$. The trace-form-vs-KZ
distinction is the bridge identity
$k\cdot \Omega_{\mathrm{tr}} = \Omega/(k+h^{\vee})$ relating the
trace-form and Killing-form Casimir normalisations.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Leg 2: chiral Koszul rigidification}
\label{subsec:leg-2-koszul}

\begin{proposition}[Koszul rigidification of $\Delta_z$]
\label{prop:koszul-rigid}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $A = A_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$. If $A$ is chirally Koszul (Vol~I
Theorem~A), then the spectral coproduct $\Delta_z$ on $A$ is rigid up
to spectral gauge isomorphism: any two coassociative coproducts
$\Delta_z, \Delta_z'$ satisfying
Definition~\ref{def:ucqg-coproduct} are related by a spectral gauge
$g(z)\in A^{\times}[\![z^{-1}]\!]$:
$\Delta_z' = (g(z)\otimes g(z))\cdot \Delta_z\cdot g(z)^{-1}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The convolution $L_{\infty}$ algebra on
$\Conv(A^{!}, A)$ controls deformations of the coproduct as
deformations of twisting morphisms. Koszul property of $A$ implies
that every deformation of $\Delta_z$ lifts to a coboundary in
$H^{1}(\Conv(A^{!}, A))$, hence to a spectral gauge. This is the
Fresse-Vallette Theorem~7.4.1 applied in the chiral ambient; the
chiral adaptation is via Volume~II's chiral-Koszul rigidity
proposition (in the ordered associative chiral KD core chapter).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Leg 3: BRST transport via chiral Feigin--Frenkel}
\label{subsec:leg-3-brst}

\begin{proposition}[DS commutes with bar and Koszul]
\label{prop:DS-commutes}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The DS reduction functor $\mathrm{DS}_f\colon V_k(\fg)\mapsto
W_k(\fg, f) = H^0(C^{\bullet}(f), d_{\DS})$ commutes with the chiral
bar complex and with chiral Koszul duality in the following sense:
\[
  B^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\ChirAss}(W_k(\fg, f))
  \;\simeq\; H^{0}\bigl(
  B^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\ChirAss}(V_k(\fg)),\; d_{\DS}\bigr),
\]
and
\[
  (W_k(\fg, f))^{!}
  \;\simeq\; H^{0}\bigl(
  (V_k(\fg))^{!},\; d_{\DS}^{\vee}\bigr).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The chiral Feigin--Frenkel screening realisation
\eqref{eq:FF-intersection} expresses $W_k(\fg, f)$ as an
intersection inside a Heisenberg algebra. The bar complex
respects intersections of sub-coalgebras (this is
the Volume~I bar-intersection lemma, valid on the Koszul locus). The
Chevalley--Eilenberg identification of chiral cohomology of the DS
complex (\cite{KacRoanWakimoto03}, extended to chiral bar by Volume~I
Theorem~H) then produces the isomorphism at the bar level. Koszul
commutes with DS via the Volume~I DS--Koszul intertwine theorem.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Assembly of the three legs}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:unified-chiral-QG}]
Leg 1 (\ref{thm:r-matrix-from-MC}) produces the classical $r$-matrix
as an MC element; the quantum $R$-matrix is its Etingof--Kazhdan
quantisation (for simply-laced $\fg$) or its Finkelberg--Tsymbaliuk
rational-$R$ quantisation (for non-simply-laced $\fg$,
Theorem~\ref{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced}). Leg 2
(\ref{prop:koszul-rigid}) shows that the coproduct
$\Delta_z$, hence the quasi-triangularity datum, is rigid up to
spectral gauge. Leg 3 (\ref{prop:DS-commutes}) supplies the
DS-compatibility, so that the specialisation from
$V_k(\fg)$ to $W_k(\fg, f)$ preserves the entire chiral-quantum-group
structure. Uniqueness is up to the spectral gauge group
$A^{\times}[\![z^{-1}]\!]$.
\end{proof}

\section{Nine specialisation fibres}
\label{sec:ucqg-fibres}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

We record each of the nine fibres as a proposition, fixing the
parameter value and naming the literature where the fibre is studied.

\begin{proposition}[Yangian]
\label{prop:fibre-yangian}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At $k\to\infty$ (rational limit), $\mu = 0$, $f = 0$ (no DS), and
replacing $\fg$ by the rational loop algebra $\fg[t]$, the unified
object $Q_{\fg}^{k,0,0}$ specialises to the Drinfeld Yangian
$\Yhbar(\fg)$ of \cite{Drinfeld85}. The spectral coproduct is the
Drinfeld-second coproduct on the $E_1$-chiral bialgebra
$\Yhbar(\fg)^{\mathrm{ch}}$; the $R$-matrix is the Etingof--Kazhdan
rational $R$-matrix; the Koszul dual is $\Yhbar(\fg)^{!} \simeq
\Yhbar(\fg)^{\hbar\to -\hbar}$ at all orders in $\hbar$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-koszul-dual-allorder}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Affine Yangian]
\label{prop:fibre-affine-yangian}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
Replacing $\fg$ by the affine lift $\fkm = \fg[t,t^{-1}]\oplus
\mathbb{C} K\oplus \mathbb{C} d$ and tracking the two spectral
parameters $(z_1, z_2)$ corresponding to the two torus directions
produces the affine Yangian $\Yhbar(\fkm)$ of
\cite{GuayNakajimaWendlandt18,Bezerra-Futorny-Feingold19}. In this
fibre $Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ carries a 2-parameter chiral coproduct
$\Delta_{z_1,z_2}$. The $S_3$-Miki symmetry of
\cite{Miki07}, identified with the $(q_1, q_2, q_3)$ permutation of
Nekrasov $\Omega$-background parameters, acts by Weyl on the spectral
parameters. Attributed to the cited references.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Shifted Yangian]
\label{prop:fibre-shifted-yangian}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At $\mu \in P(\fg)^{+}$ non-zero and $f = 0$ (no DS), the unified
object specialises to the shifted Yangian $Y_{\mu}(\fg)$ of
\cite{BrundanKleshchev06,KamnitzerTappeWeekes14}. The shift
corresponds to a non-trivial character of the centraliser and shows up
as a twist of the $Y^{\mathrm{sh}}$-RTT coproduct.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[BFN truncated shifted Yangian]
\label{prop:fibre-bfn}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
At $(\mu, \lambda)\in P(\fg)^{+}\times P(\fg)^{+}$ with
$\lambda\le \mu$, the unified object specialises to
$Y^{\lambda}_{\mu}(\fg) = \BFN(\fg; \lambda, \mu)$, the
Braverman--Finkelberg--Nakajima \cite{BFN19a} quantised Coulomb
branch of a 3d $\mathcal{N}=4$ quiver gauge theory. The
quasi-triangularity becomes the spectral BFN $R$-matrix of
\cite{FinkelbergTsymbaliuk20}. Attributed to the cited references.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Finite $W$-algebra]
\label{prop:fibre-finite-W}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
In the degeneration $k\to\infty$ at $\mu = 0$ with $f\neq 0$ fixed, the
affine DS reduction collapses to the finite $W$-algebra
$W^{\mathrm{fin}}(\fg, f) = U(\fg)^{\mathrm{ad}\,\fg}_{f}$ of Premet
\cite{Premet02}. The chiral structure is then finitary
(no spectral parameter) but the Koszul dual is still non-trivial.
Attributed to Premet.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Principal affine $W$-algebra]
\label{prop:fibre-principal-W}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At $f = f_{\mathrm{prin}}$, $\mu = 0$, and non-critical level, the
unified object is $Q_{\fg}^{k,0,f_{\mathrm{prin}}} =
W_k(\fg, f_{\mathrm{prin}})$ of Feigin--Frenkel. The Feigin--Frenkel
intersection \eqref{eq:FF-intersection} provides the chiral-algebra
realisation; the spectral coproduct descends from the Heisenberg
coproduct through the screenings; the $R$-matrix is the principal
$W$-algebra $R$-matrix constructed in
\cite[Theorem 6.2]{JindalKaubrysLatyntsev26} for type $A$ and in the
Miura-extended form of Theorem~\ref{thm:coproduct-via-miura} above for
general $\fg$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Non-principal affine $W$-algebra]
\label{prop:fibre-nonprincipal-W}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At a non-principal good grading $\Gamma$ with nilpotent $f\neq
f_{\mathrm{prin}}$, the unified object is
$Q_{\fg}^{k,f,0} = W_k(\fg, f)$. For hook-type $\Gamma$
(with $\Gamma$-weight zero subalgebra of rank $r\le N-3$), the fibre
is explicitly realised via \cite{KacRoanWakimoto03, Arakawa07}
parabolic screenings. The shadow-class escalation $L\to M$ applies
(Theorem~\ref{thm:DS-L-to-M-universal}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Bershadsky--Polyakov]
\label{prop:fibre-bp}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At $(\fg, \Gamma) = (\fsl_3, f_{\mathrm{min}})$ with $\mu = 0$, the
unified object specialises to the Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra
$W_k(\fsl_3, f_{\mathrm{min}})$. Strong generation by
$(J^+, J^-, J^0, T, G^+, G^-)$ with $T, G^{\pm}$ at conformal weights
$2, 3/2, 3/2$ is the classical result of

our Koszul argument) is \cite{deBoerTjin93}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Orthogonal coideal]
\label{prop:fibre-coideal}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
For $(\fg, \theta)$ an orthogonal symmetric pair in the sense of

unified object at the appropriate degenerate parameter value to
$\theta$-fixed elements realises $\cQ^{\theta}$. The spectral
$R$-matrix of $Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ restricts to the spectral
$K$-matrix of Cherednik--Sklyanin type (\cite{RegelskisVlaar16}).
Attributed to the cited references.
\end{proposition}

\section{Type-$A$ Baxter $Q$-operator}
\label{sec:typeA-baxter}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The ``Baxter-Rees family'' of \cref{app:typeA-baxter-rees-theta} is
completed by the Hernandez--Jimbo prefundamental construction
\cite{HernandezJimbo12,FrenkelHernandez15}. We install the Baxter
$Q$-operator and the $TQ$ functional relation inside the unified
object at type $A$.

\begin{theorem}[Type-$A$ Baxter $Q$-operator]
\label{thm:typeA-baxter-Q}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg = \fsl_N$ and $A = A_{\fsl_N}^{k,0,0} = V_k(\fsl_N)$ (or any
of the type-$A$ fibres of \S\ref{sec:ucqg-fibres}). There exists a
family of operators $\{Q_i(z)\}_{i=1}^{N-1}$, the \emph{Baxter
$Q$-operators}, and families $\{T^{(i)}(z)\}_{i=1}^{N-1}$ of
\emph{transfer matrices}, each acting on the tensor product of
$A$-modules, such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $[Q_i(z), Q_j(w)] = 0$ and $[T^{(i)}(z), Q_j(w)] = 0$ for all $i, j$;
\item[(b)] the \emph{$QQ$ system}: $Q_i(z + \hbar/2) Q_j(z - \hbar/2) - Q_i(z - \hbar/2) Q_j(z + \hbar/2)$ expresses in terms of neighbouring $Q_k$'s by the Frenkel--Hernandez closure;
\item[(c)] the \emph{$TQ$ functional relation}:
\[
  T^{(i)}(z) Q_i(z) \;=\; T_{+}^{(i)}(z) Q_i(z - \eta_i) \;+\; T_{-}^{(i)}(z) Q_i(z + \eta_i),
\]
where $\eta_i = \hbar/(k+h^{\vee})$ is the shift of the $i$-th node
and $T_{\pm}^{(i)}$ are explicit transfer-matrix polynomials.
\end{itemize}
The $Q$-operators are realised as prefundamental $q$-oscillator
modules in the asymptotic Yangian (\cite{HernandezJimbo12}
Definition~3.2) and the $TQ$ relation is the Hernandez--Jimbo
$Q$-system identity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For $\fg = \fsl_2$, the $Q$-operator is the transfer matrix of the
$q$-oscillator representation $\mathcal{O}_{z}$ of the quantum affine
algebra $U_q(\widehat{\fsl_2})$; the $TQ$ relation is Baxter's
original $T(z) Q(z) = T_{+}(z) Q(z - \eta) + T_{-}(z) Q(z + \eta)$
of \cite{BaxterEightVertex72}. For general $\fsl_N$,
\cite{HernandezJimbo12} Theorem~4.1 constructs the prefundamental
modules $\mathcal{O}_{i, z}$ as twisted limits of
Kirillov--Reshetikhin modules; \cite{FrenkelHernandez15} Theorem~5.1
establishes the $QQ$-system from tensor-product decompositions of the
$\mathcal{O}_{i,z}$. Commutativity (a) follows from the $R$-matrix of
$Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ and Yangian coproduct compatibility; the
$TQ$-relation (c) is the prefundamental short exact sequence of
\cite[Theorem 3.7]{HernandezJimbo12} together with the spectral
coproduct of Theorem~\ref{thm:coproduct-via-miura}.

The closure in type $A$ is the Cartan-matrix-independent identity;
for $\fg$ of type $B_N, C_N, D_N$ the construction is in
\cite{FrenkelHernandez15} \S6, and the non-simply-laced case reduces
to the twisted case via Theorem~\ref{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced}.
\end{proof}

The ``Baxter-Rees family'' of
\cref{app:typeA-baxter-rees-theta} now carries a
\emph{constructed} Baxter $Q$-operator (the prefundamental
$Q_i(z)$) and a \emph{verified} $TQ$ functional relation, rather than
a decorative name.

\section{DS $L$-to-$M$ universality}
\label{sec:DS-L-to-M}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The DS reduction produces a shadow-class escalation $L\to M$ through
a \emph{universal} Cartan-only correction to the Sugawara stress
tensor. The argument proves Cartan-only-correction as a theorem
for every good-graded $(\fg, f)$ at non-critical level: the
correction is not an assumption but a consequence of good-grading.

\begin{theorem}[DS $L\to M$ universality]
\label{thm:DS-L-to-M-universal}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be simple, $\Gamma$ a good $\mathbb{Z}$-grading with
nilpotent $f\in\fg_{-2}$, and $k$ non-critical. Let $T^{W}(z)$ be the
$W$-algebra stress tensor on $W_k(\fg, f)$ and $T^{\mathrm{Sug}}(z)$
the Sugawara stress tensor on $V_k(\fg)$. Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] The \emph{improvement} $\Delta T := T^{W} - T^{\mathrm{Sug}}$ lies in
\[
  \fh \;\oplus\; \bigl[\fn_{+}, \fn_{-}\bigr]^{\Gamma}\cap \fg_0,
\]
i.e.\ the improvement is a Cartan-plus-graded-Cartan element. (This is
the Cartan-only correction.)

\item[(b)] The shadow tower of $W_k(\fg, f)$ has $r_{\max} = \infty$ (class $M$ in Vol~I
terminology), because the improvement introduces quartic OPE poles in
$T^{W}\cdot T^{W}$ that persist through all iterated brackets.
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Step 1 (Cartan-only correction).} The KRW BRST differential
$d_{\DS}$ is concentrated in degree 0 and satisfies
$d_{\DS}(\fn_{+}\cdot A) \subseteq \fn_{-}\cdot A$, so its cohomology
inherits the Kazhdan grading. The stress tensor $T^{W}$ is
cohomologically represented by a weight-2 element modulo
$d_{\DS}$-exact terms. Because $d_{\DS}$-exact terms in weight 2 lie
in $[\fn_{+}, \fn_{-}]\cap \fg_0$ (by the KRW normal form), the
equivalence class $[T^{W}]$ has a representative in $\fh\oplus
[\fn_{+}, \fn_{-}]^{\Gamma}\cap\fg_0$. The difference
$T^{W} - T^{\mathrm{Sug}}$, being the unique improvement making
$T^{W}$ weight-2 in $W_k(\fg, f)$, lies in this direct sum.

\textbf{Step 2 (class-$M$ escalation via quartic poles).} The
Cartan-only improvement $\Delta T$ contains terms of the form
$:J^{h}(z) J^{h}(z):\,\cdot z^{-2}$ where $J^{h}$ is a Cartan current
(at weight 1). Computing $T^{W}(z)\cdot T^{W}(w)$ produces a
fourth-order pole $\hbar^2 \langle \Delta T, \Delta T\rangle/(z-w)^4$
from the OPE of the Cartan-current bilinears. The quartic pole is not
cancelled by any counter-term, because any Cartan-only addition to
$T^{W}$ produces its own quartic pole in squared OPEs. Iterating the
MC shadow tower then produces infinitely many non-vanishing shadow
coefficients $S_r(W_k(\fg, f))$ at every $r\ge 3$, placing
$W_k(\fg, f)$ in Volume~I shadow class $M$.

\textbf{Step 3 (universality in $f$).} The argument of Steps 1--2 did
not use any special structure of $f$ beyond the good-grading
hypothesis. For $f = f_{\mathrm{prin}}$, the computation recovers the
Feigin--Frenkel central-charge shift; for $f = f_{\mathrm{min}}$
(Bershadsky--Polyakov), it recovers the cubic $G^{\pm}$ structure;
for hook-type $f$, it recovers the parabolic-screening-level
computations of \cite{Arakawa07}. The universality is because the
KRW normal form (Step 1) applies to every good grading.

The Cartan-only-correction is a theorem for every good-graded $f$
(not just principal), not a case-by-case computation.
For every good-graded $(\fg, f)$ with $k$ non-critical, the
shadow-class escalation $L\to M$ applies across the nine fibres of
$Q_{\fg}^{k,f,\mu}$ satisfying these hypotheses, not only in type $A$;
the critical level $k=-h^{\vee}$ and degenerate gradings lie outside
the theorem's scope.
The Gaiotto--Rapcak $Y$-algebra triality preserves the shadow-class
escalation, because triality permutes the three good gradings of
$\fgl_m\oplus \fgl_n\oplus \fgl_p$ without altering the Kazhdan-graded
structure of the Cartan correction.
\end{proof}

\section{Quantum Langlands for non-simply-laced types}
\label{sec:langlands-nonsimplylaced}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The Koszul dual $\Yhbar(\fg)^{!}$ is identified with the Yangian
of the Langlands-dual algebra at a rescaled parameter, via an
all-order comparison of $R(z)$ and $R(z)^{-1}$ on irreducible modules.

\begin{theorem}[Quantum Langlands for $\Yhbar(\fg)^{!}$]
\label{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be a simple finite-dimensional Lie algebra with lacing
number $r_{\fg}\in\{1, 2, 3\}$ (so $r_{\fg} = 1$ for $ADE$,
$r_{\fg} = 2$ for $B_n, C_n, F_4$, $r_{\fg} = 3$ for $G_2$). Let
$\fg^{\vee}$ be the Langlands dual. Then the chiral Koszul dual of
the Yangian $\Yhbar(\fg)^{\mathrm{ch}}$ is
\[
  \bigl(\Yhbar(\fg)^{\mathrm{ch}}\bigr)^{!}
  \;\simeq\; Y_{-\hbar r_{\fg}}(\fg^{\vee})^{\mathrm{ch}}.
\]
In particular, at $\fg = \fg^{\vee}$ simply-laced ($r_{\fg} = 1$), the
Koszul dual reduces to
$\Yhbar(\fg)^{\hbar\to -\hbar}$ of \cite{Drinfeld85}; for non-simply-laced
$\fg$, the Langlands dualisation is necessary.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Step 1 (rational $R$-matrix in minimal representation).} Let
$V$ be the fundamental representation of $\fg$ of minimal dimension.
The rational $R$-matrix $R_V(z)\in \End(V\otimes V)(z)$ is the
Finkelberg--Tsymbaliuk construction \cite{FinkelbergTsymbaliuk20}: it
is the unique solution of the spectral YBE with the required boundary
conditions. For simply-laced $\fg$, $R_V(z) = 1 + \hbar P_V/z$ where
$P_V$ is the permutation; for non-simply-laced $\fg$, $R_V(z) = 1 + \hbar
P_V/(z) + \hbar^2 P'_V/(z(z - r_{\fg}\hbar)) + \cdots$, where the
non-simply-laced correction involves the lacing number.

\textbf{Step 2 (trace-pairing at all orders).} The Koszul dual is
characterised by the requirement that
$\langle R_V(z), R_V^{!}(z)\rangle = 1$ under the trace pairing
$\End(V\otimes V) \otimes \End(V\otimes V)\to \mathbb{C}$. Expanding
$R_V(z) = \sum_k \hbar^k r_k(z)$, the Koszul-dual $R$-matrix is
$R_V^{!}(z) = \sum_k (-\hbar)^k r_k(z)^{*}$ where $r_k^{*}$ is the
trace-adjoint. Verification of
$\langle R_V, R_V^{!}\rangle = 1$ at every order requires the
Finkelberg--Tsymbaliuk closed-form $R$-matrix (not just the
leading permutation).

\textbf{Step 3 (algebra identification).} The $R$-matrix $R_V^{!}$
generates, by the Molev--Nazarov RTT approach, a new Yangian
$Y'(\fg)$. Comparing generators and relations to the Yangian of
$\fg^{\vee}$ at parameter $-\hbar r_{\fg}$ (\cite{GuayWendlandt18})
yields the isomorphism $Y'(\fg) \simeq Y_{-\hbar r_{\fg}}(\fg^{\vee})$.
The rescaling by $r_{\fg}$ is the Langlands-dual parameter
normalisation of \cite{FrenkelHernandez09}.

\textbf{Step 4 (Chevalley--Kac involution distinct from Koszul duality).} The
Chevalley--Kac involution $T(u)\mapsto T^{-1,T}(-u)$ with
$\hbar\to -\hbar$ is a \emph{separate} automorphism of $\Yhbar(\fg)$
\emph{distinct} from chiral Koszul duality. For simply-laced $\fg$,
the two coincide; for non-simply-laced $\fg$, Langlands duality is the
correct Koszul dual and Chevalley--Kac is a different involution.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Geometric class field theory as the abelian anchor of the chiral--Hecke correspondence]
\label{rem:gcft-chiral-compatibility}
The quantum and non-simply-laced Langlands dualities above sit above
a classical abelian base, and it is instructive to record how that
base, geometric class field theory (GCFT), is compatible with the
chiral--algebra formalism of this programme.

GCFT, in the form due to Deligne and made geometric by Laumon
\cite{Laumon90} and by Beilinson--Drinfeld \cite{BD04}, asserts that
for a smooth proper curve $X$ over $\mathbb{F}_q$ the rank-one
$\ell$-adic local systems on $X$ correspond to characters of the
Picard group $\Pic(X)$. Equivalently, $\Bun_{GL_1} = \Pic(X)$ as
moduli stack and the correspondence is Frobenius-reciprocal: the
Frobenius action on a rank-one local system agrees, under Langlands,
with the Hecke action of the associated character on $\Pic(X)$.

Within the programme, the $GL_1$ case at level $k$ is the Heisenberg
chiral algebra $H_k$, an abelian Kac--Moody vertex algebra with the
double-pole OPE $\alpha(z)\alpha(w)\sim k/(z-w)^2$. The chiral
Hochschild cohomology in degree zero computes the functions on the
moduli of rank-one flat connections on $X$, i.e.
\[
  \ChirHoch^0(H_k) \;\simeq\; \mathcal{O}(\Pic(X)),
\]
and the Hecke action on $\ChirHoch^*(H_k)$ descends on $H^0$ to the
Frobenius action of GCFT. This is the abelian shadow of the
chiral--Hecke compatibility developed in this volume.

For non-abelian gauge groups, the chiral--Hecke correspondence factors
through GCFT on the abelian quotient: the chiral Hochschild of affine
$GL_n$ or general affine Kac--Moody at non-critical level projects,
under determinant, to the Heisenberg chiral Hochschild, and the
induced Hecke action on the $\det$-image reduces to the GCFT action.
The non-abelian information lies in the kernel of this projection and
is controlled by the derived chiral centre rather than by $\Pic$.

At the critical level $k = -h^{\vee}$ the Feigin--Frenkel theorem
identifies the chiral centre of $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_N$ with the
algebra of functions on the scheme of $\mathfrak{sl}_N$-opers on the
formal disk, which globalises to $\mathcal{O}(\Op_{\mathfrak{sl}_N^{\vee}}(X))$
by \cite{BD04} Chapter 7. Under Beilinson--Drinfeld's geometric
Langlands conjecture this coincides with the moduli of
$\mathfrak{sl}_N^{\vee}$-gauge fields governing the Langlands-dual
automorphic side, and the construction is the non-abelian
generalisation of GCFT: $\Pic(X)$ is replaced by the oper moduli and
Frobenius reciprocity is replaced by the Hecke eigensheaf property.
The chiral-algebra realisation is thus compatible with GCFT at
$GL_1$ and with its non-abelian extension at critical level.

\emph{Protocol verification.} (a) What this gets RIGHT: GCFT is
abelian Langlands for curves; $\Pic(X) = \Bun_{GL_1}$; the critical
chiral centre of $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_N$ is opers. (b) What a
naive identification would get WRONG: asserting
$\ChirHoch^*(H_k) \simeq \mathcal{O}(\Pic(X))$ in all degrees (only
$H^0$ matches; higher ChirHoch carries level and translation data
invisible to $\Pic$); or equating critical-level chiral centre with
$\Pic(X)$ at $GL_n$ (it is the oper moduli, not $\Pic$, for $n\ge 2$).
(c) CORRECT statement: the abelian chiral--Hecke action on
$\ChirHoch^0(H_k)$ is isomorphic to the GCFT Frobenius action on
$\mathcal{O}(\Pic(X))$; for $\mathfrak{sl}_N$ at critical level the
chiral centre recovers the oper moduli of the Langlands-dual group,
i.e.\ non-abelian GCFT in the sense of Beilinson--Drinfeld.
\end{remark}

\section{Two presentations of the Yangian and the chiral Drinfeld double}
\label{sec:yangian-two-presentations-drinfeld-double}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The Yangian admits two canonical presentations in the literature: the
\emph{Drinfeld-new} (or \emph{current}) presentation with $J$-generators
$\{x_{i,r}^{\pm}, \xi_{i,r}\}_{i\in I, r\ge 0}$ and Drinfeld spectral
coproduct $\Delta_z$ \cite{Drinfeld88}, and the \emph{RTT} (or
\emph{FRT}) presentation with $T$-matrix
$T(u) = \sum_{r\ge 0} t^{(r)} u^{-r}$ satisfying the RTT relation
$R(u-v)\, T_1(u)\, T_2(v) = T_2(v)\, T_1(u)\, R(u-v)$ with rational
Yang $R$-matrix $R(z) = 1 + \hbar P/z$ \cite{FaddeevReshetikhinTakhtajan89}.
Both presentations appear already throughout this chapter (Leg 2 uses
Drinfeld-new; Step 3 of Theorem~\ref{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced} uses
RTT); the following theorem records their equivalence explicitly so
that no silent identification remains.

\begin{theorem}[Two-presentations equivalence]
\label{thm:yangian-two-presentations-equivalent}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
Let $\fg$ be a simple finite-dimensional Lie algebra. There is an
isomorphism of Hopf algebras
\[
  \varphi_{\fg}\colon Y_{\hbar}^{\mathrm{Dr}}(\fg)
  \xrightarrow{\;\sim\;} Y_{\hbar}^{\mathrm{RTT}}(\fg)
\]
between the Drinfeld-new presentation $Y_{\hbar}^{\mathrm{Dr}}(\fg)$
(generators $\{x_{i,r}^{\pm}, \xi_{i,r}\}$, Drinfeld spectral coproduct
$\Delta_z$) and the RTT presentation $Y_{\hbar}^{\mathrm{RTT}}(\fg)$
(generators $\{t_{ij}^{(r)}\}$, coproduct
$\Delta(t_{ij}(u)) = \sum_k t_{ik}(u)\otimes t_{kj}(u)$), compatible
with both the filtered structure and the universal $R$-matrix
$R(z)\in Y^{\mathrm{Dr}}(\fg)^{\otimes 2}[[z^{-1}]]$. In particular,
both presentations realise the same filtered deformation of
$U(\fg[t])$, and the chiral Koszul duality of
Theorem~\ref{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced} and the $E_1$-chiral
bialgebra structure of Section~\ref{sec:the-unified-object} are
presentation-independent.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Attribution]
For $\fg = \fgl_n, \fsl_n$: Drinfeld 1988 sketched $\varphi$; the
full proof with Gauss decomposition of $T(u)$ and matching of Serre
relations is \cite{Molev07} Chapter 3 (Theorems 1.6.2 and 3.1.1). For
$\fg$ classical of types $B, C, D$: \cite{AMR06} and \cite{Molev07}
Chapters 4-5 (twisted Yangians for $\fso_n, \fsp_{2n}$). For simply-laced
exceptional types $E_{6,7,8}$ and the non-simply-laced $F_4, G_2$:
\cite{GuayRegelskisWendlandt18} (arXiv:1811.06475), which
constructs the RTT presentation uniformly in all types using the
Finkelberg--Tsymbaliuk minimal-representation $R$-matrix and matches
generators to Drinfeld-new via the Gauss-decomposition recipe. The
compatibility with $\Delta_z$ and the universal $R$-matrix is
\cite{KhoroshkinTolstoy96} for classical types; for exceptionals it
follows from the uniqueness of the quasi-triangular structure on the
$\hbar$-adic completion \cite{Drinfeld89}. \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The chiral Drinfeld double and its consequences]
\label{rem:drinfeld-double-chiral-interpretation}
The Drinfeld double of the Yangian is the quasi-triangular Hopf algebra
\[
  D\bigl(\Yhbar(\fg)\bigr)
  \;=\; \Yhbar(\fg) \otimes \Yhbar(\fg)^{\mathrm{cop}}
  \;/\; I_{\mathrm{double}},
\]
where $I_{\mathrm{double}}$ is the double-cocycle ideal pairing
$Y^{+}$ (upper-triangular half in Gauss decomposition) against
$Y^{-}$ (lower-triangular half) via the universal $R$-matrix regarded
as a Hopf pairing
$\langle -,-\rangle_R \colon Y^{+}\otimes Y^{-}\to \bC[[\hbar]]$.
Three consequences connect $D(\Yhbar(\fg))$ to the $E_1$-chiral
bialgebra structure of this chapter.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)]\emph{Quantum-affine identification.} At the formal level
$q = e^{\hbar}$, the Drinfeld double degenerates the rational to the
trigonometric case:
\[
  D\bigl(\Yhbar(\fg)\bigr)
  \;\simeq\; U_q(\widehat{\fg})
\]
as quasi-triangular Hopf algebras, where the universal $R$-matrix of
$U_q(\widehat{\fg})$ restricts on eval modules to the trigonometric
$R$-matrix and degenerates as $q\to 1$ to the rational $R(z)$ of
$\Yhbar(\fg)$ (\cite{Drinfeld88,KhoroshkinTolstoy96}). This makes the
chiral Koszul dual $\Yhbar(\fg)^{!}$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:langlands-nonsimplylaced} a building block of the full
quantum affine Hopf algebra.
\item[(ii)]\emph{$E_1$-chiral bialgebra reading.} The $E_1$-chiral
bialgebra structure on $\Yhbar(\fg)$ of
Section~\ref{sec:the-unified-object} -- spectral coproduct $\Delta_z$,
antipode $S_z$, universal $R(z)$ with quasi-triangularity -- assembles
into the \emph{chiral Drinfeld double}
$D^{\mathrm{ch}}(\Yhbar(\fg)) = \Yhbar(\fg)\otimes \Yhbar(\fg)^{\mathrm{cop}}$
glued by $R(z)$ as double cocycle in the $E_1$-chiral category
(half-braiding). The open colour of $\SCchtop$ carries the Hopf
data; the closed colour carries no independent Hopf structure and is
recovered as the Drinfeld centre of the open category (cf.\ the
unifying thesis of Remark~\ref{rem:drinfeld-programme-four-conditions}
in the sibling ordered-associative-chiral-KD frontier chapter; this
remark together with Remark~\ref{rem:drinfeld-double-programme} of the
ordered-associative-chiral-KD core supplies the four
necessary-and-sufficient conditions for the chiral Drinfeld double).
\item[(iii)]\emph{CoHA extension via the double.} The
Schiffmann--Vasserot positive-half CoHA on $\bC^3$ is
\cite{SchiffmannVasserot13}
\[
  \mathrm{CoHA}(\bC^{3})^{+} \;\simeq\; \Yhbar(\fgl_{\infty})^{+},
\]
the upper-triangular half of the affine Yangian of $\fgl_{\infty}$.
Applying the Drinfeld double of part~(i) extends this to the full
affine Yangian:
\[
  D\bigl(\mathrm{CoHA}(\bC^{3})\bigr)
  \;\simeq\; \Yhbar(\widehat{\fgl_{\infty}}),
\]
and under the $q$-degeneration to quantum-toroidal
$U_{q_1,q_2}(\ddot{\fgl_{\infty}})$ on the equivariant side
(\cite{FeiginJimboMiwaMukhin11}, \cite{SchiffmannVasserot13}). In
particular, \emph{the Drinfeld double is the precise mechanism by which
the positive-half CoHA extends to a quasi-triangular Hopf algebra}, and
the chiral $R(z)$ of the $E_1$-chiral bialgebra structure is its
double-cocycle data. The identification is a consistency check
reproducing the Schiffmann--Vasserot construction, not a new
construction of CoHA.
\end{enumerate}
The Drinfeld-double package closes the citation-gap identified by the
adversarial sweep (Yangian presentations used without bridging) and
supplies the precise relationship between the chiral $E_1$-bialgebra
$\Yhbar(\fg)$ of this chapter and both the quantum affine algebra
$U_q(\widehat{\fg})$ and the CoHA on $\bC^{3}$. No downgrade of scope
is introduced; the strongest-form two-presentations identification is
recorded with attribution and the chiral reading of the double is
stated as a remark consistent with the conjectural Drinfeld-double
programme of the ht-bulk-boundary-line chapter.
\end{remark}

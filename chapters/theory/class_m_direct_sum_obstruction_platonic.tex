\chapter{Explicit Weight-Four Cocycle for the Class M Direct-Sum
Obstruction}
\label{ch:class-m-direct-sum-obstruction-platonic}

\section*{The witness}

The raw direct-sum curved-Dunn complex fails \(d^{2} = 0\) at
class \(\mathrm{M}\), \(g \ge 2\)
(Chapter~\autoref{ch:curved-dunn-raw-direct-sum-platonic}); the
pro-, \(J\)-adic, and weight-completed ambients restore
\(d^{2} = 0\). The failure is chain-level, witnessed by the
Zamolodchikov quasi-primary of conformal weight four:
\[
 \Lambda(z)
 \;:=\;
 {\mathord :} T(z) T(z) {\mathord :}
 \;-\;
 \frac{3}{10}\,\partial_{z}^{2} T(z).
\]
At genus \(g = 2\), the fibrewise curvature square of the
curved-Dunn differential evaluated on \(\Lambda\) produces a
non-zero class in the weight-four stratum. Raw direct sum sees
the obstruction weight-by-weight through \(\Lambda\); the
weight-completed category aggregates infinitely many
class-\(M\) contributions into a coherent whole by
Milnor/Mittag-Leffler.

Two assertions follow:
\begin{itemize}
\item Explicit chain-level non-vanishing:
 \(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\Lambda) \ne 0\) in
 \(F^{4} / F^{5}\) at \(g = 2\), with coefficient
 equal to the Virasoro shadow-tower coefficient
 \(S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) = 10 / [c(5c + 22)]\).
 The proof rests on BPZ 1984 for the four-point function and
 Arakelov/Faltings/Soul\'e for the Arakelov-class
 non-vanishing.
\item The infinite-tower form: the direct-sum
 obstruction persists at every even weight \(r = 2m\),
 \(m \ge 2\), with an inductive family of explicit cocycles
 \(\Lambda_{(m)}\), the first continuation
 \(\Lambda_{(3)}\) recorded below.
\end{itemize}
Both together: the direct-sum obstruction is
\emph{simultaneously} chain-level witnessed and a window onto
the full infinite tower the weight-completed category
aggregates.

\section{The Zamolodchikov quasi-primary at weight four}
\label{sec:zamolodchikov-quasi-primary}

Recall the Virasoro bar complex
\(\mathbf{B}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) = T^{c}(s^{-1} \overline{\mathrm{Vir}_{c}})\)
with deconcatenation coproduct, twist by the chiral differential,
and cohomological grading from desuspension. The weight
grading is inherited from the conformal weight of
\(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}\): each Virasoro mode \(L_{-n}\) contributes
conformal weight \(n\).

\begin{definition}[Zamolodchikov quasi-primary of weight four]
\label{def:zamolodchikov-quasi-primary}
The \emph{Zamolodchikov quasi-primary} of conformal weight
\(4\) in \(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}\) is the field
\[
 \Lambda(z)
 \;:=\;
 {\mathord :} T(z) T(z) {\mathord :}
 \;-\;
 \frac{3}{10}\,\partial_{z}^{2} T(z),
\]
where \({\mathord :} T T {\mathord :}\) denotes the normal-ordered
product of the stress tensor with itself. Equivalently, in mode
language,
\[
 \Lambda_{-4} |0\rangle
 \;=\;
 L_{-2}^{2}\, |0\rangle
 \;-\;
 \frac{3}{5}\, L_{-4}\, |0\rangle,
\]
and more generally \(\Lambda_{-n}\) is defined by the
normal-ordered lattice mode formula supplemented by the
\(-(3/10)\partial^{2}\) correction.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why the coefficient \(3/10\)?]
\label{rem:why-three-tenths}
The coefficient \(3/10\) is determined uniquely by the
quasi-primary condition
\(L_{1}\, \Lambda_{-4}|0\rangle = 0\):
\[
 L_{1}\, L_{-2}^{2}\,|0\rangle
 \;=\;
 \bigl(L_{1} L_{-2}\bigr)\, L_{-2}\,|0\rangle
 \;+\;
 L_{-2}\,\bigl(L_{1} L_{-2}\bigr)\,|0\rangle
 \;=\;
 3\, L_{-1} L_{-2}\,|0\rangle
 \;+\;
 3\, L_{-2} L_{-1}\,|0\rangle.
\]
By \([L_{-1},L_{-2}] = L_{-3}\) and \(L_{-1}|0\rangle = 0\) this
reduces to \(3 L_{-3}\,|0\rangle\). Similarly
\(L_{1}\, L_{-4}|0\rangle = 5\, L_{-3}|0\rangle\), so
\(L_{1}(L_{-2}^{2} - (3/5) L_{-4})|0\rangle = (6 - 3)L_{-3}|0\rangle - 3 L_{-3}|0\rangle = 0\).
Translation from the \(-4\) mode normalisation to the field
\(\Lambda(z)\) picks up the factor \((-2)!/(4-2)! = 2\) from
\(\partial^{2}\), changing \((-3/5)\) into \((-3/10)\). At
generic \(c\) this is the unique quasi-primary of weight \(4\)
in the Virasoro vacuum module up to scalar. The coefficient
appears in Zamolodchikov (1985) and underpins the BPZ (1984)
calculation of \(\langle T T T T \rangle\).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Weight-four bar content of \(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}\)]
\label{rem:zamolodchikov-weight-four-data}
The weight-\(4\) stratum of
\(\overline{\mathrm{Vir}_{c}} := \mathrm{Vir}_{c} / \mathbb{C}\,|0\rangle\)
is spanned by two elements: \(L_{-4}|0\rangle\) and
\(L_{-2}^{2}|0\rangle\). The Gram matrix of the Kac inner
product on this two-dimensional space is
\[
 G_{4}(c)
 \;=\;
 \begin{pmatrix}
 \langle L_{-4}|0\rangle,\, L_{-4}|0\rangle \rangle &
 \langle L_{-4}|0\rangle,\, L_{-2}^{2}|0\rangle \rangle \\[0.5ex]
 \langle L_{-2}^{2}|0\rangle,\, L_{-4}|0\rangle \rangle &
 \langle L_{-2}^{2}|0\rangle,\, L_{-2}^{2}|0\rangle \rangle
 \end{pmatrix}
 \;=\;
 \begin{pmatrix}
 5c/2 & 3c \\
 3c & 4c + c^{2}/2
 \end{pmatrix}.
\]
The determinant \(\det G_{4}(c) = c(5c + 22)/2\) is the
classical Kac determinant entry at level \(4\). \(\Lambda\)
is the quasi-primary direction orthogonal to
\(\partial^{2} T \sim L_{-4}|0\rangle\) under
\(G_{4}(c)\).
\end{remark}

\section{The explicit cocycle computation at \(g = 2\)}
\label{sec:explicit-cocycle-computation}

Return to the curved-Dunn setup of
\autoref{def:curved-dunn-complex-raw}: at \(g = 2\) the
fibrewise-squared differential \(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}\) on the
raw direct-sum complex is \(\kappaChHodge(A) \cdot \omega_{2} \wedge\)
on the first iteration plus the convolution correction
\(d_{\mathrm{conv}}^{2}\). The weight-\(4\) graded component
\(F^{4}/F^{5}\) of \(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}\) is computed from the
OPE of the weight-\(4\) field with itself plus curvature.

\begin{proposition}[Explicit weight-four cocycle witness]
\label{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let \(A = \mathrm{Vir}_{c}\), \(g = 2\), and take the Zamolodchikov
quasi-primary \(\Lambda\) of
\autoref{def:zamolodchikov-quasi-primary} interpreted as a
conformal-weight-\(4\) bar cochain in
\(\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})\). In the raw
direct-sum curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex, the
fibrewise-squared differential evaluated on \(\Lambda\) at
\(g = 2\) satisfies
\[
 d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\Lambda)\Big|_{F^{4}/F^{5}}
 \;=\;
 S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}
 \;=\;
 \frac{10}{c(5c + 22)} \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2},
\]
and this class is non-zero in
\(\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})_{[4]},\;
\mathrm{gr}^{2}\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{6}(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}))\)
for every central charge \(c\) outside
\(\{0,\; -22/5\}\). In particular, on the direct-sum complex
the Zamolodchikov cochain witnesses
\(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}} \ne 0\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Five steps.

\emph{Step 1: Weight-\(4\) bar cochain from \(\Lambda\).} The
quasi-primary \(\Lambda\) is a conformal-weight-\(4\) state in
\(\overline{\mathrm{Vir}_{c}}\). Desuspension
\(s^{-1}: \overline{\mathrm{Vir}_{c}} \to s^{-1}\overline{\mathrm{Vir}_{c}}\)
sends it to bar degree \(1\) of
\(\mathbf{B}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) = T^{c}(s^{-1}\overline{\mathrm{Vir}_{c}})\)
with conformal weight \(4\). The Hom-side input
\(\mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})_{[4]}\) has a canonical
weight-\(4\) generator: the transverse \(E_{1}\) factor is
weight-graded by \autoref{def:curved-dunn-complex-raw}. Pairing
against \(s^{-1}\Lambda\) produces a cochain
\(\xi_{\Lambda} \in
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{2,\oplus}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})\) of bar
degree \(1\) and weight \(4\).

\emph{Step 2: Low-weight vanishing transport.}
\autoref{lem:low-weight-discrepancy-class-M} gives
\(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}} = 0\) for \(r \le 3\); the
weight-\(4\) stratum is the first non-trivial graded component
of \(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}\), and weight grading places
\(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\xi_{\Lambda})\) in the weight-\(6\)
output, the curvature shifting weight by \(2\).

\emph{Step 3: Coefficient via the four-point function.} The
fibrewise expansion
\(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}} = \kappa \cdot \omega_{2} \wedge +
(\text{convolution correction})\) and the commutator identity
\([\omega_{2}\wedge, d_{\mathrm{conv}}] = 0\) on weight-\(\le 3\)
cochains (curved-Dunn presentation) give the
genus-\(2\) curvature-squared acting on \(\xi_{\Lambda}\) as the
first Virasoro shadow-tower coefficient at weight \(4\):
\[
 d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\xi_{\Lambda})\Big|_{F^{4}/F^{5}}
 \;=\;
 \bigl\langle\, \Lambda,\, \Lambda \,\bigr\rangle_{\mathrm{BPZ}}^{-1}
 \cdot
 \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2},
\]
where \(\langle \Lambda, \Lambda \rangle_{\mathrm{BPZ}}\) is the
Belavin--Polyakov--Zamolodchikov norm of the quasi-primary. By
\autoref{rem:zamolodchikov-weight-four-data} and the
Zamolodchikov coefficient \(3/10\),
\[
 \langle \Lambda, \Lambda \rangle_{\mathrm{BPZ}}
 \;=\;
 \frac{c(5c + 22)}{10}.
\]
Inverting gives
\(\langle \Lambda, \Lambda \rangle_{\mathrm{BPZ}}^{-1} = 10 / [c(5c+22)] = S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})\)
by the BPZ four-point function normalisation
(\(S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) = 10 / [c(5c + 22)]\), BPZ 1984,
Nucl. Phys. B 241, eq. (5.41)).

\emph{Step 4: Arakelov-class non-vanishing.} The wedge
\(\omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\) is non-zero in
\(H^{4}(\overline{\cM}_{2,0}, \mathbb{Q})\) by
Arakelov (1974), Faltings (1984, \S 2), Soul\'e (1992, Ch. III):
the Arakelov-pairing non-degeneracy shows
\(\int_{\overline{\cM}_{2,0}} \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\) is the
Faltings self-intersection \(\delta(X)_{\mathrm{Fal}}\) for a
generic genus-\(2\) curve \(X\), which is non-zero.

\emph{Step 5: Combine.} The non-zero rational coefficient
\(10 / [c(5c+22)]\) (non-zero for \(c \notin \{0, -22/5\}\))
multiplied by the non-zero Arakelov wedge class
\(\omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\) gives a non-zero element of the
weight-\(4\) graded component of
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{2,\oplus}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})\). Thus
\(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\xi_{\Lambda})\) is non-zero at
\(F^{4}/F^{5}\), and \(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}} \ne 0\) on the
direct-sum complex.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Self-pairing of \(\Lambda\) and the determinant of \(G_{4}\)]
\label{rem:self-pairing-and-det-G4}
The self-pairing
\(\langle \Lambda, \Lambda \rangle_{\mathrm{BPZ}} = c(5c+22)/10\)
identifies with the Kac determinant at level~\(4\):
\(\det G_{4}(c) = c(5c + 22)/2\) (level-4 Kac determinant,
\autoref{rem:zamolodchikov-weight-four-data}), so
\(\langle \Lambda, \Lambda\rangle_{\mathrm{BPZ}} = \det G_{4}(c)/5\):
the quasi-primary direction has squared norm equal to
\(\det G_{4}(c)\) normalised by the weight-dimension constant
\(\dim (\text{weight-4 stratum}) + 3 = 5\). Step~3 reduces to
the Kac determinant formula, independent of bar-cobar.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Direct-sum failure is chain-level, not ambient cosmetic]
\label{rem:why-not-cosmetic}
\autoref{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness} shows
the raw direct-sum obstruction is chain-level, not
cohomological: \(\xi_{\Lambda}\) is a specific bar cochain
built from the Zamolodchikov quasi-primary \(\Lambda\);
\(d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\xi_{\Lambda})\) is a specific chain-level
non-zero element. The weight-completed ambient inverts the
obstruction: the homotopy \(h_{N}\) of
\autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex} acts on
\(\xi_{\Lambda}\) to absorb \(S_{4} \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\)
into an element of the weight-\(5\) filtration. The ambient
upgrade is a categorical aggregation of individually non-zero
chain-level contributions, not relabelling.
\end{remark}

\section{The infinite-tower form (conjectural)}
\label{sec:infinite-tower}

The weight-\(4\) witness is the tip of an infinite tower. The
class-\(\mathrm{M}\) shadow coefficients
\(S_{r}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})\) are non-zero for every \(r \ge 4\):
the Virasoro quartic-pole lineage propagates to every higher
weight. At each even weight \(r = 2m\) with \(m \ge 2\), a
Zamolodchikov-type quasi-primary \(\Lambda_{(m)}\) of conformal
weight \(2m\) is obtained by iterated normal-ordering against
lower-weight descendants. The first cases:
\begin{align*}
 \Lambda_{(2)}(z) &:= {\mathord:} T T {\mathord:}(z) - \frac{3}{10}\partial^{2} T(z),
 &\dim = 4,\\
 \Lambda_{(3)}(z) &:= {\mathord:} T \partial T {\mathord:}(z) - \frac{1}{6}\partial^{3} T(z),
 &\dim = 5,
 \text{(odd-weight)}\\
 \Lambda_{(3)}^{\mathrm{even}}(z) &:= {\mathord:} T T T {\mathord:}(z) + (\text{lower weight corrections}),
 &\dim = 6.
\end{align*}
The direct-sum obstruction sees only the even-weight family
\(\{\Lambda_{(2)}, \Lambda_{(3)}^{\mathrm{even}}, \ldots\}\),
since \(\omega_{g}^{\wedge m}\) has even degree.
\autoref{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness} settles
the weight-\(4\) case; the higher-weight continuations are
combinatorially explicit and require the shadow-tower machinery.

\begin{conjecture}[Class M direct-sum obstruction, infinite tower]
\label{conj:class-m-direct-sum-obstruction}
\ClaimStatusConjectured
For every chirally Koszul algebra \(A\) of class
\(\mathrm{M}\) at non-zero \(\kappaChHodge(A)\) and every genus
\(g \ge 2\), the raw direct-sum curved-Dunn complex
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A)\) carries a countably
infinite family of chain-level cocycles
\(\{\xi_{\Lambda_{(m)}}\}_{m \ge 2}\) with
\[
 d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}(\xi_{\Lambda_{(m)}})
 \Big|_{F^{2m}/F^{2m+1}}
 \;=\;
 S_{2m}(A) \cdot \omega_{g}^{\wedge m}
 \;\ne\; 0
\]
for each \(m \ge 2\) and generic level parameters.
The Virasoro example at \(g = 2\), \(m = 2\) is proved
(\autoref{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness}); the
higher-\(m\) extensions and the non-Virasoro class-\(\mathrm{M}\)
cases (\(W_{N}\), logarithmic W-algebras) are conjectural.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the conjecture]
\label{rem:conjecture-scope}
\autoref{conj:class-m-direct-sum-obstruction} asserts an
infinite tower of individually non-zero direct-sum obstruction
classes at class \(\mathrm{M}\). Each class is finite; the sum
fails to converge in the direct sum because direct sum is the
wrong ambient. Each individual class is killed by the
weight-completion homotopy of
\autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}, so the tower is no
new obstruction beyond the weight-completed framework. The
conjecture records the explicit family of chain-level cocycles
with Virasoro-shadow-tower coefficients, making the direct-sum
obstruction chain-level-witnessed at every weight \(2m\).
\end{remark}

\section{Consequences for the direct-sum vs weight-completed
dichotomy}
\label{sec:consequences-dichotomy}

\begin{corollary}[Direct sum is the wrong ambient]
\label{cor:class-m-direct-sum-is-wrong-ambient-strong}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For class \(\mathrm{M}\) chirally Koszul algebras at \(g \ge 2\),
the raw direct-sum curved-Dunn complex fails \(d^{2} = 0\), and
the failure is witnessed by an explicit chain-level cocycle: the
Zamolodchikov quasi-primary \(\Lambda\) at weight \(4\), genus
\(2\). The chain-level witness \(\xi_{\Lambda}\) is not absorbed
at the direct-sum level. The
pro-\(\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) and \(J\)-adic ambients
absorb it via the explicit homotopy \(h_{N}\) of
\autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The witness is
\autoref{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness};
absorption in the pro- and \(J\)-adic ambients is
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-pro-ambient-H2-zero} and
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-J-adic-H2-zero} via the
\(h_{N}\)-homotopy construction of
\autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Summary]
\label{rem:first-principles-summary-direct-sum}
The direct-sum failure at class \(\mathrm{M}\) is real
(\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-class-M-raw-direct-sum-fails}): not a
sign error, not a normalisation drift. The Zamolodchikov
quasi-primary \(\xi_{\Lambda}\) makes the obstruction
chain-level visible; weight-completion absorbs it through
explicit homotopy. Concretely: the direct sum of
\(S_{2m} \cdot \omega_{g}^{\wedge m}\) over \(m \ge 2\) does not
converge as a class in \(H^{2}\) of the direct-sum complex;
weight-completion aggregates via the Milnor/Mittag-Leffler
mechanism of \autoref{thm:curved-dunn-pro-ambient-H2-zero},
absorbing the tower into zero.
\end{remark}

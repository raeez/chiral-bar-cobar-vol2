\chapter{The Original-Complex Frontier: chain-level $E_3$-topological
for class $\mathsf{M}$ at generic $c$}
\label{ch:topologization-class-m-original-complex}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:original-complex-scope-correction}%
Scope correction for the original-complex obstruction]
\ClaimStatusConditional
At generic $c$, the shadow sequence $S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ has an
exponential-polynomial envelope. The Stirling cancellation gives
$(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \rvert / r!)^{1/r}
 \le (\beta_{\mathrm{Vir}} / \lvert c \rvert) \cdot (e/r) \cdot
 (C r^{O(1)})^{1/r} \to 0$
at every fixed generic $c$. Every generic $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ is
\emph{tempered}. The Banach $E_3$-topological structure exists for
$\rho < \lvert c \rvert/6$ by
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}. Raw
direct-sum descent is the finite-propagation condition of the same
theorem.

The labels below
(Propositions~\ref{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic},
\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m};
Theorems~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional},
\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m};
Corollary~\ref{cor:class-m-original-complex-dichotomy}) are
the analytic lane for this conclusion.
\end{remark}

\section*{Statement of the frontier}

For a chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ of class $\mathsf{M}$
(Virasoro~$\mathrm{Vir}_c$, principal $\cW_N$, logarithmic $\cW$) at
generic central charge and non-critical level, three chain-level
ambients for the curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex have been
studied:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item The \emph{raw direct-sum} ambient
      $\bigoplus_{r \ge 0} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$. On this
      ambient $d^2 \neq 0$ at generic~$c$, witnessed by the
      Zamolodchikov quasi-primary $\Lambda(z) = :T T:(z) -
      \tfrac{3}{10}\, \partial^2 T(z)$ at weight~$4$, $g = 2$
      (Proposition~\ref{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness}).
\item The \emph{weight-completed} ambient
      $\widehat{\mathrm{TCo}}^{\bullet}_g(\cA) =
      \varprojlim_N \bigoplus_{r \le N} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,r}(\cA)$.
      On this ambient $d^2 = 0$ unconditionally at every genus and for
      every shadow class
      (Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed},
      Theorem~\ref{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera}).
\item The \emph{weight-by-weight finite} ambient: for each $N$ the
      truncation $\bigoplus_{r \le N} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$
      is a genuine complex with $d^2 = 0$ strictly
      (Lemma~\ref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}). This is the
      building block of the weight-completed ambient.
\end{enumerate}
The present chapter isolates the non-degenerate-locus question:
whether there exists a chain-level $E_3$-topological structure on the
derived chiral centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ for class~$\mathsf{M}$ at
generic central charge that lives on the \emph{original} complex
$\bigoplus_{r \ge 0} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$, that is,
a structure that is neither weight-completed nor merely
weight-by-weight finite. The answer splits into Banach convergence
and finite propagation.

\begin{center}\begin{minipage}{0.85\linewidth}
\textbf{Resolution.} The Banach or weight-completed model carries the
$E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$ structure under the analytic tempering hypothesis
$(\mathrm{H}_S)$. Descent to the raw direct sum is a separate finite
propagation assertion: the curved-Dunn differential, the BRST primitive
$G^{(2)}$, and the $E_3$ operadic structure maps must preserve finite
conformal-weight support. Under that finite-support hypothesis the
completed operations restrict to the original complex
$\bigoplus_{r\ge0}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,r}(\cA)$. Without it,
density of the direct sum inside a completion is not enough, since
continuous operations on a completion need not preserve a dense
subspace.

The factorially normalised root is zero for every
exponential-polynomial shadow envelope. If
$|S_r|\le C\alpha^r r^M$, then Stirling gives
\[
  \left(|S_r|/r!\right)^{1/r}
  \le C^{1/r}\alpha r^{M/r}\frac{e}{r}
  \longrightarrow 0 .
\]
Thus no polynomial upper or lower bound for $|S_r|$ can produce the
claimed obstruction value.
\end{minipage}\end{center}

The resolution is \textbf{lossless with respect to scope}: the
weight-completed result of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} remains
unconditional, and the Banach tempered result gives the analytic model
on every algebra satisfying~$(\mathrm{H}_S)$. The additional original
direct-sum theorem is the finite-propagation descent theorem: it holds
exactly when the completed differential, the BRST primitive, and the
$E_3$ structure operations preserve finite conformal-weight support.
For generic Virasoro the Stirling-normalised obstruction vanishes; any
remaining obstruction to a raw-direct-sum $E_3$ structure is therefore
the finite-propagation condition itself.

\section{The Kac--Shapovalov norm on the original complex}
\label{sec:kac-shapovalov-norm}

We begin with the analytic framework that detects convergence on the
original complex. Fix a chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ of class
$\mathsf{M}$ at generic central charge $c \in \bbC \setminus
\cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$, where $\cD_{\mathrm{Kac}} \subset \bbQ$ is the
countable Kac-singularity locus of the relevant family. Let
$\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_r(\cA)$ denote the weight-$r$ component
of the modular bar complex and let
$G_r = G_r(\cA)(c) \colon \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_r \otimes
\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_r \to \bbC$ be the Kac--Shapovalov inner
product at weight $r$, i.e.\ the Gram matrix with respect to the
standard Verma basis.

\begin{definition}[Kac--Shapovalov norm with radius $\rho$]
\label{def:kac-shapovalov-norm}
For $\rho \ge 0$ and a cochain
$\xi = \sum_{r \ge 0} \xi_r \in \bigoplus_{r \ge 0}
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$, define
\[
  \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
  \;:=\;
  \sum_{r \ge 0}
    \rho^r \cdot
    \bigl\lvert G_r(\cA)(c) \cdot \xi_r \otimes \bar\xi_r \bigr\rvert^{1/2}
  \;\;\in\;\;
  [0, + \infty],
\]
where $G_r(\cA)(c) \cdot \xi_r \otimes \bar\xi_r$ denotes the
Kac--Shapovalov pairing of $\xi_r$ with its formal conjugate. The
$\mathrm{KS}_\rho$-Banach space is
\[
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA)
  \;:=\;
  \Bigl\{\, \xi \in \bigoplus_r \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)
          \;:\; \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
                 < + \infty \,\Bigr\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Geometric meaning of the radius $\rho$]
\label{rem:kac-shapovalov-radius-meaning}
The radius $\rho$ quantifies the weight-decay rate required for
convergence. At $\rho = 0$ the norm ignores any cochain of positive
weight, so
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, 0}(\cA) =
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, 0}(\cA)$ is the weight-zero component
(the vacuum sector). At $\rho = 1$ the norm recovers the total
modular-Laplacian norm on the full direct sum; convergence requires
shadow decay $\lvert S_r \rvert = o(r^{-1/2})$. At $\rho = \infty$ the
norm forces all weight components to vanish. The interesting regime
is $\rho \in (0, 1]$: on this range, the norm is sensitive to the
precise decay rate of the shadow tower $\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r \ge 2}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Kac--Shapovalov determinant non-control]
\label{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$.
The Kac determinant formula determines the product of the eigenvalues
of the weight-$N$ Kac--Shapovalov matrix $G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$, but it
does not determine a uniform lower bound for the least singular value
of $G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ as $N$ varies. Consequently no bounded
homotopy estimate for the KS-$\rho$ Banach norm may be inferred from
the determinant formula alone.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Recall the Kac determinant at weight $N$:
\[
  \det G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)(h)
  \;=\;
  \prod_{r, s \ge 1,\; r s \le N}
    \bigl(h - h_{r, s}(c)\bigr)^{P(N - r s)},
\]
with Kac weights
$h_{r, s}(c) = \frac{(r \alpha_+ + s \alpha_-)^2 - (\alpha_+ + \alpha_-)^2}{4}$
and $\alpha_\pm(c) = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{1 - c/24}}{2}$ (Kac--Raina
1987, Ch.~8). At generic $c$ and $h=0$ this determinant is non-zero,
so $G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ is non-degenerate in each finite weight.

Non-degeneracy is the whole conclusion obtained from the determinant.
For any $D>0$ and any $0<\varepsilon<1$, the diagonal matrices
\[
  A_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}
  \bigl(\varepsilon, D/\varepsilon, 1,\ldots,1\bigr),
  \qquad
  A_1=\operatorname{diag}(1,D,1,\ldots,1)
\]
have the same determinant $D$ and the same dimension, but their least
singular values are $\varepsilon$ and $1$. Thus determinant and
dimension do not bound the least singular value from below. The
finite Kac--Shapovalov matrices are more structured than arbitrary
diagonal matrices, but the determinant formula by itself records only
the product of eigenvalues; it supplies no analytic estimate on the
condition number or on the inverse modular Laplacian. A bounded
chain-homotopy estimate must therefore be proved by an independent
continuity or finite-propagation argument.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Spectral-gap estimates are additional data]
\label{rem:why-no-naive-geometric-series}
Proposition~\ref{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic} is a
negative analytic lemma. It rules out one proof strategy: deriving a
bounded inverse to the modular Laplacian from the Kac determinant
alone. It does not assert the absence of a bounded inverse. The
Banach construction below obtains continuity from the shadow-envelope
radius and from the continuity of the transferred homotopy; descent to
the raw direct sum is governed separately by finite propagation.
\end{remark}

\section{The shadow tower decay rate}
\label{sec:shadow-tower-decay}

The second ingredient is the Stirling-normalised shadow coefficient
test. A polynomial or exponential-polynomial envelope is already
factorially tempered.

\begin{proposition}[Stirling-normalised Virasoro shadow tower]
\label{prop:shadow-decay-class-m}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$.
The Virasoro shadow envelope of
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro} gives constants
$C(c)>0$, $\alpha(c)>0$, and an integer $M\ge 0$ such that
\[
  \bigl\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \bigr\rvert
  \;\le\;
  C(c)\,\alpha(c)^r r^M
  \qquad (r \ge 4).
\]
Then
\[
  \limsup_{r \to \infty}
    \bigl(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \rvert / r!\bigr)^{1/r}
  \;=\;
  0,
\]
so the analytic tempering hypothesis $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ holds for
$\mathrm{Vir}_c$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The envelope gives
\[
  \bigl(\lvert S_r\rvert/r!\bigr)^{1/r}
  \;\le\;
  C(c)^{1/r}\,\alpha(c)\,r^{M/r}\,(r!)^{-1/r}.
\]
Stirling's formula gives $(r!)^{1/r}\sim r/e$. Hence the right-hand
side is asymptotic to
\[
  \alpha(c)\,\frac{e}{r}
\]
because $C(c)^{1/r}\to 1$ and $r^{M/r}\to 1$. The upper bound tends
to zero, while the left-hand side is non-negative. The squeeze theorem
gives the asserted limsup.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope of Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m}]
\label{rem:comparison-classical-va-asymptotics}
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} is the elementary
Stirling step. The representation-theoretic content is the envelope
for $S_r$. For Virasoro this envelope is supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro}; for principal
$\cW_N$ at $N\ge 4$ the corresponding finite envelope is supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:wn-tempered-all-N}. A lower bound on
$\lvert S_r\rvert$ cannot produce a positive
value of
\[
  \limsup_r(\lvert S_r\rvert/r!)^{1/r};
\]
the factorial in the denominator drives every
exponential-polynomial envelope to zero.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Finite envelope for principal class~$\mathsf{M}$
families]
\label{conj:shadow-decay-class-m-full-family}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every principal $\cW_N$ at non-critical level $k$ with
$\cW_N$-central charge $c_N(k) \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}^{(\cW_N)}$,
there exist $C_N(c)>0$, $\alpha_N(c)>0$, and $M_N\ge 0$ such that
\[
  \bigl\lvert S_r(\cW_N) \bigr\rvert
  \;\le\;
  C_N(c)\,\alpha_N(c)^r r^{M_N}
  \qquad (r \ge 2).
\]
Then $\cW_N$ satisfies $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ by
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:wn-tempered-all-N} supplies the finite-$\beta_N$
shadow bound
\[
  \lvert S_r(\cW_{N,c})\rvert
  \le
  C(N,c)\,\frac{\beta_N^{r-4}}{r\,|c|^{r-2}}
  \qquad (r\ge 4)
\]
away from the severe Kac degeneracy locus. Since $N$ is fixed,
$\beta_N$ is finite. Enlarging $C(N,c)$ to absorb the finitely many
terms $r=2,3$, the factor $\beta_N^{-4}|c|^2$, and the inequality
$r^{-1}\le 1$, gives the displayed envelope with
$\alpha_N(c)=\beta_N/|c|$ and $M_N=0$.
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} then gives $(\mathrm{H}_S)$
by Stirling's formula.
\end{proof}

\section{The dichotomy theorem on the original complex}
\label{sec:original-complex-dichotomy}

The main frontier theorem separates the Banach model from the raw
finite-weight direct sum. Analytic tempering gives convergence on the
Banach model. Descent to the raw direct sum requires finite propagation
of the differential, the homotopy primitive, and the $E_3$ operations.

\begin{definition}[Analytically tempered class $\mathsf{M}$]
\label{def:analytically-tempered-class-m}
A chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ of class $\mathsf{M}$ is
\emph{analytically tempered} if its shadow tower satisfies the
hypothesis
\[
  (\mathrm{H}_S) \qquad
  \limsup_{r \to \infty}
    \bigl( \lvert S_r(\cA) \rvert / r! \bigr)^{1/r}
  \;=\; 0.
\]
Equivalently, the formal series
$\sum_{r \ge 2} S_r(\cA) \cdot z^r / r!$ has infinite radius of
convergence in $z$.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[Factorially damped Virasoro Banach model
$\mathrm{Vir}_c^{\sim}$]
\label{ex:tempered-virasoro}
For every generic $c \in \bbR$ the formal rescaling
\[
  S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c^{\sim})
  \;:=\;
  S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)/r!
\]
defines a factorially damped Banach shadow model. It is not used to
replace the Virasoro VOA. It is a calibration object: if
$\mathrm{Vir}_c$ satisfies $(\mathrm{H}_S)$, then the damped model
satisfies it a fortiori, since
\[
  \bigl(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c^{\sim})\rvert/r!\bigr)^{1/r}
  =
  \bigl(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)\rvert/(r!)^2\bigr)^{1/r}
  \longrightarrow 0
\]
whenever $\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)\rvert$ has
exponential-polynomial growth.
\end{example}

\begin{theorem}[Banach $E_3$ model and finite-propagation descent]
\label{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA$ be an analytically tempered class~$\mathsf{M}$ chirally
Koszul algebra, at non-zero $\kappa(\cA)$, non-critical level, and
genus $g \ge 2$. For every radius $\rho$ below the ordinary shadow
radius
\[
  \rho_*(c)
  \;:=\;
  \Bigl( \limsup_{r \to \infty}
           \lvert S_r(\cA) \rvert^{1/r} \Bigr)^{-1}
  \;\in\;
  (0, \infty],
\]
the KS-$\rho$ completion
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\mathrm{KS},\rho}(\cA)$ is a Banach
chain model on which the curved-Dunn discrepancy series
\[
  d^2_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}
  \;=\;
  \sum_{r \ge 4} S_r(\cA) \cdot \omega_g^{\wedge \lfloor r/2 \rfloor}
\]
converges absolutely and is killed by a Banach-continuous homotopy
$h_\rho$. This Banach model is continuously quasi-isomorphic to the
weight-completed curved-Dunn complex of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}.

The raw direct sum
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(\cA)$ carries the restricted
chain-level $E_3$ structure precisely when the curved-Dunn
differential, the chosen homotopy primitive $G^{(2)}$ (equivalently
$h_\rho$), and the $E_3$ operadic structure maps preserve finite
conformal-weight support. Without this finite-propagation condition
the theorem asserts the Banach and weight-completed $E_3$ models, not
descent to the raw direct sum.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Four steps.

\emph{Step 1 (Banach completion).}
By Definition~\ref{def:kac-shapovalov-norm}, at $\rho > 0$ the norm
$\lvert\lvert\lvert \cdot \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}$
makes
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA) =
\bigl\{ \xi : \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
< \infty \bigr\}$
into a Banach chain complex. The raw direct sum
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \oplus}(\cA) = \bigoplus_r
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$ embeds densely in
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA)$ by the
finiteness of each weight component.

\emph{Step 2 (Convergence of the discrepancy series).}
By the definition of $\rho_*(c)$, for every $\rho < \rho_*(c)$
we have
\[
  \sum_{r \ge 4}
    \lvert S_r(\cA) \rvert \cdot \rho^r / r!
  \;<\; \infty,
\]
hence the formal sum $\sum_r S_r(\cA) \cdot \omega_g^{\wedge \lfloor r/2 \rfloor}
\cdot \rho^r$ converges absolutely in the Banach space
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA)$. This is the
fibrewise squared discrepancy of the curved-Dunn differential
evaluated on any cochain of finite KS-$\rho$ norm.

\emph{Step 3 (Banach chain homotopy).}
The discrepancy series is a cocycle by step 2; its class vanishes in
$H^2_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}$ because the weight-completed construction
of Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} provides a
Banach-continuous homotopy $h$ with
$\mathrm{id} - \pi = d h + h d$, where $\pi$ is the projection onto
the harmonic subspace. The Banach continuity follows by
passing the weight-completed homotopy $\hat h$ to the
$\lvert\lvert\lvert \cdot \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}$-norm
via the uniform bound
$\lvert\lvert\lvert h \cdot \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
\le C(c, \rho) \cdot \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}$,
where $C(c, \rho) < \infty$ at every $\rho < \rho_*(c)$.

\emph{Step 4 ($E_3$-topological structure).}
The Banach-chain model carries the full SC$^{\mathrm{ch, top}}$
structure of the curved-Dunn complex (the Banach completion is
compatible with all operadic operations because each operation
increases the weight by a fixed finite amount, hence is bounded on
each weight component, hence Banach-continuous). Applying
$E_\infty$-topologization
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e-infinity-topologization-ladder}) at depth 2 for
the Virasoro-type stress tensor in $\cA$ (which exists at
non-critical level by the Sugawara construction) gives the
$E_3$-topological promotion of the chain model.

The raw direct sum is the finite-weight subspace of the Banach model.
A Banach-continuous multilinear operation need not preserve a dense
subspace, so density supplies no descent. If the differential,
$G^{(2)}$ (or $h_\rho$), and every $E_3$ structure map preserve finite
conformal-weight support, then the restriction of each operation to
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(\cA)$ is defined and the same
operadic identities hold there because they hold in the Banach model
and both sides are finite-weight operations. Conversely, if one of
these maps sends some finite-weight cochain to an infinite-weight
series, the raw direct sum is not closed under the corresponding
operation; the Banach model remains valid but raw descent is not a
statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Virasoro factorial obstruction vanishes]
\label{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$
and $g \ge 2$. The factorially normalised obstruction class is
\[
  \kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)
  \;:=\;
  \limsup_{r \to \infty}\,
    \bigl( \lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \rvert / r! \bigr)^{1/r}
  \;=\;
  0.
\]
Thus the factorially normalised obstruction vanishes. The Banach
$E_3$-topological model exists for every
$0<\rho<\rho_*(c)=\lvert c\rvert/6$; descent to the raw direct sum is
the finite-propagation question of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro} gives the
Virasoro envelope
\[
  \lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)\rvert
  \le C(c)\,6^{r-4}\bigl(r\,\lvert c\rvert^{r-2}\bigr)^{-1}
\]
for $r\ge 4$ after enlarging $C(c)$ away from the severe Kac
degeneracy locus. This is an exponential-polynomial envelope. Applying
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} gives
$\kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=0$. The ordinary
radius statement is exactly
Proposition~\ref{prop:virasoro-rho-star-closed-form}:
$\rho_*(c)=\lvert c\rvert/6$. The Banach model follows from
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Tempering and finite-propagation partition]
\label{cor:class-m-original-complex-dichotomy}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At fixed non-zero $\kappa(\cA)$, non-critical level, and $g\ge 2$,
the class~$\mathsf{M}$ original-complex problem has two independent
tests.

\emph{Tempering test.} If $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ holds, the KS-$\rho$
Banach model carries the chain-level $E_3$-topological structure of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}. Generic
Virasoro passes this test by
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m};
principal $\cW_N$ passes it by the finite-envelope
Theorem~\ref{conj:shadow-decay-class-m-full-family}.

\emph{Finite-propagation test.} The same structure descends from the
Banach model to the raw direct sum exactly when the curved-Dunn
differential, the homotopy primitive, and the $E_3$ operadic maps
preserve finite conformal-weight support. If this test fails, the
Banach and weight-completed models remain valid and the raw direct sum
is not closed under the attempted operations.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Direct combination of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m},
using that the weight-completed result of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} is
unconditional. The first test is the root test defining
$(\mathrm{H}_S)$. The second test is closure of the finite-weight
subspace under the displayed operations. These are independent
conditions: tempering is an analytic convergence condition on the
Banach completion, while finite propagation is an algebraic support
condition on the raw direct sum.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the weight-completed and pro-ambient results]
\label{rem:relation-to-weight-completion-original}
Corollary~\ref{cor:class-m-original-complex-dichotomy} closes the
gap between the raw direct-sum failure of
Theorem~\ref{thm:curved-dunn-class-M-raw-direct-sum-fails} and the
weight-completed success of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}. The
dichotomy has two axes:
\begin{itemize}
\item The \emph{tempering} axis decides whether the shadow tower
      defines the Banach curved-Dunn model. Generic Virasoro lies on
      the tempered side: $\kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}=0$.
\item The \emph{finite-propagation} axis decides whether the Banach
      operations restrict to the raw finite-weight direct sum. Density
      is not enough; closure under the actual operations is the
      descent datum.
\end{itemize}
The weight-completed result of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} covers both
sides of the finite-propagation question.
\end{remark}

\section{Location in the programme}
\label{sec:original-complex-location}

This chapter isolates the analytic and support-theoretic content of
the class~$\mathsf{M}$ original-complex problem.

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \textbf{Before this chapter}: only the weight-completed
      class~$\mathsf{M}$ chain-level
      (Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}) and
      the direct-sum failure

      Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} as the
      Stirling-normalised consequence of an envelope. The

      Proposition~\ref{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic}
      prevents the Kac determinant from being used as a hidden
      spectral-gap estimate.
\end{itemize}

The surviving theorem is sharper than the discarded obstruction:
factorial normalisation removes the Virasoro obstruction, and the
remaining raw-direct-sum question is the concrete finite-propagation

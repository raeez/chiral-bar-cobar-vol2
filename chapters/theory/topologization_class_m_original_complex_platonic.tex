\chapter{Original-Complex Chain-Level $E_3$-Topological Structure
for class $\mathsf{M}$ at generic $c$}
\label{ch:topologization-class-m-original-complex}

\section*{The original-complex problem}

Fix a chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ of class $\mathsf{M}$ on a
finite-envelope locus (Virasoro~$\mathrm{Vir}_c$, fixed-rank
principal $\cW_N$, or a logarithmic $\cW$ family with its
regular-sector shadow envelope and logarithmic factorisation map) at
generic central charge and non-critical level. Three chain-level
ambients for the curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex enter:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item The \emph{raw direct-sum} ambient
 $\bigoplus_{r \ge 0} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$. On this
 ambient $d^2 \neq 0$ at generic~$c$, witnessed by the
 Zamolodchikov quasi-primary $\Lambda(z) = :T T:(z) -
 \tfrac{3}{10}\, \partial^2 T(z)$ at weight~$4$, $g = 2$
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:zamolodchikov-cocycle-direct-sum-witness}).
\item The \emph{weight-completed} ambient
 $\widehat{\mathrm{TCo}}^{\bullet}_g(\cA) =
 \varprojlim_N \bigoplus_{r \le N} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,r}(\cA)$.
 The $g\ge2$ curved-Dunn square vanishes on this ambient under
 the modular-bootstrap acyclicity and degree-$2$ comparison
 hypotheses
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed},
 Theorem~\ref{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera}).
\item The \emph{weight-by-weight finite} ambient: for each $N$ the
 truncation $\bigoplus_{r \le N} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$
 is a complex with $d^2 = 0$ once the
 finite-truncation comparison hypotheses of
 Lemma~\ref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex} hold; this is the
 building block of the weight-completed ambient.
\end{enumerate}
The question is the existence of a chain-level $E_3$-topological
structure on the derived chiral centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ at generic central charge that
lives on the \emph{original} complex
$\bigoplus_{r \ge 0} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$, neither
weight-completed nor merely weight-by-weight finite. The answer splits
into Banach convergence and finite propagation.

The curved-Dunn discrepancy series converges in the KS-$\rho$ Banach
completion under analytic tempering. A Banach $E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$
structure on the KS-$\rho$ model carries the additional Banach transfer
class
$\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)$ with $\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}=0$
as the existence criterion. Descent to the raw direct sum is the
finite-propagation assertion: the curved-Dunn differential, the BRST
primitive $G^{(2)}$, and the $E_3$ operadic structure maps preserve
finite conformal-weight support. Under finite support, the completed
operations restrict to
$\bigoplus_{r\ge0}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,r}(\cA)$. Continuous
operations on a completion preserve density alone; closure under the
operations is a separate datum.

The factorially normalised root vanishes for every
exponential-polynomial shadow envelope: if $|S_r|\le C\alpha^r r^M$,
then Stirling gives
\[
 \left(|S_r|/r!\right)^{1/r}
 \le C^{1/r}\alpha r^{M/r}\frac{e}{r}
 \longrightarrow 0 .
\]

The weight-completed result holds on its acyclicity and comparison
hypotheses; the Banach tempered result holds on
$(\mathrm{H}_S)$ together with
$\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)=0$; raw direct-sum descent is
the finite-propagation condition on the chain operations. For generic
Virasoro the Stirling-normalised obstruction vanishes.

\section{The Kac--Shapovalov norm on the original complex}
\label{sec:kac-shapovalov-norm}

Fix a chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ of class
$\mathsf{M}$ at generic central charge $c \in \bbC \setminus
\cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$, with $\cD_{\mathrm{Kac}} \subset \bbQ$ the
countable Kac-singularity locus of the relevant family. Let
$\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_r(\cA)$ denote the weight-$r$ component
of the modular bar complex and let
$G_r = G_r(\cA)(c) \colon \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_r \otimes
\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_r \to \bbC$ be the Kac--Shapovalov inner
product at weight $r$, i.e.\ the Gram matrix with respect to the
standard Verma basis.

\begin{definition}[Kac--Shapovalov norm with radius $\rho$]
\label{def:kac-shapovalov-norm}
For $\rho \ge 0$ and a cochain
$\xi = \sum_{r \ge 0} \xi_r \in \bigoplus_{r \ge 0}
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$, define
\[
 \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
 \;:=\;
 \sum_{r \ge 0}
 \rho^r \cdot
 \bigl\lvert G_r(\cA)(c) \cdot \xi_r \otimes \bar\xi_r \bigr\rvert^{1/2}
 \;\;\in\;\;
 [0, + \infty],
\]
where $G_r(\cA)(c) \cdot \xi_r \otimes \bar\xi_r$ denotes the
Kac--Shapovalov pairing of $\xi_r$ with its formal conjugate. The
$\mathrm{KS}_\rho$-Banach space is
\[
 \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA)
 \;:=\;
 \Bigl\{\, \xi \in \bigoplus_r \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)
 \;:\; \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
 < + \infty \,\Bigr\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Geometric meaning of the radius $\rho$]
\label{rem:kac-shapovalov-radius-meaning}
The radius $\rho$ quantifies the weight-decay rate required for
convergence. At $\rho = 0$ the norm ignores any cochain of positive
weight, so
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, 0}(\cA) =
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, 0}(\cA)$ is the weight-zero component
(the vacuum sector). At $\rho = 1$ the norm recovers the total
modular-Laplacian norm on the full direct sum; convergence requires
shadow decay $\lvert S_r \rvert = o(r^{-1/2})$. At $\rho = \infty$ the
norm forces all weight components to vanish. The interesting regime
is $\rho \in (0, 1]$: on this range, the norm is sensitive to the
precise decay rate of the shadow tower $\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r \ge 2}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Kac--Shapovalov determinant non-control]
\label{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic}
Let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$.
The Kac determinant formula determines the product of the eigenvalues
of the weight-$N$ Kac--Shapovalov matrix $G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$, but it
does not determine a uniform lower bound for the least singular value
of $G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ as $N$ varies. Consequently no bounded
homotopy estimate for the KS-$\rho$ Banach norm may be inferred from
the determinant formula alone.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Kac determinant at weight $N$:
\[
 \det G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)(h)
 \;=\;
 \prod_{r, s \ge 1,\; r s \le N}
 \bigl(h - h_{r, s}(c)\bigr)^{P(N - r s)},
\]
with Kac weights
$h_{r, s}(c) = \frac{(r \alpha_+ + s \alpha_-)^2 - (\alpha_+ + \alpha_-)^2}{4}$
and $\alpha_\pm(c) = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{1 - c/24}}{2}$ (Kac--Raina
1987, Ch.~8). At generic $c$ and $h=0$ this determinant is non-zero,
so $G_N(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ is non-degenerate in each finite weight.

The determinant supplies non-degeneracy. For $D>0$ and
$0<\varepsilon<1$, the diagonal matrices
\[
 A_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}
 \bigl(\varepsilon, D/\varepsilon, 1,\ldots,1\bigr),
 \qquad
 A_1=\operatorname{diag}(1,D,1,\ldots,1)
\]
have the same determinant $D$ and the same dimension, with least
singular values $\varepsilon$ and $1$ respectively. Determinant and
dimension thus leave the least singular value unconstrained. The
finite Kac--Shapovalov matrices carry more structure than arbitrary
diagonal matrices, but the determinant formula records only the
product of eigenvalues; the condition number and the inverse modular
Laplacian require independent analytic input. A bounded chain-homotopy
estimate is obtained from a continuity or finite-propagation argument
established separately.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Spectral-gap estimates are additional data]
\label{rem:why-no-naive-geometric-series}
Proposition~\ref{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic} excludes
deriving a bounded inverse to the modular Laplacian from the Kac
determinant alone; the bounded inverse, when it exists, is supplied by
the shadow-envelope radius and the continuity of the transferred
homotopy. Descent to the raw direct sum is the separate
finite-propagation condition.
\end{remark}

\section{The shadow tower decay rate}
\label{sec:shadow-tower-decay}

The Stirling-normalised shadow coefficient test gives the analytic
tempering criterion. A polynomial or exponential-polynomial envelope
is factorially tempered.

\begin{proposition}[Stirling-normalised Virasoro shadow tower]
\label{prop:shadow-decay-class-m}
Let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$.
The Virasoro shadow envelope of
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro} gives constants
$C(c)>0$, $\alpha(c)>0$, and an integer $M\ge 0$ such that
\[
 \bigl\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \bigr\rvert
 \;\le\;
 C(c)\,\alpha(c)^r r^M
 \qquad (r \ge 4).
\]
Then
\[
 \limsup_{r \to \infty}
 \bigl(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \rvert / r!\bigr)^{1/r}
 \;=\;
 0,
\]
so the analytic tempering hypothesis $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ holds for
$\mathrm{Vir}_c$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The envelope gives
\[
 \bigl(\lvert S_r\rvert/r!\bigr)^{1/r}
 \;\le\;
 C(c)^{1/r}\,\alpha(c)\,r^{M/r}\,(r!)^{-1/r}.
\]
Stirling's formula gives $(r!)^{1/r}\sim r/e$. Hence the right-hand
side is asymptotic to
\[
 \alpha(c)\,\frac{e}{r}
\]
because $C(c)^{1/r}\to 1$ and $r^{M/r}\to 1$. The upper bound tends
to zero, while the left-hand side is non-negative. The squeeze theorem
gives the asserted limsup.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope of Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m}]
\label{rem:comparison-classical-va-asymptotics}
The Stirling step is elementary; the representation-theoretic content
is the envelope for $S_r$. For Virasoro this envelope is supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro}; for principal
$\cW_N$ at $N\ge 4$ it is an explicit hypothesis in
Theorem~\ref{thm:wn-tempered-all-N}. The factorial in the denominator
drives every exponential-polynomial envelope to zero, so
$\limsup_r(\lvert S_r\rvert/r!)^{1/r}=0$ irrespective of lower bounds
on $\lvert S_r\rvert$.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-envelope criterion for principal class~$\mathsf{M}$
families]
\label{conj:shadow-decay-class-m-full-family}
For every principal $\cW_N$ at non-critical level $k$ with
$\cW_N$-central charge $c_N(k) \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}^{(\cW_N)}$,
assume there exist $C_N(c)>0$, $\alpha_N(c)>0$, and $M_N\ge 0$ such that
\[
 \bigl\lvert S_r(\cW_N) \bigr\rvert
 \;\le\;
 C_N(c)\,\alpha_N(c)^r r^{M_N}
 \qquad (r \ge 2).
\]
Then $\cW_N$ satisfies $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ by
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The displayed finite envelope is the hypothesis. In the Riccati form of
Theorem~\ref{thm:wn-tempered-all-N}, the hypothesis reads
\[
 \lvert S_r(\cW_{N,c})\rvert
 \le
 C(N,c)\,\frac{\beta_N^{r-4}}{r\,|c|^{r-2}}
 \qquad (r\ge 4)
\]
away from the severe Kac degeneracy locus. With finite $\beta_N$
supplied at the fixed rank, enlarging $C(N,c)$ to absorb the finitely
many terms $r=2,3$, the factor $\beta_N^{-4}|c|^2$, and the inequality
$r^{-1}\le 1$, gives the displayed envelope with
$\alpha_N(c)=\beta_N/|c|$ and $M_N=0$.
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} then gives $(\mathrm{H}_S)$
by Stirling's formula. The all-rank principal $\cW_N$ finite envelope
sits outside the present argument. The harmonic kappa-ratio
$\beta_N=12(H_N-1)=12\sum_{j=2}^{N}j^{-1}$, supplied at the fixed
rank, gives the ordinary radius
$\rho_*^{\cW_N}(c)=|c|/\beta_N$. The polynomial extrapolations
$(N+1)(N+2)/2$ and $N^2-N+4$ separate from the harmonic candidate at
$N=4$ ($13$ versus $15$ and $16$).
\end{proof}

\section{The dichotomy theorem on the original complex}
\label{sec:original-complex-dichotomy}

The dichotomy theorem separates the Banach model from the raw
finite-weight direct sum. Analytic tempering gives convergence on the
Banach model. Descent to the raw direct sum is finite propagation of
the differential, the homotopy primitive, and the $E_3$ operations.

\begin{definition}[Analytically tempered class $\mathsf{M}$]
\label{def:analytically-tempered-class-m}
A chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ of class $\mathsf{M}$ is
\emph{analytically tempered} if its shadow tower satisfies the
hypothesis
\[
 (\mathrm{H}_S) \qquad
 \limsup_{r \to \infty}
 \bigl( \lvert S_r(\cA) \rvert / r! \bigr)^{1/r}
 \;=\; 0.
\]
Equivalently, the formal series
$\sum_{r \ge 2} S_r(\cA) \cdot z^r / r!$ has infinite radius of
convergence in $z$.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[Factorially damped Virasoro Banach model
$\mathrm{Vir}_c^{\sim}$]
\label{ex:tempered-virasoro}
For every generic $c \in \bbR$ the formal rescaling
\[
 S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c^{\sim})
 \;:=\;
 S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)/r!
\]
defines a factorially damped Banach shadow model, used as a
calibration object alongside the Virasoro VOA. If $\mathrm{Vir}_c$
satisfies $(\mathrm{H}_S)$, then the damped model satisfies it a
fortiori,
\[
 \bigl(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c^{\sim})\rvert/r!\bigr)^{1/r}
 =
 \bigl(\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)\rvert/(r!)^2\bigr)^{1/r}
 \longrightarrow 0
\]
whenever $\lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)\rvert$ has
exponential-polynomial growth.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Banach transfer and finite-propagation obstruction]
\label{def:banach-transfer-finite-propagation-obstruction}
Let $\cA$ satisfy $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ and fix
$0<\rho<\rho_*(c)$. The absolutely convergent curved-Dunn discrepancy
defines a degree-$2$ continuous endomorphism
\[
 \Delta_\rho
 =
 \sum_{r\ge4}S_r(\cA)\,
 \omega_g^{\wedge\lfloor r/2\rfloor}
 \in
 \operatorname{End}^2_{\mathrm{cont}}
 \bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\mathrm{KS},\rho}(\cA)\bigr).
\]
Its Banach transfer obstruction is
\[
 \mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)
 :=
 [\Delta_\rho]
 \in
 H^2\!\left(
 \operatorname{End}^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{cont}}
 \bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\mathrm{KS},\rho}(\cA)\bigr),
 [d_{\mathrm{KS},\rho},-]\right).
\]
The equality
$\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)=0$ means that there is a
bounded homotopy $h_\rho$ with
$\Delta_\rho=[d_{\mathrm{KS},\rho},h_\rho]$.

Let $q_\oplus$ be the quotient map from the KS-$\rho$ completion to
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\mathrm{KS},\rho}(\cA)/
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(\cA)$. After a Banach transfer has
been chosen, the finite-propagation obstruction is
\[
 \mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}(\cA,\rho)
 :=
 q_\oplus \circ
 \bigl(d_{\rho},\,h_\rho,\,G^{(2)}_{\rho},\,
 \{\mu_\theta\}_{\theta\in E_3^{\mathrm{top}}}\bigr)
 \Bigm|_{\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(\cA)} .
\]
It vanishes precisely when the differential, the homotopy primitive,
and the $E_3$ operadic structure maps preserve finite
conformal-weight support.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Banach $E_3$ model and finite-propagation descent]
\label{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}
Let $\cA$ be an analytically tempered class~$\mathsf{M}$ chirally
Koszul algebra, at non-zero $\kappaChHodge(\cA)$, non-critical level, and
genus $g \ge 2$. For every radius $\rho$ below the ordinary shadow
radius
\[
 \rho_*(c)
 \;:=\;
 \Bigl( \limsup_{r \to \infty}
 \lvert S_r(\cA) \rvert^{1/r} \Bigr)^{-1}
 \;\in\;
 (0, \infty],
\]
the KS-$\rho$ completion
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\mathrm{KS},\rho}(\cA)$ is a Banach
chain model on which the curved-Dunn discrepancy series
\[
 d^2_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}
 \;=\;
 \sum_{r \ge 4} S_r(\cA) \cdot \omega_g^{\wedge \lfloor r/2 \rfloor}
\]
converges absolutely. If
$\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)=0$, the discrepancy is killed
by a Banach-continuous homotopy $h_\rho$, and this Banach model is
continuously comparable with the weight-completed curved-Dunn complex
on the acyclicity and comparison hypotheses of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}.

The raw direct sum
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(\cA)$ carries the restricted
chain-level $E_3$ structure precisely when
$\mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}(\cA,\rho)=0$. Convergence of the Banach
discrepancy series follows from $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ alone; the transferred
Banach $E_3$ model requires $\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}=0$; descent
from the completed model to the raw direct sum requires
$\mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}=0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Four steps.

\emph{Step 1 (Banach completion).}
By Definition~\ref{def:kac-shapovalov-norm}, at $\rho > 0$ the norm
$\lvert\lvert\lvert \cdot \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}$
makes
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA) =
\bigl\{ \xi : \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
< \infty \bigr\}$
into a Banach chain complex. The raw direct sum
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \oplus}(\cA) = \bigoplus_r
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, r}(\cA)$ embeds densely in
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA)$ by the
finiteness of each weight component.

\emph{Step 2 (Convergence of the discrepancy series).}
By the definition of $\rho_*(c)$, at every $\rho < \rho_*(c)$
\[
 \sum_{r \ge 4}
 \lvert S_r(\cA) \rvert \cdot \rho^r / r!
 \;<\; \infty,
\]
hence the formal sum $\sum_r S_r(\cA) \cdot \omega_g^{\wedge \lfloor r/2 \rfloor}
\cdot \rho^r$ converges absolutely in the Banach space
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{KS}, \rho}(\cA)$. This is the
fibrewise squared discrepancy of the curved-Dunn differential
evaluated on any cochain of finite KS-$\rho$ norm.

\emph{Step 3 (Banach chain homotopy).}
The discrepancy series is a cocycle by step 2. The hypothesis
$\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)=0$ states that its class
vanishes in continuous endomorphism cohomology, producing a
Banach-continuous homotopy $h$ with
$\mathrm{id} - \pi = d h + h d$, where $\pi$ is the projection onto
the harmonic subspace. The bounded homotopy is the data measured by
Definition~\ref{def:banach-transfer-finite-propagation-obstruction},
independent of any density datum. When the homotopy is induced from a
weight-completed homotopy $\hat h$, the continuity datum is the
uniform bound
$\lvert\lvert\lvert h \cdot \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}
\le C(c, \rho) \cdot \lvert\lvert\lvert \xi \rvert\rvert\rvert_{\mathrm{KS}, \rho}$,
with $C(c, \rho) < \infty$ at every $\rho < \rho_*(c)$.

\emph{Step 4 ($E_3$-topological structure).}
The Banach-chain model carries the ambient
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ bulk--boundary operations of the
curved-Dunn complex. The $E_3$ structure is the topological operation
extracted on $Q_{\mathrm{tot}}$-cohomology once the chiral direction
has been made homotopically topological; this is the bulk--boundary
structure, not a single-coloured $E_3$ assertion. Each operation
increases the weight by a fixed finite amount, hence is bounded on
each weight component and Banach-continuous, and the Banach completion
inherits the operations.
$E_\infty$-topologization
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e-infinity-topologization-ladder}) at depth 2 for
the Virasoro-type stress tensor in $\cA$, present at non-critical
level by the Sugawara construction, gives the $E_3$-topological
promotion of the chain model.

The raw direct sum is the finite-weight subspace of the Banach model.
A Banach-continuous multilinear operation acts on the completion;
restriction to the dense subspace is a separate datum. When
$\mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}(\cA,\rho)=0$, each operation restricts to
$\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(\cA)$ and the operadic identities
descend, since both sides are finite-weight operations agreeing in the
Banach model. When a structure map sends a finite-weight cochain to an
infinite-weight series, the raw direct sum is not closed under the
operation; the Banach and weight-completed models retain the
structure.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Virasoro factorial obstruction vanishes]
\label{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m}
Let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c \notin \cD_{\mathrm{Kac}}$
and $g \ge 2$. The factorially normalised obstruction class is
\[
 \kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)
 \;:=\;
 \limsup_{r \to \infty}\,
 \bigl( \lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \rvert / r! \bigr)^{1/r}
 \;=\;
 0.
\]
Thus the factorially normalised obstruction vanishes. The Banach
$E_3$-topological model exists for
$0<\rho<\rho_*(c)=\lvert c\rvert/6$ after the Banach transfer class
$\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c,\rho)$ vanishes; descent
to the raw direct sum is the finite-propagation question
$\mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}(\mathrm{Vir}_c,\rho)=0$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro} gives the
Virasoro envelope
\[
 \lvert S_r(\mathrm{Vir}_c)\rvert
 \le C(c)\,6^{r-4}\bigl(r\,\lvert c\rvert^{r-2}\bigr)^{-1}
\]
for $r\ge 4$, with $C(c)$ enlarged away from the severe Kac
degeneracy locus. The envelope is exponential-polynomial.
Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} gives
$\kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=0$, and the ordinary
radius is
$\rho_*(c)=\lvert c\rvert/6$ by
Proposition~\ref{prop:virasoro-rho-star-closed-form}. The Banach
model is the conclusion of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional} once the
Banach transfer obstruction vanishes.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Tempering and finite-propagation partition]
\label{cor:class-m-original-complex-dichotomy}
At fixed non-zero $\kappaChHodge(\cA)$, non-critical level, and $g\ge 2$,
the class~$\mathsf{M}$ original-complex problem has two independent
tests.

\emph{Tempering test.} If $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ holds, the curved-Dunn
discrepancy series converges in the KS-$\rho$ Banach completion. The
completion carries the chain-level $E_3$-topological structure of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional} exactly
when $\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}(\cA,\rho)=0$. Generic Virasoro passes
the tempering part of this test by
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m};
principal $\cW_N$ passes it on the finite-envelope locus of
Theorem~\ref{conj:shadow-decay-class-m-full-family}.

\emph{Finite-propagation test.} The structure descends from the
Banach model to the raw direct sum precisely when
$\mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}(\cA,\rho)=0$; the Banach and weight-completed
models carry the structure independently of the descent.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Combine
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-conditional} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-level-e3-original-complex-obstruction-generic-class-m};
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} supplies the
completed comparison on its hypotheses. The first test is the root
test defining $(\mathrm{H}_S)$ together with the Banach transfer
class of
Definition~\ref{def:banach-transfer-finite-propagation-obstruction};
the second is closure of the finite-weight subspace under the
operations. Analytic convergence on the Banach completion,
continuous-homotopy transfer, and algebraic support preservation on
the raw direct sum are independent conditions.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the weight-completed and pro-ambient results]
\label{rem:relation-to-weight-completion-original}
Corollary~\ref{cor:class-m-original-complex-dichotomy} interpolates
between the raw direct-sum failure of
Theorem~\ref{thm:curved-dunn-class-M-raw-direct-sum-fails} and the
weight-completed result of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}. The two axes:
\begin{itemize}
\item The \emph{tempering} axis decides whether the shadow tower
 defines the Banach curved-Dunn model; generic Virasoro lies on
 the tempered side, $\kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}=0$.
\item The \emph{finite-propagation} axis decides whether the Banach
 operations restrict to the raw finite-weight direct sum; closure
 under the operations is the descent datum.
\end{itemize}
The weight-completed result of
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} covers the
completed side of the finite-propagation question on its acyclicity and
comparison hypotheses.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Original-complex analytic structure]
\label{rem:first-principles-summary-original-complex}
The original-complex problem has the following analytic structure.
\begin{itemize}
\item The determinant of the Kac--Shapovalov form supplies
 finite-weight non-degeneracy; a uniform inverse estimate requires
 additional input.
 Proposition~\ref{prop:kac-shapovalov-eigenvalue-asymptotic}
 isolates the missing analytic datum.
\item Exponential-polynomial shadow growth is factorially tempered.
 Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-decay-class-m} is the Stirling step;
 Theorem~\ref{thm:tempered-stratum-contains-virasoro} supplies the
 Virasoro envelope and gives
 $\kappa^{(\infty)}_{\mathrm{orig}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=0$.
\item Analytic tempering gives convergence in the Banach completion.
 The Banach $E_3$ model is conditional on
 $\mathfrak{o}_{\mathrm{Ban}}=0$; raw direct-sum descent on
 $\mathfrak{o}_{\oplus}=0$.
\end{itemize}
Factorial normalisation removes the numerical obstruction; the
remaining raw-direct-sum content is support preservation.
\end{remark}

 $C^{1/r}\alpha r^{M/r}(r!)^{-1/r}\sim \alpha e/r\to 0$.

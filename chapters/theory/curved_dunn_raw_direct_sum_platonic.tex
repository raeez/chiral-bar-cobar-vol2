\chapter{Ambient Reconstitution of Curved Dunn at Class M, \texorpdfstring{$g \ge 2$}{g>=2}}
\label{ch:curved-dunn-raw-direct-sum-platonic}

\section*{Statement of the frontier}

The chapter \autoref{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus} establishes the
vanishing of the curved-Dunn \(H^{2}\) at every genus in the
weight-completed category. For class \(\mathrm{M}\) (Virasoro,
\(W_{N}\), logarithmic W-algebras) the statement on the raw
direct-sum complex
\(\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}(A) = \bigoplus_{r \ge 0}
\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r}(A)\)
fails in a precise, computable way: the shadow coefficient
\(S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) = 10/[c(5c + 22)]\) is non-zero at generic
central charge, and the induced harmonic discrepancy at weight four
satisfies \(\delta_{4}^{\mathrm{harm}} \neq 0\) on the Virasoro
example at \(g = 2\).

This chapter shows that the direct-sum failure is a property of the
\emph{ambient category}, not of the curved-Dunn construction. In the
pro-chain category \(\mathrm{pro\text{-}Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\), in the
\(J\)-adic topological chain category with \(J\) the positive-weight
ideal, and in the weight-completed category (which agrees with both),
the curved-Dunn complex for class \(\mathrm{M}\) at \(g \ge 2\) has
\(H^{2} = 0\) unconditionally. The three ambients are equivalent;
the choice of ambient is a modelling convention, not a mathematical
constraint. The only remaining non-degenerate-locus frontier entry
of the programme (item 20) is closed by ambient upgrade.

The parallel with the class-M reconstitution of Master Conjecture~5
(Theorem~\ref{V1-thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong} and
Proposition~\ref{V1-prop:standard-strong-filtration} \textup{(}Volume~I\textup{)}) is exact: in both cases
the direct-sum \(d^{2} \neq 0\) artifact at generic \(c\) is dissolved
by replacing the bounded-direct-sum ambient with a pro-completion,
and the harmonic mechanism provides the explicit homotopy contracting
the obstruction.

\section{Set-up and the harmonic discrepancy}
\label{sec:setup-harmonic-discrepancy}

Fix a chirally Koszul algebra \(A\) of class \(\mathrm{M}\) on a
smooth projective curve \(X\) at non-critical level with non-zero
modular characteristic \(\kappa(A)\). Let \(\omega_{g}\) denote the
Arakelov form on \(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}\) normalised so that
\(\int_{\overline{\cM}_{1,1}} \omega_{1} = 1\).

\begin{definition}[Curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex, raw form]
\label{def:curved-dunn-complex-raw}
The \emph{raw direct-sum curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex at
genus \(g \ge 2\)} is
\[
  \bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A),\; d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A)
  \;=\;
  \bigoplus_{r \ge 0}
  \mathrm{Hom}\!\Bigl(
     \mathbf{B}\bigl(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}}\bigr)_{[r]},\;
     \mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r}\bigl(
        \mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\bigr)\Bigr),
\]
where the subscript \([r]\) selects the conformal-weight-\(r\)
component and the direct sum is taken in \(\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\)
(bounded direct sums, no completion). The differential is
\(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}} = d_{0} + \kappa(A) \cdot \omega_{g} \wedge\),
the convolution differential plus the curvature term.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Weight filtration and harmonic discrepancy]
\label{def:harmonic-discrepancy}
The weight filtration
\(F^{r}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g} = \bigoplus_{s \ge r}
\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})_{[s]}, \mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{s})\)
is descending, exhaustive, and compatible with
\(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}\). The
\emph{harmonic discrepancy at weight \(r\)} is the obstruction
\[
  \delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}}(A,g)
  \;:=\;
  d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2}\Big|_{F^{r}/F^{r+1}}
  \;\in\;
  \mathrm{Hom}\!\bigl(
    \mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})_{[r]},\;
    \mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r+2}\bigr).
\]
Equivalently, \(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}}\) is the associated-graded
component of \(d^{2}\) at weight \(r\); if
\(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2} = 0\) on the direct sum then
every \(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}}\) vanishes, while if \(d^{2}\) is
non-zero there exists a least weight \(r^{\ast}\) at which
\(\delta_{r^{\ast}}^{\mathrm{harm}} \neq 0\).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Low-weight harmonic discrepancy for class \(\mathrm{M}\)]
\label{lem:low-weight-discrepancy-class-M}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For \(r \le 3\) and any chirally Koszul algebra \(A\) of class
\(\mathrm{M}\) at non-zero \(\kappa(A)\) and genus \(g \ge 2\), the
harmonic discrepancy vanishes: \(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}}(A, g) = 0\).
At weight four on the Virasoro example \(A = \mathrm{Vir}_{c}\) and
\(g = 2\),
\[
  \delta_{4}^{\mathrm{harm}}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}, 2)
  \;=\;
  S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}
  \;=\;
  \frac{10}{c(5c + 22)} \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}
  \;\neq\; 0
\]
at generic \(c\) (in particular at every \(c\) outside the
countable exceptional set \(\{0, -22/5\}\)).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For \(r \le 3\) the harmonic-projector
\(H_{g}\colon \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r} \to \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r}\)
admits a contracting homotopy \(h\) with
\(\mathrm{id} - H_{g} = d h + h d\) within the direct-sum category,
because the relevant weight spaces of
\(\mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}\) are finite-dimensional
and the spectral decomposition of the modular Laplacian is simple
on each (no accidental zero eigenvalues at generic \(c\)). The
homotopy cancels the contribution of
\(\kappa \cdot \omega_{g} \wedge\) against \(d_{0}\) weight-wise,
and the class-M quartic-pole contribution to the shadow tower enters
only at \(r \ge 4\) (shadow coefficient \(S_{2} = \kappa\),
\(S_{3} = 0\) for Virasoro, \(S_{4} = 10/[c(5c+22)]\)). Hence
\(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}} = 0\) for \(r \le 3\).

At \(r = 4\) and \(g = 2\), the explicit computation of
\(S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c})\) via the
\texttt{MC3-shadow-tower} engine (Vol I \texttt{compute/lib/}) gives
the rational function \(10/[c(5c+22)]\) (Vol I true-formula census
entry C17; Vol II shadow-tower archive through \(m_{8}\)). The
genus-\(2\) Arakelov class \(\omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\) is non-zero in
\(H^{4}(\overline{\cM}_{2,0}, \mathbb{Q})\), giving
\(\delta_{4}^{\mathrm{harm}}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}, 2) \neq 0\) for
\(c \neq 0, -22/5\). The fractional expression arises because the
Virasoro bar-complex shadow tower has a first obstruction at weight
four (class \(\mathrm{M}\) is characterised precisely by
\(S_{4} \neq 0\); classes \(\mathrm{G}, \mathrm{L}, \mathrm{C}\) have
\(S_{4} = 0\) and fail only at \(r = \infty\)).
\end{proof}

\section{Raw direct-sum failure for class \(\mathrm{M}\)}
\label{sec:raw-direct-sum-failure}

\begin{theorem}[Raw direct-sum curved Dunn at class \(\mathrm{M}\),
\(g \ge 2\): harmonic discrepancy does not vanish]
\label{thm:curved-dunn-class-M-raw-direct-sum-fails}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For class \(\mathrm{M}\) chirally Koszul algebras \(A\) at non-zero
\(\kappa(A)\), the raw direct-sum curved-Dunn twisting-cochain
complex \(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A)\) at genus \(g \ge 2\)
satisfies
\[
  d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2}
  \Big|_{\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A)}
  \;\neq\; 0
\]
at generic level parameters. Explicitly, for
\(A = \mathrm{Vir}_{c}\) and \(g = 2\), the square of the curved-Dunn
differential has non-zero weight-four associated-graded component:
\[
  d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2}\Big|_{F^{4}/F^{5}}
  \;=\;
  \frac{10}{c(5c + 22)} \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}
  \;\neq\; 0
\]
for \(c \notin \{0, -22/5\}\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:harmonic-discrepancy}, the condition
\(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2} = 0\) on the direct sum is
equivalent to \(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}} = 0\) for every weight
\(r\). By \autoref{lem:low-weight-discrepancy-class-M}, the
weight-four component \(\delta_{4}^{\mathrm{harm}}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}, 2)
= S_{4}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) \cdot \omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\) is non-zero
at generic \(c\). The genus-\(2\) Arakelov wedge \(\omega_{2}^{\wedge 2}\)
is a non-zero class in \(H^{4}(\overline{\cM}_{2,0}, \mathbb{Q})\), so
the cohomological obstruction does not vanish. Hence
\(d^{2}_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}\) is non-zero on the direct sum.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why the failure is quantitatively mild]
\label{rem:direct-sum-failure-is-mild}
The \(d^{2}\) violation in
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-class-M-raw-direct-sum-fails} is finite at
each weight (of bounded order \(S_{r}\) in the shadow tower) but
does not aggregate to zero across the infinite direct sum. This
distinguishes class \(\mathrm{M}\) sharply from classes
\(\mathrm{G}, \mathrm{L}, \mathrm{C}\) where all \(S_{r}\) with
\(r \ge 2\) vanish (class \(\mathrm{G}\)), or truncate after finitely
many weights (classes \(\mathrm{L}, \mathrm{C}\)). The fact that the
violation is bounded weight-by-weight is exactly the input for the
pro-ambient and \(J\)-adic reconstitutions below: at each
\emph{finite} weight truncation the discrepancy is absorbed by an
explicit homotopy, and the inverse limit of truncations gives the
Platonic form.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Parallel with MC5 class-M ambient reconstitution]
\label{rem:parallel-mc5-class-m}
The analogous situation for Master Conjecture~5 was resolved on
(Volume~I Theorem~\ref{V1-thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong},
Proposition~\ref{V1-prop:standard-strong-filtration}; restated and applied in the
BV/BRST chapter of Volume~II):
direct-sum chain-level equality with BV fails at class \(\mathrm{M}\)
because \(S_{4}\) forces bar cohomology at weight four, but the
weight-completed category absorbs each weight-\(N\) harmonic
discrepancy by an explicit \(h \cdot m_{0}^{j-1}\) homotopy, and
Milnor-\(\lim^{1}\) vanishes in the passage to the inverse limit.
The present chapter makes the same move for the curved-Dunn \(H^{2}\)
statement and arrives at the same conclusion: the direct-sum failure
is an ambient artifact.
\end{remark}

\section{Pro-\(\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) reconstitution}
\label{sec:pro-ambient-reconstitution}

\begin{definition}[Pro-ambient curved-Dunn complex]
\label{def:pro-curved-dunn}
The \emph{pro-curved-Dunn complex} at genus \(g\) is the pro-object
\[
  \mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A)
  \;=\;
  \bigl\{\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)\bigr\}_{N \in \mathbb{N}},
  \qquad
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)
  \;=\;
  \bigoplus_{r \le N}
  \mathrm{Hom}\!\bigl(
    \mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})_{[r]},\;
    \mathrm{gr}^{g}\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r}\bigr),
\]
viewed in the pro-chain category
\(\mathrm{pro\text{-}Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) with transition maps the
weight-truncation quotients
\(\pi_{N+1, N}\colon \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N+1}
\twoheadrightarrow \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}\). Each finite
truncation \(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)\) inherits the
restriction of \(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}\) and becomes a
genuine cochain complex with \(d^{2} = 0\) via the truncated
harmonic homotopy (see \autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Finite truncation is a complex]
\label{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every \(N \ge 0\), the truncated curved-Dunn complex
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)\) satisfies
\(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2} = 0\) strictly. Explicitly,
there is an \(N\)-dependent homotopy
\(h_{N}\colon \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A) \to
\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet - 1}_{g, \le N}(A)\) with
\[
  d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}^{2}\Big|_{\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)}
  \;=\;
  d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}} \cdot h_{N}
  + h_{N} \cdot d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}},
\]
and the projection onto \(H^{\bullet}\) sends \(d^{2}\) to zero;
within the shifted dgLa presentation one may absorb \(h_{N}\) into
a strictification so that \(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}\)
squares to zero on the nose at each finite \(N\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The harmonic discrepancy
\(\delta_{r}^{\mathrm{harm}}(A, g)\) is zero for \(r \le 3\) (by
\autoref{lem:low-weight-discrepancy-class-M}) and non-zero only at
\(r \ge 4\), with \(S_{r}\) a rational function of \(c\) decreasing
polynomially in \(r\). For each finite \(N\), the accumulated
discrepancy is a finite sum
\(\sum_{r = 4}^{N} S_{r}(\mathrm{Vir}_{c}) \cdot
\omega_{g}^{\wedge \lfloor r/2 \rfloor}\), hence lies in a
finite-dimensional space of cochains. By the modular harmonic-theory
normalisation (the \(\omega_{g}^{\wedge \bullet}\) wedge powers
span a finite-dimensional subspace of \(H^{\bullet}(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})\)
for any fixed \(g\)), this finite sum of cocycles is a coboundary
via the explicit contracting homotopy
\(h_{N}(\xi) = (d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}})^{-1} \xi\) defined on
the sub-complex where the modular Laplacian is invertible (the
complement of \(\mathrm{ker}(\kappa^{-1} \cdot \Delta_{g})\) at
generic \(c\)). The inverse is well-defined because the fiberwise
modular Laplacian has non-zero eigenvalues on every weight space
\(\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r}\) with \(r \le N\) at generic \(c\)
(Kac determinant non-vanishing on the relevant Verma module at
generic \(c\)). Hence \(d^{2} - (d \cdot h_{N} + h_{N} \cdot d) = 0\)
on \(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)\).

The strictification at each \(N\) follows by passing to the twisted
strict model \(d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}} + h_{N}\) within the
completed convolution Lie algebra; the Maurer--Cartan equation
\((d + h_{N})^{2} = 0\) holds strictly because \(h_{N}\) is a
bounded-degree correction and the bracket
\([h_{N}, d_{\mathrm{curved\text{-}Dunn}}]\) absorbs the residual
discrepancy weight-by-weight.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pro-ambient curved-Dunn \(H^{2} = 0\) at \(g \ge 2\),
class \(\mathrm{M}\)]
\label{thm:curved-dunn-pro-ambient-H2-zero}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let \(A\) be a chirally Koszul algebra of class \(\mathrm{M}\) at
non-zero \(\kappa(A)\) and \(g \ge 2\). The pro-curved-Dunn complex
\(\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A) \in
\mathrm{pro\text{-}Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) has
\[
  H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A)\bigr)
  \;=\; 0.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex} each truncation
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)\) is a cochain complex in the
strict sense. The bridge
\autoref{prop:modular-bootstrap-to-curved-dunn-bridge} (proved in
\autoref{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus}) identifies the
curved-Dunn differential at each truncation with the image of the
modular-bootstrap differential under the chain map \(\Phi\);
since the modular-bootstrap complex has \(H^{2} = 0\) at every
genus (\autoref{thm:modular-bootstrap-H2-vanishing} of
\autoref{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus}, via twisted K\"unneth on
the Arakelov class), and the bridge is surjective on \(H^{2}\), we
obtain
\(H^{2}(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)) = 0\) for every
\(N\).

Pass to the pro-object. Cohomology commutes with directed
projective limits in \(\mathrm{pro\text{-}Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\)
under the Mittag-Leffler condition; the transition maps
\(\pi_{N+1, N}\) are surjective and the kernel
\(\ker(\pi_{N+1, N})\) is a single weight space (the
weight-\((N+1)\) component) of finite dimension, so the inverse
system of \(H^{1}\) groups satisfies Mittag-Leffler, and
\(\lim^{1} H^{1}(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)) = 0\)
(Milnor--Stasheff axiomatic lim\(^{1}\) vanishing under ML).
The Milnor exact sequence
\[
  0 \;\to\;
  {\lim}^{1} H^{1}\bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}\bigr)
  \;\to\;
  H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}\bigr)
  \;\to\;
  \lim H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}\bigr)
  \;\to\; 0
\]
has vanishing first term by ML and vanishing third term by the
per-truncation bridge, so the middle term is zero.
\end{proof}

\section{\(J\)-adic topological ambient}
\label{sec:j-adic-ambient}

\begin{definition}[Positive-weight ideal \(J\) and \(J\)-adic complex]
\label{def:j-adic-complex}
Let \(J \subset \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A)\) be the
positive-weight ideal
\(J = \bigoplus_{r \ge 1} \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet, [r]}_{g}(A)\),
where \(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet, [r]}_{g}(A) =
\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_{1}^{\mathrm{tr}})_{[r]}, \mathrm{gr}^{g}
\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{r})\). The \(J\)-adic \emph{topological}
curved-Dunn complex is the completion
\[
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, J\text{-adic}}(A)
  \;=\;
  \varprojlim_{N}
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g,\oplus}(A) \big/ J^{N+1},
\]
equipped with the restriction of the curved-Dunn differential
(which descends to each \(J^{N+1}\)-quotient because the
curvature term \(\kappa \cdot \omega_{g} \wedge\) shifts weight by
at least two, hence is \(J\)-filtration-compatible).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\(J\)-adic curved-Dunn \(H^{2} = 0\) at \(g \ge 2\),
class \(\mathrm{M}\)]
\label{thm:curved-dunn-J-adic-H2-zero}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let \(A\) be a chirally Koszul algebra of class \(\mathrm{M}\) at
non-zero \(\kappa(A)\) and \(g \ge 2\). The \(J\)-adic topological
curved-Dunn complex satisfies
\[
  H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, J\text{-adic}}(A)\bigr)
  \;=\; 0.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The quotient
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \oplus}(A) / J^{N+1}\) coincides with
the weight-\(\le N\) truncation
\(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \le N}(A)\) of
\autoref{def:pro-curved-dunn} as a graded vector space, and the
curved-Dunn differential induced on the quotient coincides with the
truncated differential of
\autoref{lem:finite-truncation-is-complex}. The inverse limit
taken in the category of topological (pronilpotent) dg algebras
agrees with the pro-limit taken in
\(\mathrm{pro\text{-}Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) under the finite-weight
dimension condition, because the transition maps are surjective and
the system is Mittag-Leffler. Hence
\[
  H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, J\text{-adic}}(A)\bigr)
  \;=\;
  H^{2}\bigl(\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A)\bigr)
  \;=\; 0
\]
by \autoref{thm:curved-dunn-pro-ambient-H2-zero}.
\end{proof}

\section{Three ambients agree}
\label{sec:three-ambients-agree}

\begin{proposition}[Equivalence of three ambients for curved-Dunn at
class \(\mathrm{M}\), \(g \ge 2\)]
\label{prop:three-ambients-equivalent}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For class \(\mathrm{M}\) chirally Koszul algebras at \(g \ge 2\) and
non-zero \(\kappa\), there are natural quasi-isomorphisms between
the three reconstituted curved-Dunn complexes:
\[
  \mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g}(A)
  \;\simeq\;
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, J\text{-adic}}(A)
  \;\simeq\;
  \mathrm{TCo}^{\bullet}_{g, \mathrm{wc}}(A),
\]
where the third is the weight-completed curved-Dunn complex of
\autoref{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus}. All three carry the same
\(H^{2}\), which vanishes at class \(\mathrm{M}\), \(g \ge 2\),
\(\kappa \neq 0\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The pro-to-\(J\)-adic equivalence is
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-J-adic-H2-zero}'s proof (Mittag-Leffler
transition maps identify the pro-limit with the topological
completion). The \(J\)-adic-to-weight-completed equivalence
records that the weight-completed category of
\autoref{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus} (where
\texttt{thm:curved-dunn-H2-vanishing-all-genera} proved
\(H^{2} = 0\)) is precisely the full subcategory of
\(\mathrm{pro\text{-}Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) spanned by
\(J\)-adic-complete objects; the identification is the universal
property of the \(J\)-adic completion functor as the left adjoint
to inclusion. Both sides have \(H^{2} = 0\) by
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-pro-ambient-H2-zero} and
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-J-adic-H2-zero}, and the
weight-completed vanishing was the content of the earlier chapter.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Direct-sum obstruction is an ambient choice, not a
mathematical limitation]
\label{rem:direct-sum-obstruction-is-ambient-choice}
The content of
\autoref{thm:curved-dunn-class-M-raw-direct-sum-fails} is that the
bounded direct-sum category \(\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\) is the
\emph{wrong ambient} for the curved-Dunn construction at class
\(\mathrm{M}\). The chain complex one writes down is genuinely a
pro-object (equivalently, a \(J\)-adic topological chain complex,
equivalently, an object of the weight-completed category); the
direct-sum is a lossy truncation of that pro-object. The same
lossy truncation fails for Master Conjecture~5's direct-sum
statement (MC5 class-\(\mathrm{M}\) ambient reconstitution,
\autoref{rem:parallel-mc5-class-m}) and is cured by the same ambient
upgrade. In both cases the class-\(\mathrm{M}\) shadow coefficient
\(S_{4} = 10/[c(5c+22)]\) is what forces the chosen ambient to be
topologically complete; once one works in the correct ambient, the
obstruction vanishes unconditionally.

The parallel has a conceptual source. The class-\(\mathrm{M}\) bar
complex has non-trivial content at every conformal weight (the
quartic OPE pole propagates to bar cocycles at all weights via the
shadow tower); the ambient that correctly records this infinite

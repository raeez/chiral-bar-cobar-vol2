\chapter*{Introduction}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Introduction}

Volume~I constructed the bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ of
a chiral algebra on a curve as a categorical logarithm: the
multiplicative structure of the OPE maps to the nilpotent structure
of the bar differential ($d^2 = 0$), and the bar-cobar adjunction
inverts this map on the Koszul locus.  Five theorems established
the existence, invertibility, complementary Lagrangian structure,
modular characteristic, and Hochschild coefficient ring of this
logarithm.

This volume asks: \emph{what does the logarithm compute?}

The answer is three-dimensional.  The bar complex carries two
algebraic structures---a differential and a coproduct---and these
two structures live in two different geometric directions.  The
differential $d_{\barB}$, built from OPE residues along collision
divisors in the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\FM_k(\C)$,
encodes the holomorphic chiral product.  The coproduct~$\Delta$,
the ordered deconcatenation of tensor factors along configurations
in~$\R$, encodes topological interval-cutting.  A bar element of
degree~$k$ is therefore parametrized by
\[
\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R),
\]
the product of holomorphic and topological configuration spaces.
This product is the operadic fingerprint of a three-dimensional
holomorphic--topological quantum field theory on $\C_z \times \R_t$,
in which local observables factorize holomorphically in~$z$ and
associatively in~$t$.  The relevant operad is the two-colored
Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$, whose closed (holomorphic) color
has operation spaces $\FM_k(\C)$ and whose open (topological) color
has operation spaces $\FM_k(\C) \times \Eone(m)$.

The central theorem of this volume: \emph{the bar complex of
Volume~I, equipped with its coproduct, is an algebra over the
Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$.}  The bar differential is the
closed color.  The bar coproduct is the open color.  The
no-open-to-closed axiom---that open inputs cannot produce closed
outputs---reflects the physical directionality that bulk
interactions restrict to boundaries but not conversely.

\section*{The Steinberg parallel}

The relationship between the bar complex and the Swiss-cheese
algebra echoes a classical phenomenon.  The Steinberg variety
$\widetilde{\mathcal{N}} \times_{\mathcal{N}}
\widetilde{\mathcal{N}}$---the fiber product of the Springer
resolution with itself over the nilpotent cone---carries a
convolution product from correspondence composition and a
Bruhat decomposition from the stratification by relative
position.  Together they \emph{present} the Hecke algebra: the
algebra is recovered not from generators and relations but from
the geometry of the correspondence space.

The bar complex plays the same role for the Swiss-cheese algebra.

\noindent
The parallel is not merely suggestive.  In both settings, the
presentation-by-correspondence is the content of a Koszul duality
theorem: the Steinberg variety presents the Hecke algebra through
convolution of perverse sheaves on the flag variety, just as the
bar complex presents the Swiss-cheese algebra through the bar-cobar
adjunction on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$.  The Kazhdan--Lusztig
polynomials are the shadow of the perverse filtration; the spectral
$R$-matrix is the shadow of the $\lambda$-bracket.  Koszul duality
mediates both: the Bernstein--Gelfand--Gelfand correspondence
between Verma modules and projectives is the representation-theoretic
shadow of the same structural phenomenon that makes line operators
into modules for the Koszul dual~$\cA^!$.

At genus~$g \geq 1$, the parallel deepens.  The Steinberg variety
acquires a deformation from the Hecke parameter~$q$; the bar complex
acquires curvature $d^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ from the Hodge
bundle.  In both cases, the deformation is controlled by a single
scalar (the Hecke parameter; the modular characteristic) and
organizes into a genus tower governed by universal classes.

\section*{The descent calculus}

The bar complex encodes the full quantum content of the 3d theory
as a single dg coalgebra.  The descent calculus extracts invariants
by two mechanisms.

\emph{Cohomological descent.}  On cohomology $H^\bullet(\cA, Q)$,
the higher operations $m_{k \geq 3}$ vanish via explicit
homotopies; the binary operation~$m_2$ decomposes into a regular
part (commutative product) and a singular part ($\lambda$-bracket).
Together they satisfy the axioms of a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex
algebra (Theorem~\ref{thm:cohomology_PVA}).  The Jacobi identity
comes from Stokes' theorem on $\FM_3(\C)$; the Leibniz rule comes
from the projection to the regular-singular decomposition at
$n = 3$; skewsymmetry comes from the involution exchanging the two
insertions.  All three are consequences of Arnold relations and
Stokes exactness---the same identities that ensure $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$
in Volume~I.

\emph{Genus descent.}  At genus~$g \geq 1$, the bar differential
acquires curvature $d^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$, and the
Swiss-cheese algebra becomes \emph{curved}: the $\R$-factorization
persists, but the coderivation property holds only modulo the
curvature term.  The genus tower of curved Swiss-cheese algebras,
parametrized by $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$, is controlled by
Volume~I's Theorem~D.

The bridge between the two descent mechanisms is the classical
$r$-matrix formula
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:intro-r-matrix}
r(z) \;=\; \int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda z}\,
\{{\cdot}_\lambda{\cdot}\}\, d\lambda,
\end{equation}
which converts the $\lambda$-bracket (a PVA datum) into the
classical $r$-matrix (a Yang--Baxter datum) via Laplace transform.
The quantum $R$-matrix $R(z)$ from bulk-boundary composition
(Section~\ref{sec:spectral_braiding}) deformation-quantizes this
classical limit.

\section*{The Koszul triangle}

The Swiss-cheese structure produces three algebraic objects,
related by Koszul duality:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item the \emph{bulk} chiral algebra $\cA_{\mathrm{bulk}}$,
  living on $\C \times \R$;
\item the \emph{boundary} vertex algebra
  $\cA^!$ (the Koszul dual), living on~$\R$;
\item the \emph{line} category $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{line}}$,
  equivalent to $\cA^!$-modules.
\end{itemize}
The corrected relationship (Section~\ref{sec:ht-bulk-boundary-line})
is $A_{\mathrm{bulk}} \simeq Z_{\mathrm{der}}(B_\partial)$: the
bulk is the \emph{derived center} of the boundary, not the boundary
itself.  This is the mathematical content of the holographic
principle in the HT setting.

Homotopy-Koszulity of $\SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}, proved via Kontsevich formality
and transfer from the classical Swiss-cheese operad) makes all
three legs of the triangle unconditional: the bar-cobar Quillen
equivalence, the identification $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{line}}
\simeq \cA^!\text{-}\mathbf{mod}$, and the dg-shifted Yangian
structure on~$\cA^!$ are all consequences of
homotopy-Koszulity.

\section*{Computational preview: the Heisenberg atom}

The Heisenberg algebra $\cH_k$---a single generator~$J$, one
OPE pole $J(z)J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$---is the simplest nontrivial
test of the full machine.  In the Swiss-cheese reading:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \emph{Swiss-cheese structure.}
  The bar complex $\barB(\cH_k) = T^c(s^{-1}\C \cdot J)$ has
  bar differential determined by
  $m_2(J, J) = k \cdot \mathbf{1}$ (the OPE residue) and
  $m_{k \geq 3} = 0$ (no higher interactions).  The coproduct is
  deconcatenation.  This is a \emph{formal} Swiss-cheese algebra:
  the $\R$-factorization is exact.

\item \emph{PVA on cohomology.}
  The $(-1)$-shifted PVA on $H^\bullet(\cH_k, Q) = \C[J]$ has
  $\lambda$-bracket $\{J {}_\lambda J\} = k\lambda$.  At $\lambda = 0$
  the Poisson bracket is trivial; the entire physical content is in
  the $\lambda$-linear term, which is the level.

\item \emph{Genus-$1$ curvature.}
  The genus-$1$ bar differential satisfies $d^2 = k \cdot \omega_1$.
  The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$; the free energy
  $F_1 = -\frac{k}{24} \log\, \eta(\tau)$ recovers the Dedekind
  $\eta$-function.

\item \emph{Spectral braiding.}
  The $R$-matrix $R(z) = \exp(k/z)$, the exponential of a single
  pole, trivially satisfies Yang--Baxter.
\end{itemize}

\noindent
Every datum of the Heisenberg atom is controlled by the single
parameter~$k$: the bar differential, the PVA, the genus-$1$
curvature, the $R$-matrix are all determined by $\kappa = k$.
The Rosetta Stone chapter (Section~\ref{sec:rosetta-stone})
verifies that each of these identifications instantiates the
corresponding theorem from Volume~I.

Beyond the Heisenberg atom, the Landau--Ginzburg model provides the
first \emph{interacting} example---a genuine $m_3$ from a cubic
superpotential---and abelian Chern--Simons provides the first
\emph{braided} example, with an explicit spectral $R$-matrix
from the CS propagator.

\chapter*{Introduction}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Introduction}

\providecommand{\cH}{\mathcal{H}}
\providecommand{\fg}{\mathfrak{g}}

The bar complex of a chiral algebra carries a differential that
lives in~$\C$ and a coproduct that lives in~$\R$.

The differential $d_{\barB}$ extracts OPE residues along collision
divisors in the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\FM_k(\C)$:
it records how operators interact when they collide in the
holomorphic variable~$z$.  The coproduct~$\Delta$ splits an
ordered sequence of tensor factors into two sub-sequences: it
records how an interval is cut at a point in the topological
variable~$t$.  A bar element of degree~$k$ is parametrised by
the product
\[
\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R).
\]
The differential sees the first factor.  The coproduct sees the
second.  This product is the operation space of a two-coloured
operad---the holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad
$\SCchtop$---and the bar complex, equipped with both structures,
is an algebra over it.  That identification is the central theorem
of this volume.

If Volume~I built the categorical logarithm and its modular tower,
Volume~II asks what that logarithm actually does in three
dimensions.  The answer is not ``it produces a few extra
operations.''  The answer is: it becomes a full
holomorphic--topological organism.  The bar complex is re-read as
a Swiss-cheese object; its cohomological shadow becomes a Poisson
vertex algebra; its dual open sector becomes a category of lines;
its interactions are governed by brace operations, spectral
$R$-matrices, and bulk--boundary--line triangles; and the full
homotopy package---$\Ainf$, $\Linf$, brace---becomes the natural
language rather than a background correction.  Every strict dg
structure in this volume is the visible spine of a larger
homotopy-coherent world.  In the three-dimensional
holomorphic--topological setting, this is exactly what physics
forces on us: BRST anomaly brackets, defect operations, line
OPE's, and boundary transfer are higher operations \emph{before}
they are strict algebras.

To see all of this at work, consider the simplest chiral algebra
with nontrivial content.


\section*{The Heisenberg atom}

The Heisenberg algebra $\cH_k$ has a single generator~$J$, a
single OPE
\[
J(z)\,J(w) \;\sim\; \frac{k}{(z-w)^2},
\]
and no composite fields.  One generator, one pole order, no
composites: every step of the three-dimensional machine is
checkable by hand.

The bar complex is $\barB(\cH_k) = T^c(s^{-1}\C \cdot J)$, the
cofree tensor coalgebra on a single desuspended generator.  Its
differential is the coderivation whose cogenerator projection in
arity~$2$ is
\[
m_2(J, J) \;=\; k \cdot \mathbf{1},
\]
the OPE residue, with all higher operations vanishing:
$m_{k \geq 3} = 0$, because the Heisenberg OPE has no higher
singularities and there are no composite fields to produce them.
The coproduct is deconcatenation.  Together---differential from
$\FM_2(\C)$, coproduct from $\Conf_k(\R)$, no higher
interactions---the bar complex is a Swiss-cheese algebra, and a
formal one: the $\R$-factorisation is exact.

On cohomology $H^\bullet(\cH_k, Q) = \C[J]$, the binary
operation~$m_2$ decomposes.  Its regular part is a commutative
product; its singular part is a $\lambda$-bracket:
\[
\{J \,{}_\lambda\, J\} \;=\; k\lambda.
\]
Together they form a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra.  The
Jacobi identity is vacuous (single generator); the Leibniz rule
is the derivation property of~$\partial$; skew-symmetry is the
antisymmetry of the OPE.  The entire physical content of this PVA
is in the $\lambda$-linear term---the level~$k$.

The Laplace transform of the $\lambda$-bracket produces the
classical $r$-matrix $r(z) = k/z^2$, and the quantum $R$-matrix
$R(z) = \exp(k/z)$---the exponential of a single pole---trivially
satisfies Yang--Baxter.

The Koszul dual is $\cH_k^! = \cH_{-k}$: the Heisenberg at the
\emph{opposite} level.  Line operators are
$\cH_{-k}\text{-mod} = \Vect$---a single simple object, the vacuum
module---and the category is trivially braided with $R$-matrix
$R(z) = \exp(k/z)$.  The bulk--boundary--line triangle collapses:
\[
\cH_k
\;\simeq\;
Z_{\mathrm{der}}(\cH_{-k})
\;\simeq\;
\HH^\bullet(\cH_{-k}),
\]
with $\cH_{-k}$ as the boundary vertex algebra of the 3d theory.

At genus~$1$, the Arnold relation acquires a defect from the
period matrix of the elliptic curve.  The bar differential
acquires curvature:
\[
d^2 \;=\; k \cdot \omega_1.
\]
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$: the same
scalar that governs the OPE, the $\lambda$-bracket, the
$R$-matrix, and now the genus-$1$ obstruction.  The free energy
$F_1 = -(k/24)\,\log\,\eta(\tau)$ recovers the Dedekind
$\eta$-function.  Every datum of the Heisenberg
atom---bar differential, PVA, $R$-matrix, genus-$1$ curvature,
free energy---is controlled by this single parameter.  The
Rosetta Stone chapter (Section~\ref{sec:rosetta-stone}) verifies
that each identification instantiates the corresponding theorem of
Volume~I.


\section*{The Swiss-cheese operad and the recognition theorem}

The Heisenberg computation exhibits a general phenomenon.  The
product $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R)$ that parametrises bar
elements is the operation space of the two-coloured Swiss-cheese
operad $\SCchtop$ (Definition~\ref{def:SC}), whose closed
(holomorphic) colour has operations on $\FM_k(\C)$ and whose open
(topological) colour has operations on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \Eone(m)$.  The bar complex is an algebra over
this operad (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}).

The construction is explicit.  Define $\SCchtop$ as follows
(Section~\ref{sec:foundations}, Section~\ref{sec:HT-operad}).  The
set of colours is $\{\mathsf{ch}, \mathsf{top}\}$---closed
(holomorphic) and open (topological).  The spaces of operations are:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Closed--to--closed} (pure holomorphic):
  $\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k;\,\mathsf{ch}) = \FM_k(\C)$, the
  Fulton--MacPherson compactification of~$k$ points in~$\C$.
\item \textbf{Mixed--to--open} (holomorphic + topological):
  $\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k, \mathsf{top}^m;\,\mathsf{top})
  = \FM_k(\C) \times \Eone(m)$, the product of holomorphic
  configuration space with the little-intervals operad.
\item \textbf{Open--to--closed} (\emph{forbidden}):
  $\SCchtop(\ldots, \mathsf{top}, \ldots;\,\mathsf{ch}) = \varnothing$.
  Open inputs cannot produce closed outputs.
\end{itemize}
The directionality axiom---open-to-closed is empty---is the
operadic expression of the physical principle that bulk interactions
restrict to boundaries, but not conversely.  Composition in
$\SCchtop$ is by substitution: holomorphic nesting via the FM
operad structure, topological nesting via $\Eone$ composition, and
mixed operations combine both (Definition~\ref{def:SC-composition}).

The $\Ainf$ chiral operations $m_k$ are the bar-complex
cogenerators, parametrised by $\FM_k(\C)$: the operation~$m_k$
maps $A^{\otimes k}$ to the space of Laurent series in
$(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_{k-1})$ and satisfies the hierarchy
of $\Ainf$ relations
\[
\sum_{p+q=n+1} \sum_{j} (-1)^{\epsilon}\, m_p(\ldots, m_q(\ldots), \ldots) = 0,
\]
which are precisely the boundary decompositions of $\FM_n(\C)$
(Section~\ref{sec:axioms-Ainfty-chiral},
Section~\ref{sec:FM_calculus}).  The concrete axioms---sesquilinearity
(Definition~\ref{def:sesquilinearity}), the $\Ainf$ relations
(Definition~\ref{def:ainfty-relations}), and their compatibility
with the spectral parameters~$\lambda_i$---are extracted from the
operad in Section~\ref{sec:equivalence} and shown to be equivalent
to $W(\SCchtop)$-algebra structure for tame algebras
(Theorem~\ref{thm:rectification}).

\begin{maintheorem}[Recognition; Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:recognition}}]
\label{thm:intro-recognition}
Let $\cA$ be an $\Ainf$ chiral algebra satisfying the standing
analytic hypotheses \textrm{(H1)--(H4)}.  Then $\cA$ carries the
structure of a $C_\ast(W(\SCchtop))$-algebra.  The bar differential
$d_{\barB}$ is the holomorphic factorisation; the bar coproduct
$\Delta$ is the topological factorisation.
\end{maintheorem}

\noindent
The Recognition Theorem is proved in Section~\ref{sec:foundations}
(product decomposition and retract argument,
Proposition~\ref{prop:compatibility}).  Its content is that the
combined data---differential from~$\C$, coproduct from~$\R$,
higher operations from $\FM_k(\C)$---are not independent choices
but a single package, once the holomorphic factorisation of
Volume~I is granted and the four analytic hypotheses are verified.


\section*{Homotopy-Koszulity and the Quillen equivalence}

The Recognition Theorem asserts that the bar complex
\emph{presents} the Swiss-cheese algebra.  But in what sense?
The bar complex is not the algebra itself; it is a coalgebraic
resolution.  The word ``presents'' is precise: the bar-cobar
adjunction for the two-coloured operad $\SCchtop$ is a Quillen
equivalence, so the bar complex determines the algebra up to
quasi-isomorphism, as a free resolution determines a module.

The decisive input is homotopy-Koszulity.

\begin{maintheorem}[Homotopy-Koszulity; Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}}]
\label{thm:intro-homotopy-Koszul}
The two-coloured topological operad $\SCchtop$ is
homotopy-Koszul: the canonical map
$\Omega \mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \xrightarrow{\,\sim\,} \SCchtop$
is a quasi-isomorphism of dg operads.
\end{maintheorem}

\noindent
The proof (Section~\ref{sec:bar-cobar}) proceeds in three steps.
\textbf{Step~1}: the classical Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}$---built from the individually Koszul operads
$\Etwo$ and $\Ass = \Eone$ via a distributive law---is Koszul.
\textbf{Step~2}: Kontsevich formality provides a quasi-isomorphism
$\Phi \colon \SCchtop \to \mathsf{SC}$ of two-coloured dg operads
(the identity on the open colour, the formality map
$C_\ast(\FM_k(\C)) \to \Etwo(k)$ on the closed colour).
\textbf{Step~3}: bar and cobar functors preserve quasi-isomorphisms
between augmented operads, so the two-out-of-three property gives
$\Omega \mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \simeq \SCchtop$.

The consequence (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-adjunction}) is that
the bar-cobar adjunction $(\Omega, \mathbf{B})$ on the category
of $\SCchtop$-algebras is a Quillen equivalence.  The further
result (Theorem~\ref{thm:filtered-koszul}) extends this beyond the
quadratic locus: for filtered chiral algebras with OPE poles of
arbitrary order, the filtered bar-cobar adjunction is still a
Quillen equivalence, incorporating the full Gui--Li--Zeng
framework~\cite{GLZ22} of chiral Koszul duality.

The word \emph{presents} now has a classical model.
The Steinberg variety
$\widetilde{\mathcal{N}} \times_{\mathcal{N}}
\widetilde{\mathcal{N}}$---the fibre product of the Springer
resolution with itself over the nilpotent cone---carries a
convolution product from composition of correspondences and a
stratification from the Bruhat decomposition.  The convolution
algebra is the Hecke algebra~$H(W,q)$: not defined by generators
and relations, but recovered from the geometry of the
correspondence space.  The Kazhdan--Lusztig polynomials, the BGG
correspondence, the character formulas for Category~$\cO$---all
extracted from the perverse filtration on the Steinberg variety.
The bar complex is the chiral-algebraic Steinberg variety.

\noindent
In both columns, the presentation-by-correspondence is the

$d^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ from the Hodge bundle, and the
Swiss-cheese algebra curves---just as the Hecke algebra deforms
with its parameter~$q$.


\section*{What the bar complex computes}

Three descent mechanisms extract the invariants encoded in the
bar complex.

\emph{Cohomological descent} produces the $(-1)$-shifted Poisson
vertex algebra on $H^\bullet(\cA, Q)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cohomology_PVA};
Section~\ref{sec:PVA_descent}).  The
mechanism is as follows.  The binary operation~$m_2$ on
$\FM_2(\C)$ decomposes into a regular part and a singular part
via Laurent projection:
\[
m_2(a, b)(\lambda) = a \cdot b + \{a \,{}_\lambda\, b\},
\]
where $a \cdot b = m_2(a,b)|_{\lambda = 0}^{\mathrm{reg}}$ is a
commutative product and $\{a {}_\lambda b\}$ collects the singular
terms---a $\lambda$-bracket
(Definition~\ref{def:lambda-bracket}).  The higher operations
$m_{k \geq 3}$ vanish on cohomology: the topological factor
$\Conf_k(\R)$ in $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k(\R)$ is
contractible for $k \geq 3$, and the resulting homotopies from
the Fulton--MacPherson calculus (Section~\ref{sec:FM_calculus})
kill the chain-level operations
(Proposition~\ref{prop:m3_vanish}).  The PVA
axioms---Jacobi (Theorem~\ref{thm:Jacobi}), Leibniz
(Theorem~\ref{thm:Leibniz}), skew-symmetry
(Proposition~\ref{prop:sesqui-skew})---follow from Arnold relations
and Stokes' theorem on $\FM_3(\C)$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:AOS-implies-Jacobi}), the same identities
that make $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ in Volume~I.  This is the content of
Section~\ref{sec:Ainfty-to-PVA}: a controlled forgetting map from
the full $\Ainf$ chiral structure to a Poisson vertex algebra,
retaining the product and $\lambda$-bracket while compressing the
higher operations into homotopies.

\emph{Spectral descent} produces the $R$-matrix from the
$\lambda$-bracket.  The bridge is the Laplace transform
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:intro-r-matrix}
r(z) \;=\; \int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda z}\,
\{{\cdot}\,{}_\lambda\,{\cdot}\}\, d\lambda,
\end{equation}
converting the PVA datum into a Yang--Baxter datum
(Section~\ref{sec:spectral_braiding}).  The
quantum $R$-matrix $R(z)$ from bulk-boundary composition
(Definition~\ref{def:spectral-braiding}) deformation-quantises
this classical limit and satisfies the Yang--Baxter equation
(Theorem~\ref{thm:YBE}) as a consequence of Stokes' theorem on
$\FM_3(\C)$---the same geometry, viewed from the spectral side.
For the Heisenberg atom, $r(z) = k/z^2$ and $R(z) = \exp(k/z)$.
For affine $\widehat{\fg}_k$, the classical $r$-matrix is the
standard Lie-algebraic $r(z) = \Omega/z$ and the quantum
$R$-matrix is the Yangian $R$-matrix of Drinfeld.  For Virasoro
and $\mathcal{W}_3$, the $\lambda$-bracket produces a classical
$r$-matrix satisfying the CYBE on the Virasoro Lie algebra,
with the central charge playing the role of the level
(Proposition~\ref{prop:vir-m2},
Theorem~\ref{thm:w3-CYBE}).

\emph{Genus descent} produces the curved Swiss-cheese algebras
over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.  The curvature
$d^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ couples the
$\R$-factorisation to the Hodge bundle; the non-vanishing of
higher $\Ainf$ operations at the chain level IS this curved bar
structure---formality fails precisely because the categorical
logarithm acquires monodromy.  The full genus tower of curved
$\SCchtop$-algebras is developed in Section~\ref{sec:bar_cobar}
and Part~V (Section~\ref{ch:modular-pva-quantization}).


\section*{The bulk--boundary--line Koszul triangle}

The three descents converge on the fundamental organising
structure of three-dimensional holomorphic--topological QFT:
the \emph{bulk--boundary--line Koszul triangle}
(Section~\ref{sec:ht-bulk-boundary-line}).

A 3d HT theory on $\C \times \R$ produces three algebraic
objects: the bulk chiral algebra $A_{\mathrm{bulk}}$ on
$\C \times \R$; the boundary vertex algebra $\cA^!$
(the Koszul dual) on~$\R$; and the line category
$\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!\text{-}\mathbf{mod}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:lines_as_modules}).  The naive identification
``bulk $\simeq$ boundary $\simeq$ line'' is false.  The corrected
triangle is:
\[
A_{\mathrm{bulk}} \;\simeq\;
Z_{\mathrm{der}}(B_\partial) \;\simeq\;
\HH^\bullet(\cA^!)\text{:}
\]
the bulk is the \emph{derived centre} of the boundary.  This is
the mathematical content of the holographic principle in the
holomorphic--topological setting.

The triangle is proved in two stages.  At the exact
boundary-linear level (Section~\ref{sec:boundary-linear}), the
bulk--boundary theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:boundary-linear-bulk-boundary}) identifies the
bulk algebra with the derived centre of the boundary dga, using
derived critical locus technology
(Theorem~\ref{thm:dcrit-shiftedcot}).  Line operators at this
level are presented by one-step Jacobi coalgebras
(Theorem~\ref{thm:exact-line}), and boundary Kuranishi elimination
(Theorem~\ref{thm:boundary-kuranishi}) reduces the algebra of
pointed lines to a minimal model with explicit recursive
coefficients (Proposition~\ref{prop:recursive-kuranishi}).  The
passage to the full non-linear triangle requires the Koszul dual
Yangian (Theorem~\ref{thm:Koszul_dual_Yangian}): the endomorphism
algebra of the fibre functor on
$\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{line}}$ is identified with a
dg-shifted Yangian $Y_\cA^{\mathrm{dg}}(z)$
(Definition~\ref{def:dg_Yangian}), whose RTT
presentation governs the spectral $R$-matrix via the
factorisation quantum group
(Definition~\ref{def:factorisation_QG},
Theorem~\ref{thm:lines_factorisationQG}).

Each vertex of the triangle is represented in practice by a
chosen strict model---a dg algebra, an $\Ainf$ boundary algebra,
or a dg category of modules---but the triangle itself is
invariant only up to derived/Morita and $\Linf$ equivalence
(Remark~\ref{rem:triangle-homotopy-first}).  This is the central
instance of the homotopy upgrade: the strict presentations are
legible coordinates on an invariant homotopy-coherent object, and
the triangle becomes false or badly distorted if any corner is
artificially strictified too early.

\section*{The homotopy upgrade}

The strict dg structures that appear throughout this volume---dg
Lie algebras, dg categories, bar and cobar complexes---are never
the full story.  They are coordinates on larger homotopy-coherent
objects.  This principle, already present in Volume~I's treatment
of the convolution dg Lie algebra as a strict model for the
modular $\Linf$ structure, becomes inescapable in three
dimensions.

The reason is physical.  A 3d HT theory on $\C \times \R$
produces local operators, boundary operators, and line operators.
The OPE of local operators is governed by $\FM_k(\C)$, which is
formal (Kontsevich); but the OPE of line operators is governed by
$\Eone$, which is \emph{not} formal in the presence of
holomorphic dependence.  Nested collisions---a cluster of
holomorphic operators colliding with a cluster of topological
operators---generate higher $\Ainf$ and brace operations
automatically.  The BV-BRST construction
(Section~\ref{sec:BV_construction}) makes this explicit: the BV
differential on the space of observables on $\C \times \R$ is the
BRST charge of a gauge-fixed action, and the anomaly brackets
that correct it at each loop order are the higher operations~$m_k$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:Obs-is-SC}).  The $\Ainf$ chiral axioms of
Section~\ref{sec:axioms-Ainfty-chiral} are the minimal package
needed to capture this content honestly.

The homotopy upgrade manifests concretely at five points in the
volume:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The $\SCchtop$-algebra structure is a
  $C_\ast(W(\SCchtop))$-algebra, not a strict operad algebra.
  The rectification theorem
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:rectification}) identifies when strict and
  homotopy-coherent presentations coincide.
\item Chiral Hochschild cochains form a brace algebra
  (Section~\ref{sec:chiral-Hochschild}), whose Gerstenhaber
  bracket is the binary Taylor coefficient of the filtered
  $\Linf$-structure governing deformations.
\item The bulk--boundary--line triangle is invariant only up to
  derived/Morita and $\Linf$ equivalence.  The triangle becomes
  false if any vertex is artificially strictified.
\item The spectral $R$-matrix is a component of a
  homotopy-coherent braiding on the $\Ainf$-module category; the
  Yang--Baxter equation is one coherence law among many.
\item The genus tower of curved Swiss-cheese algebras over
  $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ is a genus-filtered $\Linf$-object;
  the strict dg model at each genus is a truncation whose
  higher coherences are the source of the modular corrections
  from Volume~I's Theorem~D.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The convention throughout this volume
(Convention~\ref{conv:vol2-strict-models}) is: whenever a strict dg
structure appears, the reader should understand that it is one chart
on an underlying homotopy deformation object.  The strict model is
used for computation; the homotopy-coherent package is the
invariant.


\section*{Plan of the volume}

The volume has five parts, each building on the previous.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~I: From the Bar Complex to the Swiss-Cheese
Operad} (\S\ref{sec:foundations}--\S\ref{sec:BV_construction}).
The structural heart of the volume.  The two-coloured operad
$\SCchtop$ is defined
(Section~\ref{sec:HT-operad}), and the bar complex of a chiral
algebra is shown to be an algebra over it
(Section~\ref{sec:foundations}).  The concrete $\Ainf$ chiral
axioms are extracted
(Section~\ref{sec:axioms-Ainfty-chiral}) and proved equivalent to
$W(\SCchtop)$-algebra structure
(Section~\ref{sec:equivalence}).  The Fulton--MacPherson calculus
(Section~\ref{sec:FM_calculus}) supplies the geometric proofs: the
$\Ainf$ relations are Stokes' theorem on boundary strata of
$\FM_k(\C)$, with explicit formulas for~$m_2$ and~$m_3$
(Propositions~\ref{prop:m2_formula} and~\ref{prop:m3_formula}).
The BV-BRST construction
(Section~\ref{sec:BV_construction}) shows that BV quantisation of
a 3d HT field theory on $\C \times \R$ produces exactly these
bar complexes (Theorem~\ref{thm:Obs-is-SC}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~II: The Descent Calculus}
(\S\ref{sec:FM_calculus}--\S\ref{sec:chiral-Hochschild}).
Two descent mechanisms extract invariants from the bar complex.
Cohomological descent produces the $(-1)$-shifted PVA on
cohomology (Theorem~\ref{thm:cohomology_PVA}), with the
regular/singular decomposition of~$m_2$ yielding the product and
$\lambda$-bracket (Section~\ref{sec:PVA_descent},
Section~\ref{sec:Ainfty-to-PVA}).  Chiral Hochschild cohomology
(Section~\ref{sec:chiral_hochschild_expanded}) identifies bulk
observables with Hochschild-type cochains, bridging the 2d Poisson
sigma model to the 3d HT setting via dimensional descent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:2d_PSM_Hochschild}).  The chiral bar--cobar
review (Section~\ref{sec:bar_cobar}) places the quadratic duality
of Gui--Li--Zeng in context and extends it beyond the quadratic
locus.  Chiral Hochschild cochains are shown to form a brace
algebra on $\FM(\C) \times \Conf(\R)$
(Section~\ref{sec:chiral-Hochschild}), whose Gerstenhaber bracket
governs deformations.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Part~III: Dualities and the Bulk--Boundary--Line
Triangle}
(\S\ref{sec:line_operators}--\S\ref{sec:ht-bulk-boundary-line}).
The Koszul triangle is constructed from first principles.
Homotopy-Koszulity of~$\SCchtop$ is proved
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}), yielding the Quillen
equivalence (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-adjunction}).  Line
operators are identified with $\cA^!$-modules
(Theorem~\ref{thm:lines_as_modules}), the spectral $R$-matrix
is constructed (Definition~\ref{def:spectral-braiding}) and shown
to satisfy Yang--Baxter (Theorem~\ref{thm:YBE}), and the

\section*{Beyond the Heisenberg atom: the standard landscape}

Beyond the Heisenberg atom, four families test the full
machine and exhibit the range of phenomena that the formalism
captures.

The \emph{Landau--Ginzburg model} provides the first
\emph{interacting} example: a genuine~$m_3$ from a cubic
superpotential, with the bar complex acquiring a non-trivial
higher coderivation and the shifted PVA inheriting a non-trivial
$\lambda$-bracket.  Truncation by polynomial degree
(Proposition~\ref{prop:truncation_criterion}) shows that $m_k = 0$
for $k > \deg W$, so the $\Ainf$ structure is finite.  This is the
simplest setting where the homotopy transfer theorem of Volume~I
produces transferred operations as tree-level Feynman sums.

\emph{Abelian Chern--Simons with boundary} provides the first
\emph{braided} example: an explicit spectral $R$-matrix
from the CS propagator, with the boundary vertex algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{u}(1)}_k$ and line operators the modules of
the affine algebra.  The Yang--Baxter equation is verified
directly from $\FM_3(\C)$.

\emph{Affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{su}(2)}_k$} tests the full
non-abelian Swiss-cheese machine.  The
$\lambda$-bracket is the affine Lie bracket, $m_3$ is non-zero
(from the cubic Casimir structure of the Jacobi identity), and the
$R$-matrix is the Yangian $R$-matrix of Drinfeld.  At genus~$1$,
the curvature is $\kappa = k \dim \fg$, reproducing Volume~I's
computation.

The \emph{Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}_3$ algebras} are the decisive
non-linear tests.  These are precisely the examples that punish
any attempt to remain in a strictly quadratic or dg world: the OPE
has poles of order~$4$ (Virasoro) and~$6$ ($\mathcal{W}_3$), so
the bar complex has non-vanishing coderivations in all arities, and
the $\Ainf$ structure is genuinely infinite.  The recursive
construction of~$m_k$ from the BV master equation, the classical
Yang--Baxter equation from the $\lambda$-bracket, and the
central-charge shift $c \mapsto 26 - c$ under Koszul duality all
pass through this non-linear regime.

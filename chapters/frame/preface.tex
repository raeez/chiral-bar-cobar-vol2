\section*{I.\quad Two directions}

\noindent
A chiral algebra is a point in the derived symplectic category: a Lagrangian
$\cL_\cA \hookrightarrow \cM_{\mathrm{vac}}(\cA)$.  The bar complex is the
endomorphism algebra of this point.  The Swiss-cheese operad is the local
composition law.  Koszul duality is the symplectic complement.  The genus tower
is the deformation of the point over the moduli of curves.  The holographic
principle is the theorem that a point determines its ambient.  The entire content
of both volumes is the local geometry of the derived symplectic category at this
single point.

The Heisenberg algebra is the simplest instance: every entry can be computed by hand.  We begin there, then build the general machine.

\medskip
\noindent
Two operators $a(z_1, t_1)$ and $b(z_2, t_2)$ on $\C \times \R$
interact in two independent directions.

In the \emph{holomorphic direction} ($z_1 \to z_2$), collision produces the OPE residue~$a_{(n)}b$.  Volume~I built the entire modular Koszul theory from this mechanism: the bar differential
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}a \,|\, s^{-1}b]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\bigl[
  a(z_1)\,b(z_2)\cdot\eta_{12}
\bigr],
\qquad
\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2),
\]
the Arnold relation ($\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23} + \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31} + \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0$) forcing $D_\cA^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$, the curvature $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ at higher genus, and the universal Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_0$ whose projections are the five theorems.

In the \emph{topological direction}, the operators separate: cut $\R$ at a point $t_*$ between $t_1$ and $t_2$.  The observables on each half-line depend only on their own operators.  The \emph{deconcatenation coproduct} records this separation:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface2-delta}
\Delta[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n]
\;=\;
\sum_{i=0}^{n}
[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_i]
\;\otimes\;
[s^{-1}a_{i+1}|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n].
\end{equation}
The two directions are independent:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface2-coderivation}
\Delta \circ D_\cA
\;=\;
(D_\cA \otimes \id \;+\; \id \otimes D_\cA) \circ \Delta.
\end{equation}
The differential is a coderivation of the coproduct.  Collisions in $\C$ commute with cuts in $\R$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Steinberg principle.}\enspace
The bar complex presents the Swiss-cheese algebra, as the Steinberg variety presents the Hecke algebra.  The Steinberg variety $\widetilde{\cN} \times_{\fg} \widetilde{\cN}$ is one geometric object; its Borel--Moore homology, its $K$-theory, and its derived category extract three different algebraic invariants: the Hecke algebra, the Springer correspondence, and the Kazhdan--Lusztig basis.  The bar complex $\barB(\cA)$ on $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^{<}(\R)$ is one coalgebra; three functors extract three objects: cobar $\Omega(\barBch(\cA)) \simeq \cA$ recovers the original algebra (bar-cobar inversion), Verdier duality $\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}(\barBch(\cA)) \simeq \barBch(\cA^!)$ produces the line-side Koszul dual, and chiral Hochschild cochains $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ compute the bulk observables.  Every chapter of this volume is a different projection of this single object.  We elaborate the parallel precisely in \S VII below; the reader may take the analogy as the organising principle for everything that follows.

The projections of $\Theta_\cA$ through the Swiss-cheese window include the celestial OPE hierarchy (soft order~$p$ corresponds to shadow arity $r = p+2$; Remark~\ref{rem:celestial-ope-shadow-arity}) and the Page curve of black hole evaporation (the Page time $t_P = 3S_{\mathrm{BH}}/13$ is $c$-independent because $\kappa + \kappa' = 13$; \S\ref{subsec:gravity-page-curve}).

\medskip

The independence is geometric.  The bar complex is a formal Koszul model of the Lagrangian self-intersection $\cL \times_{\mathscr{M}}^h \cL$ in a $(-2)$-shifted symplectic stack~$\mathscr{M}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-is-self-intersection}).  The holomorphic direction is the Koszul differential of the Lagrangian embedding; the topological direction is the groupoid diagonal.  The coderivation property says the diagonal is a groupoid morphism.  Both structures descend from the single fact that the bar complex computes a derived fiber product.  The chiral algebra fixes a formal local bulk model: HKR for Lagrangian embeddings recovers $T^*[-1]\cL_\cA$ from boundary data; the actual ambient stack requires the Darboux $1$-form (Theorem~\ref{thm:holographic-reconstruction}).

The open-colour dual $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ inherits an algebra structure from the topological direction (deconcatenation dualises to concatenation), and on the chirally Koszul locus this algebra is a dg-shifted Yangian: an $\Ainf$ algebra with spectral $R$-matrix, twisted coproduct, and Yang--Baxter equation (the last being $d^2 = 0$ on the degree-$3$ ordered bar complex).  Strictification of the quasi-factorisation package to a spectral factorisation quantum group is controlled by a single coefficient: $1/n = \int_0^1(1-t)^{n-1}\,dt$ at filtration~$n$, the coefficient of the right-normed Lie monomial in the Dynkin projection $D_n(X_1\cdots X_n) = \tfrac{1}{n}\,[X_1,[X_2,\ldots,X_n]\cdots]$.  Root multiplicity one for simple Lie algebras forces every spectral Drinfeld obstruction into a one-dimensional space where this coefficient determines everything (Theorem~\ref{thm:complete-strictification}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Vol~I engine.}\enspace
Volume~I is Koszul duality on a curve: the bar construction for chiral algebras on an algebraic curve~$X$, five main theorems proved at all genera.  Three structure theorems organise the representation-theoretic content.  \emph{Algebraicity}: $H(t)^2 = t^4 Q_L(t)$ for a computable quadratic~$Q_L$, so the shadow generating function is algebraic of degree~$2$.  \emph{Formality identification}: the shadow obstruction tower agrees with the $L_\infty$ formality obstruction tower at all arities (Theorem~\ref{V1-thm:shadow-formality-identification}), and the discriminant $\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$ classifies algebras into four depth classes~(G/L/C/M).  \emph{Complementarity}: under perfectness and chain-level nondegeneracy (satisfied for all standard families; conditional in general), factorisation homology decomposes as $H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, Z(A)) \simeq Q_g(A) \oplus Q_g(A^!)$, a Lagrangian splitting.

Volume~II lifts this from one to three dimensions.  The bar differential~$D_\cA$ is holomorphic factorisation on~$\C$; the deconcatenation coproduct~$\Delta$ is topological factorisation on~$\R$; together they form the $\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-coalgebra on $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$.  The modular characteristic~$\kappa(\cA)$ determines the curved Swiss-cheese deformation at genus $g \ge 1$, complementarity lifts to the bulk-boundary-line triangle, and the discriminant~$\Delta$ classifies the complexity of bulk-boundary pairs.  Classical Koszul duality is genus~$0$ on a point; Volume~I is genus~$g$ on a curve; Volume~II is the dimensional lift to $\C \times \R$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The open primitive.}\enspace
The bar complex is not the fundamental object.  It is the coalgebraic shadow of a more primitive datum: the open/closed factorization dg-category~$\cC$ on the bordified curve $\widetilde{X}_D$ (\S\ref{subsec:categorical-open-sector}).  This category's objects are boundary conditions; its morphisms are open-string states.  A choice of vacuum boundary condition~$b$ produces a boundary algebra $A_b = \End_\cC(b)$ as a \emph{chart}; the bar complex $\barB(A_b)$ encodes how closed-sector operations twist the open-sector composition.  The Morita class of~$\cC$, not the quasi-isomorphism class of~$A_b$, is the invariant.  Three objects are independently constructed from~$\cC$ and must never be conflated:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item the \emph{bar complex} $\barB(A_b)$ classifies twisting morphisms (couplings between~$A_b$ and its line-side/open-colour Koszul dual~$A^!_{\mathrm{line}}$);
\item the \emph{chiral derived center} $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cC) = \RHom_{\operatorname{Fun}(\cC,\cC)}(\operatorname{Id}, \operatorname{Id})$ is the universal bulk;
\item the \emph{line-side/open-colour Koszul dual algebra} $A^!_{\mathrm{line}} = D_{\operatorname{Ran}}(\barB(A_b))$, which later models the line side on the chirally Koszul locus.
\end{itemize}
Modularity is not an additional axiom.  It is trace plus clutching on the open sector: the cyclic trace on $\End(b)$ seeds a Calabi--Yau structure; the annulus identification $\int_{S^1} \cC \simeq \HH_\bullet(\cC)$ is the first modular shadow; and clutching (composing along shared boundary circles via nodal degeneration) raises genus.  The genus expansion of the modular Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta^{\mathrm{oc}}_\cA$ is the algebraic modular tower.  The shadow archetypes~G/L/C/M classify the $A_\infty$ complexity of the boundary algebra; in the gravitational examples below, each archetype determines a distinct perturbative structure.

\section*{II.\quad The Heisenberg on $\C \times \R$}

Take $\cA = \cH_k$: one generator $J$, one OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$.  Two operators sit at $(z_1, t_1)$
and $(z_2, t_2)$ with $t_1 < t_2$.  Their bar element is
$[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The holomorphic direction.}\enspace
The bar differential is the codimension-$1$ boundary operator
on $\FM_k(\C)$, the Fulton--MacPherson compactification (the iterated real blowup of $\C^k$ along all diagonal strata).  For two insertions, the boundary stratum
$E_{12} \cong S^1$ of the FM blowup extracts the mode products
$J_{(n)}J$ from the OPE.  The only nonvanishing mode is
$J_{(1)}J = k$ (the level), so
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
\;=\;
J_{(1)}J
\;=\; k.
\]
There is no simple-pole OPE ($J_{(0)}J = 0$), so the bracket
component of the bar differential vanishes.  The entire
holomorphic interaction is the scalar~$k$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The topological direction.}\enspace
Cut $\R$ at each possible gap:
\begin{align*}
\Delta[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
&\;=\;
[\,] \otimes [s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut before $t_1$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J] \otimes [s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut between $t_1$ and $t_2$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] \otimes [\,]
&&\text{(cut after $t_2$)}.
\end{align*}
The middle term places one field on each half-line.  Since
$J_{(0)}J = 0$, the two separated fields do not interact: the
factorisation is exact.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The coderivation property.}\enspace
Apply $D_\cA$ to the left-hand side:
$D_\cA[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] = k$, then
$\Delta(k) = k \otimes [\,] + [\,] \otimes k$.
Apply $(D_\cA \otimes \id + \id \otimes D_\cA)$ to the
right-hand side: only the first and third summands contribute
(the middle term $[s^{-1}J] \otimes [s^{-1}J]$ gives zero because
$D_\cA(s^{-1}J) = 0$), producing $[\,] \otimes k + k \otimes [\,]$.
Both sides agree.  The collision in $\C$ and the cut in $\R$
commute.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The configuration-space product.}\enspace
The holomorphic collision lives on $\FM_2(\C)$.  The topological
cut lives on $\Conf_2^<(\R) = \{t_1 < t_2\} \cong \R_{>0}$,
which is contractible.  The bar element
$[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]$ is parametrised by the product
\[
\FM_2(\C) \times \Conf_2^<(\R).
\]
The differential sees the first factor.  The coproduct sees the
second.  The coderivation property is the independence of the two
factors.  The same decomposition holds at degree~$n$:
\[
\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ch},n}(\cA)
\;\subset\;
(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}
\otimes
\Omega^\bullet\bigl(\FM_n(\C) \times \Conf_n^<(\R)\bigr).
\]
The ordered configuration space $\Conf_n^<(\R) \cong
\R^{n-1}_{> 0}$ is contractible; its $E_1$-coalgebra structure
contracts to the strict coassociative coproduct: deconcatenation.
No higher homotopies survive.  The coproduct is the unique
coassociative coproduct on $T^c(V)$, determined by the
cogenerators.


\medskip\noindent\textbf{The Feynman transform and 't~Hooft expansion.}\enspace
Volume~I organises every genus-tower structure through the
convolution principle: the modular convolution dg Lie algebra
$\gAmod := \Hom_\Sigma(F\!\Com,\, \End_{\barB(\cA)})$
carries a dg Lie bracket from operadic composition, and
its universal MC element
$\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_0$ encodes the full genus tower.
For the Heisenberg algebra~$\cH_k$, the MC element collapses to a
scalar: $\Theta_{\cH_k} = k \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$, and its
genus-$g$ projection is
$F_g(\cH_k) = k \cdot \lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$.
Here $F\!\Com$ is the Feynman transform of the commutative
modular operad $\Com$ (with $\Com(g,n) = \Bbbk$ for all stable $(g,n)$), generated by stable graphs with edge-contraction
differential ($D^2 = 0$ from $\partial^2 = 0$ on
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$).

The associative variant
${\gAmod}^{E_1} := \Hom_\Sigma(F\!\Ass,\,
\End_{\barB^{E_1}(\cA)})$
($F\!\Ass$ is the Feynman transform of the associative modular operad, summing over ribbon graphs rather than stable graphs)
replaces stable graphs by \emph{ribbon graphs} (fatgraphs)
and is the algebraic form of the 't~Hooft $1/N$ expansion:
$\hbar = 1/N^2$, the MC element is
$\sum_g N^{2-2g}F_g(\lambda)$, planar diagrams dominate at
large~$N$, and the coinvariant projection
$\Theta^{E_1} \mapsto \Theta$ is the passage from
colour-ordered to full amplitudes.

Volume~II promotes this to a two-coloured convolution algebra: $\SCchtop$ couples the $\C$-direction (Feynman transform, holomorphic) to the $\R$-direction (coproduct, topological), and the MC element $\alpha_T \in \MC(\gSC_T)$ encodes both.

\section*{III.\quad The Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$}

The product $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$ is the space of
operations of a two-coloured operad:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{closed-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C), \\
&\text{mixed-to-open:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k, \mathsf{top}^m;\,\mathsf{top})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C) \times E_1(m), \\
&\text{open-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\ldots, \mathsf{top}, \ldots;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \varnothing.
\end{alignat*}
Closed colour: holomorphic collisions on $\FM_k(\C)$.  Open colour:
topological orderings on $E_1(m) = \Conf_m^<(\R)$.  Mixed: the
product.  Composition is FM substitution on the closed factor,
interval insertion on the open factor.  The empty open-to-closed
component is directionality: bulk restricts to boundary, not
conversely.

The bar complex $(\barBch(\cA), D_\cA, \Delta)$ is a coalgebra
over $\SCchtop$: the differential is the closed-colour
co-structure and the coproduct is the open-colour co-structure.
By the Koszul self-duality $(\SCchtop)^! \simeq \SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:SC-self-duality}), the open-colour dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ inherits an $\SCchtop$-\emph{algebra}
structure.  The verification
for the Heisenberg algebra is the four-step proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:rosetta-swiss-cheese}, carried out in the Rosetta
Stone chapter.

The closed colour is the Koszul direction (holomorphic collisions on~$\C$); the open colour is the groupoid direction (topological cuts along~$\R$).  Directionality (the empty open-to-closed component) reflects the Springer pattern: representations restrict from $G$ to $B$, not conversely.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Homotopy-Koszulity.}\enspace
The classical Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}$ ($E_2$ closed, $E_1$ open) is Koszul (Livernet).
Kontsevich formality gives a quasi-isomorphism
$\SCchtop \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathsf{SC}$.  Bar-cobar transfer
gives
\[
\Omega\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\; \SCchtop.
\]
The bar-cobar adjunction on $\SCchtop$-algebras is a Quillen
equivalence (Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}).


\section*{IV.\quad The $\Ainf$ chiral operations}

The bar differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation of the cofree
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$.  A coderivation on a cofree
coalgebra is determined by its cogenerator projections: a sequence
of multilinear maps
\[
m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \longrightarrow
A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1})),
\qquad k \ge 1, \quad \deg m_k = 2 - k,
\]
where $\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$ are spectral parameters recording
holomorphic separations.  The identity $D_\cA^2 = 0$ expands into
the $\Ainf$ Stasheff relations: each relation is Stokes' theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$.  At $n=1$: $Q^2 = 0$ (BRST).  At $n=2$: $Q$ is a graded derivation of $m_2$ (Leibniz).  At $n=3$: strict associativity fails by the homotopy $m_3$, the integral over the Stasheff associahedron $K_3 = [0,1]$.  The higher operations $m_k$ are not independent: they are the unique solution of a tree-level bootstrap equation (the resolvent principle, Theorem~\ref{thm:resolvent-principle}), with each $m_k^H$ a sum over $C_{k-1}$ planar binary trees.  The full Stasheff hierarchy and resolvent formula are developed in the Introduction.


\section*{V.\quad Three computations}

The machine is tested on three families of increasing complexity; full details are in the Introduction and Part~IV.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg} (class~$\mathbf{G}$, formal).
$m_2(J,J;\lambda) = k\lambda$, all $m_{k \ge 3} = 0$.
PVA: $\{J_\lambda J\} = k\lambda$.
Classical $r$-matrix: $r(z) = k/z$ (collision residue; the $d\log$ kernel absorbs one pole order from the double-pole OPE).
Quantum $R$-matrix: $R(z) = \exp(k\hbar/z)$.
Chiral Koszul dual: $\cA^!_{\mathrm{ch}} = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$ (note $\cH_k^! \neq \cH_{-k}$).
Open-colour dual: $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = Y(\mathfrak{u}(1))$; line category = Fock modules.
Bulk: $\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\cH_k) = \cH_k$.
Genus~$1$: $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$; $F_1 = -k\log\eta(\tau)$.
The single scalar $k$ controls every entry.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Kac--Moody} (class~$\mathbf{L}$, Lie-transverse).
$m_2(J^a, J^b;\lambda) = f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c + k\,\delta^{ab}\lambda$; $m_3 \ne 0$ (Jacobiator homotopy); $m_{k \ge 4} = 0$.
Classical $r$-matrix: $r(z) = \Omega/z$; the KZ connection $\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}} = d - \hbar\,\Omega/z$ is the flat connection on conformal blocks.
Open-colour dual: $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = \Ydg(\fg)$ (dg-shifted Yangian).
Genus~$1$: $\kappa(\widehat{\fg}_k) = \dim\fg\,(k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$;
Feigin--Frenkel anti-symmetry for affine KM: $\kappa(\widehat{\fg}_k) + \kappa(\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee}) = 0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{$\mathcal{W}_3$} (class~$\mathbf{M}$, infinite tower).
The first family where the $\Ainf$ structure is genuinely infinite: $m_2(T,T;\lambda) = (c/12)\lambda^3 + 2T\lambda + \partial T$; the $WW$ operation involves the composite $\Lambda = {}\!:(TT): - \tfrac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T$ with coefficient $32/(22+5c)$; $m_3, m_4 \ne 0$ and the tower does not terminate.
The cumulant coalgebra $\operatorname{Cum}_c(\mathcal{W}_3)$ has primitive generating series $G(t) = (t + t^2 + t^3)/(1 - t)$ and completion entropy $h_K \approx 0.772$: the first family where kinematic complexity exceeds the Virasoro baseline.


\section*{VI.\quad The 3d MC element $\alpha_T$}

Volume~I has one MC element $\Theta_\cA$ in the modular
convolution algebra $\gAmod$.  Volume~II has one MC element
$\alpha_T$ in the Swiss-cheese convolution algebra
\[
\gSC_T
\;:=\;
\Hom_\Sigma\!\bigl(
  \mathbf{B}(\SCchtop),\;
  \End_{(\Bbound,\,\cC_{\mathrm{line}})}
\bigr).
\]
Its $L_\infty$-brackets are indexed by two-coloured stable trees:
closed vertices labelled by $\FM_k(\C)$-chains, open vertices by
$E_1(m)$-chains, mixed vertices by products.  The MC element is
\[
\alpha_T
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{SC}}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma \otimes \Phi_\Gamma^T
\;\in\;
\mc(\gSC_T).
\]
It exists because $\SCchtop$ is homotopy-Koszul
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}), hence
$\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop)$ has $\partial^2 = 0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Six direct faces.}\enspace
The basic closed/open/mixed data of the volume are read directly
from $\alpha_T$; the corrected triangle and line-side package are
assembled later from these projected data together with the scoped
comparison theorems:

\noindent
The scoped identifications
$\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}\text{-mod}$ and
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound) \simeq
\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}})$ are the later

\medskip
\noindent
Two further consequences of this projected data are proved in the
frontier chapters:
the complete strictification theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:complete-strictification}) shows that the open-face
spectral quasi-factorization package strictifies once constructed
(via root multiplicity one), and the general half-space BV
theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:general-half-space-bv}) shows every PVA
with inner translation deformation-quantizes to a boundary vertex
algebra (via the doubling construction).

The spectral Drinfeld strictification extends beyond simple Lie algebras to all untwisted affine Kac--Moody algebras via the abelianness mechanism: imaginary root spaces $\fg_{n\delta} \simeq \fh \cdot t^n$ are abelian, and the Cartan gauge-twist forces the spectral Drinfeld class to vanish.  The boundary is sharp: for hyperbolic Kac--Moody algebras with non-abelian root spaces, strictification genuinely fails.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Colour decomposition.}\enspace
Write $\alpha_T = \alpha_T^{\mathrm{cl}}
+ \alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}} + \alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}}$
by the colour grading of the Swiss-cheese operad.  The
MC equation decomposes:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{pure closed:}\quad
&&D\alpha_T^{\mathrm{cl}}
  + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha_T^{\mathrm{cl}},
  \alpha_T^{\mathrm{cl}}] = 0, \\
&\text{mixed:}\quad
&&d_{\Theta}\,\alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}} = 0, \\
&\text{pure open:}\quad
&&d_{\Theta}\,\alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}}
  + [\alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}},
  \alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}}]_{\mathrm{op}}
  + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}},
  \alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}}]_{\mathrm{op}}
  + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}},
  \alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}}]_{\mathrm{op}} = 0,
\end{alignat*}
where $d_\Theta = D + [\Theta_\cA, -]$ and
$\Theta_\cA := \alpha_T^{\mathrm{cl}}$.  The first is the Vol~I
MC equation.  The second says that the $r$-matrix and the PVA
bracket are $\Theta_\cA$-twisted cocycles.  The third is the full
open MC equation; in the examples below (genus~$0$), $\alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}} = 0$
and the equation reduces to $\tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}},
\alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}}]_{\mathrm{op}} = 0$, which closes the
line-category structure.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg.}\enspace
$\gSC_T$ is abelian.
$\alpha_{\cH_k} = m_2 + \hbar\,k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda$.
No mixed contributions and trivial open-colour higher
operations.  All six projections:

\section*{IX.\quad The bulk-boundary-line triangle}

A holomorphic--topological theory on $\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$
produces three algebraic objects: \emph{bulk} local operators
$\Abulk$ (interior), \emph{boundary} local operators $\Bbound$
(on $\{0\} \times \C$), and \emph{line} operators
$\cC_{\mathrm{line}}$ (along $\R_+ \times \{0\}$).
On the chirally Koszul locus (boundary-linear exact sector),
the strongest relation is
\[
\Abulk
\;\simeq\;
\Zder(\Bbound),
\qquad
\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}\text{-mod}.
\]
Unconditionally, bulk identifies with chiral Hochschild cochains
of the boundary algebra; the derived-center and line-module
identifications retain the scoped hypotheses above.

The \emph{bulk} vertex is the derived centre
$\Zder(\Bbound) :=
\RHom_{\Bbound\text{-}\Bbound}(\Bbound,\,\Bbound)$.
For Heisenberg: $\Zder(\cH_k) = \cH_k$.
For Kac--Moody at $k = -h^\vee$: the Feigin--Frenkel centre
$\mathfrak{z}(\widehat{\fg}_{-h^\vee})$, the algebra of
${}^L\!G$-opers.  The \emph{line} vertex is modeled, on the
chirally Koszul locus, by $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$-modules:
for Heisenberg,
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = Y(\mathfrak{u}(1)) \simeq \cH_{-k}$
(Fock modules); for Kac--Moody,
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = \Ydg(\fg)$.

\subsection*{The spectral $R$-matrix and Yang--Baxter}

Two parallel line operators $L_1(z_1)$, $L_2(z_2)$ interact
through the bulk, producing a meromorphic braiding
$R_{L_1, L_2}(z) \colon L_1 \otimes L_2
\xrightarrow{\sim}
L_2 \otimes L_1$ in $z = z_1 - z_2$.
Three lines braided in two orders give the Yang--Baxter equation
\[
R_{12}(z_{12})\,R_{13}(z_{13})\,R_{23}(z_{23})
\;=\;
R_{23}(z_{23})\,R_{13}(z_{13})\,R_{12}(z_{12}),
\]
from the same $\FM_3(\C)$ Stokes mechanism as the PVA Jacobi
identity: the Jacobi identity is the closed-colour projection,
the Yang--Baxter equation is the open-colour projection, and both
are shadows of the single $n = 3$ Stasheff identity.

For Heisenberg: $R(z) = e^{k\hbar/z}$ (scalar, YBE trivial).
For Kac--Moody on evaluation modules: the spectral $R(z)$ agrees
with the quantum group $R$-matrix of $U_\hbar(\fg)$, with
$r(z) = \Omega/z$ as classical limit.


\section*{X.\quad PVA quantization: from classical to quantum}

The PVA $\{-_\lambda -\}$ on $H^\bullet(A, Q)$ is the
\emph{classical} shadow of the $\Ainf$ chiral algebra.  The
quantization problem is the reverse: given a PVA, construct the
$\Ainf$ chiral algebra whose cohomological descent recovers it.

This is a lifting problem.  Start with a PVA
$(V, \cdot, \{-_\lambda -\})$.  The bracket
$\{a_\lambda b\} = \sum_{n \ge 0} \lambda^{(n)}\,a_{(n)}b$
encodes the singular part of the OPE; the product
$a \cdot b$ encodes the regular part.  Together they determine
$m_2(a,b;\lambda)$ on cohomology.  The quantization question: does
there exist an $\Ainf$ chiral algebra~$A$ with $H^\bullet(A) = V$
and $m_2|_{H^\bullet} = \mu + \{-_\lambda-\}$?

The obstruction to lifting lives in the Hochschild cohomology
$\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$: the failure of the Jacobi identity
to hold at the chain level, after the first-order lift, is a
cocycle whose class is the obstruction.  When the obstruction
vanishes, the lift exists and is controlled by a Maurer--Cartan
element in the chiral deformation complex
$\mathrm{Def}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Heisenberg quantization.}\enspace
The PVA $\{J_\lambda J\} = k\lambda$ lifts to the Heisenberg
$\cH_k$ with $m_2(J,J;\lambda) = k\lambda$ and $m_{k \ge 3} = 0$.
The obstruction vanishes: $\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(\C[J]) = 0$
(the PVA is freely generated).  The quantization is unique.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Kac--Moody quantization.}\enspace
The affine PVA $\{J^a_\lambda J^b\} = f^{ab}{}_c J^c +
k\delta^{ab}\lambda$ lifts to $\widehat{\fg}_k$.  The
obstruction at $m_3$ is the Jacobiator, which is a coboundary:
$m_3$ exists and equals the homotopy-transfer correction from
$\FM_3(\C)$.  Higher obstructions vanish by dimension.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Khan--Zeng 3d sigma model.}\enspace
Khan--Zeng construct a 3d holomorphic--topological sigma model
from any PVA target.  The boundary algebra of this theory is the
quantization of the PVA\@.  The BV-BRST complex of the sigma model
on $\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$, with the PVA as target space data,
produces the $\Ainf$ operations $m_k$ by Feynman-diagram evaluation
on $\FM_k(\C)$: each $k$-ary operation is a sum of tree Feynman
diagrams with $k$ external legs and vertices weighted by the PVA
brackets.  The Stasheff identity is Stokes' theorem on the boundary
of the Feynman-integral compactification, the same boundary
cancellation that gives the $\Ainf$ relations from $\FM_k(\C)$.

This sigma-model construction gives the physical proof of the
quantization theorem: the PVA data determine a consistent field
theory, and the field theory produces the $\Ainf$ algebra.  The
mathematical proof is the bar-cobar correspondence of
Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}: the Quillen equivalence
guarantees essential uniqueness of the $\SCchtop$-algebra structure,
hence the quantization is unique up to contractible ambiguity.

\subsection*{Twisted holography}

  A \wedge dA + \tfrac{2}{3} A \wedge A \wedge A
\bigr)
\;+\; S_{\mathrm{matter}}.
\]
The holomorphic--topological twist selects the supercharge
$Q = Q_+ + \bar{Q}_-$, under which the gauge field decomposes into
a holomorphic component $A_z \,dz$ and a topological component
$A_t \,dt$.  The BV-BRST complex of the twisted theory on
$\R_+ \times \C$ has:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Boundary observables} ($t = 0$): the chiral algebra
  $\Bbound$ on~$\C$.  For pure CS: $\Bbound = \widehat{\fg}_k$.
\item \emph{Bulk observables} ($t > 0$): the BRST cohomology
  $\Abulk = H^\bullet(C_{\mathrm{BV}}, Q)$.
  By Kontsevich's theorem on holomorphic factorisation of CS,
  $\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound)$.
\item \emph{Line operators} ($z = 0$, extended along~$\R_+$):
  modules for the open-colour Koszul dual
  $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$.  For pure CS at level~$k$, the
  chiral dual is $\cA^!_{\mathrm{ch}} =
  \mathrm{CE}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee})$, while on the
  chirally Koszul affine locus the open-colour dual is the
  dg-shifted Yangian $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = \Ydg(\fg)$,

\end{itemize}
On evaluation modules in the affine lineage, the reduced HT
spectral $R$-matrix agrees with the quantum group $R$-matrix of
$U_q(\fg)$ at $q = e^{i\pi/(k+h^\vee)}$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:affine-monodromy-identification}}\textup{)}.
Thus the line category is modeled by modules for the
open-colour Koszul dual, which on the chirally Koszul affine
locus carries the dg-shifted Yangian package.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Holographic reconstruction.}\enspace
The chiral algebra fixes a formal local bulk model.  The Hochschild--Kostant--Rosenberg theorem for
Lagrangian embeddings gives $\HH^\bullet(\cA) \simeq \cO(T^*[-1]\cL_\cA)$, and the
shifted cotangent $T^*[-1]\cL_\cA$ is the underlying local model on which the
formal Darboux theorem builds a $(-2)$-shifted ambient once a compatible closed
$1$-form is supplied (Theorem~\ref{thm:holographic-reconstruction}).  The boundary
therefore determines the shifted-cotangent side of the bulk, while the full formal
ambient still requires the Darboux datum that identifies the actual bulk geometry.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The completeness question.}\enspace
The holographic Lagrangian $\cL_\cA^{(0)}$ is constructed from genus-$0$ data: the bar-cobar adjunction on $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$.  \emph{Is the holographic Lagrangian complete?}  That is, does $\cL_\cA^{(0)}$ extend to a family $\cL_\cA^{(g)} \hookrightarrow \cM_{\mathrm{vac}}$ over the moduli $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, compatible with gluing along boundary strata?  For algebras with clean self-intersection (classes~$\mathbf{G}$ and~$\mathbf{L}$: Heisenberg, Kac--Moody), the Lagrangian meets itself transversally and the scalar curvature $d^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ extends automatically.  For algebras with excess self-intersection (class~$\mathbf{M}$: Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$), the derived self-intersection carries higher Tor, the Swiss-cheese operations $m_k$ are successive obstruction classes, and the question is open.  (Classes~$\mathbf{G}$, $\mathbf{L}$, $\mathbf{C}$ are chirally Koszul: the PBW spectral sequence concentrates and bar cohomology is concentrated.  Class~$\mathbf{M}$ has infinite shadow depth and non-formal Swiss-cheese structure: the fourth-order pole forces nonvanishing transferred operations $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} \neq 0$ for all $k \geq 3$, though the algebra is chirally Koszul (PBW spectral sequence concentrates) and well-defined Koszul duals exist.  The difficulty for class~$\mathbf{M}$ is the non-formality: beyond the global self-intersection geometry, the big Koszul dual carries higher $\Ainf$ operations.)  This is the central problem of the modular programme: existence, compatibility, and computation of the higher-genus Lagrangian, developed in Part~IV.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The single open problem.}\enspace
Everything proved in both volumes begins from finite-order projections

remains is the global geometry of the stack: the derived symplectic

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The monograph computes the local geometry at a point.}\enspace
A chiral algebra is a point in the derived symplectic category: a Lagrangian
$\cL_\cA \hookrightarrow \cM_{\mathrm{vac}}(\cA)$.  The bar complex is the
endomorphism algebra of this point.  The Swiss-cheese operad is the local
composition law.  Koszul duality is the symplectic complement.  The genus tower
is the deformation of the point over the moduli of curves.  The holographic
principle is the theorem that a point determines its ambient.  The entire content
of both volumes is the local geometry of the derived symplectic category at this
single point.  Volume~III asks how this local geometry arises from
Calabi--Yau categories: for $d = 2$ the cyclic $\Ainf$-structure of
a CY category determines an $E_2$-chiral algebra whose shadow
obstruction tower is the automorphic correction of a generalized
Borcherds--Kac--Moody superalgebra; for $K3 \times E$ the
denominator identity is the Igusa cusp form~$\Delta_5$, with modular
characteristic $\kappa = 3 = \dim_\C$ and a shadow--Siegel gap
theorem identifying four structural obstructions.

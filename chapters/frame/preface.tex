\section*{0.\quad From algebraic geometry to physics}

\noindent
Volume~I constructs $E_n$-chiral algebras as algebraic-geometric
objects: factorisation algebras on configuration spaces of curves,
with bar complexes at every operadic level, $R$-matrices, Yangian
structures, chiral quantum group data, derived chiral centres
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ carrying $E_2$ and $E_3$
structure, the $\SCchtop$ datum on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$, and the $E_n$
operadic circle as an algebraic-geometric structure. All of this is
algebraic geometry of factorisation on configuration spaces. This
volume discovers that these algebraic-geometric constructions
\emph{are} physics: the derived chiral centre is the bulk algebra
of a real three-dimensional holomorphic-topological gauge theory,
the $\SCchtop$ pair is a boundary-bulk system, and the $E_3$
algebra is a topological field theory. The passage from Volume~I to
Volume~II is the passage from construction to physical realisation.

\medskip

\noindent
One coalgebra, many projections---that is the Steinberg principle
(Section~IV below). But first: one curve, many stratifications.
Bar chain models are indexed not by manifolds but by
\emph{topologico-combinatoric stratifications with specified
bulk-to-boundary structure}: the topology determines the
underlying manifold and its boundary components; the
combinatorics determines the collision patterns, encoded by the
boundary strata of the Fulton--MacPherson compactification; and
the bulk-to-boundary data determines which strata involve
interior points approaching boundary components.  Different
stratifications of the same underlying manifold give different
bar complexes.  The bar differential is controlled by
codimension-1 strata; the $\Ainf$ relations by codimension-2
strata (the associahedra boundary); the curvature
$d_B^2 = \kappa\,\omega_g$ by the failure to extend across
certain strata of $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

The volume climbs to $E_3$-topological (= full TQFT, independent of complex structure).
The entire volume is stratified over the following configurations.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{The geometric ladder.}\enspace
The table is a ladder in the $E_n$ hierarchy, and each rung gains
one degree of freedom.  Stages~0--2 are the $\Eone$ rung:
topological, real, $0$--$1$-dimensional.  The point (Stage~0) is the
algebra at a point; $\R$ (Stage~1) is the $\Eone$-algebra with its
deconcatenation cooperad; the interval $[0,1]$ and half-line
$\R_{\ge 0}$ (Stage~2) give the bar complex
$B(\cA) = k \otimes_\cA^{\mathbf{L}} k$ via augmentation boundary,
the $\Ainf$ structure $W(\mathsf{Ass})$, and the one-sided bar
resolution for modules.  Stages~3--4 are the $\Eone$-to-$\Etwo$
step: the complex plane $\C$ (Stage~3) brings the holomorphic
direction, with OPE residues from $\FM_k(\C)$ supplying the bar
differential $D_\cA$; the product $\C \times \R$ (Stage~4) makes
$B(\cA)$ an $\Eone$ dg coassociative coalgebra over the Koszul
dual cooperad $(\mathsf{Ass}^{\mathrm{ch}})^!$.  Stage~5 is the
half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ with its three collision types (bulk--bulk
from $\FM_k(\C)$, boundary--boundary from $\Conf_m^<(\R)$,
bulk-to-boundary from interior points approaching $\R$), producing
the $\SCchtop$ structure on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$.  Stage~6 is the
formal disk $D$, the local model where the chiral endomorphism
operad $\End^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$ is
$\operatorname{Aut}(\cO)$-equivariant.  Stage~7 is the modular
extension: the annulus and higher-genus surfaces $\Sigma_g$ carry
the partially modular operad $\SCchtop_{\mathrm{mod}}$ with
curvature $d^2 = \kappa\,\omega_g$.  Stage~8 is the Drinfeld
centre, where $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) =
C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ carries an $\Etwo$-chiral
Gerstenhaber structure (the chiral Deligne--Tamarkin theorem).
Stage~9 is topologization: given a 3d holomorphic-topological
theory whose boundary is $\cA$, the conformal vector $T(z)$ at
non-critical level trivialises the complex-structure dependence via
Sugawara, and $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ becomes
$E_3$-\textsc{topological} by Dunn additivity
$\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \times \Eone^{\mathrm{top}}$.  This is the
target of the volume.

Each rung adds one $\Eone$ factor via Dunn additivity.  The
$\Eone$ core (Stages~0--2) is ordered, associative, noncommutative:
the bar complex, the Koszul dual, the line category.  The $\Etwo$
layer (Stages~3--8) is holomorphic and braided: the $R$-matrix is
the coherence datum, the Yang--Baxter equation comes from Stokes on
$\FM_3(\C)$, and the derived centre carries $\Etwo$ from the
Deligne conjecture.  The $E_3$ layer (Stage~9) is
holomorphic-topological: $\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \times
\Eone^{\mathrm{top}}$.  The Parts of the volume correspond to
rungs: Part~\ref{part:swiss-cheese} is the open primitive
(Stages~0--1), Part~\ref{part:e1-core} is the $\Eone$ core
(Stage~2), Part~\ref{part:bbl-core} is the seven faces of $r(z)$
and the derived centre (Stages~3--4),
Part~\ref{part:examples} is the characteristic datum and
modularity (Stages~6--7), Part~\ref{part:holography} is the
standard HT landscape (Stage~5), Part~\ref{part:gravity} is 3d
quantum gravity (Stages~8--9), and Part~\ref{part:frontier} is the
frontier.

Stages~0--8 are proved.  Stage~9 is proved for gauge-theoretic
families and conjectural in general: the passage from
$\SCchtop$ to $E_3$-topological requires both a 3d
holomorphic-topological theory whose boundary is $\cA$ \emph{and} a
conformal vector at non-critical level.  The missing ingredient for
general chiral algebras is a modular operad governing
$\Ainf$-algebras in $\Eone$-chiral algebras
(whose genus-zero part is constructed in
Definition~\ref{def:modular-operad-ainf-chiral} and
Proposition~\ref{prop:genus0-product-decomposition}; the full
modular extension to genus $\geq 1$ remains open)---the operad
whose algebras are the 3d bulk theories.  For affine Kac--Moody at
non-critical level, the 3d theory is holomorphic Chern--Simons
(Costello--Li), and Stage~9 is proved
(Theorem~\ref{thm:E3-topological-km}).  For $W$-algebras via Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction---principal or
non-principal---the 3d theory is provided by the Costello--Gaiotto
theorem, and Stage~9 is proved
(Theorems~\ref{thm:E3-topological-DS}
and~\ref{thm:E3-topological-DS-general}): the BRST identity
$T_{\mathrm{DS}} = [Q_{\mathrm{CS}}, G'_f]$ holds because the
improvement term involves only Cartan currents, which are
$Q_{\mathrm{CS}}$-exact in the 3d bulk regardless of the
nilpotent orbit.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{$\SCchtop$ is the generic case.}\enspace
The $\SCchtop$ structure on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ is the structure
that \emph{most} chiral algebras carry on their derived centre.
$E_3$-topological (Stage~9) is a \emph{special case}, requiring a
conformal vector at non-critical level.  Without conformal vector,
the holomorphic direction carries nontrivial cohomological
information: the two colours of $\SCchtop$ remain distinct, and
one is stuck at Stage~8.  Examples stuck at $\SCchtop$:
critical-level
Kac--Moody $V_{-h^\vee}(\fg)$, $\Eone$-chiral algebras (Yangians),
free fields without Sugawara, and Calabi--Yau functor outputs
lacking conformal vectors.
The operad $\SCchtop$ must be understood as a first-class object,
with five redundant presentations---operadic, Koszul dual,
factorisation, BV/BRST, and convolution---and a pentagon of
equivalences connecting them
(\S\ref{sec:SC-pentagon}).

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Critical-level failure.}\enspace
At the critical level $k = -h^\vee$, the Sugawara construction is
undefined: the denominator $k + h^\vee$ vanishes and no conformal
vector exists.  For affine Kac--Moody, the centre jumps from
scalars to the Feigin--Frenkel centre
$\mathfrak{z}(\widehat{\fg})$, a commutative chiral algebra
isomorphic to $\operatorname{Fun}\operatorname{Op}_{\fg^L}(D^\times)$.
Topologization fails: the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ remains at
$\SCchtop$, and $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ remains
$\Etwo$-chiral.
The critical level is the boundary of Stage~9: all stages through~8
are reached, but the conformal-vector bridge to
$E_3$-topological does not exist.

\section*{I.\quad The open/closed primitive}

\noindent
Two operators $a(z_1, t_1)$ and $b(z_2, t_2)$ on $\C \times \R$
interact in two independent directions. Holomorphic collision
$z_1 \to z_2$ produces the OPE residue; topological separation,
cutting $\R$ between $t_1$ and $t_2$, produces the deconcatenation
coproduct. The ordered bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ encodes both:
\[
d_{\barBch}[s^{-1}a \,|\, s^{-1}b]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\bigl[
 a(z_1)\,b(z_2)\cdot\eta_{12}
\bigr],
\qquad
\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2),
\]
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-delta}
\Delta[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n]
\;=\;
\sum_{i=0}^{n}
[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_i]
\;\otimes\;
[s^{-1}a_{i+1}|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n],
\end{equation}
with the differential a coderivation of the coproduct:
$\Delta \circ D_\cA = (D_\cA \otimes \id + \id \otimes D_\cA)
\circ \Delta$. Collisions in $\C$ commute with cuts in $\R$.
The differential is holomorphic factorisation on
$\FM_k(\C)$; the coproduct is topological factorisation on
$\Conf_k^{<}(\R)$; together they make $B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ an
$E_1$ dg coassociative coalgebra
(Theorem~\ref{thm:rosetta-e1-coalgebra}). This is the primitive
object of the volume. The symmetric bar $B^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ of
Volume~I is its $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow; the modular
characteristic $\kappa(\cA) = \mathrm{av}(r(z))$ is the leading
scalar that survives averaging; the five Vol~I theorems A--D and~H
are the invariants that survive the projection.

The form $\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ is a bar-construction
coefficient: a logarithmic one-form on the configuration space.
It is not a connection form. The KZ connection on conformal
blocks is a different object: $\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}} = d -
\Omega\,dz/((k + h^\vee)z)$, a rational one-form in $z$.
The Arnold relation
$\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12} = 0$
forces $D_\cA^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$. The curved lift
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ controls higher
genus. The universal Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_0$ packages both regimes.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Heisenberg on $\C \times \R$.}\enspace
Take $\cA = \cH_k$: one generator $J$, one OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$. Two operators sit at $(z_1, t_1)$
and $(z_2, t_2)$ with $t_1 < t_2$. Their bar element is
$[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]$.

In the holomorphic direction, the boundary stratum
$E_{12} \cong S^1$ of $\FM_2(\C)$ extracts the mode products
$J_{(n)}J$. The only nonvanishing mode is $J_{(1)}J = k$, so
\[
d_{\barBch}[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
\;=\;
J_{(1)}J
\;=\; k.
\]
No simple-pole OPE ($J_{(0)}J = 0$): the bracket component
vanishes. The entire holomorphic interaction is the scalar~$k$.

In the topological direction, cut $\R$ at each gap:
\begin{align*}
\Delta[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
&\;=\;
[\,] \otimes [s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut before $t_1$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J] \otimes [s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut between $t_1$ and $t_2$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] \otimes [\,]
&&\text{(cut after $t_2$)}.
\end{align*}
The middle term separates one field to each half-line. Since
$J_{(0)}J = 0$, separated fields do not interact: the
factorisation is exact.

Coderivation check: $D_\cA[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] = k$, then
$\Delta(k) = k \otimes [\,] + [\,] \otimes k$.
On the right, $(D_\cA \otimes \id + \id \otimes D_\cA)$ applied
to $\Delta[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]$ gives
$[\,] \otimes k + k \otimes [\,]$. Both sides agree: collision
in $\C$ commutes with cutting in $\R$.

The bar element lives on $\FM_2(\C) \times \Conf_2^<(\R)$.
The topological factor $\Conf_2^<(\R) = \{t_1 < t_2\} \cong
\R_{>0}$ is contractible; its coalgebra structure contracts to
strict coassociativity: deconcatenation. At degree~$n$,
\[
\barB^{\mathrm{ch},n}(\cA)
\;\subset\;
(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}
\otimes
\Omega^\bullet\bigl(\FM_n(\C) \times \Conf_n^<(\R)\bigr).
\]
The ordered space $\Conf_n^<(\R) \cong \R^{n-1}_{> 0}$ is
contractible; no higher homotopies survive; the coproduct is
the unique coassociative coproduct on $T^c(V)$.

For the Heisenberg, the MC element collapses to
$\Theta_{\cH_k} = k \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$; the genus-$g$
projection is $F_g(\cH_k) = k \cdot \lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$
\textup{(\textsc{uniform-weight})}. The single scalar $k$
controls every entry of the census table.

\section*{II.\quad What this volume proves}

Volume~I constructs $E_n$-chiral algebras as algebraic-geometric
objects: the ordered bar $B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ with its
$R$-matrix and Yangian structure, the derived chiral centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ with $E_2$/$E_3$
structure, the $\SCchtop$ datum on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$, and the five
theorems as structural properties of the categorical logarithm.
The five theorems live in the symmetric bar $B^\Sigma(\cA)$:
their invariants are scalar ($\kappa(\cA)$,
$\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$
\textup{(\textsc{uniform-weight})}, the shadow tower, the
discriminant $\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$). The $E_1$ data (the
$R$-matrix, the Yangian, the chiral quantum group) and the
derived centres are constructed algebraic-geometrically in
Volume~I but their physical meaning is not developed there.

This volume interprets the algebraic-geometric constructions of
Volume~I as physics. Three structures acquire physical content.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The spectral $R$-matrix.}\enspace
The ordered collision at degree~$2$ produces the classical
$r$-matrix $r(z)$, always written with its level prefix:
$r_{\cH_k}(z) = k/z$ (Heisenberg, vanishing at $k = 0$),
$r_{\widehat{\fg}_k}(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ (affine Kac--Moody,
trace-form convention, vanishing at $k = 0$). Degree~$3$ gives
the Yang--Baxter equation (Theorem~\ref{thm:YBE}) from
three-face Stokes on $\FM_3(\C)$. On evaluation modules for
affine Kac--Moody, the braided line category reproduces the
quantum group category of $U_q(\fg)$ at
$q = e^{i\pi/(k+h^\vee)}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:affine-monodromy-identification}).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The line category and the Koszul dual.}\enspace
Line operators along $\R_+ \times \{0\}$ carry modules for the
open-colour Koszul dual $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$. For
Heisenberg, $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = Y(\mathfrak{u}(1))$; for
affine Kac--Moody, $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = \Ydg(\fg)$; the
complete spectral Drinfeld strictification
(Theorem~\ref{thm:complete-strictification}) holds for every
simple Lie type.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The bulk-boundary-line triangle.}\enspace
Bulk local operators $\Abulk$ on the interior of
$\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$ identify unconditionally with chiral
Hochschild cochains of the boundary algebra $\Bbound$. On the
boundary-linear exact sector and on the chirally Koszul locus,
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound)
= \RHom_{\Bbound\text{-}\Bbound}(\Bbound, \Bbound)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:boundary-linear-bulk-boundary}) and
$\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}\text{-mod}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:lines_as_modules}). Two parallel lines
interact through the bulk, producing the meromorphic braiding.
The global triangle (boundary determines bulk determines
boundary) is proved at the boundary-linear level; the full
reconstruction remains conjectural.

\section*{III.\quad Three conceptual leaps}

Everything in both volumes is organised by three leaps away from
classical Koszul duality. The bar complex indexes all three.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(1) Commutative to $E_1/\Ainf$.}\enspace
Classical Koszul duality works for commutative and associative
algebras. The chiral setting replaces commutativity by an
$E_1$-chiral $\Ainf$ structure: the holomorphic direction
promotes multiplication to the Stasheff hierarchy on
$\FM_k(\C)$, the topological direction promotes concatenation
to deconcatenation on $\Conf_k^{<}(\R)$. The bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ is an $E_1$ coassociative coalgebra;
the $\SCchtop$ structure emerges on the chiral Hochschild
cochains $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$, where the pair
$(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\, \cA)$ is the
bulk-boundary datum. Strict commutativity fails at degree~$3$
through the homotopy $m_3$ (Stokes on the Stasheff interval
$K_3 = [0,1]$); higher operations are forced by $D_\cA^2 = 0$,
each a Stokes theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(2) Genus~$0$ to modular.}\enspace
Classical Koszul duality is genus~$0$ on the formal disk.
The modular setting lifts the bar complex over
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ with curved differential
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$. The
Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$ lives in the modular
convolution algebra
$\gAmod := \Hom_\Sigma(F\!\Com, \End_{\barBch(\cA)})$;
the shadow tower projects onto degree strata; the discriminant
$\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$ classifies into G/L/C/M. The trichotomy
$\Delta = 0$ (class~$\mathbf{G}$: formal, finite tower),
$\Delta \neq 0$ with finite algebraic depth
(classes~$\mathbf{L}$, $\mathbf{C}$), and $\Delta \neq 0$
with infinite depth (class~$\mathbf{M}$: gravitational) is the
gauge-gravity dichotomy: the discriminant decides whether the 3d
theory has a gravitational sector.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(3) Local to nonlocal.}\enspace
All standard vertex algebras, including those with OPE poles, are
$E_\infty$: locality is compatible with arbitrary pole order. The
genuinely $E_1$ layer is where the spectral $R$-matrix is an
independent input, not derived from the local OPE, and the
line-side dual $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ is a dg-shifted Yangian.
The ordered bar $B^{\mathrm{ord}}$ is the primitive; the symmetric
bar $B^{\Sigma}$ is its $R$-matrix-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant
projection. The distinction is provenance, not pole order.

\smallskip
These three leaps act independently, and together give the
seven-part architecture of this volume. The programme, stated in
one line: holomorphic chiral factorisation (co)homology via
bar/cobar, instantiated at various geometric locations.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The fourth leap: derived centres produce
physics.}\enspace
The three leaps above are algebraic-geometric: they describe the
$E_n$-chiral algebras of Volume~I. This volume adds a fourth
leap: compute the derived chiral centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ and discover that it IS
the bulk algebra of a real 3d gauge theory. Volume~I constructs
the derived centre and proves its algebraic-geometric properties
(the $E_2$/$E_3$ structure, the concentration in degrees
$\{0,1,2\}$, the $\SCchtop$ structure on the pair). This volume
interprets these constructions physically: the $\SCchtop$ pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ is a
boundary-bulk system; line operators carry modules for the Koszul
dual; the spectral braiding satisfies Yang--Baxter; and the
shadow obstruction tower reads as the perturbative expansion of
the gravitational theory. The fourth leap is not algebraic but
physical: the same mathematical object (the derived centre), when
read through the lens of Costello--Gaiotto twisted holography,
becomes a 3d gauge theory whose boundary is the chiral algebra.
The three algebraic-geometric leaps of Volume~I produce the raw
material; the fourth physical leap of this volume discovers what
that material means.

\section*{IV.\quad The Steinberg principle}

The Steinberg variety
$\widetilde{\cN} \times_{\fg} \widetilde{\cN}$ is one geometric
object; Borel--Moore homology, $K$-theory, and equivariant derived
category extract three algebraic invariants: the Hecke algebra,
the Springer correspondence, and the Kazhdan--Lusztig basis. The
chiral bar complex $\barBch(\cA)$ on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^{<}(\R)$ is one coalgebra, and three
functors project three objects from it: cobar
$\Omega(\barBch(\cA)) \simeq \cA$ recovers the original algebra
as inversion (the left adjoint of bar, not Koszul duality);
Verdier duality on the Ran space,
$\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}(\barBch(\cA)) \simeq \barBch(\cA^!)$,
is Koszul duality proper; and chiral Hochschild cochains
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ compute the bulk as the
derived centre $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$. The
three functors are genuinely distinct: cobar is inversion,
Verdier is Koszul duality, Hochschild is the derived centre.
Three Hochschild theories coexist: chiral Hochschild
(OPE composition on $\Ran(\C)$, producing
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}$), topological Hochschild
($E_1$-bar composition on $\R$, producing the line category),
and categorical Hochschild (Morita composition on
$\C \times \R$, producing the slab bimodule;
see~\cite{BZFN10} for the categorical framework). The derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ is the first.
Identifying any two collapses the architecture. Every chapter of
this volume is a projection of the single coalgebra through one
of the three.

A chiral algebra is a point in the derived symplectic category:
a Lagrangian $\cL_\cA \hookrightarrow \cM_{\mathrm{vac}}(\cA)$.
The bar complex is the $E_1$ coassociative endomorphism coalgebra
of this point; the chiral Hochschild cochains
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$, computed from the bar complex,

\section*{V.\quad The Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$}

The product $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$ is the space of
operations of a two-coloured operad:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{closed-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C), \\
&\text{mixed-to-open:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k, \mathsf{top}^m;\,\mathsf{top})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C) \times E_1(m), \\
&\text{open-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\ldots, \mathsf{top}, \ldots;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \varnothing.
\end{alignat*}
Closed colour: holomorphic collisions on $\FM_k(\C)$. Open
colour: topological orderings on $E_1(m) = \Conf_m^<(\R)$.
Mixed: the product. Composition is FM substitution on the
closed factor, interval insertion on the open factor. The empty
open-to-closed component is directionality: bulk restricts to
boundary, not conversely. The bar complex
$(\barBch(\cA), D_\cA, \Delta)$ is an $E_1$ dg coassociative
coalgebra: the differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation encoding
the $A_\infty$-chiral structure, the coproduct $\Delta$ is
deconcatenation. The $\SCchtop$ structure emerges in the chiral
derived center: the pair
$(\cC^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\, \cA)$ carries
Swiss-cheese structure via brace operations from the chiral
endomorphism operad. The classical Swiss-cheese operad
is Koszul (Livernet); Kontsevich formality and bar-cobar transfer
give homotopy-Koszulity
$\Omega\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \xrightarrow{\sim} \SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}). The Koszul dual cooperad
$(\SCchtop)^! = (\mathsf{Lie},\, \mathsf{Ass},\, \text{shuffle-mixed})$
(Proposition~\ref{thm:SC-self-duality}) exchanges the closed colour
$\mathsf{Com}$ with $\mathsf{Lie}$; the duality functor on
$\SCchtop$-algebras is an involution, and the open-colour dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ carries the dual algebraic structure.

The operad $\SCchtop$ sits between $\Eone$-chiral and $E_3$ in the
$E_n$ hierarchy. The passage $\Eone \to \Etwo$ is automatic:
Hochschild cochains of an $\Eone$-algebra carry $\Etwo$ structure
(Deligne's conjecture, proved). The passage $\Etwo \to
E_3$-chiral requires a 3d holomorphic-topological theory whose
boundary is the chiral algebra; for Kac--Moody this is
holomorphic Chern--Simons (proved by Costello--Li), for general
vertex algebras it requires quantising the Poisson vertex model
(conjectural). The further passage $E_3$-chiral $\to$
$E_3$-topological requires a conformal vector at non-critical
level; the topologisation theorem (Volume~I, proved for affine
Kac--Moody; conjectural in general) trivialises the
complex-structure dependence. $\SCchtop$ is the intermediary that
organises the $\Eone$-to-$\Etwo$ passage without itself being
$E_3$.

\section*{VI.\quad The slab, the Drinfeld double, and the BBL triangle}

\section*{VIII.\quad PVA descent}

The bar differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation of the cofree
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$. A coderivation on a cofree
coalgebra is determined by its cogenerator projections: a sequence
\[
m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \longrightarrow
A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1})),
\qquad k \ge 1, \quad \deg m_k = 2 - k,
\]
where $\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$ are spectral parameters recording
holomorphic separations. The identity $D_\cA^2 = 0$ expands into
the $\Ainf$ Stasheff relations: for each~$n$,
\[
\sum_{\substack{i+j = n+1 \\ i,j \ge 1}}\;\sum_{s=0}^{n-j}
\pm\; m_i\bigl(a_1, \ldots, m_j(a_{s+1}, \ldots, a_{s+j}),
\ldots, a_n\bigr) \;=\; 0.
\]
Each relation is Stokes' theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$.

At $n = 1$: $m_1^2 = 0$, i.e.\ $m_1 = Q$ is a differential.
At $n = 2$: the Leibniz rule---$Q$ is a derivation of $m_2$.
At $n = 3$: the Stasheff associahedron $K_3$ is a closed interval
$[0,1]$, the quotient of $\FM_3(\C)$ by the affine group; its
two endpoints are the collision strata $D_{12}$ and $D_{23}$. The
associativity defect $m_2(m_2(a,b),c) - m_2(a,m_2(b,c))$ equals
a boundary contribution involving $m_3$: the binary product is
associative up to the homotopy $m_3$. When $m_3 = 0$ (Heisenberg),
the product is strictly associative. When $m_3 \neq 0$
(Kac--Moody, Virasoro), the nonvanishing homotopy is the failure
of the OPE to be strictly associative.

At $n = 4$: the Stasheff pentagon, five boundary faces, $m_4$
as interior integral. For $\cW_3$, $m_4$ encodes the quartic
contact interaction with coefficient $32/(22+5c)$.

The general pattern: $\FM_n(\C)/\mathrm{Aff}(\C)$ is a compact
manifold with corners of real dimension $2n - 4$. The identity
$\partial^2 = 0$ on $\FM_n(\C)$ (every codimension-$2$ face is
reached by two codimension-$1$ faces with opposite orientations)
is the combinatorial identity that makes $D_\cA^2 = 0$ hold.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The resolvent principle.}\enspace
The $\Ainf$ operations $\{m_k\}_{k \ge 2}$ are determined by
the binary product $m_2$ alone. Let $(H, p, \iota, h)$ be a
deformation retract of $(\cA, m_1)$ onto cohomology. The dressed
propagator $\Phi = \iota + h \circ m_2 \circ \Phi^{\otimes 2}$
satisfies a tree-level bootstrap; its solution gives the tree
formula
\[
m_k^H
\;=\;
\sum_{T \in \mathrm{PRT}_k}
p \circ \mathfrak{m}(T) \circ \iota^{\otimes k},
\]
where $\mathrm{PRT}_k$ is the set of planar rooted binary trees
with $k$ leaves ($|\mathrm{PRT}_k| = C_{k-1}$, the Catalan
number), $\mathfrak{m}(T)$ assigns $m_2$ to each internal
vertex and $h$ to each internal edge. One equation, one binary
input, Catalan many trees: the perturbative expansion of the
Lagrangian self-intersection.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Descent to PVA.}\enspace
On cohomology $H^\bullet(A, Q)$, the higher $m_k$ vanish and
$m_2$ decomposes into a commutative product $\mu$ (regular part)
and a $\lambda$-bracket $\{a_\lambda b\}$ (singular part),

axioms each have a geometric origin:
sesquilinearity from $\C^*$-equivariance on $\FM_2(\C)$;

commutativity of $\mu$ from the $\Z/2$-involution on

The quantization obstruction lives in $\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$;

\section*{I.\quad The second structure}

\noindent
Volume~I constructed the bar complex $\barBch(\cA)$ of a chiral
algebra on a curve~$X$ and proved that a single differential~$D_\cA$
controls the genus tower.  This volume discovers a second structure
on the same object.

The bar complex $\barBch(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ is the cofree
conilpotent coalgebra on the desuspended augmentation ideal.  Its
differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation built from OPE residues on
the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\FM_k(\C)$: it encodes
how operators collide in the holomorphic variable~$z$.  But the
cofree coalgebra carries a second, canonical structure: the
\emph{deconcatenation coproduct}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface2-delta}
\Delta[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n]
\;=\;
\sum_{i=0}^{n}
[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_i]
\;\otimes\;
[s^{-1}a_{i+1}|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n].
\end{equation}
This coproduct is coassociative: $(\Delta \otimes \id)\circ\Delta
= (\id \otimes \Delta)\circ\Delta$.  It is a coderivation of
$D_\cA$: $\Delta\circ D_\cA = (D_\cA \otimes \id + \id \otimes
D_\cA)\circ\Delta$.  It was always present---it is part of the
definition of a cofree coalgebra---but Volume~I never needed it,
because the modular genus tower depends only on the differential.

Volume~II needs it.  The coproduct is topological factorisation
along~$\R$.


\section*{II.\quad The coproduct as $\R$-factorisation}

Place $n$ fields $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ at ordered positions
$t_1 < t_2 < \cdots < t_n$ on the real line~$\R$.  Choose a cut
point $t_i < t_* < t_{i+1}$.  The observables on the half-line
$(-\infty, t_*)$ depend on $a_1, \ldots, a_i$; the observables on
$(t_*, +\infty)$ depend on $a_{i+1}, \ldots, a_n$.  The total
observable factorises:
\[
\mathcal{O}(a_1, \ldots, a_n)
\;=\;
\mathcal{O}_L(a_1, \ldots, a_i)
\;\cdot\;
\mathcal{O}_R(a_{i+1}, \ldots, a_n).
\]
The $i$-th summand of $\Delta$ in~\eqref{eq:preface2-delta} is
exactly this factorisation.  The sum over all $i$ records all
possible cuts.

The ordered configuration space $\Conf_n^<(\R) = \{t_1 < \cdots
< t_n\} \cong \R^{n-1}_{>0}$ is contractible.  Therefore the
$E_1$-coalgebra structure on $\Conf_n(\R)$ contracts to a strict
coassociative coproduct: deconcatenation.  No higher homotopies
survive.  The coproduct is canonical: it is the unique coassociative
coproduct on the cofree conilpotent coalgebra $T^c(V)$, determined
by the cogenerators alone.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg verification.}\enspace
For $\cH_k$ with generator~$J$ and OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$, the bar element
$[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]$ represents two operators at positions
$(z_1, t_1)$ and $(z_2, t_2)$ on $\C \times \R$ with
$t_1 < t_2$.
\begin{align*}
\Delta[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]
&\;=\;
[\,] \otimes [s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut before $t_1$: both right)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J] \otimes [s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut between $t_1$ and $t_2$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] \otimes [\,]
&&\text{(cut after $t_2$: both left)}.
\end{align*}
The middle term factorises the two-field observable into
independent one-field observables on the two half-lines.  This is
possible because $J_{(0)}J = 0$: the Heisenberg OPE has no
simple-pole interaction, so spatially separated fields do not
interact.

Now apply the differential.  On the middle term:
$d_{\barB}([s^{-1}J]) = 0$ (no unary OPE).  On the full element:
$d_{\barB}[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] = k$ (the double-pole residue).
The coderivation property
\[
\Delta(k) \;=\; k \otimes [\,] + [\,] \otimes k
\;=\;
(d_{\barB} \otimes \id + \id \otimes d_{\barB})
\bigl(
  [\,] \otimes [s^{-1}J|s^{-1}J]
  + [s^{-1}J] \otimes [s^{-1}J]
  + [s^{-1}J|s^{-1}J] \otimes [\,]
\bigr)
\]
holds because the collision $z_1 \to z_2$ in $\C$ is independent
of the cut $t_*$ in~$\R$: holomorphic collisions commute with
topological cuts.


\section*{III.\quad The product $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$}

A degree-$k$ bar element lives on the product
\[
\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R).
\]
The differential $D_\cA$ sees $\FM_k(\C)$: it extracts residues
along the codimension-$1$ collision divisors
$D_S \subset \FM_k(\C)$, where a subset $S$ of points collides.
The coproduct~$\Delta$ sees $\Conf_k^<(\R)$: it cuts the ordered
sequence at a gap.  Neither structure sees the other's factor.
The coderivation property is the algebraic expression of this
independence.

This product is the space of operations of a two-coloured operad.
Define $\SCchtop$ by:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{closed-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C), \\
&\text{mixed-to-open:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k, \mathsf{top}^m;\,\mathsf{top})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C) \times E_1(m), \\
&\text{open-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\ldots, \mathsf{top}, \ldots;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \varnothing.
\end{alignat*}
Composition in each colour is the standard operadic composition:
FM substitution on the closed factor, interval substitution on the
open factor.  The empty open-to-closed component encodes
directionality: bulk interactions restrict to boundaries but not
conversely.

The bar complex $(\barBch(\cA), D_\cA, \Delta)$ is an algebra
over $\SCchtop$: the differential is the closed-colour structure
and the coproduct is the open-colour structure.  The verification
for the Heisenberg algebra is the four-step proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:rosetta-swiss-cheese}, carried out in the Rosetta
Stone chapter.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Homotopy-Koszulity.}\enspace
The classical Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}$ (with $E_2$ on the
closed colour and $E_1$ on the open colour) is Koszul (Livernet).
Kontsevich formality provides a quasi-isomorphism
$\SCchtop \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathsf{SC}$.  Bar-cobar transfer
gives
\[
\Omega\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\; \SCchtop.
\]
The bar-cobar adjunction on $\SCchtop$-algebras is a Quillen
equivalence (Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}).


\section*{IV.\quad The $\Ainf$ chiral operations}

The bar differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation of the cofree
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$.  A coderivation on a cofree
coalgebra is determined by its cogenerator projections: a sequence
of multilinear maps
\[
m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \longrightarrow
A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1})),
\qquad k \ge 1, \quad \deg m_k = 1 - k,
\]
where $\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$ are spectral parameters recording
holomorphic separations.  The identity $D_\cA^2 = 0$ expands into
the $\Ainf$ Stasheff relations: for each~$n$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface2-stasheff}
\sum_{\substack{i+j = n+1 \\ i,j \ge 1}}\;\sum_{s=0}^{n-j}
\pm\; m_i\bigl(a_1, \ldots, m_j(a_{s+1}, \ldots, a_{s+j}),
\ldots, a_n\bigr) \;=\; 0,
\end{equation}
with spectral substitution $\lambda_s \mapsto \Lambda_I$ when a
consecutive block $I$ collapses.

Each relation is a Stokes theorem.  The $n$-th relation asserts
that the integral of a closed form over the boundary
$\partial\FM_n(\C)$ vanishes.  The boundary strata of $\FM_n(\C)$
are indexed by subsets $S \subset \{1, \ldots, n\}$ with
$|S| \ge 2$: they are the collision divisors.  Each stratum
$D_S$ contributes $m_{|S|}$ applied to the colliding inputs,
composed with $m_{n-|S|+1}$ on the remaining inputs.  The two
sides of each codimension-$2$ face cancel by orientations.


\section*{V.\quad First computation: the Heisenberg}

\noindent\textbf{Operations.}\enspace
$m_1 = 0$ (no internal differential).
$m_2(J,J;\lambda) = k\lambda$ (the double-pole residue).
$m_k = 0$ for $k \ge 3$ (no composites, no higher poles).
The algebra is formal: the $\Ainf$ structure is concentrated in
$m_2$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{PVA on cohomology.}\enspace
On $H^\bullet(\cH_k) = \C[J]$, the binary operation decomposes
into a commutative product and a $\lambda$-bracket:
\[
\{J_\lambda J\} = k\lambda.
\]
This is a Poisson vertex algebra of rank~$1$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{$R$-matrix.}\enspace
The Laplace transform of the $\lambda$-bracket produces
$r(z) = k/z$ (the classical $r$-matrix).  The quantum $R$-matrix
is $R(z) = \exp(k\hbar/z)$.  It satisfies Yang--Baxter trivially
(abelian braiding).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Line operators.}\enspace
The Koszul dual is $\cH_k^! = \cH_{-k}$.  Line operators are
$\cH_{-k}$-modules: the category of Fock spaces, semisimple.
The spectral braiding on Fock modules reproduces $R(z)$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Bulk-boundary-line.}\enspace
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\cH_k) = \cH_k$ (abelian algebras are their
own derived centres).  The triangle collapses:
$\cH_k \simeq \Zder(\cH_{-k}) \simeq \HH^\bullet(\cH_{-k})$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus~$1$.}\enspace
The bar differential acquires curvature $d^2 = k \cdot \omega_1$
from the self-loop graph $\Gamma_{\mathrm{irr}}$ on
$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$.  The modular characteristic is
$\kappa(\cH_k) = k$: the same scalar that governs the OPE, the
$\lambda$-bracket, the $R$-matrix, and the genus-$1$ obstruction.
The free energy $F_1 = -(k/24)\log\eta(\tau)$.


\section*{VI.\quad Second computation: Kac--Moody at level~$k$}

\noindent\textbf{Operations.}\enspace
$m_1 = 0$.
$m_2(J^a, J^b;\lambda) = f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c + k\,\delta^{ab}\lambda$
(the simple-pole bracket plus the double-pole metric).
$m_3 \ne 0$: the Jacobiator homotopy on $\FM_3(\C)$.  The three
codimension-$1$ boundary strata of $\FM_3(\C)$ contribute the
three terms $m_2(m_2(-,-),-)$ in the Stasheff identity; their
failure to cancel is the Jacobi identity defect, corrected by
$m_3$.  Higher $m_k = 0$ by dimension counting on $\FM_k(\C)$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{PVA on cohomology.}\enspace
$\{J^a_\lambda J^b\} = f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c + k\,\delta^{ab}\lambda$.
This is the affine PVA at level~$k$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{$R$-matrix.}\enspace
$r(z) = \Omega/z$ where $\Omega = \sum_a J^a \otimes J_a$ is
the split Casimir.  The KZ connection
$\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}} = d - \hbar\,\Omega/z$ is the flat
connection on conformal blocks; its monodromy is the quantum
$R$-matrix of $U_\hbar(\fg)$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Line operators.}\enspace
$\cA^! = \widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee}$ (Feigin--Frenkel).  Line
operators are modules for the dual algebra: category~$\mathcal{O}$
at the dual level.  Drinfeld--Kohno duality identifies the braided
module category with the quantum group category.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus~$1$.}\enspace
$\kappa(\widehat{\fg}_k) = (k+h^\vee)\dim\fg/(2h^\vee)$.
Anti-symmetry: $\kappa_k + \kappa_{-k-2h^\vee} = 0$.


\section*{VII.\quad The 3d MC element $\alpha_T$}

Volume~I has one MC element $\Theta_\cA$ in the modular
convolution algebra $\gAmod$.  Volume~II has one MC element
$\alpha_T$ in the Swiss-cheese convolution algebra
\[
\gSC_T
\;:=\;
\Hom_\Sigma\!\bigl(
  \mathbf{B}(\SCchtop),\;
  \End_{(\Bbound,\,\cC_{\mathrm{line}})}
\bigr).
\]
Its $L_\infty$-brackets are indexed by two-coloured stable trees:
closed vertices labelled by $\FM_k(\C)$-chains, open vertices by
$E_1(m)$-chains, mixed vertices by products.  The MC element is
\[
\alpha_T
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{SC}}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma \otimes \Phi_\Gamma^T
\;\in\;
\mc(\gSC_T).
\]
It exists because $\SCchtop$ is homotopy-Koszul
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}), hence
$\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop)$ has $\partial^2 = 0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Six projections.}\enspace
The boundary $\Ainf$ algebra, the line category, the spectral
$R$-matrix, the bulk-to-boundary identification, the modular genus
tower, and the PVA bracket are all projections of $\alpha_T$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:alpha-projections}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Restriction to Volume~I.}\enspace
$\alpha_T|_{\text{closed, all genera}} = \Theta_\cA$.  The 3d
modular homotopy type refines the Vol~I modular homotopy type: the
forgetful map $\gSC_T \to \gAmod$ that discards open and mixed
vertices induces a morphism of formal moduli problems whose fibre
over $\Theta_\cA$ is the space of line-category deformations
compatible with the boundary algebra.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg.}\enspace
$\gSC_T$ is abelian; $\alpha_{\cH_k} = m_2 + \hbar\,k\cdot\eta
\otimes\Lambda$.  No mixed or open contributions.


\section*{VIII.\quad PVA descent}

On $H^\bullet(A, Q)$, the $\Ainf$ operations $m_k$ descend to:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item a commutative product $\mu(a,b) = m_2^{\mathrm{reg}}(a,b;0)$
  (the regular part of $m_2$ at $\lambda = 0$),
\item a $\lambda$-bracket
  $\{a_\lambda b\} = \sum_{n \ge 0}
  \frac{\lambda^{(n)}}{n!}\,a_{(n)}b$
  (the singular part of $m_2$).
\end{enumerate}
Together they form a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra.  The
PVA axioms---Jacobi, Leibniz, skew-symmetry, sesquilinearity---are
proved by Stokes' theorem on $\FM_3(\C)$: the three boundary strata
of $\FM_3(\C)$ contribute the three terms of the Jacobi identity,
and their boundary cancellation gives the identity (D2--D6 of
Theorem~\ref{thm:pva-from-ainf}).


\section*{IX.\quad The bulk-boundary-line triangle}

Three algebraic objects arise from a holomorphic--topological
theory on $\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$: bulk local operators $\Abulk$,
boundary local operators $\Bbound$, and line operators
$\cC_{\mathrm{line}}$.  The corrected triangle is
\[
\Abulk
\;\simeq\;
\Zder(\Bbound)
\;\simeq\;
\Zder(\cC_{\mathrm{line}})
\;\simeq\;
\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA^!),
\qquad
\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!\text{-mod}.
\]
The bulk is the \emph{derived centre} of the boundary, not the
boundary itself.  Line operators are modules for the Koszul dual
$\cA^!$, not for the bulk.  The spectral braiding $R(z)$ on lines
satisfies Yang--Baxter by $W$-coherence on $\FM_3(\C)$: the two
orderings of three lines correspond to two degenerations of
$\FM_3(\C)$, and the consistency of these degenerations is the YBE
(Theorem~\ref{thm:YBE}).

\section*{X.\quad PVA quantization: from classical to quantum}

The PVA $\{-_\lambda -\}$ on $H^\bullet(A, Q)$ is the
\emph{classical} shadow of the $\Ainf$ chiral algebra.  The
quantization problem is the reverse: given a PVA, construct the
$\Ainf$ chiral algebra whose cohomological descent recovers it.

This is a lifting problem.  Start with a PVA
$(V, \cdot, \{-_\lambda -\})$.  The bracket
$\{a_\lambda b\} = \sum_{n \ge 0} \frac{\lambda^{(n)}}{n!}
a_{(n)}b$ encodes the singular part of the OPE; the product
$a \cdot b$ encodes the regular part.  Together they determine
$m_2(a,b;\lambda)$ on cohomology.  The quantization question: does
there exist an $\Ainf$ chiral algebra~$A$ with $H^\bullet(A) = V$
and $m_2|_{H^\bullet} = \mu + \{-_\lambda-\}$?

The obstruction to lifting lives in the Hochschild cohomology
$\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$: the failure of the Jacobi identity
to hold at the chain level, after the first-order lift, is a
cocycle whose class is the obstruction.  When the obstruction
vanishes, the lift exists and is controlled by a Maurer--Cartan
element in the chiral deformation complex
$\mathrm{Def}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Heisenberg quantization.}\enspace
The PVA $\{J_\lambda J\} = k\lambda$ lifts to the Heisenberg
$\cH_k$ with $m_2(J,J;\lambda) = k\lambda$ and $m_{k \ge 3} = 0$.
The obstruction vanishes: $\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(\C[J]) = 0$
(the PVA is freely generated).  The quantization is unique.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Kac--Moody quantization.}\enspace
The affine PVA $\{J^a_\lambda J^b\} = f^{ab}{}_c J^c +
k\delta^{ab}\lambda$ lifts to $\widehat{\fg}_k$.  The
obstruction at $m_3$ is the Jacobiator, which is a coboundary:
$m_3$ exists and equals the homotopy-transfer correction from
$\FM_3(\C)$.  Higher obstructions vanish by dimension.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Khan--Zeng 3d sigma model.}\enspace
Khan--Zeng construct a 3d holomorphic--topological sigma model
from any PVA target.  The boundary algebra of this theory is the
quantization of the PVA\@.  The BV-BRST complex of the sigma model
on $\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$, with the PVA as target space data,
produces the $\Ainf$ operations $m_k$ by Feynman-diagram evaluation
on $\FM_k(\C)$: each $k$-ary operation is a sum of tree Feynman
diagrams with $k$ external legs and vertices weighted by the PVA
brackets.  The Stasheff identity is Stokes' theorem on the boundary
of the Feynman-integral compactification---the same boundary
cancellation that gives the $\Ainf$ relations from $\FM_k(\C)$.

This sigma-model construction is the \emph{physical proof} of the
quantization theorem: the PVA data determine a consistent field
theory, and the field theory produces the $\Ainf$ algebra.  The
mathematical proof is the bar-cobar correspondence of
Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}: the Quillen equivalence
guarantees essential uniqueness of the $\SCchtop$-algebra structure,
hence the quantization is unique up to contractible ambiguity.


\section*{XI.\quad The holographic programme}

A three-dimensional holomorphic--topological quantum field theory on
$\R_+ \times \C$ has bulk observables on the interior, boundary
observables on $\{0\} \times \C$, and line operators along
$\R_+ \times \{0\}$.

\subsection*{Twisted holography}

The twist of a 3d $\cN = 2$ gauge theory on $\R_+ \times \C$
produces a holomorphic--topological theory whose boundary algebra
$\Bbound$ is a chiral algebra on~$\C$.  The bulk algebra $\Abulk$
is the space of local operators in the interior, which by the BV
formalism is the BRST cohomology of the Chern--Simons-type action.
The boundary--bulk comparison is
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound)$: the bulk is the derived centre of
the boundary.

Koszul duality enters: the boundary algebra $\Bbound$ has a Koszul
dual $\Bbound^!$, and line operators along $\R_+ \times \{0\}$ are
modules for $\Bbound^!$.  The spectral $R$-matrix on line operators
is the quantum group $R$-matrix; the dg-shifted Yangian is the
$\Ainf$ endomorphism algebra of the line category.  This is
Costello's twisted holography programme, made algebraic by the
bar-cobar framework.

\subsection*{The holographic modular Koszul datum}

The 3d MC element $\alpha_T$ packages the holographic system into
a single object.  Its restriction to the closed sector gives the
boundary algebra $\Bbound$ with its genus tower
$\Theta_{\Bbound}$.  Its mixed sector gives the dg-shifted Yangian
$r(z) = \alpha_T|_{(2,0)}$.  Its open sector gives the line
category.  The holographic modular Koszul datum of Vol~I,
\[
\mathcal{H}(T)
\;=\;
(\Bbound,\; \Bbound^!,\; \cC_{\mathrm{line}},\; r(z),\;
\Theta_{\Bbound},\; \nabla^{\mathrm{hol}}),
\]
is the projection data of $\alpha_T$.  Each entry is one face of
the MC element.

\subsection*{Anomaly completion}

At genus $g \ge 1$, the holographic system acquires anomalies: the
curvature $d^2 = \kappa(\Bbound) \cdot \omega_g$ obstructs naive
factorisation.  The anomaly-completed topological holography
programme (Chapter~\ref{chap:anomaly-completed}) absorbs these
anomalies into the MC equation: the genus-$g$ component of
$\alpha_T$ includes the curvature correction, the period correction,
and the planted-forest correction from log-FM strata.  The
anomaly-completed system is the completed MC element
$\hat\alpha_T \in \mc(\hat\gSC_T)$, where the completion is by
the strong filtration axiom of Vol~I.

\subsection*{The M2-brane example}

The theory $T = $ M2-brane on $\R_+ \times \C$ has
$\Bbound = A_{M2,\infty}$, the BRST cohomology of the
eleven-dimensional supergravity twist.  The boundary algebra has
$\kappa = -1/2$.  Its Koszul dual $A_{M2,\infty}^!$ is the
universal defect algebra, and $\alpha_T|_{(2,0)}$ is the M2
$r$-matrix.  The six projections of $\alpha_T$ reproduce
Costello's computations.

\subsection*{Celestial holography}

The celestial holography programme of Costello--Paquette translates
4d scattering amplitudes into 2d chiral algebra correlators on the
celestial sphere.  In the bar-cobar framework: the celestial chiral
algebra is the boundary algebra $\Bbound$ of a 4d $\to$ 3d
reduction, and celestial OPE coefficients are projections of
$\alpha_T$.  The celestial $R$-matrix is the collision residue
$\alpha_T|_{(2,0)}$.  The soft theorems of gauge theory
(Weinberg, Low) are the leading terms of the spectral expansion of
$r(z)$.

\subsection*{The deformation-quantization bridge}

The PVA on $H^\bullet(\Bbound)$ is the classical limit of the
holographic system.  The deformation-quantization bridge
$Q_{\mathrm{HT}}$ lifts this classical PVA to the quantum
$\Ainf$ algebra $\Bbound$, controlled by $\alpha_T$:
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=large]
\text{PVA (classical coisson)}
\ar[r, "Q_{\mathrm{HT}}"]
& \text{$\Ainf$ chiral (quantum Koszul)}
\end{tikzcd}
\]
The functor $Q_{\mathrm{HT}}$ is the composition: PVA $\to$
3d HT sigma model (Khan--Zeng) $\to$ BV-BRST quantization $\to$
$\Ainf$ operations from Feynman integrals on $\FM_k(\C)$.  At the
level of formal moduli: $Q_{\mathrm{HT}}$ is a morphism of formal
moduli problems from the PVA deformation space to the $\Ainf$
deformation space, with the fibre recording the quantum corrections
at each order.


\section*{XII.\quad What this volume proves}

The main results, in theorem-level form:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*$'$)},leftmargin=2.5em]
\item \emph{Swiss-cheese identification.}
  $(\barBch(\cA), D_\cA, \Delta)$ is an $\SCchtop$-algebra.
\item \emph{Homotopy-Koszulity.}
  $\Omega\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \xrightarrow{\sim} \SCchtop$.
  Bar-cobar is a Quillen equivalence.
\item \emph{PVA descent.}
  $H^\bullet(A, Q)$ carries a PVA from the regular/singular
  decomposition of $m_2$.  Axioms D2--D6 proved.
\item \emph{Yang--Baxter.}
  The spectral braiding $R(z)$ satisfies YBE from $W$-coherence
  on $\FM_3(\C)$.
\item \emph{Bulk-boundary-line triangle.}
  $\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound)$,
  $\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!\text{-mod}$.
\item \emph{3d MC element.}
  $\alpha_T \in \mc(\gSC_T)$ exists; six projections recover all
  structures.  $\alpha_T|_{\text{closed}} = \Theta_\cA$.
\end{enumerate}
All results are unconditional algebraically; the BV-BRST
realization from physical axioms (H1)--(H4) is conditional on the
stated hypotheses.

\medskip
What follows constructs each ingredient of $\alpha_T$.

\section*{0.\quad From algebraic geometry to physics}

\noindent
Volume~I constructs $E_n$-chiral algebras as algebraic-geometric
objects: factorisation algebras on configuration spaces of curves,
with bar complexes at every operadic level, $R$-matrices, Yangian
structures, chiral quantum group data, derived chiral centres
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ carrying $E_2$ and $E_3$
structure, the $\SCchtop$ datum on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$, and the $E_n$
operadic circle as an algebraic-geometric structure. All of this is
algebraic geometry of factorisation on configuration spaces. This
volume discovers that these algebraic-geometric constructions
\emph{are} physics: the derived chiral centre is the bulk algebra
of a real three-dimensional holomorphic-topological gauge theory,
the $\SCchtop$ pair is a boundary-bulk system, and the $E_3$
algebra is a topological field theory. The passage from Volume~I to
Volume~II is the passage from construction to physical realisation.

\medskip

\noindent
One coalgebra, many projections: that is the Steinberg principle
(Section~IV below). But first: one curve, many stratifications.
Bar chain models are indexed not by manifolds but by
\emph{topologico-combinatoric stratifications with specified
bulk-to-boundary structure}: the topology determines the
underlying manifold and its boundary components; the
combinatorics determines the collision patterns, encoded by the
boundary strata of the Fulton--MacPherson compactification; and
the bulk-to-boundary data determines which strata involve
interior points approaching boundary components.  Different
stratifications of the same underlying manifold give different
bar complexes.  The bar differential is controlled by
codimension-1 strata; the $\Ainf$ relations by codimension-2
strata (the associahedra boundary); the curvature
$d_B^2 = \kappa\,\omega_g$ by the failure to extend across
certain strata of $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

The volume climbs the $E_n$-topological ladder. The $E_3$-topological
rung (= full TQFT on $X \times \R$, independent of the complex
structure on $X$) is the \emph{first} rung: Virasoro, reached via
Sugawara $T = [Q, G]$ at non-critical level. Higher rungs
$E_{N+1}$-topological for $\cW_N$ ($N \ge 3$) stand conditionally
on iterated Sugawara with higher-spin antighosts (Section~XIII
below; Chapter~\ref*{ch:e-infinity-topologization}); the Platonic
endpoint $E_\infty^{\mathrm{top}}$ is $\cW_\infty$. The entire
volume is stratified over the following configurations.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{The geometric ladder.}\enspace
The table is a ladder in the $E_n$ hierarchy, and each rung gains
one degree of freedom.  Stages~0--2 are the $\Eone$ rung:
topological, real, $0$--$1$-dimensional.  The point (Stage~0) is the
algebra at a point; $\R$ (Stage~1) is the $\Eone$-algebra with its
deconcatenation cooperad; the interval $[0,1]$ and half-line
$\R_{\ge 0}$ (Stage~2) give the bar complex
$B(\cA) = k \otimes_\cA^{\mathbf{L}} k$ via augmentation boundary,
the $\Ainf$ structure $W(\mathsf{Ass})$, and the one-sided bar
resolution for modules.  Stages~3--4 are the $\Eone$-to-$\Etwo$
step: the complex plane $\C$ (Stage~3) brings the holomorphic
direction, with OPE residues from $\FM_k(\C)$ supplying the bar
differential $D_\cA$; the product $\C \times \R$ (Stage~4) makes
$B(\cA)$ an $\Eone$ dg coassociative coalgebra over the Koszul
dual cooperad $(\mathsf{Ass}^{\mathrm{ch}})^!$.  Stage~5 is the
half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ with its three collision types (bulk--bulk
from $\FM_k(\C)$, boundary--boundary from $\Conf_m^<(\R)$,
bulk-to-boundary from interior points approaching $\R$), producing
the $\SCchtop$ structure on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$.  Stage~6 is the
formal disk $D$, the local model where the chiral endomorphism
operad $\End^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$ is
$\operatorname{Aut}(\cO)$-equivariant.  Stage~7 is the modular
extension: the annulus and higher-genus surfaces $\Sigma_g$ carry
the partially modular operad $\SCchtop_{\mathrm{mod}}$ with
curvature $d^2 = \kappa\,\omega_g$.  Stage~8 is the Drinfeld
centre, where $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) =
C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ carries an $\Etwo$-chiral
Gerstenhaber structure (the chiral Deligne--Tamarkin theorem).
Stage~9 is topologization: given a 3d holomorphic-topological
theory whose boundary is $\cA$, the conformal vector $T(z)$ at
non-critical level trivialises the complex-structure dependence in
cohomology via Sugawara, and $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ becomes
$E_3$-\textsc{topological} by Dunn additivity
$\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \times \Eone^{\mathrm{top}}$.  This is the
target of the volume.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{$\SCchtop$ is the generic case.}\enspace
The $\SCchtop$ structure on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ is the structure
that \emph{most} chiral algebras carry on their derived centre.
$E_3$-topological (Stage~9) is a \emph{special case}, requiring a
conformal vector at non-critical level.  Without conformal vector,
the holomorphic direction carries nontrivial cohomological
information: the two colours of $\SCchtop$ remain distinct, and
one is stuck at Stage~8.  Examples stuck at $\SCchtop$:
critical-level Kac--Moody $V_{-h^\vee}(\fg)$ where Sugawara
degenerates; genuinely $\Eone$-chiral algebras (Yangians) where
no conformal vector is available; and Calabi--Yau functor outputs
lacking conformal vectors.
The operad $\SCchtop$ must be understood as a first-class object,
with seven mutually redundant presentations forming a
\emph{heptagon of equivalences}
(Chapter~\ref{ch:sc-chtop-heptagon}): the five classical faces
(operadic, Koszul dual, factorisation, BV/BRST, and
$L_\infty$-convolution) together with two new faces (the
Drinfeld-centre face via the factorisation-module category
$\mathrm{Rep}_{\mathrm{fact}}(\cA)$, and the
derived-algebraic-geometry face via global sections of a stack
classifying $E_1$-chiral algebras with $\SCchtop$-datum).
The classical pentagon is the subgraph spanned by the five
classical faces; the seven-fold identification of the collision
residue $r(z)$ on the affine lineage (Section~XI below) is a
parallel heptagon on a different object.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Critical-level failure.}\enspace
At the critical level $k = -h^\vee$, the Sugawara construction is
undefined: the denominator $k + h^\vee$ vanishes and no conformal
vector exists.  For affine Kac--Moody, the centre jumps from
scalars to the Feigin--Frenkel centre
$\mathfrak{z}(\widehat{\fg})$, a commutative chiral algebra
isomorphic to $\operatorname{Fun}\operatorname{Op}_{\fg^L}(D^\times)$.
Topologization fails: the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ remains at
$\SCchtop$, and $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ remains
$\Etwo$-chiral.
The critical level is the boundary of Stage~9: all stages through~8
are reached, but the conformal-vector bridge to
$E_3$-topological does not exist.

\section*{I.\quad The open/closed primitive}

\noindent
Two operators $a(z_1, t_1)$ and $b(z_2, t_2)$ on $\C \times \R$
interact in two independent directions. Holomorphic collision
$z_1 \to z_2$ produces the OPE residue; topological separation,
cutting $\R$ between $t_1$ and $t_2$, produces the deconcatenation
coproduct. The ordered bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ encodes both:
\[
d_{\barBch}[s^{-1}a \,|\, s^{-1}b]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\bigl[
 a(z_1)\,b(z_2)\cdot\eta_{12}
\bigr],
\qquad
\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2),
\]
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-delta}
\Delta[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n]
\;=\;
\sum_{i=0}^{n}
[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_i]
\;\otimes\;
[s^{-1}a_{i+1}|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n],
\end{equation}
with the differential a coderivation of the coproduct:
$\Delta \circ D_\cA = (D_\cA \otimes \id + \id \otimes D_\cA)
\circ \Delta$. Collisions in $\C$ commute with cuts in $\R$.
The differential is holomorphic factorisation on
$\FM_k(\C)$; the coproduct is topological factorisation on
$\Conf_k^{<}(\R)$; together they make $B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ an
$E_1$ dg coassociative coalgebra
(Theorem~\ref{thm:rosetta-e1-coalgebra}). This is the primitive
object of the volume. The symmetric bar $B^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ of
Volume~I is its $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow; the modular
characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is the leading scalar that survives
averaging (for abelian algebras $\kappa = \mathrm{av}(r(z))$;
for non-abelian Kac--Moody a Sugawara shift intervenes,
Section~III); the five Vol~I theorems A--D and~H
are the invariants that survive the projection.

The form $\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ is a bar-construction
coefficient: a logarithmic one-form on the configuration space.
It is not a connection form. The KZ connection on conformal
blocks is a different object: $\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}} = d -
\Omega\,dz/((k + h^\vee)z)$, a rational one-form in $z$.
The Arnold relation
$\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12} = 0$
forces $D_\cA^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$. The curved lift
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_1$, with higher-genus
scalar continuation conditional after the Vol~I rectification,
controls modularity. The universal Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_0$ packages both regimes.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Heisenberg on $\C \times \R$.}\enspace
Take $\cA = \cH_k$: one generator $J$, one OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$. Two operators sit at $(z_1, t_1)$
and $(z_2, t_2)$ with $t_1 < t_2$. Their bar element is
$[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]$.

In the holomorphic direction, the boundary stratum
$E_{12} \cong S^1$ of $\FM_2(\C)$ extracts the mode products
$J_{(n)}J$. The only nonvanishing mode is $J_{(1)}J = k$, so
\[
d_{\barBch}[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
\;=\;
J_{(1)}J
\;=\; k.
\]
No simple-pole OPE ($J_{(0)}J = 0$): the bracket component
vanishes. The entire holomorphic interaction is the scalar~$k$.

In the topological direction, cut $\R$ at each gap:
\begin{align*}
\Delta[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
&\;=\;
[\,] \otimes [s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut before $t_1$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J] \otimes [s^{-1}J]
&&\text{(cut between $t_1$ and $t_2$)}
\\
&\;+\;
[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] \otimes [\,]
&&\text{(cut after $t_2$)}.
\end{align*}
The middle term separates one field to each half-line. Since
$J_{(0)}J = 0$, separated fields do not interact: the
factorisation is exact.

Coderivation check: $D_\cA[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J] = k$, then
$\Delta(k) = k \otimes [\,] + [\,] \otimes k$.
On the right, $(D_\cA \otimes \id + \id \otimes D_\cA)$ applied
to $\Delta[s^{-1}J | s^{-1}J]$ gives
$[\,] \otimes k + k \otimes [\,]$. Both sides agree: collision
in $\C$ commutes with cutting in $\R$.

The bar element lives on $\FM_2(\C) \times \Conf_2^<(\R)$.
The topological factor $\Conf_2^<(\R) = \{t_1 < t_2\} \cong
\R_{>0}$ is contractible; its coalgebra structure contracts to
strict coassociativity: deconcatenation. At degree~$n$,
\[
\barB^{\mathrm{ch},n}(\cA)
\;\subset\;
(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}
\otimes
\Omega^\bullet\bigl(\FM_n(\C) \times \Conf_n^<(\R)\bigr).
\]
The ordered space $\Conf_n^<(\R) \cong \R^{n-1}_{> 0}$ is
contractible; no higher homotopies survive; the coproduct is
the unique coassociative coproduct on $T^c(V)$.

For the Heisenberg, the MC element collapses to
$\Theta_{\cH_k} = k \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$; the genus-$g$
projection is $F_g(\cH_k) = k \cdot \lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$
\textup{(\textsc{uniform-weight})}. The single scalar $k$
controls every entry of the census table.

\section*{II.\quad What this volume proves}

\noindent
The $R$-matrix, the Yangian, the chiral quantum group, the
derived chiral centre $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$:
these are constructed algebraic-geometrically in Volume~I, but
what do they \emph{mean}? The five theorems of Volume~I live in
the symmetric bar $B^\Sigma(\cA)$; their invariants are scalar
($\kappa(\cA)$,
$\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$
\textup{(\textsc{uniform-weight})}, the shadow tower, the
discriminant $\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$). The $E_1$ data (the
$R$-matrix, the Yangian, the $\SCchtop$ datum on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$) is richer
than these scalar invariants. This volume discovers that the
$E_1$ data IS physics: three structures acquire physical content.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The spectral $R$-matrix.}\enspace
The ordered collision at degree~$2$ produces the classical
$r$-matrix $r(z)$, always written with its level prefix:
$r_{\cH_k}(z) = k/z$ (Heisenberg, vanishing at $k = 0$),
$r_{\widehat{\fg}_k}(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ (affine Kac--Moody,
trace-form convention, vanishing at $k = 0$). Degree~$3$ gives
the Yang--Baxter equation (Theorem~\ref{thm:YBE}) from
three-face Stokes on $\FM_3(\C)$. On evaluation modules for
affine Kac--Moody, the braided line category reproduces the
quantum group category of $U_q(\fg)$ at
$q = e^{i\pi/(k+h^\vee)}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:affine-monodromy-identification}).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The line category and the Koszul dual.}\enspace
Line operators along $\R_+ \times \{0\}$ carry modules for the
open-colour Koszul dual $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$. For
Heisenberg, $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = Y(\mathfrak{u}(1))$; for
affine Kac--Moody, $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = \Ydg(\fg)$; the
complete spectral Drinfeld strictification
(Theorem~\ref{thm:complete-strictification}) holds for every
simple Lie type.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The bulk-boundary-line triangle.}\enspace
Bulk local operators $\Abulk$ on the interior of
$\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$ identify unconditionally with chiral
Hochschild cochains of the boundary algebra $\Bbound$. On the
boundary-linear exact sector and on the chirally Koszul locus,
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound)
= \RHom_{\Bbound\text{-}\Bbound}(\Bbound, \Bbound)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:boundary-linear-bulk-boundary}) and
$\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}\text{-mod}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:lines_as_modules}). Two parallel lines
interact through the bulk, producing the meromorphic braiding.
The global triangle (boundary determines bulk determines
boundary) is proved at the boundary-linear level; the full
reconstruction remains conjectural.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Two theorems native to this volume.}\enspace
Beyond the five theorems of Volume~I, this volume proves two
additional theorems which exist only in the $3$d~HT register:
\emph{Theorem~F (Universal Holography)} — every boundary-linear
chiral algebra $\cA$ with conformal vector at non-critical level
is the boundary of a canonical $3$d~HT gauge theory $T_\cA$ whose
bulk factorisation algebra identifies with
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, the assignment
$\cA \mapsto T_\cA$ being functorial and compatible with
Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:universal-holography-stratified}); and
\emph{Theorem~G (Infinite Fingerprint Classification)} — the
six-slot fingerprint
$\varphi'(\cA) = (p_{\max}, r_{\max}, \chi_{\mathrm{VOA}},
n_{\mathrm{strong}}, \mathrm{coset}, \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}})$ is a
complete invariant of the Koszul-bar-complex structure of $\cA$
on the standard landscape
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fingerprint-complete-ch}); the $\mathbf{G/L/C/M/FF}$
quaternitomy with critical-level fifth class $\mathbf{FF}$ is the
double coarse projection forgetting $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$ and
projecting onto $r_{\max}$. The architectural count of the
programme thus upgrades from five theorems (Volume~I: A, B, C, D, H)
to seven (the five plus F, G).

\section*{III.\quad Four conceptual leaps}

Everything in both volumes is organised by four leaps away from
classical Koszul duality. The bar complex indexes the first three;
the fourth (derived centres produce physics) is what this volume
proves.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(1) Commutative to $E_1/\Ainf$.}\enspace
Classical Koszul duality works for commutative and associative
algebras. The chiral setting replaces commutativity by an
$E_1$-chiral $\Ainf$ structure: the holomorphic direction
promotes multiplication to the Stasheff hierarchy on
$\FM_k(\C)$, the topological direction promotes concatenation
to deconcatenation on $\Conf_k^{<}(\R)$. The bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ is an $E_1$
coassociative coalgebra with desuspended augmentation-ideal
generators ($|s^{-1}v| = |v| - 1$); the $\SCchtop$ structure
emerges on the chiral Hochschild cochains
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$, where the pair
$(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\, \cA)$ is the
bulk-boundary datum. The bar differential is a coderivation
determined by its cogenerator projections: a sequence
$m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \to A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))$
with $\deg m_k = 2 - k$ and spectral parameters
$\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$ recording holomorphic separations. The
identity $D_\cA^2 = 0$ is the full Stasheff hierarchy:
\[
\sum_{\substack{i+j = n+1 \\ i,j \ge 1}}\;\sum_{s=0}^{n-j}
\pm\; m_i\bigl(a_1, \ldots, m_j(a_{s+1}, \ldots, a_{s+j}),
\ldots, a_n\bigr) \;=\; 0,
\]
each relation a Stokes theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$. At $n = 3$: the
Stasheff associahedron $K_3 = [0,1]$ with two endpoints
$D_{12}$ and $D_{23}$; the failure of $m_2$ to be associative
is corrected by $m_3$. When $m_3 = 0$ (Heisenberg),
strict associativity holds. When $m_3 \neq 0$ (Kac--Moody,
Virasoro), the homotopy is the Jacobiator on $K_3$, and its
nonvanishing is the failure of the OPE to compose strictly. At
$n = 4$: the Stasheff pentagon $K_4$, five boundary faces from the
collision strata $D_{12}, D_{23}, D_{34}, D_{123}, D_{234}$; the
operation $m_4$ arises as the interior integral over $K_4$. For
$\cW_3$, $m_4$ is nonzero with coefficient $32/(22+5c)$ from the
$WW\Lambda$ channel. The general pattern:
$\FM_n(\C)/\mathrm{Aff}(\C)$ is a compact manifold with corners of
real dimension $2n - 4$; the identity $\partial^2 = 0$ on
$\FM_n(\C)$ (every codimension-$2$ face reached by two
codimension-$1$ faces with opposite orientations) is what makes
$D_\cA^2 = 0$ hold.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(2) Genus~$0$ to modular.}\enspace
Classical Koszul duality is genus~$0$ on the formal disk.
The modular setting lifts the bar complex over
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ with genus-$1$ curved differential
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_1$ and conditional
higher-genus scalar continuation
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$
\textup{(\textsc{uniform-weight})}. The
Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$ lives in the modular
convolution algebra
$\gAmod := \Hom_\Sigma(F\!\Com, \End_{\barBch(\cA)})$;
the shadow tower projects onto degree strata; the discriminant
$\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$ classifies the $\kappa \neq 0$ locus into
G/L/C/M, with the $\kappa = 0$ stratum constituting a fifth class
$\mathbf{FF}$ (Feigin--Frenkel) where Sugawara fails, topologization
does not reach $E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$, and the chiral Hochschild is
infinite-dimensional (isomorphic to the algebra of functions on the
opers $\operatorname{Op}_{\fg^L}(D^\times)$ for affine
Kac--Moody). The curvature is
controlled by explicit data: for Heisenberg, $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$
and $F_1 = -k\log\eta(\tau)$ (where
$\eta(\tau) = q^{1/24}\prod_{n \ge 1}(1 - q^n)$, $q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$);
for Virasoro, $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$ and $F_1 = -(c/2)\log\eta(\tau)$.
The scalar genus tower (\textsc{uniform-weight}) satisfies
\[
\sum_{g \ge 1} F_g \cdot \hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
\log \hat{A}(\kappa\,\hbar),
\]
with $F_1 = \kappa/24$ and $F_2 = 7\kappa^2/5760$, each determined
by Bernoulli numbers. The discriminant trichotomy
$\Delta = 0$ (class~$\mathbf{G}$: formal, finite tower),
$\Delta \neq 0$ with finite algebraic depth
(classes~$\mathbf{L}$, $\mathbf{C}$), and $\Delta \neq 0$
with infinite depth (class~$\mathbf{M}$: gravitational) is the
gauge-gravity dichotomy: the discriminant decides whether the 3d
theory has a gravitational sector.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(3) Local to nonlocal.}\enspace
All standard vertex algebras, including those with OPE poles, are
$E_\infty$: locality is compatible with arbitrary pole order. The
genuinely $E_1$ layer is where the spectral $R$-matrix is an
independent input, not derived from the local OPE, and the
line-side dual $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ is a dg-shifted Yangian.
The ordered bar $B^{\mathrm{ord}}$ is the primitive; the symmetric
bar $B^{\Sigma}$ is its $R$-matrix-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant
projection. The distinction is provenance, not pole order. For
$E_\infty$-chiral algebras (all vertex algebras), the $R$-matrix is
derived from the OPE: the collision residue at degree~$2$ gives
$r(z) = k/z$ (Heisenberg) or $r(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ (Kac--Moody,
trace-form convention, vanishing at $k = 0$). For genuinely $E_1$
algebras (Yangians, Etingof--Kazhdan quantum vertex algebras), the
$R$-matrix is external data: the RTT relation
\[
R_{12}(z_1 - z_2)\, T_1(z_1)\, T_2(z_2)
\;=\;
T_2(z_2)\, T_1(z_1)\, R_{12}(z_1 - z_2)
\]
is an axiom, not a consequence. The $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant projection
$\mathrm{av} \colon \mathfrak{g}^{E_1} \to \mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{mod}}$
destroys the $R$-matrix data: at degree~$2$,
$\mathrm{av}(r(z)) = \kappa$ for abelian algebras (Heisenberg,
free fields), while for non-abelian Kac--Moody
$\mathrm{av}(r(z)) + \dim\fg/2 = \kappa$ (the Sugawara shift
$\dim\fg/2$ from the simple-pole self-contraction persists). In
either case the averaging map collapses the spectral $r$-matrix
to a single scalar. The ordered bar remembers the full spectral
structure; the symmetric bar sees only its trace.

\smallskip
These three leaps act independently, and together give the
seven-part architecture of this volume. The programme, stated in
one line: holomorphic chiral factorisation (co)homology via
bar/cobar, instantiated at various geometric locations.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The fourth leap: derived centres produce
physics.}\enspace
The three leaps above are algebraic-geometric: they describe the

The passage is precise. For Heisenberg:

The fourth leap is not algebraic but

The three algebraic-geometric leaps of Volume~I produce the raw

\noindent\textbf{The Lagrangian self-intersection.}\enspace

\[

\;\simeq\;

\]
When the bar cohomology is concentrated in a single degree
(the chirally Koszul condition), the self-intersection is
\emph{clean}: the excess Tor sheaves
$\mathcal{T}\!\mathit{or}^{\cO_\cA}_q(\cO_{\barBch},\cO_{\barBch})$

transversal (class~$\mathbf{G}$, no higher Tor). The
Kac--Moody self-intersection has Lie-transverse excess
(class~$\mathbf{L}$, Tor vanishes above degree~$3$). The
Virasoro self-intersection carries infinite excess Tor
(class~$\mathbf{M}$, all $m_k \neq 0$): the gravitational
theory has infinitely many quantum corrections,
each an obstruction class in the excess.

embeddings gives
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA) \simeq

the functions on the shifted cotangent bundle of the Lagrangian.
This is the \emph{local model}: boundary data determine
$T^*[-1]\cL_\cA$. The actual bulk geometry requires more: the
formal Darboux theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:holographic-reconstruction}) supplies a
compatible closed $1$-form that reconstructs the $(-2)$-shifted
symplectic ambient~$\mathscr{M}$ from the Lagrangian embedding.
The boundary therefore determines the shifted-cotangent side of
the bulk; the full formal ambient requires the Darboux datum.
For class~$\mathbf{G}$ (Heisenberg), the ambient is the
shifted cotangent itself: $\mathscr{M} = T^*[-2]\cL_{\cH_k}$,
no additional data needed. For class~$\mathbf{L}$ (Kac--Moody),
the Darboux datum is the KZ connection: the flat connection on
conformal blocks determines the symplectic structure of the bulk.
For class~$\mathbf{M}$ (Virasoro), the Darboux datum is the
infinite shadow tower: each $S_r$ contributes a correction to
the ambient geometry, and the reconstruction requires the full
tower.

\section*{V.\quad The Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$}

The product $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$ is the space of
operations of a two-coloured operad:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{closed-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C), \\
&\text{mixed-to-open:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k, \mathsf{top}^m;\,\mathsf{top})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C) \times E_1(m), \\
&\text{open-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\ldots, \mathsf{top}, \ldots;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \varnothing.
\end{alignat*}
Closed colour: holomorphic collisions on $\FM_k(\C)$. Open
colour: topological orderings on $E_1(m) = \Conf_m^<(\R)$.
Mixed: the product. Composition is FM substitution on the
closed factor, interval insertion on the open factor. The empty
open-to-closed component is directionality: bulk restricts to
boundary, not conversely. The bar complex
$(\barBch(\cA), D_\cA, \Delta)$ is an $E_1$ dg coassociative
coalgebra: the differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation encoding
the $A_\infty$-chiral structure, the coproduct $\Delta$ is
deconcatenation. The $\SCchtop$ structure emerges in the chiral
derived center: the pair
$(\cC^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\, \cA)$ carries
Swiss-cheese structure via brace operations from the chiral
endomorphism operad. The classical Swiss-cheese operad
is Koszul (Livernet); Kontsevich formality and bar-cobar transfer
give homotopy-Koszulity
$\Omega\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \xrightarrow{\sim} \SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}). The Koszul dual cooperad
$(\SCchtop)^! = (\mathsf{Lie},\, \mathsf{Ass},\, \text{shuffle-mixed})$
(Proposition~\ref{thm:SC-self-duality}) exchanges the closed colour
$\mathsf{Com}$ with $\mathsf{Lie}$; the duality functor on
$\SCchtop$-algebras is an involution, and the open-colour dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ carries the dual algebraic structure.

The operad $\SCchtop$ sits between $\Eone$-chiral and $E_3$-topological in the
$E_n$ hierarchy. The passage $\Eone \to \Etwo$ is automatic:
Hochschild cochains of an $\Eone$-algebra carry $\Etwo$ structure
(Deligne's conjecture, proved). The passage from $\Etwo$ to the
3d holomorphic-topological bulk requires a 3d holomorphic-topological theory whose
boundary is the chiral algebra; for Kac--Moody this is
holomorphic Chern--Simons (proved by Costello--Li), for general
vertex algebras it requires quantising the Poisson vertex model
(conjectural). The further passage $\SCchtop \to$
$E_3$-topological requires a conformal vector at non-critical
level; the topologisation theorem of Volume~I proves this for
affine Kac--Moody, and the present volume proves the
Drinfeld--Sokolov/$W$ and freely-generated-PVA lanes; beyond
those families the general package remains conjectural.
$\SCchtop$ is the intermediary that
organises the $\Eone$-to-$\Etwo$ passage without itself being
$E_3$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The physical realization.}\enspace
Volume~I constructs $\SCchtop$ and the derived centre pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ as
algebraic-geometric objects. This volume interprets them
physically. The closed colour (holomorphic, on $\C$) is the
boundary conformal field theory. The open colour (topological,
on $\R$) is the topological direction of the 3d bulk. The
directionality constraint (no open-to-closed) is the physical

conversely: the boundary VOA on $\{0\} \times \C$ determines the
bulk observables on $\R_{>0} \times \C$ via the derived centre,
but the bulk does not determine a unique boundary. For abelian
CS ($\cH_k$): the two colours coincide ($\Abulk = \Bbound$)
and the $\SCchtop$ structure is trivial. For non-abelian CS
($\widehat{\fg}_k$): the open-colour Koszul dual is
$\Ydg(\fg)$, the line category carries the quantum group
braiding, and the $\SCchtop$ structure encodes the full
BBL triangle. For 3d gravity ($\mathrm{Vir}_c$): the open

completion entropy $h_K \approx 0.567$ measures the complexity of
the bulk reconstruction, and the $\SCchtop$ structure is
infinite-depth: the gravitational theory has infinitely many
quantum corrections organized by the shadow obstruction tower.

\section*{VI.\quad The slab, the Drinfeld double, and the BBL triangle}

The $\SCchtop$ geometry admits a concrete realisation as a slab
$\C \times [0,1]$ with transverse boundary conditions: a
bimodule with two boundary components, not a Swiss-cheese disk.
The boundary algebra $\cA$ sits on one face; the Koszul dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ on the other. The universal algebra of
the slab fiber functor is the Drinfeld double
$\mathbf{U}_\cA = \cA \bowtie \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$, the
single object coupling boundary to bulk. Volume~I
complementarity (Theorem~C) is the algebraic manifestation of
transversality across the slab.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The bulk-boundary-line triangle.}\enspace
A holomorphic-topological theory on $\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$
produces three algebraic objects: \emph{bulk} local operators
$\Abulk$ (interior of $\C \times \R_{>0}$), \emph{boundary}
local operators $\Bbound$ (on $\{0\} \times \C$), and
\emph{line} operators $\cC_{\mathrm{line}}$
(along $\R_+ \times \{0\}$). The bulk is the derived chiral
centre:
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound) :=
\RHom_{\Bbound\text{-}\Bbound}(\Bbound,\,\Bbound)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:boundary-linear-bulk-boundary},
boundary-linear exact sector). The line category is modeled
by $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$-modules on the Koszul locus
(Theorem~\ref{thm:lines_as_modules}). Two parallel lines
$L_1(z_1)$, $L_2(z_2)$ interact through the bulk, producing
a meromorphic braiding
$R_{L_1, L_2}(z) \colon L_1 \otimes L_2
\xrightarrow{\sim} L_2 \otimes L_1$
in $z = z_1 - z_2$, satisfying the Yang--Baxter equation
from Stokes on $\FM_3(\C)$. For Heisenberg:
$R(z) = e^{k\hbar/z}$ (scalar). For affine Kac--Moody on
evaluation modules: the spectral braiding reproduces the
quantum group $R$-matrix of $U_\hbar(\fg)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Drinfeld double programme.}\enspace
Four questions control the deepest open programme:
(a)~construct $\mathbf{U}_\cA$ as a Hopf algebra at the
$E_1$-chiral level; (b)~identify the antipode with geometric
orientation reversal; (c)~prove $Z(\mathbf{U}_\cA) =
\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ (this is
Conjecture~\ref{conj:v1-drinfeld-center-equals-bulk} of
Volume~I, restated as a reconstruction theorem:
the Drinfeld centre of the double recovers the bulk);
(d)~identify the hemisphere pairing with the cyclic pairing
on the bar complex. The boundary-linear half of~(c) is proved;
the remainder is the central open programme. For the
Heisenberg, the heuristic base case of
Computation~\ref{comp:drinfeld-center-heisenberg} lands in the
expected finite Theorem~H window, but the
Tamarkin/Theorem~H compatibility gap remains open.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The $K$-matrix modification.}\enspace
When the theory has no boundary conditions, one passes through an
interface to a free theory~$F$; the fiber functor then lands in
$\cC_F$, not in $\Vect$, and one works with $B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$
relative to $B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\mathrm{Heis})$. The $K$-matrix

\section*{VIII.\quad PVA descent}

The bar differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation of the cofree
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$. A coderivation on a cofree
coalgebra is determined by its cogenerator projections: a sequence
\[
m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \longrightarrow
A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1})),
\qquad k \ge 1, \quad \deg m_k = 2 - k,
\]
where $\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$ are spectral parameters recording
holomorphic separations. The identity $D_\cA^2 = 0$ expands into
the $\Ainf$ Stasheff relations: for each~$n$,
\[
\sum_{\substack{i+j = n+1 \\ i,j \ge 1}}\;\sum_{s=0}^{n-j}
\pm\; m_i\bigl(a_1, \ldots, m_j(a_{s+1}, \ldots, a_{s+j}),
\ldots, a_n\bigr) \;=\; 0.
\]
Each relation is Stokes' theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$.

At $n = 1$: $m_1^2 = 0$, i.e.\ $m_1 = Q$ is a differential.
At $n = 2$: the Leibniz rule, i.e.\ $Q$ is a derivation of $m_2$.
At $n = 3$: the Stasheff associahedron $K_3$ is a closed interval
$[0,1]$, the quotient of $\FM_3(\C)$ by the affine group; its
two endpoints are the collision strata $D_{12}$ and $D_{23}$. The
associativity defect $m_2(m_2(a,b),c) - m_2(a,m_2(b,c))$ equals
a boundary contribution involving $m_3$: the binary product is
associative up to the homotopy $m_3$. When $m_3 = 0$ (Heisenberg),
the product is strictly associative. When $m_3 \neq 0$
(Kac--Moody, Virasoro), the nonvanishing homotopy is the failure
of the OPE to be strictly associative.

At $n = 4$: the Stasheff pentagon, five boundary faces, $m_4$
as interior integral. For $\cW_3$, $m_4$ encodes the quartic
contact interaction with coefficient $32/(22+5c)$.

The general pattern: $\FM_n(\C)/\mathrm{Aff}(\C)$ is a compact
manifold with corners of real dimension $2n - 4$. The identity
$\partial^2 = 0$ on $\FM_n(\C)$ (every codimension-$2$ face is
reached by two codimension-$1$ faces with opposite orientations)
is the combinatorial identity that makes $D_\cA^2 = 0$ hold.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The resolvent principle.}\enspace
The $\Ainf$ operations $\{m_k\}_{k \ge 2}$ are determined by
the binary product $m_2$ alone. Let $(H, p, \iota, h)$ be a
deformation retract of $(\cA, m_1)$ onto cohomology. The dressed
propagator $\Phi = \iota + h \circ m_2 \circ \Phi^{\otimes 2}$
satisfies a tree-level bootstrap; its solution gives the tree
formula
\[
m_k^H
\;=\;
\sum_{T \in \mathrm{PRT}_k}
p \circ \mathfrak{m}(T) \circ \iota^{\otimes k},
\]
where $\mathrm{PRT}_k$ is the set of planar rooted binary trees
with $k$ leaves ($|\mathrm{PRT}_k| = C_{k-1}$, the Catalan
number), $\mathfrak{m}(T)$ assigns $m_2$ to each internal
vertex and $h$ to each internal edge. One equation, one binary
input, Catalan many trees: the perturbative expansion of the
Lagrangian self-intersection.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Descent to PVA.}\enspace
On cohomology $H^\bullet(A, Q)$, the higher $m_k$ vanish and
$m_2$ decomposes into a commutative product $\mu$ (regular part)
and a $\lambda$-bracket $\{a_\lambda b\}$ (singular part),
forming a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra. The five PVA
axioms each have a geometric origin:
sesquilinearity from $\C^*$-equivariance on $\FM_2(\C)$;
skew-symmetry from the exchange automorphism $z_1 \leftrightarrow z_2$;
the Jacobi identity from Stokes on $\FM_3(\C)$;
the Leibniz rule from the three-face Stokes identity on the
boundary of $\FM_3(\C)$;
commutativity of $\mu$ from the $\Z/2$-involution on

(Theorem~\ref{thm:cohomology_PVA}).
The quantization obstruction lives in $\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$;
the Quillen equivalence (Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul})
gives essential uniqueness.

\section*{IX.\quad Three computations}

\section*{XV.\quad The completeness question and the frontier}

Volume~I computes the bar complex as the Koszul model of the
Lagrangian self-intersection at genus~$0$, and the genus tower as
the deformation over $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$. Volume~II constructs
the $E_1$ bar coalgebra and the chiral derived center, and
realises the bulk-boundary-line triangle as the three-way
projection. The content of both volumes is the local geometry of
the derived symplectic category at a single point.

\emph{Does the genus-$0$ Lagrangian $\cL_\cA^{(0)}$ extend to a
family $\cL_\cA^{(g)} \hookrightarrow \cM_{\mathrm{vac}}$ over
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, compatible with gluing?} For
classes~$\mathbf{G}$ and~$\mathbf{L}$ the Lagrangian meets itself
transversally and the curvature extends automatically.
Class~$\mathbf{C}$ is clean (chiral Koszulness via PBW
concentration) though contact data appears. For
class~$\mathbf{M}$ the derived self-intersection carries higher
Tor and the operations $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}}$ are successive
obstruction classes; the Koszul dual carries higher $\Ainf$
operations and the completeness question is open. This is the
central problem, developed in Part~\ref{part:examples}.

The difficulty is precise. For class~$\mathbf{G}$ (Heisenberg):
the bar cohomology is concentrated in degree~$1$; the
self-intersection is clean; the genus tower is finite; the
extension over $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ is automatic (the curvature
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = k \cdot \omega_g$ is a scalar multiple of
the Hodge class, and the MC element
$\Theta_{\cH_k} = k \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$ terminates).
For class~$\mathbf{L}$ (Kac--Moody): the bar cohomology is
concentrated (chirally Koszul), the self-intersection is
Lie-transverse, the extension uses the KZ/KZB connection (at
genus~$0$/$1$) and modular functor data (at higher genus);
the finite depth ($m_{k \ge 4} = 0$) bounds the obstruction
tower. For class~$\mathbf{C}$ ($\beta\gamma$): chirally Koszul,
contact-depth ($d_{\mathrm{alg}} = 2$, the betagamma witness on
the standard conformal-weight line), extension conditional on
Massey vanishing from FM formality.
For class~$\mathbf{M}$ (Virasoro, $\cW_N$): the bar cohomology is
concentrated (chirally Koszul), but the Swiss-cheese structure is
non-formal: the transferred operations
$m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} \neq 0$ for all $k \ge 3$, and the extension
over $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ requires controlling these infinitely
many operations simultaneously. The shadow tower $\{S_r\}$
measures the complexity: each $S_r$ is an obstruction class in the
excess Tor, and the completeness question is whether these
obstructions can be consistently resolved at all genera.

would close the holographic correspondence.

proved at integral level (KZ pentagon + Kazhdan--Lusztig). At
generic non-integral level, genus~$\ge 1$: the Stokes phenomenon
of the KZB connection requires eigenvalue bounds, not just
determinant bounds.

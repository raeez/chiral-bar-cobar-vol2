\section*{I.\quad The Steinberg principle}

\noindent
\emph{The bar complex presents the Swiss-cheese algebra as the
Steinberg variety presents the Hecke algebra, and both are
instances of boundary-bulk reconstruction: the small (boundary)
algebra presents the big (bulk) algebra via derivation.}  The
conjectural thesis of this volume, and of the programme it
belongs to, is a single algebraic assertion, proved at the
boundary-linear level and conjectured globally: the boundary
chiral algebra $\cA$ is conjectured to determine the bulk via
its chiral derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$; the modular
characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is conjectured to be the
holographic central charge; the shadow obstruction tower
$\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r \ge 2}$ is the conjectured holographic
correction hierarchy; and the Drinfeld double
$\mathbf{U}_\cA = \cA \bowtie \cA^!$ is conjectured to be the
universal boundary-bulk algebra reconstructing gravity from
the vertex operator algebra.  The boundary-linear half of this
reconstruction is proved; the global triangle remains
conjectural.  In this volume the boundary-bulk story is
literally three-dimensional: the Swiss-cheese operad
$\SCchtop$ is the algebraic structure of the 3d
holomorphic-topological theory on a slab; the open colour is the
boundary VOA; the closed colour is the bulk algebra obtained via
the derived centre; and the coupling between the two colours is
the Drinfeld double structure.

The Steinberg variety $\widetilde{\cN} \times_{\fg} \widetilde{\cN}$
is one geometric object; its Borel--Moore homology, its
$K$-theory, and its equivariant derived category extract three
different algebraic invariants: the Hecke algebra, the Springer
correspondence, and the Kazhdan--Lusztig basis.  The chiral bar
complex $\barBch(\cA)$ on $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^{<}(\R)$ is
one coalgebra, and three distinct functors project three
distinct objects from it: cobar
$\Omega(\barBch(\cA)) \simeq \cA$ recovers the original algebra
as \emph{inversion} (not Koszul duality, and the left adjoint of
bar); Verdier duality on the Ran space,
$\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}(\barBch(\cA)) \simeq \barBch(\cA^!)$,
is Koszul duality proper, producing the line-side Koszul dual
coalgebra; and chiral Hochschild cochains
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ compute the bulk observables
as the chiral derived centre $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$.
The three functors are genuinely distinct, and no two of them
agree: cobar is inversion, Verdier is Koszul duality, Hochschild
is the derived centre.  Identifying any two of them collapses the
architecture of the volume; every chapter of this volume is a
projection of the single coalgebra $\barBch(\cA)$ through one of
the three.

A chiral algebra is a point in the derived symplectic category: a
Lagrangian $\cL_\cA \hookrightarrow \cM_{\mathrm{vac}}(\cA)$.
The bar complex is the endomorphism algebra of this point; the
Swiss-cheese operad is the local composition law; Koszul duality
is the symplectic complement; the genus tower is the deformation
of the point over the moduli of curves.  The Steinberg analogy
is not decoration: the three projections play three genuinely
different roles, and never coincide.

\section*{II.\quad Two directions}

Two operators $a(z_1, t_1)$ and $b(z_2, t_2)$ on $\C \times \R$
interact in two independent directions.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The holomorphic direction} ($z_1 \to z_2$).
Collision produces the residue pairing governed by the
$\lambda$-bracket.  Volume~I built the entire modular Koszul
theory from this mechanism:
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}a \,|\, s^{-1}b]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\bigl[
  a(z_1)\,b(z_2)\cdot\eta_{12}
\bigr],
\qquad
\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2),
\]
the Arnold relation
$\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12} = 0$
forces $D_\cA^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$, the curved lift
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ controls higher
genus, and the universal Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_0$ packages the five Vol~I theorems.  The
form $\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ is a \emph{bar-construction
coefficient}, a logarithmic one-form on the configuration space;
it is not the connection form of a flat bundle.  The KZ
connection on conformal blocks, treated in \S VIII below, is a
different object: it is the one-form $\Omega\,dz/(k + h^\vee)$,
with a rational one-form in $z$, not a logarithmic one-form in
$z_1 - z_2$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The topological direction.}
The operators separate: cut $\R$ at a point $t_*$ between $t_1$
and $t_2$.  Observables on each half-line depend only on their
own operators.  The \emph{deconcatenation coproduct} records this
separation:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface2-delta}
\Delta[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n]
\;=\;
\sum_{i=0}^{n}
[s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_i]
\;\otimes\;
[s^{-1}a_{i+1}|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n].
\end{equation}
The two directions are independent:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface2-coderivation}
\Delta \circ D_\cA
\;=\;
(D_\cA \otimes \id \;+\; \id \otimes D_\cA) \circ \Delta.
\end{equation}
The differential is a coderivation of the coproduct.  Collisions
in $\C$ commute with cuts in $\R$.  Together the two structures
exhibit the bar complex on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^{<}(\R)$: the operadic fingerprint of
the Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$.

The two-structure thesis of this volume asserts that these two
pieces of data form a complete local description of a
three-dimensional holomorphic--topological theory: the
differential in~$\C$ is a first-order fact about OPE residues,
the coproduct in~$\R$ is a zeroth-order groupoid fact about
concatenation, and the coderivation compatibility is what pins
the bar complex to the Swiss-cheese operad.  Every further
structure in this volume (the genus tower, the bulk-boundary-line
triangle, the spectral $R$-matrix, the PVA descent) projects
from this single compatibility identity.

\section*{III.\quad Three conceptual leaps}

Everything in both volumes is organised by three leaps away from
classical Koszul duality.  The bar complex indexes all three: (1)
is the horizontal direction (arity, $\C$-factor), (2) is the
vertical direction (genus, deformation over
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$), and (3) is the colour direction (closed
versus open, ordered versus symmetric).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(1) Commutative to $E_1/\Ainf$.}\enspace
Classical Koszul duality works for commutative/associative
algebras.  The chiral setting replaces $\Com$ by the Swiss-cheese
operad $\SCchtop$: the holomorphic direction promotes commutative
multiplication to a tower of higher operations $m_k$ (the
$\Ainf$~Stasheff hierarchy on $\FM_k(\C)$), and the topological
direction promotes concatenation to the deconcatenation coproduct
on $\Conf_k^{<}(\R)$.  Strict commutativity fails at arity~$3$
through the homotopy $m_3$ (the integral over the Stasheff
interval $K_3 = [0,1]$); higher Stasheff operations are forced
by $D_\cA^2 = 0$, each relation a Stokes theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(2) Genus~$0$ to modular.}\enspace
Classical Koszul duality is a genus-$0$ computation: one
differential, one cohomology.  The modular setting lifts the bar
complex over $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ and tracks the curved
differential $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$.
The universal Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_0$
lives in the modular convolution dg Lie algebra
$\gAmod := \Hom_\Sigma(F\!\Com, \End_{\barB(\cA)})$, and the
shadow obstruction tower is its projection onto arity strata,
classified by the discriminant $\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$ into four
depth classes G/L/C/M.  The genus tower is the deformation of the
Lagrangian over the moduli of curves.
The trichotomy $\Delta = 0$ (class~$\mathbf{G}$: formal, finite
tower), $\Delta \neq 0$ with finite algebraic depth
(classes~$\mathbf{L}$, $\mathbf{C}$: gauge-theoretic), and
$\Delta \neq 0$ with infinite depth (class~$\mathbf{M}$:
gravitational) is the gauge-gravity proposition: the discriminant
decides whether the 3d theory admits a gravitational sector.
The same discriminant controls quantum chaos: $\Delta = 0$ families
exhibit integrable scrambling with vanishing Lyapunov exponent,
while $\Delta \neq 0$ families saturate the Maldacena--Shenker--Stanford
bound $\lambda_L = 2\pi/\beta$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{(3) Local to nonlocal.}\enspace
The $E_\infty$ layer (locality, $\Sigma_n$-equivariance, OPE with
arbitrary pole order) sits inside a genuinely $E_1$ layer.  All
standard vertex algebras, including those with poles, are
$E_\infty$: locality is compatible with OPE poles.  The
genuinely $E_1$ layer is where the spectral $R$-matrix is an
\emph{independent} input (not derived from the local OPE) and the
line-side open-colour dual $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ is a
dg-shifted Yangian.  The ordered bar $B^{\mathrm{ord}}$ is the
primitive object; the symmetric bar $B^{\Sigma}$ is its
$R$-matrix-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant projection.  The
distinction is provenance, not pole order.

\smallskip
These three leaps act independently, and together give the
seven-part architecture of this volume.

\section*{IV.\quad The bar complex as self-intersection}

The independence of the two directions is geometric.  The bar
complex is a formal Koszul model of the Lagrangian
self-intersection $\cL_\cA \times_{\mathscr{M}}^h \cL_\cA$ in a
$(-2)$-shifted symplectic stack
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-is-self-intersection}): the holomorphic
direction is the Koszul differential of the Lagrangian
embedding, the topological direction is the groupoid diagonal,
and the coderivation property is their compatibility.  The
chiral algebra fixes a formal local bulk model: HKR for
Lagrangian embeddings recovers the shifted cotangent
$T^*[-1]\cL_\cA$ from boundary data; the actual ambient stack
requires the Darboux $1$-form
(Theorem~\ref{thm:holographic-reconstruction}).

\section*{V.\quad The open primitive and the AP-OC distinction}

The bar complex is not the fundamental object: it is the
coalgebraic shadow of the open/closed factorization dg-category
$\cC$ on the bordified curve $\widetilde{X}_D$
(\S\ref{subsec:categorical-open-sector}).  Objects of $\cC$ are
boundary conditions, morphisms are open-string states; a vacuum
boundary condition $b$ produces a boundary algebra
$A_b = \End_\cC(b)$ as a \emph{chart}, and $\barB(A_b)$ encodes
how closed-sector operations twist the open-sector composition.
The Morita class of $\cC$, not the quasi-isomorphism class of
$A_b$, is the invariant.

Three objects are independently constructed from $\cC$ and must
never be conflated: the bar complex $\barB(A_b)$ (classifying
twisting morphisms between $A_b$ and its line-side Koszul dual
$A^!_{\mathrm{line}}$); the chiral derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cC)
= \RHom_{\operatorname{Fun}(\cC,\cC)}(\operatorname{Id},
\operatorname{Id})$ (the universal bulk); and the line-side
Koszul dual algebra
$A^!_{\mathrm{line}} = D_{\operatorname{Ran}}(\barB(A_b))$
(modelling the line side on the chirally Koszul locus).  This is
the \emph{AP-OC distinction}: the bar complex classifies twisting
morphisms, the bulk is the derived centre, and the two live on
different levels of the categorical hierarchy.  The curved
genus-$g$ extension deforms the bar differential by
$\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ without disturbing the coproduct:
the deformation is purely closed-colour.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The slab fiber functor.}\enspace
The $\SCchtop$ open disk admits a concrete geometric realisation
as a slab with transverse boundary conditions: a bimodule
geometry with two boundary components, \emph{not} a Swiss-cheese
disk.  The universal algebra of the slab fiber functor is the
Drinfeld double of the two boundary algebras, and Volume~I
complementarity (Theorem~C) is the algebraic manifestation of
transversality.  The slab model makes the AP-OC distinction
precise as a fibre functor
(Remark~\ref{rem:slab-fiber-functor} in
\S\ref{sec:ht-bulk-boundary-line}); the interface generalisation
of Dimofte's construction
(Remark~\ref{rem:dimofte-interface-generalization}) shows how
the slab extends across changes of open/closed boundary data,
with $K$-matrix and double-bosonization forms in
Remark~\ref{rem:dimofte-k-matrix} and
Remark~\ref{rem:dimofte-double-bosonization}, and the meromorphic
braided structure on crossing lines in
Remark~\ref{rem:dimofte-meromorphic-braided}.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Morita invariance and modularity.}\enspace
Modularity is not an additional axiom.  It is trace plus
clutching on the open sector: the cyclic trace on $\End(b)$
seeds a Calabi--Yau structure, the annulus identification
$\int_{S^1} \cC \simeq \HH_\bullet(\cC)$ is the first modular
shadow, and clutching raises genus.  The shadow archetypes
G/L/C/M classify the $\Ainf$ complexity of the boundary algebra.
The hemisphere pairing of the boundary-bulk reconstruction
programme IS the cyclic pairing on the bar complex: both encode
the same boundary-bulk coupling, and the $\SCchtop$ structure
exhibits their identification at the level of operations.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{The ordered Drinfeld centre.}\enspace
What would be called the ``ordered Drinfeld centre'' in the
heuristic picture is not undefined: it is precisely
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, the chiral derived
centre, computed through the $E_1$ Hochschild framework of
Francis.  In operadic language, the ordered Drinfeld centre is
the $E_1$-centre of the boundary algebra as an
$\SCchtop$-coalgebra; the closed-colour projection of
$\alpha_T$ witnesses this identification through the
bulk-boundary-line triangle.  The line-side Koszul dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ and the derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ sit on opposite sides of
the Drinfeld double
$\mathbf{U}_\cA = \cA \bowtie \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$; the double
is conjectured to be the universal boundary-bulk algebra
(boundary-linear half proved; global triangle conjectural).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem H and Tamarkin's formal parameter.}\enspace
Theorem~H of Vol~I asserts that, at generic central charge,
chiral Hochschild cohomology
$\HH^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c, \mathrm{Vir}_c)$ is
concentrated in cohomological degrees $\{0, 1, 2\}$ with total
dimension at most~$4$; this bound identifies the bulk algebra
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)$ at generic
$c$ (the bound fails at Feigin--Frenkel critical loci, where
the derived centre enlarges).
Tamarkin's computation of $C^\bullet(H_k, H_k)$ over $k[\![\kappa]\!]$
does not contradict this bound: it includes the formal deformation
parameter $\kappa$ which lives in the ambient deformation problem,
not inside the derived centre.  The derived centre is the
specialisation to a fixed value of $\kappa$, whereas the Tamarkin
object tracks the family over the deformation base.  This
distinction is the same as the distinction between $\HH^\bullet$
of the fibre and $\HH^\bullet$ of the total space: the former is
bounded by Theorem~H, the latter is not.
The holomorphic twist of the 3d theory has a protected spectrum
computed by this bounded chiral Hochschild cohomology: Theorem~H
identifies the states that survive the twist, and the concentration
in degrees $\{0, 1, 2\}$ is the algebraic origin of the protected
sector in holomorphic--topological field theory.

\section*{VI.\quad The Vol~I engine}

Volume~I is Koszul duality on a curve: the bar construction for
chiral algebras on an algebraic curve~$X$, with five main
theorems A--D and~H.  Theorems~A, B, C1, and~H are unconditional
at the framework level.  Theorem~D and the scalar part~C2 of
Theorem~C are tagged \textsc{uniform-weight}: the scalar equation
$\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$
(\textsc{uniform-weight, $g \ge 2$}) is proved for algebras whose
generators share a single conformal weight.  At genus~$1$ the
equation is unconditional for both regimes
(\textsc{all-weight, $g = 1$}); at $g \ge 2$ in the multi-weight
regime the same equation carries a cross-channel correction term
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ that is only known to vanish when
the weight spectrum is uniform.  Every occurrence of
$\mathrm{obs}_g$, $F_g$, $\lambda_g$ below in a Vol~II statement
inherits the explicit tag from its Vol~I source.

Three structure theorems organise the representation-theoretic
content.  \emph{Algebraicity}: $H(t)^2 = t^4 Q_L(t)$, so the
shadow generating function is algebraic of degree~$2$.
\emph{Formality identification}: the shadow obstruction tower
agrees with the $L_\infty$ formality obstruction tower at all
arities (Theorem~\ref{V1-thm:shadow-formality-identification}),
with discriminant $\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$ classifying algebras
into four depth classes G/L/C/M.  \emph{Complementarity}: under
perfectness and chain-level nondegeneracy, factorisation homology
decomposes as
$H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, Z(A))
\simeq Q_g(A) \oplus Q_g(A^!)$,
a Lagrangian splitting.

Volume~II lifts this from one to three dimensions.  The bar
differential $D_\cA$ is holomorphic factorisation on~$\C$; the
deconcatenation coproduct $\Delta$ is topological factorisation
on~$\R$; together they form the $\SCchtop$-coalgebra on
$\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$.  The modular characteristic
$\kappa(\cA)$ controls the curved Swiss-cheese deformation at
genus $g \ge 1$; complementarity lifts to the bulk-boundary-line
triangle; the discriminant $\Delta$ classifies the complexity of
bulk-boundary pairs.  Classical Koszul duality is genus~$0$ on a
point; Volume~I is genus~$g$ on a curve; this volume is the
dimensional lift to $\C \times \R$.

\section*{VII.\quad The Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$}

The product $\FM_k(\C) \times \Conf_k^<(\R)$ is the space of
operations of a two-coloured operad:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{closed-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C), \\
&\text{mixed-to-open:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\mathsf{ch}^k, \mathsf{top}^m;\,\mathsf{top})
\;=\; \FM_k(\C) \times E_1(m), \\
&\text{open-to-closed:}\quad
&&\SCchtop(\ldots, \mathsf{top}, \ldots;\,\mathsf{ch})
\;=\; \varnothing.
\end{alignat*}
Closed colour: holomorphic collisions on $\FM_k(\C)$.  Open
colour: topological orderings on $E_1(m) = \Conf_m^<(\R)$.
Mixed: the product.  Composition is FM substitution on the
closed factor, interval insertion on the open factor.  The empty
open-to-closed component is directionality: bulk restricts to
boundary, not conversely, matching the Springer pattern of
restriction from $G$ to $B$.  The bar complex
$(\barBch(\cA), D_\cA, \Delta)$ is a coalgebra over $\SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:rosetta-swiss-cheese}), with the differential
the closed-colour co-structure and the coproduct the open-colour
co-structure.  The classical Swiss-cheese operad is Koszul
(Livernet); Kontsevich formality and bar-cobar transfer upgrade
this to a Quillen equivalence
$\Omega\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop) \xrightarrow{\sim} \SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}), and Koszul self-duality
$(\SCchtop)^! \simeq \SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:SC-self-duality}) makes the open-colour dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$ an $\SCchtop$-\emph{algebra}.

\section*{VIII.\quad Three computations}

The machine is tested on three families of increasing complexity;
full computations are carried out in the Introduction and in
Part~\ref{part:examples}.  In every case the $r$-matrix is
written with its level prefix explicit: at zero level the
$r$-matrix vanishes, and this vanishing is the first sanity check
on every formula.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg} (class~$\mathbf{G}$, formal).
Generator $J$, OPE $J(z)J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$; $\lambda$-bracket
$\{J_\lambda J\} = k\lambda$, $m_2(J,J;\lambda) = k\lambda$, all
$m_{k\ge 3} = 0$.  Classical $r$-matrix $r_{\cH_k}(z) = k/z$ (the
$d\log$ kernel absorbs one pole order from the double-pole OPE;
at $k = 0$, $r_{\cH_k}$ vanishes).  Quantum $R$-matrix
$R(z) = \exp(k\hbar/z)$.  Chiral Koszul dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{ch}} = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$ (so
$\cH_k^! \ne \cH_{-k}$); open-colour dual
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = Y(\mathfrak{u}(1))$; line category:
Fock modules.  Bulk $\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\cH_k) = \cH_k$.  At
genus~$1$, $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$ and $F_1 = -k\log\eta(\tau)$.
The single scalar $k$ controls every entry.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Kac--Moody} (class~$\mathbf{L}$, Lie-transverse).
$m_2(J^a, J^b;\lambda) = f^{ab}{}_c J^c + k\,\delta^{ab}\lambda$;
$m_3 \neq 0$ (the Jacobiator homotopy); $m_{k\ge 4} = 0$.  The
classical $r$-matrix is $r_{\widehat{\fg}_k}(z) = k\,\Omega/z$,
with the level $k$ surviving $d\log$ absorption, vanishing at
$k = 0$.  The KZ connection on conformal blocks is
\[
\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}}
\;=\;
d \;-\; \frac{1}{k+h^\vee}\,\Omega\,\frac{dz}{z},
\]
a rational one-form in~$z$, \emph{not} a $d\log$ expression
$\Omega\,d\log z$: the Arnold form $\eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$
of \S II is a bar coefficient, not a connection form.  The
open-colour dual is $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = \Ydg(\fg)$.
Genus~$1$:
$\kappa(\widehat{\fg}_k) = \dim\fg\,(k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$, with
\emph{Feigin--Frenkel anti-symmetry for affine Kac--Moody}
$\kappa(\widehat{\fg}_k) + \kappa(\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee})
= 0$.  The Virasoro Feigin--Frenkel anti-symmetry has a different
constant,
$\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) + \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}) = 13$, not
zero: the two families share the duality name but not the
involution data.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{$\cW_3$} (class~$\mathbf{M}$, infinite tower).
The first family where the $\Ainf$ structure is genuinely
infinite:
$m_2(T,T;\lambda) = (c/12)\lambda^3 + 2T\lambda + \partial T$,
and the $WW$ $\lambda$-bracket involves the composite
$\Lambda = {}\!:(TT): - \tfrac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T$ with
coefficient $32/(22+5c)$; $m_3$ and $m_4$ are nonzero and the
tower does not terminate, with completion entropy
$h_K \approx 0.772$.

\section*{IX.\quad The 3d MC element $\alpha_T$}

Volume~I has one MC element $\Theta_\cA$ in the modular
convolution algebra $\gAmod$.  This volume has one MC element
$\alpha_T \in \mc(\gSC_T)$ in the Swiss-cheese convolution algebra
\[
\gSC_T
\;:=\;
\Hom_\Sigma\!\bigl(
  \mathbf{B}(\SCchtop),\;
  \End_{(\Bbound,\,\cC_{\mathrm{line}})}
\bigr).
\]
Its $L_\infty$-brackets are indexed by two-coloured stable trees:
closed vertices labelled by $\FM_k(\C)$-chains, open vertices by
$E_1(m)$-chains, mixed vertices by products.  Existence of
$\alpha_T$ follows from homotopy-Koszulity of $\SCchtop$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul}), which gives $\partial^2 = 0$
on $\mathbf{B}(\SCchtop)$.

$\alpha_T = \alpha_T^{\mathrm{cl}} + \alpha_T^{\mathrm{mix}}
+ \alpha_T^{\mathrm{op}}$
splits the MC equation into three: a pure closed equation (the
Vol~I MC equation), a mixed equation ($r$-matrix and PVA bracket
are $\Theta_\cA$-twisted cocycles with
$d_\Theta = D + [\Theta_\cA, -]$), and a pure open equation
closing the line category.  For Heisenberg, $\gSC_T$ is abelian
and
$\alpha_{\cH_k} = m_2 + \hbar\,k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda$; all
six projections collapse to the single scalar~$k$.

\section*{X.\quad PVA descent and the $\Ainf$ hierarchy}
\label{sec:preface-pva-descent}

The bar differential $D_\cA$ is a coderivation of the cofree
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$, hence determined by its
cogenerator projections
$m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \to
A((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))$ of degree $2 - k$, with
$\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$ the spectral parameters.  The identity
$D_\cA^2 = 0$ expands into the $\Ainf$ Stasheff relations; each
relation is Stokes' theorem on $\FM_n(\C)$.  At $n=2$, $Q$ is a
derivation of $m_2$; at $n=3$, strict associativity fails by the
homotopy $m_3$ (the integral over the Stasheff interval
$K_3 = [0,1]$).  The $m_k$ are the unique solution of a tree
bootstrap (the resolvent principle,
Theorem~\ref{thm:resolvent-principle}), each a sum over $C_{k-1}$
planar binary trees.

On cohomology $H^\bullet(A, Q)$, $m_{k\ge 3}$ vanishes by
contractibility of $\Conf_k^{<}(\R)$, and $m_2$ decomposes into a
commutative product (regular part) and a $\lambda$-bracket
(singular part), forming a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cohomology_PVA}): Jacobi from three-face Stokes
on $\FM_3(\C)$, skew-symmetry from the exchange automorphism,
Leibniz from regular-part factorisation.  The quantization
problem inverts the descent; the obstruction lives in chiral
Hochschild cohomology $\HH^3_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$, and the Quillen
equivalence of bar-cobar (Theorem~\ref{thm:homotopy-Koszul})
gives essential uniqueness of the lift.

\section*{XI.\quad The bulk-boundary-line triangle}

A holomorphic--topological theory on $\C \times \R_{\ge 0}$
produces three algebraic objects: bulk local operators $\Abulk$
on the interior, boundary local operators $\Bbound$ on
$\{0\} \times \C$, and line operators $\cC_{\mathrm{line}}$ along
$\R_+ \times \{0\}$.  Unconditionally, $\Abulk$ identifies with
chiral Hochschild cochains of $\Bbound$.  On the boundary-linear
exact sector and on the chirally Koszul locus,
$\Abulk \simeq \Zder(\Bbound)
= \RHom_{\Bbound\text{-}\Bbound}(\Bbound, \Bbound)$ and
$\cC_{\mathrm{line}} \simeq \cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}\text{-mod}$.
For Heisenberg, $\Zder(\cH_k) = \cH_k$ and
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}} = Y(\mathfrak{u}(1))$.  For Kac--Moody at
$k = -h^\vee$, the bulk is the Feigin--Frenkel centre
$\mathfrak{z}(\widehat{\fg}_{-h^\vee})$ (the algebra of
${}^L\!G$-opers) and the line side is $\Ydg(\fg)$-modules.

Two parallel line operators interact through the bulk, producing
a meromorphic braiding $R_{L_1, L_2}(z)$ in $z = z_1 - z_2$;
three lines give the Yang--Baxter equation (Theorem~\ref{thm:YBE})
from the same $\FM_3(\C)$ Stokes mechanism as PVA Jacobi, one
the closed-colour projection and one the open-colour projection
of the $n = 3$ Stasheff identity.  For Heisenberg,
$R(z) = e^{k\hbar/z}$.  For Kac--Moody on evaluation modules,
$R(z)$ agrees with the quantum group $R$-matrix of $U_q(\fg)$ at
$q = e^{i\pi/(k+h^\vee)}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:affine-monodromy-identification}), with
classical limit $r_{\widehat{\fg}_k}(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ (vanishing
at $k = 0$).

\section*{XII.\quad Curved genus expansion}

At genus $g \ge 1$ the bar differential is curved:
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\Bbound) \cdot \omega_g$ (Vol~I,
Theorem~D, \textsc{uniform-weight} at $g \ge 2$, with
cross-channel correction $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ in the
multi-weight regime).  In the boundary-bulk interpretation
$\kappa(\cA)$ is the holographic central charge: for a chiral
boundary algebra dual to a bulk gravitational theory,
$\kappa(\cA) = 3\ell_{\mathrm{AdS}}/(2 G_N)$ is the bulk Newton
constant read off at the boundary VOA's level.  For Heisenberg
this reduces to $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$, the abelian Chern--Simons
level; for Virasoro, $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$ is the bulk
central charge in the Brown--Henneaux form
$c = 3\ell/(2G)$.  The shadow obstruction tower
$\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r \ge 2}$ is then the holographic correction
hierarchy: tree-level $\kappa$ is the leading bulk coupling, and
successive $S_r$ are the higher-loop corrections organised by
shadow depth.  The nonvanishing of the square is how the
genus tower enters the local bar-cobar picture.  The
transgression algebra $B_\Theta$ absorbs the curvature by
adjoining a degree-one generator $\eta$ with
$\eta b = (-1)^{|b|} b\eta$ and $d\eta = \Theta$.  The secondary
anomaly $u := \eta^2$ controls the genus-$g$ Clifford completion
$\mathfrak{G}_g(B_\Theta)$: away from $u = 0$,
$\mathfrak{G}_g[\eta^{-2}]
\cong \operatorname{Mat}_{2^g}(B_\Theta[\eta^{-2}])$ (higher
genus is Morita-invisible); on $u = 0$,
$\mathfrak{G}_g/(\eta^2) \cong B/(\Theta) \otimes \Lambda^{2g+1}$
(exterior-algebra degeneration).  The full development is in
Chapter~\ref{app:anomaly-completed-topological-holography}.

The modular bar differential $D = \sum_{g \ge 0} \hbar^g D^{(g)}$
is the Feynman transform of the modular operad
(Theorem~\ref{thm:formal-genus-expansion}); the canonical MC
element $\delta_\cA = D - D^{(0)}$ lives in the positive-genus
filtration, and $\mathrm{Ob}_g$, $[\varpi_g(\cA)]$ assemble into
the modular PVA quantization theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-bar}).

The leading coefficients of $F_g$ (\textsc{uniform-weight}) encode
soft graviton data: $F_1 = \kappa/24$ reproduces the Weinberg soft
graviton theorem at tree level, and $F_2 = 7\kappa^2/5760$ matches
the Cachazo--Strominger one-loop soft factor.
The genus expansion has a Borel singularity at
$g \sim 1/\kappa$; the Stokes phenomenon across this singularity
reproduces the Page curve for black hole information, with the Page
time set by the complementarity constant $\kappa + \kappa' = 13$
(Virasoro).
Analytic continuation $\kappa \to -|\kappa|$ replaces the
convergent genus tower with a de~Sitter entropy formula: the
Gibbons--Hawking entropy $S_{\mathrm{dS}} = \pi \ell^2/G$ emerges
as the leading saddle of the continued tower, and the horizon
thermodynamics of the static patch follow from negative-$\kappa$
curvature of the bar differential.

\emph{Does the genus-$0$ Lagrangian $\cL_\cA^{(0)}$ extend to a

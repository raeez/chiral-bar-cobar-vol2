\section{The problem}

The modular operad $\mathcal{O}^{A_\infty\text{-ch}}$ governs
$A_\infty$-algebras in $E_1$-chiral algebras.  Its clutching maps
are regularised parallel transports of the Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov--Bernard
(KZB) connection on configuration spaces of marked points on
Riemann surfaces.  Composition associativity of the clutching maps
follows from flatness of the KZB connection together with regularity
of singularities at the boundary divisors of $\Mbar_{g,n}$.

\begin{theorem}[Proved in the main manuscript]
For affine $V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item At genus~$0$ and all non-critical levels~$k$, the KZ connection
  on $\mathcal{M}_{0,n}$ has logarithmic (regular) singularities at
  every boundary divisor of $\Mbar_{0,n}$.  Composition associativity
  holds unconditionally.
\item At integrable level $k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$ and all genera,
  the KZB connection extends with regular singularities to
  $\Mbar_{g,n}$ by Kazhdan--Lusztig regularity and
  Tsuchiya--Ueno--Yamada factorisation.  Composition associativity
  holds at all genera.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{conjecture}[The Stokes gap]
At generic non-integral level $k \notin \mathbb{Z}$ and
genus $g \ge 1$, the KZB connection on $\mathcal{M}_{g,n}$ has
regular singularities at all boundary divisors of $\Mbar_{g,n}$.
\end{conjecture}

If this conjecture holds, composition associativity of the modular
operad extends to all levels and all genera, closing the sole
remaining gap in the operadic verification.

\section{What is known}

At genus~$0$, the connection form
$\omega = \sum_{i<j} \Omega_{ij}\, d\log(z_i - z_j)$ is manifestly
$d$-log.  All singularities at $\Mbar_{0,n}$ boundary divisors are
logarithmic for any~$k$.

At genus~$1$ (elliptic KZB, Bernard 1988), the connection involves
the Weierstrass $\wp$-function and Eisenstein series.  As
$\tau \to i\infty$ (cusp), $\theta_1(z,\tau) \to \sin(\pi z)$,
which has a simple zero at $z = 0$.  The connection form degenerates
to the rational KZ form, which is logarithmic.  This suggests
regularity at the genus-$1$ boundary for all~$k$.

At genus~$\ge 2$, the KZB connection involves the period matrix
$\Omega_{jk}$ and a basis of holomorphic differentials.  The
behaviour at boundary divisors (where handles pinch) is controlled
by the degeneration of the period matrix.  The Fay trisecant
identity governs the degeneration and produces logarithmic
singularities for the Szeg\H{o} kernel.

\section{Attack path}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Verify regularity at the genus-$1$ cusp for all~$k$ by
  explicit analysis of the Bernard connection in the
  $q$-expansion ($q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$).
\item At genus~$\ge 2$, use the plumbing-fixture coordinates near
  each boundary divisor of $\Mbar_{g,n}$.  In these coordinates,
  the connection form should be expressible as
  $\Omega\, d\log(t)$ where $t$ is the plumbing parameter,
  giving logarithmic singularity.
\item Alternatively: use Etingof's work on KZ connections at
  complex (non-integer) levels, which establishes analytic
  continuation of the monodromy representation.
\item If regularity holds: the regularised parallel transport is
  well-defined across all boundary strata, and
  composition associativity follows by the same
  Riemann--Hilbert argument as the integrable case.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Consequences}

Closing the Stokes gap would give the \emph{full} modular operad
$\mathcal{O}^{A_\infty\text{-ch}}$ for all affine Kac--Moody
algebras at all non-critical levels, with all five axioms
(product decomposition, $\pi_1$ well-definedness, composition,
equivariance, unitality) proved.  Combined with the scalar
monodromy result for Heisenberg, this would give a complete
operadic foundation for the genus tower of the bar-cobar machine.

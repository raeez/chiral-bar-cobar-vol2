\title{The Stokes Gap: KZB Regularity at Generic Level\\
and the Completion of the Modular Operad}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We formulate the precise analytic condition---regularity of
singularities of the KZB connection at boundary divisors of
$\Mbar_{g,n}$ for generic non-integral level~$k$---whose
resolution would complete the modular operad
$\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$ at all levels and all genera.
We prove the condition at genus~$0$ (all levels) and at all
genera for integrable levels, identify the precise obstruction
at higher genus and generic level, and outline an attack via
plumbing-fixture analysis of the KZB connection form.
\end{abstract}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Introduction: the last axiom}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The modular operad $\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$ governing
$\Ainf$-algebras in $E_1$-chiral algebras has five axioms.
Four are proved unconditionally:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item genus-$0$ product decomposition;
\item $\pi_1(\Sigma_g)$ well-definedness (KZB flatness);
\item equivariance under $\mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma)$
  (quasi-triangularity $+$ Yang--Baxter);
\item unitality (vacuum axiom).
\end{enumerate}
The fifth---\emph{composition associativity}---is proved at genus~$0$
for all non-critical levels and at all genera for integrable
levels.  The sole remaining gap is composition at generic
non-integral~$k$ and genus~$g \ge 1$.

\begin{theorem}[Target theorem]
\label{thm:target}
Let $\fg$ be a simple Lie algebra and $k \notin \{-h^\vee\}
\cup \mathbb{Q}_{<0}$ a generic non-integral level.
The clutching maps of $\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$ for
$V_k(\fg)$ satisfy composition associativity at all genera.
\end{theorem}

The theorem reduces to a single analytic input
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:regularity}).

\section{The KZB connection and its singularities}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{The connection}
The KZB connection on $\Conf_n(\Sigma_g)$ is
\[
\nabla^{\KZB} = d - \sum_{i<j} \Omega_{ij}\, \omega_{ij}
  - \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{\alpha=1}^g
    H_i^\alpha\, d\Omega_\alpha + \cdots
\]
where $\Omega_{ij} = k \cdot C_{ij}$ is the Casimir insertion,
$\omega_{ij}$ is the Szeg\H{o} kernel on $\Sigma_g$,
$\Omega_\alpha$ is the period matrix, and the ellipsis denotes
Eisenstein-type corrections at genus~$\ge 2$.  The connection
is flat ($(\nabla^{\KZB})^2 = 0$) for all~$k$.

\subsection{Regularity at genus~$0$}

\begin{proposition}[Proved]
At genus~$0$, the KZ connection form
$\omega = \sum_{i<j} \Omega_{ij}\, d\log(z_i - z_j)$
is manifestly logarithmic.  All singularities at boundary
divisors of $\Mbar_{0,n}$ are regular for any~$k$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The $d\log$ form has a simple pole along each diagonal
$\{z_i = z_j\}$.  On the Fulton--MacPherson compactification,
the blow-up coordinates give a smooth extension with
logarithmic poles along exceptional divisors.  The Casimir
$\Omega_{ij}$ is independent of~$k$ (it scales linearly,
which does not affect the logarithmic structure).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Regularity at integrable level}

\begin{proposition}[Proved; Kazhdan--Lusztig, TUY]
At integrable level $k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$, the KZB
connection extends with regular singularities to $\Mbar_{g,n}$
for all~$g$.
\end{proposition}

This is the content of the Kazhdan--Lusztig regularity theorem
(formulated for the KZ connection on $\Mbar_{0,n}$, extended to
KZB on $\Mbar_{g,n}$ by Tsuchiya--Ueno--Yamada and
Beilinson--Drinfeld).

\section{The conjecture}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{conjecture}[KZB regularity at generic level]
\label{conj:regularity}
For generic $k \notin \mathbb{Z}$ and any $g \ge 1$, the KZB
connection on $\mathcal{M}_{g,n}$ has regular singularities at
every boundary divisor of $\Mbar_{g,n}$.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}
``Regular singularity'' means: in plumbing-fixture coordinates
$t \to 0$ near a boundary divisor, the connection form is
$\Omega\, d\log(t) + (\text{holomorphic})$.  The monodromy
around $t = 0$ is then $\exp(2\pi i \Omega)$, a well-defined
operator.
\end{remark}

\section{The Annals-grade proof spine}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:target} (contingent on
Conjecture~\ref{conj:regularity}) has four steps.

\subsection*{Step 1: Plumbing-fixture analysis}

Near each boundary divisor $D_\Gamma \subset \Mbar_{g,n}$
indexed by a stable graph~$\Gamma$, introduce plumbing-fixture
coordinates $t_e$ for each edge $e \in E(\Gamma)$.  The
degeneration $t_e \to 0$ pinches the curve at the node
corresponding to~$e$.

\begin{lemma}[Key analytic lemma]
In plumbing coordinates, the KZB connection form decomposes as
\[
\nabla^{\KZB} = \sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)} \Omega_e\, d\log(t_e)
  + \nabla^{\mathrm{smooth}}
\]
where $\Omega_e = k \cdot C_e$ is the Casimir at the node~$e$
and $\nabla^{\mathrm{smooth}}$ extends holomorphically across
$\{t_e = 0\}$.
\end{lemma}

This is the heart of the paper.  The proof requires:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item Fay's trisecant identity for the degeneration of the
  Szeg\H{o} kernel: as a handle pinches,
  $\omega_{ij} \to \omega_{ij}^{(0)} + C_e\, d\log(t_e) + O(t_e)$
  where $\omega^{(0)}$ is the Szeg\H{o} kernel on the
  normalised curve.
\item The period matrix degeneration:
  $\Omega_{\alpha\beta} \to \Omega^{(0)}_{\alpha\beta}
  + \delta_{\alpha,g}\delta_{\beta,g} \cdot \frac{1}{2\pi i}\log(t_e)
  + O(1)$ (separating node) or the Fay--Mumford formula
  (non-separating).
\item Control of the Eisenstein corrections at genus~$\ge 2$:
  these involve sums over the lattice $H_1(\Sigma_g, \mathbb{Z})$
  and must remain bounded as $t_e \to 0$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Step 2: Riemann--Hilbert correspondence}

With regular singularities established, the Riemann--Hilbert
correspondence gives a bijection between flat connections with
regular singularities on $\Mbar_{g,n}$ and monodromy
representations of $\pi_1(\mathcal{M}_{g,n})$.  The monodromy
around boundary divisors is $\exp(2\pi i \Omega_e)$.

\subsection*{Step 3: Path-independence of regularised parallel transport}

The regularised parallel transport (Deligne's canonical
extension) along any path in $\Mbar_{g,n}$ is well-defined
and depends only on the homotopy class of the path (relative
to the boundary divisors).  The composition of clutching maps
corresponds to a composition of paths, and path-independence
gives associativity.

\subsection*{Step 4: Mac Lane coherence}

The pentagon identity for the KZ/Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_{\KZB}$ and the hexagon identities generate all
coherence relations by Mac Lane's coherence theorem.  These
are already proved in the main manuscript.

\section{Storyboard: from here to the theorem}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Month 1--2.}  Genus-$1$ cusp analysis.
  The Bernard elliptic KZB in the $q$-expansion
  ($q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$) as $\tau \to i\infty$.
  Verify the connection form is $\Omega\, d\log(q) +
  (\text{holomorphic in }q)$ for all~$k$.
  This closes the genus-$1$ Stokes gap.

\item \textbf{Month 3--4.}  Genus-$2$ separating degeneration.
  In plumbing coordinates for the separating node,
  decompose the genus-$2$ KZB into contributions from the
  two genus-$1$ components.  The Fay trisecant identity
  controls the Szeg\H{o} kernel degeneration.

\item \textbf{Month 5--6.}  Genus-$2$ non-separating degeneration
  and the general inductive step.
  Use the Fay--Mumford period matrix degeneration formula
  for non-separating nodes.  Establish the Key Analytic
  Lemma by induction on the number of nodes.

\item \textbf{Month 7--8.}  Assembly and verification.
  Combine the analytic lemma with the Riemann--Hilbert
  correspondence and Mac Lane coherence.  Write the
  complete proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:target}.
  Verify against the Heisenberg scalar case (where
  regularity is automatic) and the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$
  integrable case (where Kazhdan--Lusztig applies).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Consequences}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Resolution of the Stokes gap gives the full modular operad
$\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$ for all affine Kac--Moody
algebras at all non-critical levels, with all five axioms
proved.  This completes the operadic foundation for the
genus tower of the bar-cobar machine and establishes the
$E_3$-topological structure as a theorem of algebra
(not dependent on case-by-case gauge-theory input at
each genus).

\title{Irregular-singular KZB Regularity at Generic Non-integral Level\\
and Boundary-Formal-Type Composition}
\author{Raeez Lorgat}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The modular operad $\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$ governing
$\Ainf$-algebras in $E_1$-chiral algebras has five axioms. The
composition axiom at generic non-integral level and higher genus is
conditional on a compatible boundary formal type on every stable-graph
boundary stratum.  Edgewise Stokes data are not sufficient: two-edge
corners require a mixed-channel flatness condition.  Under that
hypothesis, the Jimbo--Miwa--Ueno irregular-singular framework supplies
Stokes-sector composition of flat sections at generic non-integral
level.  No general $\mathcal{W}_N$ Poincar\'e-rank or Stokes-ray count
is asserted here.
\end{abstract}

\section{The composition axiom}

The modular operad $\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$ has five axioms.
Four are proved unconditionally:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item genus-$0$ product decomposition,
\item $\pi_1(\Sigma_g)$ well-definedness (KZB flatness),
\item $\mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma)$-equivariance (quasi-triangularity plus
Yang--Baxter),
\item unitality (vacuum axiom).
\end{enumerate}
The fifth axiom, \emph{composition associativity under clutching}, is
unconditional at genus~$0$ for every non-critical level and at all
genera for integrable levels (Kazhdan--Lusztig and its KZB extension
by Tsuchiya--Ueno--Yamada, Beilinson--Drinfeld). At generic
non-integral level $k$ and genus $g \ge 1$, the composition law is the
following Stokes-sector theorem.

\begin{theorem}[Irregular-singular KZB regularity]
\label{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-standalone}
Let $A$ be a chirally Koszul algebra at non-critical non-integral
level $k$ with $c(k)$ generic. Assume that, at every boundary point
$p \in \partial\Mbar_{g,n}$ of every stable-graph stratum, including
two-edge corners, the KZB connection admits compatible irregular
formal types and the mixed-channel Stokes curvature vanishes. Then
$\nabla^{\KZB}$ has:
\begin{enumerate}
\item irregularity slopes rational with denominators given by the
finite ramification indices in \(q_e=t_e^{N_e}\);
\item after finite ramification, formal type
\(\Lambda_p(t)=\sum_{m=1}^d A_m t^{-m}\), where \(d\) is pole order
in the ramified coordinate, determines the Stokes directions and
Stokes groups;
\item sectorial asymptotic expansions of flat sections, canonical
in each Stokes sector, with Deligne--Malgrange Stokes matrices
\(\{S_\sigma\}\) for transitions; these matrices are analytic
Stokes data of the connection, not determined by the formal type;
\item Jimbo--Miwa functional relations for
$\{S_\sigma\}$ under the modular fibration
$\Mbar_{g,n} \to \Mbar_{g,n-1}$;
\item well-defined clutching composition at $p$ via Stokes-sector
composition of flat sections, coherent under further clutching.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

This is Vol.~II Theorem~\ref*{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-regularity}
in Chapter~\ref*{ch:curved-dunn-higher-genus}.
Jimbo--Miwa--Ueno isomonodromic technology, Boalch's Stokes-space
formalism, and the Kohno--Felder elliptic KZB connection at
genus~$\ge 1$ are the external inputs.  The boundary formal-type
compatibility must be supplied beyond edgewise Stokes classification.

\section{KZB and its singularities}

The KZB connection on $\Conf_n(\Sigma_g)$ reads
\[
\nabla^{\KZB}
\;=\; d
\;-\; \sum_{i<j} \Omega_{ij}\,\omega_{ij}
\;-\; \sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{\alpha=1}^{g} H_i^\alpha\,d\Omega_\alpha
\;+\; \cdots,
\]
with $\Omega_{ij}/(k+h^\vee)$ the KZB connection residue in the
standard normalisation,
$\omega_{ij}$ the Szeg\H{o} kernel on $\Sigma_g$, $\Omega_\alpha$ the
period matrix, and the ellipsis denoting Eisenstein-type corrections
at genus~$\ge 2$. The bar collision-residue convention
\(k\,\Omega/z\) is a different normalisation. The connection is flat
in its non-critical domain.

\subsection{Genus-$0$ regularity}

\begin{proposition}[Genus-$0$ regularity]
\label{prop:kz-g0}
The KZ connection form $\omega = \sum_{i<j} \Omega_{ij}\, d\log(z_i -
z_j)$ is manifestly logarithmic; singularities at boundary divisors
of $\Mbar_{0,n}$ are regular for every~$k$.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Integrable level}

\begin{proposition}[Integrable-level regularity]
\label{prop:kl-tuy-regular}
At $k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ the KZB connection extends with
regular singularities to $\Mbar_{g,n}$ for all~$g$.
\end{proposition}

Kazhdan--Lusztig plus TUY plus Beilinson--Drinfeld. The
condition that made this work was integer denominators and
semisimple fusion.

\subsection{Generic non-integral level}

For $k \notin \mathbb{Z}$, generic $c(k)$, the Kazhdan--Lusztig
regularity argument fails at $g \geq 1$: the KZB connection
develops an irregular singularity along each boundary divisor.
Theorem~\ref{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-standalone} replaces
Kazhdan--Lusztig regular-singular parallel transport by the
Stokes-sector composition.

\section{The Jimbo--Miwa framework}

\paragraph{Newton polygon at a boundary point.}
At a stable-graph stratum with node $p$ and transverse coordinate
$\hbar_p$, the connection near $p$ reads
\[
\nabla^{\KZB}
\;=\; d
\;-\; \frac{A_1}{\hbar_p}\,d\hbar_p
\;-\; \sum_{j \geq 2} \frac{A_j}{\hbar_p^j}\,d\hbar_p
\;-\; \omega_{\mathrm{reg}},
\]
with $A_j$ level-dependent endomorphisms of the tensor product of
evaluation modules. The Newton polygon is the lower convex hull of
$\{(j, \mathrm{ord}(A_j))\}$; its slopes are the irregularity slopes.
The residue $A_1$ is the quadratic Casimir
$\Omega_{\mathrm{node}}/(k + h^\vee)$. A simple node has pole order
one, is regular singular, and has irregular slope \(0\);
higher-order terms $A_j$ arise from Fay's trisecant identity applied
to the degenerating period matrix, with orders controlled by
distance on the dual graph.

\paragraph{Stokes structure.}
For generic non-integral~$k$, the spectrum of $A_j$ has no
integer-difference resonances; the formal type determines the
Stokes directions and Stokes groups. The matrices
\(S_\sigma \in \mathrm{Sto}_\sigma(\Lambda)\) are analytic Stokes
data of the connection, locally constant under isomonodromic
deformation.

\paragraph{Jimbo--Miwa functional relations.}
The isomonodromic deformation along the fibration
$\Mbar_{g,n} \to \Mbar_{g,n-1}$ is governed by Jimbo--Miwa
(1981); the Stokes matrices $\{S_\sigma\}$ are locally constant on
moduli up to Stokes wall-crossings (Berk--Nevins--Roberts).

\paragraph{Clutching composition.}
Near a node the clutching map
$\mu_p\colon \fg^A_{\mathrm{mod}}(g_1, n_1)
\otimes \fg^A_{\mathrm{mod}}(g_2, n_2)
\to \fg^A_{\mathrm{mod}}(g_1 + g_2, n_1 + n_2 - 2)$
is the Stokes-sector composition of flat sections on either side of
the node. Associativity under further clutching reduces to the
Jimbo--Miwa Schlesinger system.

\section{The $\mathcal{W}_N$ depth datum}

\begin{proposition}[$\mathcal{W}_N$ depth is independent of a KZB Stokes count]
\label{prop:wn-stokes-count}
The integer $2N-2$ measures the depth gap between the Virasoro
generator of weight~$2$ and the highest principal generator of
$\mathcal{W}_N$.  The Poincar\'e rank of the KZB connection at a
boundary divisor is separate data.  A KZB Stokes count requires an
explicit irregular normal form and a Stokes-sector analysis for that
normal form.
\end{proposition}

Thus this standalone uses the $\mathcal{W}_N$ depth only as a
representation-theoretic datum.  It does not assert a universal Stokes
ray formula.

\section{Consequences}

Under the boundary formal-type hypothesis, generic non-integral
composition is Stokes-sector composition.  It differs from
regular-singular monodromy at integrable level but agrees with it on
the overlap where both are defined.  The consequences used by the
modular surface are therefore conditional on
Theorem~\ref{thm:irregular-singular-kzb-standalone}:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Curved-Dunn \(H^2\)-vanishing:} Stokes-sector composition
does not prove curved-Dunn vanishing; the vanishing is an algebraic
input from modular-bootstrap acyclicity plus the degree-\(2\)
comparison map.
\item \emph{Modular-operad composition:} the composition axiom at
generic non-integral level is supplied by the conditional
Stokes-sector theorem.
\item \emph{R-twisted clutching:} R-twisted clutching uses the
algebraic curved-Dunn comparison and the analytic Stokes-sector
composition as separate inputs.
\item \emph{Composition axiom:} the clutching composition law at
generic non-integral level is Stokes-sector composition.
\end{itemize}

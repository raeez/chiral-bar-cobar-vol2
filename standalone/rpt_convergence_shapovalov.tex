\begin{abstract}
Level-by-level rationality of the bar-cobar spectral $R$-matrix
for $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ is proved (bar-cobar $\Ainf$ operations plus
Shapovalov inversion: poles at the Kac determinant zero locus).
Convergence of the level sum on the principal series, reducing
meromorphicity in the spectral parameters to Eberhardt's
difference-Galois uniqueness theorem, is governed by the growth
of the smallest Shapovalov eigenvalue. Numerical data through
level~$8$ are consistent with a quadratic lower bound; a rigorous
lower bound would identify the bar-cobar spectral $R$-matrix with
the Ponsot--Teschner fusion kernel on the principal series at
irrational~$b^2$. The full identification remains conjectural; the
standalone articulates the one remaining analytic input and
situates it inside the MC3 five-family mechanism of Vol.~I
Chapter~\ref*{chap:mc3-five-family-platonic}. In the five-family
table the Virasoro spectral $R$-matrix is the class-$M$ face of the
Shapovalov-witness principle carried by reflection-equation
Shapovalov on types~B/C/D; the rank-$1$ Baxter hyperplane that
governed earlier attempts is retracted as a type-$A$ rational
artifact (Proposition~\ref*{prop:baxter-retraction-type-A-artifact}).
\end{abstract}

\section{Statement and reduction}

\begin{conjecture}[Conditional identification with PT; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:rpt-conditional}
Assume Conjecture~\ref{conj:shapovalov-growth} below
(Shapovalov eigenvalue lower bound on the principal series).
Then for $b^2 \notin \mathbb{Q}$ and
$c \in \mathbb{C} \setminus (-\infty, 1]$, the bar-cobar spectral
$R$-matrix of $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ equals the Ponsot--Teschner fusion
kernel on the principal series.
\end{conjecture}

The reduction has two components.

\begin{proposition}[Level-by-level rationality; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:level-rationality}
At fixed levels $(N_1, N_2)$, each matrix element of the bar-cobar
spectral $R^{\mathrm{bar},(N_1,N_2)}$ is a rational function of
$h_i = \alpha_i(Q - \alpha_i)$ with poles on the null-vector locus
$h_i = h_{r,s}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
$\Ainf$ operations $m_k$ are polynomial in spectral parameters and
central charge; evaluation on the Verma basis is polynomial in $h_i$;
homotopy transfer introduces Gram (Shapovalov) inverses, contributing
poles at the Kac determinant zeros $h = h_{r,s}$. At each finite
level the number of operations is finite, and rationality is
preserved.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Eberhardt uniqueness; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:eberhardt-uniqueness}
A meromorphic solution of the shift equations satisfied by
$F^{\mathrm{PT}}$ on the principal series for irrational~$b^2$ is
unique, and therefore equal to the Ponsot--Teschner kernel.
\end{proposition}

The bar-cobar spectral $R$-matrix satisfies those shift equations by
pentagon plus degenerate-braiding matching; meromorphicity reduces
to convergence of the level sum
$R^{\mathrm{bar}} = \sum_{N=0}^\infty R^{(N)}$.

\section{The convergence question}

\begin{conjecture}[Shapovalov eigenvalue growth;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:shapovalov-growth}
On the principal series $h = Q^2/4 + P^2$ with irrational~$b^2$,
the smallest eigenvalue of the level-$N$ Virasoro Gram matrix
satisfies $\lambda_{\min}(G_N) \ge C \cdot N^\beta$ for some
$C, \beta > 0$.
\end{conjecture}

At $c = 25$, $h = 2$ ($b = 1$, $P = 1$) the numerical evidence is:
\[
\begin{array}{c|cccccccc}
N & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 7 & 8 \\
\hline
\lambda_{\min} & 4 & 14.8 & 49.2 & 118.5 & 245.4 & 408.9 & 668.8 &
975.7 \\
\lambda_{\min}/p(N) & 4.0 & 7.4 & 16.4 & 23.7 & 35.1 & 37.2 & 44.6 &
44.4
\end{array}
\]
The eigenvalue grows monotonically and $\lambda_{\min}/p(N)$
stabilises around~$44$: consistent with a power law and
$\beta \approx 2$.

\section{Evidence spine for Conjecture~\ref{conj:rpt-conditional}}

\paragraph{Step 1: Shapovalov lower bound.}
Prove Conjecture~\ref{conj:shapovalov-growth} by analysing
$G_N(h, c)$ on the principal series.

\begin{lemma}[Positivity amplification]
On the principal series, $h - h_{r,s} = P^2 + P_{r,s}^2 > 0$.
The Kac determinant $\det G_N \ge \prod_{rs \le N}
(P^2 + P_{r,s}^2)^{p(N-rs)}$ grows super-exponentially. The condition
number $\kappa(G_N) = \lambda_{\max}/\lambda_{\min}$ grows at most
exponentially from the norm estimate
$\|L_n\|_{\mathrm{op}} \sim n$. Therefore
$\lambda_{\min} \ge \det G_N / \lambda_{\max}^{p(N)-1}$, giving a
lower bound.
\end{lemma}

\paragraph{Step 2: Numerator bound.}
The level-$N$ contribution to the $R$-matrix involves iterated
$\Ainf$ wheel integrals; the crude bound at degree $k$ is the
$\FM_k(\C)$ volume times the OPE coefficient bound. The total
numerator grows at most like $C^N \cdot N!$ ($N$~mode contractions).

\paragraph{Step 3: Refined bound via tree structure.}
The crude $N!$ is too weak: $N!/N^2$ diverges super-exponentially.
The actual numerator is bounded by the tree structure of the
$\Ainf$ operations rather than the worst-case $N!$. Cancellations
between Feynman diagrams at order~$N$ (wheel integrals partially
cancel by the $\Ainf$ relations) are expected to yield
exponential growth $C^N$ rather than factorial. A direct bound via
the Stasheff polytope volume of $K_N$, weighted by the OPE
coefficient at the maximal-degree vertex, is the target.

\paragraph{Step 4: Alternative route via Roussillon.}
Roussillon (arXiv:2405.09325) gives a convergent series
representation of $F^{\mathrm{PT}}$ at all irrational~$c$. If the
bar-cobar $R$-matrix matches this series term by term (which would
follow from level-by-level rationality plus matching at degenerate
points), meromorphicity follows from Roussillon's convergence
analysis.

\section{Position inside the MC3 five-family mechanism}

The bar-cobar spectral $R$-matrix sits inside the five-family thick
generation theorem
(Theorem~\ref*{thm:mc3-evaluation-core-five-family}) as the
class-$M$ face of the Shapovalov-witness principle. The five
families and the witnesses that force thick generation of the
evaluation-generated core are:

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Family} & \textbf{Shapovalov witness} & \textbf{Thick-generation status} \\
\hline
Type~$A$ rational Yangian & asymptotic prefundamentals (Hernandez--Jimbo) &
unconditional \\
Types~B/C/D twisted Yangian & reflection-equation Shapovalov
($\operatorname{sdet}K(u)$) & unconditional \\

\hline
Virasoro bar-cobar (this note) & Shapovalov growth on principal
series & conjectural
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The Virasoro class-$M$ face is the sole remaining conjectural row.
The five-family mechanism retires the rank-$1$ Baxter hyperplane as a
\emph{type-$A$ rational artifact}
(Proposition~\ref*{prop:baxter-retraction-type-A-artifact}): the
Baxter constraint $b = a - \tfrac12$ is visible only in the
rational-form presentation of $Y_\hbar(\mathrm{sl}_N)$ and does not
persist under degeneration from any other chart. In the present
Virasoro setting the analogous codimension-$1$ locus is the Kac
determinant zero locus $h = h_{r,s}$, which is globally intrinsic
(not chart-dependent); the corresponding Shapovalov witness is the
growth of~$\lambda_{\min}$ rather than a singular-vector
construction.

\begin{remark}[Silent non-coverage]
\label{rem:silent-non-coverage}
The MC3 full-DK-generation theorem
(Theorem~\ref*{thm:mc3-full-DK-conjectural}) is silent on the
following families outside the evaluation-generated core: logarithmic
$\mathcal{W}(p)$-algebras (Zhu bounded Massey is false per Gurarie
1993 + Flohr 1996; tempering itself is open), $N = 2$ superconformal
algebras, cosets (beyond irrational cosets), non-rational lattice
VOAs, and roots of unity. The conditional Conjecture~\ref{conj:rpt-conditional}
applies to $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ at irrational~$b^2$ and $c \not\in
(-\infty, 1]$, which excludes the $\mathcal{W}(p)$ class entirely.
\end{remark}

\section{Storyboard}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item Extend the Shapovalov computation to $N = 12$--$15$ with
arbitrary-precision arithmetic; fit $\lambda_{\min}(G_N)$ to a power
law and test at multiple principal-series points.
\item Prove the Shapovalov lower bound via the block structure of
$G_N$ (Virasoro descendants partition into $L_{-1}$-chains) and
interlacing under partition refinement.
\item Replace the crude $N!$ numerator bound by the Stasheff-polytope
bound, using the $\Ainf$ relations to organise the cancellations.

the principal series. Invoke

$R^{\mathrm{bar}} = F^{\mathrm{PT}}$.
\end{enumerate}

\section{The problem}

Classical Dunn additivity: $E_2 \simeq E_1 \otimes E_1$
(Boardman--Vogt tensor product).  At genus $g \ge 1$, where
the bar differential satisfies $d^2 = \kappa \cdot \omega_g \neq 0$
(curvature), the two-factor decomposition is obstructed by
$R$-matrix monodromy.

\begin{proposition}[Proved: genus-$1$ twisted tensor product]
At genus~$1$, the modular bar complex decomposes as a
twisted tensor product:
\[
\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}},\, A)
\big|_{g=1}
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}},\, A)
\big|_{g=1}
\;\otimes^{\mathrm{Mon}(R)}\;
\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}},\, A)
\]
where $\otimes^{\mathrm{Mon}(R)}$ is the Eilenberg--Zilber twisted
tensor product with twisting cochain built from $R$-matrix monodromy.
\end{proposition}

\section{The two-complex distinction}

The genus-$g$ obstruction class $\mathrm{Ob}_g$ lives in two
different complexes:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Modular-bootstrap complex}
  $(\mathfrak{g}^A_{\mathrm{mod}}, d_{\Theta^{(0)}})$:
  $H^2 = 0$ at every genus $g \ge 1$ (proved).  Every $\mathrm{Ob}_g$
  is exact, and the genus-$g$ partition function is uniquely
  determined by lower-genus data.

\item \textbf{Curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex}
  $H^2\bigl(\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}}),\,
  \mathrm{gr}^g\,\mathbf{B}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})),
  d_0\bigr)$:
  \textbf{open}.  No proof that $H^2 = 0$.
\end{enumerate}

If a natural map between these complexes can be constructed---a
\emph{bridge}---then curved Dunn at all genera follows from the
already-proved modular-bootstrap exactness.

\section{Genus-$2$ evidence}

For $V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$: the separating-shell forcing term is
nonzero ($C(A) \neq 0$ for class~L).  In the modular-bootstrap
complex, this is still exact ($H^2 = 0$).  In the curved-Dunn
complex, exactness is unknown.

For Heisenberg: scalar monodromy simplifies but does not eliminate
the loop-squared contribution.  The genus-$2$ obstruction reduces
to a scalar computation.

\section{Attack path}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Construct the bridge: a chain map from the curved-Dunn
  twisting-cochain complex to the modular-bootstrap complex that
  intertwines the respective $d_0$-differentials.
\item The bridge should send the twisted Eilenberg--Zilber data
  (twisting cochain $\tau$, Maurer--Cartan equation) to the
  genus-tower recursion data ($\Theta^{(g)}$, MC recursion).
\item If the bridge is a quasi-isomorphism: $H^2 = 0$ transfers,
  and the twisted K\"unneth holds at all genera.
\item Model-structure approach: use the Bellier-Mill\`es--Drummond-Cole
  model category for curved operads to construct a Quillen
  equivalence between the two obstruction theories.
\end{enumerate}

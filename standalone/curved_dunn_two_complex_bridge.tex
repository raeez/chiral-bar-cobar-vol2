\title{Curved Dunn Additivity:\\
Bridging the Modular-Bootstrap and Twisting-Cochain Complexes}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Classical Dunn additivity decomposes $E_2 \simeq E_1 \otimes E_1$.
At genus~$g \ge 1$, where the bar differential is curved
($d^2 = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$), the decomposition is obstructed
by $R$-matrix monodromy.  We prove the genus-$1$ twisted tensor
product decomposition, establish a three-level refinement of
curved Dunn additivity, and identify the precise frontier: a
\emph{bridge map} between two obstruction complexes whose
construction would close curved Dunn at all genera.
\end{abstract}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{The three levels}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Level 1: genus~$0$ (proved, uncurved Dunn)}
$\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}\big|_{g=0} =
\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}} \times E_1^{\mathrm{tr}}$.
The bar complex factors as an ordinary tensor product.

\subsection{Level 2: obstruction theory}
The obstruction to extending the genus-$0$ product to genus~$g$
lies in
$H^2\bigl(\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}}),\,
\mathrm{gr}^g\,\Bmod(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})),\, d_0\bigr)$.
At genus~$1$: controlled by $\kappa_R(A) \cdot \omega_1$ via
$\mathrm{Mon}(R)$.  Vanishes when $\mathrm{Mon}(R) = \mathrm{id}$.

\subsection{Level 3: twisted K\"unneth (the frontier)}

\begin{proposition}[Proved at genus~$1$]
$\Bmod(\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}})\big|_{g=1}
\simeq
\Bmod(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})\big|_{g=1}
\otimes^{\mathrm{Mon}(R)}
\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}})$
where $\otimes^{\mathrm{Mon}(R)}$ is the Eilenberg--Zilber
twisted tensor product.
\end{proposition}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{The two-complex distinction}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The genus-$g$ obstruction class $\mathrm{Ob}_g$ lives in two
complexes with different $H^2$:

\begin{definition}
\begin{enumerate}
\item The \emph{modular-bootstrap complex}
  $(\mathfrak{g}^A_{\mathrm{mod}}, d_{\Theta^{(0)}})$:
  the tree-twisted Maurer--Cartan recursion governing the
  genus tower $\{\Theta^{(g)}\}$.
  \textbf{Result:} $H^2 = 0$ at all $g \ge 1$ (proved).
  Every $\mathrm{Ob}_g$ is exact.

\item The \emph{curved-Dunn twisting-cochain complex}
  $\bigl(\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}}),\,
  \mathrm{gr}^g\,\Bmod(\mathrm{SC})),\, d_0\bigr)$:
  the Eilenberg--Zilber obstruction complex for extending
  the twisted tensor product from genus~$g-1$ to genus~$g$.
  \textbf{Result:} $H^2 = {?}$ (open).
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{conjecture}[The bridge]
\label{conj:bridge}
There exists a chain map
$\Phi\colon (\mathfrak{g}^A_{\mathrm{mod}}, d_{\Theta^{(0)}})
\to (\mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}}),\,
\Bmod(\mathrm{SC})), d_0)$
that is a quasi-isomorphism.
\end{conjecture}

If the bridge exists: $H^2 = 0$ transfers from the
modular-bootstrap complex to the curved-Dunn complex, and
curved Dunn holds at all genera.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{The Annals-grade proof spine}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[Target]
The modular bar complex of $\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}$
decomposes as a twisted tensor product at all genera:
\[
\Bmod(\mathcal{O}^{\Ainf\text{-ch}}, A)
\;\simeq\;
\Bmod(\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}, A)
\;\otimes^{\tau}\;
\mathbf{B}(E_1^{\mathrm{tr}}, A)
\]
with twisting cochain $\tau$ built recursively from $R$-matrix
monodromy at each genus-raising clutching.
\end{theorem}

\subsection*{Step 1: Construct the bridge $\Phi$}
The modular-bootstrap complex governs deformations of the
genus-tower.  The curved-Dunn complex governs deformations of
the tensor product.  Both are controlled by the same underlying
data: the $R$-matrix monodromy at sewing circles.  The bridge
$\Phi$ should send $\Theta^{(g)}$ (the genus-$g$ partition
function datum) to $\tau_g$ (the genus-$g$ twisting cochain).

At genus~$1$: $\Theta^{(1)}$ is the annular bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ann}}$ with $\mathrm{Mon}(R)$ insertion.  The
twisting cochain $\tau_1$ is the $\mathrm{Mon}(R)$ insertion.
So $\Phi|_{g=1}$ is the identity (they are the same datum).

At genus~$2$: $\Theta^{(2)}$ is determined by the genus-$2$
stable-graph clutching.  The twisting cochain $\tau_2$ is
determined by the Maurer--Cartan equation
$d_0\tau_2 + \frac{1}{2}[\tau_1, \tau_1] = 0$.  The bridge
must identify the genus-$2$ clutching data with the MC solution.

\subsection*{Step 2: Prove $\Phi$ is a quasi-isomorphism}
Use the Bellier-Mill\`es--Drummond-Cole model structure on
curved operads.  Both complexes are cofibrant resolutions
of the same curved modular operad (from different directions:
bootstrap from the genus tower, Dunn from the product
decomposition).  Quillen equivalence gives $\Phi$ is a
quasi-isomorphism.

\subsection*{Step 3: Transfer $H^2 = 0$}
With $\Phi$ a quasi-isomorphism: $H^2 = 0$ in the
modular-bootstrap complex implies $H^2 = 0$ in the
curved-Dunn complex.  Every $\mathrm{Ob}_g$ is exact.
The twisted K\"unneth extends to all genera.

\section{Storyboard}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Phase 1.}  Genus-$2$ bridge.  Construct
  $\Phi|_{g=2}$ explicitly for Heisenberg (where both complexes
  are scalar and the bridge is a scalar identity) and
  $V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ (where the matrix structure is
  non-trivial).

\item \textbf{Phase 2.}  Abstract bridge construction.
  Use the universal property of the modular bar construction
  (as a left adjoint to the modular coalgebra forgetful functor)
  to construct $\Phi$ at all genera simultaneously.

\item \textbf{Phase 3.}  Model-structure argument.
  Prove both complexes are cofibrant resolutions of the same
  curved modular operad using the Bellier-Mill\`es--Drummond-Cole
  model structure.

\item \textbf{Phase 4.}  Assembly.  Combine bridge +
  quasi-isomorphism + $H^2 = 0$ transfer + Eilenberg--Zilber
  to get the all-genera twisted K\"unneth.
\end{enumerate}

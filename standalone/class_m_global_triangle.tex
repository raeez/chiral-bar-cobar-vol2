\section{The problem}

The bulk--boundary--line triangle asserts quasi-isomorphisms
\[
A_{\mathrm{bulk}}
\;\simeq\;
Z^{\mathrm{der}}(B_{\mathrm{boundary}})
\;\simeq\;
Z^{\mathrm{der}}(C_{\mathrm{line}})
\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{HH}^*_{\mathrm{ch}}(A^!_{\mathrm{line}}).
\]

\begin{theorem}[Proved]
The global triangle closes for all boundary-linear families
(classes G, L, C): Heisenberg, lattice VOAs, affine $V_k(\mathfrak{g})$
at generic level, $\beta\gamma$-systems.  The proof uses the
derived HKR theorem for boundary-linear superpotentials, the
vanishing of the quartic contact class $q_4 = 0$ (since $m_n = 0$
for $n \ge 3$), and Morita invariance of derived centres.
\end{theorem}

\begin{conjecture}
The global triangle closes for class M (Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$).
\end{conjecture}

\section{The two obstructions}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Non-formal $\Ainf$ structure.}
  For class M, $m_k \neq 0$ for all $k \ge 3$ (infinite shadow depth).
  The quartic contact class $q_4(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = 10/[c(5c+22)] \neq 0$.
  The pointwise reduction to the derived centre is obstructed at
  degree~$3$ and above.

\item \textbf{Nonlinear DS superpotential.}
  The Drinfeld--Sokolov boundary conditions produce a superpotential
  nonlinear in normal directions.  The derived HKR theorem
  (boundary-linear bulk--boundary) does not apply.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Cohomological compatibility is complete}

The DS-induced map on chiral Hochschild cohomology is verified at
all degrees:
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^0$: $\mathbb{C} \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathbb{C}$;
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^1$: $\mathfrak{sl}_2 \to 0$
(BRST-exactness, inner absorption, screening);
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^2$: $\mathbb{C} \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathbb{C}$
($\Theta_k \mapsto (dc/dk)\,\Theta_c$, nonzero at generic~$k$);
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^{\ge 3}$: $0 \to 0$.
The obstruction is purely chain-level.

\section{Attack path: DS transport via HPL}

\textbf{Strategy C (strongest).}
The global triangle holds for $V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ (class L).
DS reduction gives $\mathrm{Vir}_c$.  Bar commutes with DS (proved).
If chiral Hochschild cochains also commute with DS at chain level,
the triangle transports.

The missing input: an HPL (homological perturbation lemma) transfer
for $\RHom_A(M,N)$ through the DS strong deformation retract
$(p, i, h)$.  The contracting homotopy~$h$ has ghost number~$-1$.
The HPL higher homotopies $h_n$ have ghost number~$-n$.  The
ghost-number bottleneck that trivialises the DS coproduct (proved)
acts on the same filtration and may force the transferred $\RHom$
to be strict.  At fixed conformal weight, the SDR data are explicit
finite-dimensional matrices.

\section{Open questions}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Does the ghost-number bottleneck force the HPL transfer of
  $\RHom$ to terminate at finite order?
\item Can the $D^{\mathrm{co}}$-level BV=bar identification
  (proved for all classes) be promoted to ordinary derived category
  for class~M?
\item Is there a deformation argument from class C ($\beta\gamma$,
  where the triangle holds) to class M (Virasoro)?
\end{enumerate}

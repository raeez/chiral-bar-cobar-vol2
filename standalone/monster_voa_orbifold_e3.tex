\begin{abstract}
The Moonshine module $V^\natural$ is a chain-level target of the
universal holography functor~$\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}$. It has central
charge $c = 24$, a conformal vector, and no freely generated PVA
associated graded, so it lies outside the scope of the
Khan--Zeng theorem. The inscribed proof (Volume~II,
Chapter~\ref*{ch:monster-chain-level-e3-top-platonic}) proceeds
through the Leech lattice VOA: $V_\Lambda$ carries chain-level
$E_3$-topological structure from abelian holomorphic Chern--Simons
at charge lattice~$\Lambda$, the Dijkgraaf--Witten anomaly class
of the $\Z/2$-orbifold $\alpha_{\mathrm{orb}} \in
H^3(B\Z/2;\mathrm{U}(1)) \cong \Z/2$ is trivial
(Kapustin--Saulina cocycle; $\sigma$-equivariance of the FLM
cocycle; $\Lambda^\sigma = 0$), and finite-orbifold BV descent plus
Dong--Mason quantum Galois uniqueness transport the $E_3$-top
structure to $V^\natural$. The van~Ekeren--M\"oller--Scheithauer
classification handles the other Schellekens strata by separate
mechanisms, inscribed as
Theorem~\ref*{thm:schellekens-71-all-alpha-zero}.
This standalone records the structural scope, the two principal
Annals-grade inputs (DW anomaly vanishing; Dong--Mason
uniqueness), and the position of the result inside the class-$M$
topologization landscape, in which the generic class-$M$ statement is
weight-completed/pro-ambient while the Monster is an exceptional
finite-orbifold descent from class~G.
\end{abstract}

\section{Structural scope of the orbifold theorem}

The inscribed theorem
(Theorem~\ref*{thm:monster-chain-level-e3-top}, Vol.~II
Chapter~\ref*{ch:monster-chain-level-e3-top-platonic}) has three
clauses: (i)~$\alpha_{\mathrm{orb}} = 0$, (ii)~chain-level
$E_3$-topological structure on the descended orbifold
Costello--Gwilliam factorisation algebra
$\cF_\Lambda^{\mathrm{orb}}$, whose boundary chiral algebra is
$V^\natural$, and (iii)~$V^\natural$ lies in the chain-level image of
the universal holography functor~$\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}$
(Theorem~\ref*{thm:universal-holography-functor}). This standalone restates each clause
at the level required for the programme digest; the full proofs are
inscribed in the Vol.~II chapter.

\begin{theorem}[Monster orbifold $E_3$-topological descent; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:monster-e3-standalone}
Let $V^\natural = V_\Lambda^+ \oplus V_\Lambda^{\mathrm{tw},+}$ be
the FLM Moonshine module (the $\widetilde\sigma$-invariant part of
the $\Z/2$-orbifold of the Leech lattice VOA, with canonical cocycle
lift $c(\lambda) \equiv 1$). Then the descended orbifold
Costello--Gwilliam factorisation algebra $\cF_\Lambda^{\mathrm{orb}}$
carries a chain-level $E_3$-topological algebra structure; its boundary
chiral algebra is $V^\natural$; and $V^\natural$ lies in the
chain-level image of~$\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The three-step argument from
Theorem~\ref*{thm:monster-chain-level-e3-top}:

\emph{Step 1 (FLM cocycle $\sigma$-equivariance).} The Frenkel--Lepowsky--Meurman
cocycle $\epsilon\colon \Lambda \times \Lambda \to \{\pm 1\}$ satisfies
$\epsilon(\sigma\lambda, \sigma\mu) = \epsilon(-\lambda, -\mu) =
\epsilon(\lambda, \mu)$ as a chain identity; the fixed sublattice
$\Lambda^\sigma$ is zero because $\Lambda$ is torsion-free, so
$\epsilon|_{\Lambda^\sigma} = \epsilon(0, 0) = 1$.

\emph{Step 2 (Kapustin--Saulina DW cocycle).} The lattice-involution
DW cocycle reads
$\alpha_{\mathrm{DW}}(\sigma) = \mathrm{sign}\bigl(\det(1 - \sigma|_\Lambda)\bigr)
\cdot [\epsilon|_{\Lambda^\sigma}]$. For $\sigma = -1$ on rank-$24$
Leech, $\det(1 - \sigma) = 2^{24} > 0$ and
$[\epsilon|_{\Lambda^\sigma}] = 0$, so $\alpha_{\mathrm{DW}} = 0$.

\emph{Step 3 (Twisted Jacobi plus Dong--Mason).} The FLM twisted
module $V_\Lambda^{\mathrm{tw}}$ has half-spinor ground-state
multiplicity $2^{12}$, and FLM Chapters 8--10 prove the twisted
Jacobi identity at chain level. Dong--Mason quantum Galois uniqueness
(Journal of Algebra 214, 1999) fixes the orbifold VOA up to
isomorphism. Chain-level $E_3$-topological structure passes to the
finite orbifold BV model when the orbifold BV anomaly vanishes; Steps
1--2 establish that vanishing.
Therefore $V^\natural$ inherits chain-level $E_3$-topological
structure from $V_\Lambda$ (abelian holomorphic Chern--Simons at
charge lattice $\Lambda$, Theorem~\ref*{thm:E3-topological-km}
restricted to the abelian case).
\end{proof}

\section{Schellekens 71 and the EMS bijection}

The van~Ekeren--M\"oller--Scheithauer classification exhibits every
one of the 71 holomorphic $c = 24$ VOAs as a cyclic-group orbifold
of a Niemeier lattice VOA; the classification stratifies into three
types tied to the Niemeier parent and the orbifold order.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Type A} (pure Niemeier, 24 cases): $N \in \{1\}$, no
orbifold. Chain-level $E_3$-top is the abelian-case of
Theorem~\ref*{thm:E3-topological-km}.
\item \emph{Type B} (Leech $\Z/2$, 1 case): the Moonshine module
$V^\natural$; Theorem~\ref{thm:monster-e3-standalone}.
\item \emph{Type C} (Leech or Niemeier $\Z/n$ for
$n \in \{3,4,5,6,7,8,10,\geq 12\}$, 46 cases): generalised deep
holes; cyclic-orbifold level matching plus M\"oller--Scheithauer
deep-hole bijection carries the DW anomaly computation.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{theorem}[Schellekens 71; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:schellekens-71-standalone}
For each of the 71 holomorphic $c = 24$ VOAs in the Schellekens list,
the Dijkgraaf--Witten anomaly class of the EMS orbifold
presentation vanishes: $\alpha_{\mathrm{orb}} = 0$. Consequently each
carries chain-level $E_3$-topological structure and lies in the
chain-level image of the universal holography functor
$\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}$.
\end{theorem}

This is Theorem~\ref*{thm:schellekens-71-all-alpha-zero}, inscribed
in Vol.~II Chapter~\ref*{ch:schellekens-71-alpha-classification-platonic}.
The three type-specific propositions
(\ref*{prop:schellekens-typeA-alpha-zero},
\ref*{prop:schellekens-typeB-alpha-zero},
\ref*{cor:schellekens-typeC-alpha-zero}) assemble to the main theorem
through Proposition~\ref*{prop:ems-level-matching}.

\section{Position in the topologization landscape}

The currently proved scopes are:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item affine Kac--Moody $V_k(\fg)$ at $k \neq -h^\vee$
(Costello--Gaiotto Sugawara route;
Theorem~\ref*{thm:E3-topological-km}),
\item the class-$M$ DS landscape in the weight-completed/pro ambient,
with strict original-complex chain lift only in the stated conditional
scope,
\item freely generated PVAs (Khan--Zeng),
\item $V^\natural$ and every Schellekens $c = 24$ VOA (this note),
\item Heisenberg and all lattice VOAs (abelian hCS).
\end{itemize}
Chain-level $E_3$-topological structure on the weight-completed
bar complex is proved for class-$M$ principal algebras (Virasoro,
$\mathcal{W}_N$) via the DS--Hochschild and MC5 pattern; the strict
original-complex chain lift has the conditional status recorded in
the chiral Higher Deligne and universal-holography chapters. The
Leech $\Z/2$ inscription does \emph{not} change that generic class-M
scope, because $V^\natural$ is reached by finite-orbifold descent from
the class-G lattice model.
